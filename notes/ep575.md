# EP-575

## Problem Statement
If a finite forbidden family \(F\) contains a bipartite graph, must there exist a bipartite \(G\in F\) such that
\[
\operatorname{ex}(n;G)\ll_F \operatorname{ex}(n;F)\ ?
\]

## Literature / Context
- This asks whether the asymptotically hardest member of \(F\) can always be chosen bipartite when \(F\) has at least one bipartite member.
- The statement is a structural reduction principle for extremal families.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built a finite proxy comparing:
  - \(\operatorname{ex}(n;\{C_{2k-1},C_{2k}\})\),
  - \(\operatorname{ex}(n;C_{2k})\) (chosen bipartite member),
  for \(k=2,3\) over matched \(n\)-windows.
- No theorem-level proof yet, but this directly probes the target mechanism.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Deep run (\(\sim 17.1\)s):
  - \(k=2\): \(\operatorname{ex}(n;C_4)\) is about \(1.08\times\operatorname{ex}(n;\{C_3,C_4\})\) in tested windows.
  - \(k=3\): \(\operatorname{ex}(n;C_6)\) is within about \(1\%\) of \(\operatorname{ex}(n;\{C_5,C_6\})\).
- This is consistent with the conjectural reduction, at least for these model families.

## Next Proof Targets
- Prove family-to-member comparison for families of the form \(\{C_{2k-1},C_{2k}\}\) as a first formal lemma.
- Extend to mixed families by decomposing odd-cycle constraints into stability corrections over a bipartite core.
