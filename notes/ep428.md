# EP-428 partial resolution

## Statement
Is there `A subset N` with positive lower prime-density profile such that for
infinitely many `n`, all numbers `n-a` (for relevant `a in A`) are prime?

## What is resolved
Background records a conditional result (prime k-tuple conjecture): the property
holds with `limsup` in place of `liminf`.

## What remains open in this note
The stronger lower-density (`liminf`) formulation is not resolved by the cited
conditional theorem.

## Status
- conditional `limsup` version: known.
- `liminf` positive-density version: unresolved.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Settle whether there exists \(A\subset\mathbb N\) with positive lower density profile such that infinitely many \(n\) make all \(n-a\) (relevant \(a\in A\)) prime, in liminf-strength form.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a uniform prime-tuple lower bound with density control: for admissible \(A\)-patterns of size \(t\), count of \(n\le x\) with all \(n-a\) prime is
\[
\gg \frac{x}{(\log x)^t}
\]
uniformly over a positive-density family of truncations \(A\cap[1,X]\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (difference-set prime condition)
Condition “all \(n-a\) prime” is a prime \(t\)-tuple condition on shifts \(\{a\}\).

**Proof.**
It requires simultaneous primality of linear forms \(n-a\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (limsup vs liminf gap)
Limsup positivity follows from infinitely many successful \(n\), while liminf positivity requires uniform success frequency across growing windows.

**Proof.**
By definitions of limsup/liminf of normalized counts. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies liminf statement)
If A yields uniform lower counts for a positive-density family, liminf-positive density follows.

**Proof.**
Normalize the lower bound by window size and pass to limits using uniformity. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is obtaining prime-tuple lower bounds uniform enough across many shift-patterns to upgrade limsup to liminf.
