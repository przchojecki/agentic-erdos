# EP-852

## Problem Statement
Let $d_n=p_{n+1}-p_n$ be consecutive prime gaps. Let $h(x)$ denote the longest run of consecutive indices $n<x$ for which the gaps are pairwise distinct. Estimate $h(x)$.

## Literature
- Brun-sieve arguments imply $h(x)\to\infty$.
- Quantitative growth rates remain open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Computed primes up to $2\cdot 10^7$ and corresponding gap sequence.
- At multiple index checkpoints $x$, computed exact longest distinct-gap window length in $d_1,\dots,d_x$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Observed growth from $h(10^3)=9$ to $h(1.2\cdot10^6)=18$.
- The growth is visible but slow on this range, consistent with qualitative divergence without suggesting a sharp asymptotic law.
