# EP-1016

## Problem Statement
Study the minimal edge excess above $n$ needed to force (or guarantee existence of) pancyclic behavior in the EP-1016 formulation.

## Literature
- Pancyclicity thresholds are classical extremal objects; finite witness constructions give rigorous upper bounds for existence-type parameters.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented standalone random witness search with exact cycle-length verification:
  - random $n$-vertex graphs with $m=n+h$ edges,
  - exact detection of present cycle lengths,
  - pancyclic check requiring all lengths $3,\dots,n$,
  - first successful $h$ per $n$ recorded.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In this run, witness excesses found were:
  - $h(8)\le2$,
  - $h(10)\le3$,
  - $h(12)\le4$,
  - $h(14)\le6$.
- These are finite constructive upper bounds only; absence of a witness at smaller $h$ in random search is not a lower bound proof.

## Status
- Open asymptotically.
- Added standalone exact-cycle pancyclic witness search profile for small $n$.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine minimal excess \(h(n)\) (above \(n\) edges) needed for pancyclic behavior in EP-1016 formulation.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a robust expansion lemma: if \(e(G)\ge n+h\) and every proper induced subgraph has average degree below a threshold, then \(G\) contains cycles in a full interval of lengths whose span is \(\gg h\), eventually covering \(3,\dots,n\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (pancyclicity criterion)
If \(G\) contains cycles of every length \(3,\dots,n\), then \(G\) is pancyclic by definition.

**Proof.**
Immediate from definition. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (edge-excess to average degree)
\[
e(G)=n+h\quad\Rightarrow\quad \bar d(G)=2+\frac{2h}{n}.
\]

**Proof.**
Average degree equals \(2e(G)/n\). Substitute \(e(G)\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies asymptotic upper bounds on \(h(n)\))
If A turns excess \(h\) into complete cycle-length coverage for large \(n\), then \(h(n)\) is bounded above by that threshold function.

**Proof.**
Any graph meeting the threshold satisfies pancyclic conclusion by A; minimal forcing excess is at most that value. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The key gap is converting moderate global edge excess into guaranteed full cycle-spectrum coverage rather than long but incomplete intervals of lengths.
