# EP-1016

## Problem Statement
Study the minimal edge excess above $n$ needed to force (or guarantee existence of) pancyclic behavior in the EP-1016 formulation.

## Literature
- Pancyclicity thresholds are classical extremal objects; finite witness constructions give rigorous upper bounds for existence-type parameters.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented standalone random witness search with exact cycle-length verification:
  - random $n$-vertex graphs with $m=n+h$ edges,
  - exact detection of present cycle lengths,
  - pancyclic check requiring all lengths $3,\dots,n$,
  - first successful $h$ per $n$ recorded.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In this run, witness excesses found were:
  - $h(8)\le2$,
  - $h(10)\le3$,
  - $h(12)\le4$,
  - $h(14)\le6$.
- These are finite constructive upper bounds only; absence of a witness at smaller $h$ in random search is not a lower bound proof.
