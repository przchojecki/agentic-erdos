# EP-1131

## Problem Statement
For interpolation nodes $x_1<\cdots<x_n$ in $[-1,1]$, define
\[
I(x_1,\dots,x_n)=\int_{-1}^1\sum_k |\ell_k(x)|^2\,dx,
\]
with Lagrange basis functions $\ell_k$.
What is $\min I$ asymptotically? In particular, is
\[
\min I=2-(1+o(1))\frac1n?
\]

## Literature
- Known bounds imply
\[
2-O\!\left(\frac{(\log n)^2}{n}\right)\le \min I \le 2-\frac{2}{2n-1}.
\]
- Exact first-order constant remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone deep numeric minimization (`scripts/ep1131.mjs`):
  - barycentric evaluation of $\sum_k \ell_k(x)^2$,
  - Simpson quadrature on dense grids,
  - long-budget local node optimization for each $n$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
Deep run (`DEPTH=4`) with
- $n\in\{8,12,16,20,24,28,32\}$,
- 14s optimization budget per $n$,
- grid size 1600 for numerical integration.

Found values satisfy:
- $I$ very close to 2 from below,
- $n(2-I)$ stays around 1 in this range (roughly $0.98$ to $1.11$),
- profiles are numerically consistent with the conjectural $2-c/n$ scale and with $c$ near 1.

Recovered near-optimal nodes are close to classical Chebyshev-like distributions for larger $n$.

Caveat: this is numerical optimization and does not prove global minimizers.

## Status
- Partially resolved in literature (sharp scale bounds known).
- Added deep standalone numerical evidence on finite-$n$ asymptotics and candidate constants.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove asymptotic
\[
\min I = 2-\frac{1+o(1)}{n}.
\]

### Step A: Sharp Lower Bound Improvement
Upgrade
\[
2-O\!\left(\frac{(\log n)^2}{n}\right)
\]
to
\[
2-\frac{C_1+o(1)}{n}
\]
with explicit \(C_1\).

### Step B: Matching Constructive Upper Bound
Construct node sets with
\[
I\le 2-\frac{C_2+o(1)}{n}
\]
and \(C_2\) matching Step A constant.

### Step C: Stability/Uniqueness at First Order
Show near-optimal families are \(o(1)\)-close (after normalization) to the
extremal node distribution, fixing the first-order constant.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a first-order expansion theorem for
\[
\int_{-1}^1\sum_k |\ell_k(x)|^2\,dx
\]
under perturbations of near-Chebyshev nodes, with error \(o(1/n)\).
