# EP-840

## Problem Statement
Let $f(N)$ be the largest size of a quasi-Sidon set $A\subseteq\{1,\dots,N\}$, meaning
$$|A+A|=(1+o(1))\binom{|A|}{2}.$$
How does $f(N)$ grow?

## Literature
- Erdos-Freud proved bounds of order $N^{1/2}$:
  $$\left(\frac{2}{\sqrt3}+o(1)\right)N^{1/2}\le f(N)\le (2+o(1))N^{1/2}.$$
- Asymptotic constant optimization remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built deeper randomized greedy construction enforcing a ratio threshold
  $$\frac{|A+A|}{\binom{|A|}{2}}\ge \tau.$$
- Ran 80 trials for each $(N,\tau)$ over
  $$N\in\{500,1000,2000,3000,5000\},\quad \tau\in\{0.9,0.95,0.98,0.99\}.$$

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Best and average sizes scale like $c\sqrt N$ with $c$ roughly in the expected finite range.
- Increasing $\tau$ lowers achievable size, consistent with stronger quasi-Sidon quality constraints.
- Finite data supports $\sqrt N$ growth but does not sharpen asymptotic constants.
