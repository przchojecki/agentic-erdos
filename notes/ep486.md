# EP-486 partial resolution

## Statement
For forbidden residue sets X_n mod n over n in A, ask whether the uncovered set B
always has logarithmic density.

## What is resolved
Background gives positive result when each X_n={0} (Davenport-Erdos), with an
alternative elementary proof.

## What remains open in this note
The general case with arbitrary residue subsets X_n is unresolved.

## Status
- singleton-residue case solved.
- full multi-residue generalization open.

## Literature Search Update

- The singleton case \(X_n=\{0\}\) is tied to the Davenport–Erdős logarithmic-density theorem for multiples/covering systems.
- The general multi-residue setting appears to remain open in the current problem record and does not have a standard one-line extension theorem in the same strength.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Show logarithmic density exists for \(B\) under arbitrary forbidden-residue families \((X_n)_{n\in A}\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a generalized harmonic almost-multiplicativity estimate:
\[
\sum_{\substack{m\le x\\ m\in B}}\frac1m
=
\left(\prod_{n\in A}\left(1-\frac{|X_n|}{n}\right)\right)\log x + O(1)
\]
under explicit overlap-control hypotheses weaker than independence.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (indicator factorization)
\[
1_B(m)=\prod_{n\in A}\mathbf 1_{m\bmod n\notin X_n}.
\]

**Proof.**
Membership means simultaneously avoiding every forbidden residue class set. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (log-density criterion)
Existence of limit
\[
\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac1{\log x}\sum_{\substack{m\le x\\ m\in B}}\frac1m
\]
is exactly existence of logarithmic density of \(B\).

**Proof.**
Definition of logarithmic density. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies theorem)
If A provides asymptotic linear-in-\(\log x\) harmonic mass, B2 gives logarithmic density existence.

**Proof.**
Normalize by \(\log x\) and pass to limit. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is handling high-order correlation among residue constraints when \(X_n\) are large and overlapping.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove logarithmic density existence for uncovered set \(B\) under arbitrary residue subsets \(X_n\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Establish a generalized Davenport-Erdős criterion: harmonic sums of indicators of exclusion classes admit a convergent-renormalized limit under mild overlap control for arbitrary \(X_n\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (indicator decomposition)
\[
1_B(m)=\prod_{n\in A}\mathbf 1_{m\bmod n\notin X_n}.
\]

**Proof.**
\(m\in B\) iff it avoids every forbidden residue set. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (harmonic-density form)
Log density existence is equivalent to convergence of
\[
\frac{1}{\log x}\sum_{\substack{m\le x\\ m\in B}}\frac1m.
\]

**Proof.**
Definition of logarithmic density. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies existence)
If A gives limit of normalized harmonic sums, logarithmic density exists.

**Proof.**
Direct from B2. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is controlling high-order overlap terms when many \(X_n\) are non-singleton and strongly correlated.
