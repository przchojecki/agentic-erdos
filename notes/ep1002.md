# EP-1002 resolved in background (as written)

## Statement
For
`f(alpha,n)=(1/log n) sum_{k<=n} (1/2-{alpha k})`,
ask existence of an asymptotic distribution over `alpha in (0,1)`.

## Resolution already in background
Kesten's theorem is stated for
`f(alpha,beta,n)=(1/log n) sum_{k<=n} (1/2-{beta+alpha k})`
with explicit limiting distribution. Setting `beta=0` gives the exact function in the
statement.

## Conclusion
The written statement follows directly from the cited more general theorem.
(Background-derived closure, not a new result here.)

## Formal reduction to the cited theorem
Define
\[
f(\alpha,\beta,n):=\frac1{\log n}\sum_{k\le n}\left(\frac12-\{\beta+\alpha k\}\right).
\]
Kesten's theorem (as cited in background) gives convergence in distribution of \(f(\alpha,\beta,n)\) over \(\alpha\in(0,1)\) for fixed \(\beta\).

Setting \(\beta=0\) yields exactly
\[
f(\alpha,n)=\frac1{\log n}\sum_{k\le n}\left(\frac12-\{\alpha k\}\right).
\]
Hence the EP-1002 statement is an immediate special case.

So the problem is resolved as written, conditional only on the cited theorem.
