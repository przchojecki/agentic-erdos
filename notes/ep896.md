# EP-896

## Problem Statement
For $A,B\subseteq\{1,\dots,N\}$, let $F(A,B)$ be the number of integers $m$ that have exactly one representation
$$
m=ab,\qquad a\in A,\ b\in B.
$$
Determine the maximal possible value of $F(A,B)$ as a function of $N$.

## Literature
- Known two-sided bounds (as summarized in existing project notes) are of shape
$$
(1+o(1))\frac{N^2}{\log N}\ \le\ \max_{A,B}F(A,B)
$$
and an upper bound of type
$$
\max_{A,B}F(A,B)\ll \frac{N^2}{(\log N)^\delta(\log\log N)^{3/2}}
$$
for explicit $\delta>0$.
- Exact leading term and sharp logarithmic exponent remain open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Exact exhaustive optimization for small $N\in\{6,7,8,9,10\}$ by checking all pairs $(A,B)$.
- Deep constructive scans for larger $N\in\{1000,2000,4000,8000,12000\}$ using two families:
  - $A=[1,\lfloor N/2\rfloor]$, $B=$ primes in $(N/2,N]$.
  - $A=[1,N]$, $B=$ primes in $(N/2,N]$.
- Recorded unique-product counts, collision bins, and normalized ratio against $N^2/\log N$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For $A=[1,\lfloor N/2\rfloor]$, $B$ as above, collision bins are exactly $0$ in all tested deep cases, giving fully unique products in this construction.
- At $N=12000$ this construction gives $F=3{,}930{,}000$ and
$$
\frac{F}{N^2/\log N}\approx 0.25634.
$$
- Using full $A=[1,N]$ increases raw count but introduces collisions; at $N=12000$:
  - unique count $7{,}431{,}630$,
  - collision bins $214{,}185$,
  - normalized ratio $\approx 0.48474$.
- Small-$N$ exact maxima (up to $N=10$) provide calibration examples but are too small to determine asymptotic constants.
