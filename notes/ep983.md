# EP-983 deeper attempt

## Statement
Study `f(k,n)` and in particular whether
`2*pi(sqrt(n)) - f(pi(n)+1,n) -> infinity`.

## Exact finite result proved in this attempt
I computed `f(pi(n)+1,n)` exactly for every `8<=n<=24` by full enumeration of all
`A subset [1,n]` with `|A|=pi(n)+1`.

Data file:
- `data/ep983_exact_small_n.json`

Sample values:
- `n=20`: `f=4`, so `2*pi(sqrt(20))-f=0`
- `n=24`: `f=5`, so `2*pi(sqrt(24))-f=-1`

## Interpretation
Finite exact values are informative but do not decide the asymptotic limit question.

## Rigorous finite consequence
Because the computation is exact for all \(8\le n\le 24\), the recorded values are theorems in that range.
In particular:
\[
2\pi(\sqrt{24})-f(\pi(24)+1,24)=-1<0.
\]
So the difference is not even nonnegative uniformly at small \(n\), and any eventual-divergence claim must allow substantial finite irregularity before asymptotic behavior emerges.
