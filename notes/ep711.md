# EP-711

## Problem Statement
Let \(f(n,m)\) be minimal such that interval \((m,m+f(n,m))\) contains distinct integers \(a_1,\dots,a_n\) with \(k\mid a_k\) for all \(1\le k\le n\).
Study:
\[
\max_m f(n,m) \le n^{1+o(1)}
\]
and
\[
\max_m\bigl(f(n,m)-f(n,n)\bigr)\to\infty.
\]

## Literature
- The divergence statement for \(\max_m(f(n,m)-f(n,n))\) is now known affirmative (recent 2026 update).
- The near-linear upper-growth question remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented exact Hopcroft-Karp-based computation of \(f(n,m)\) for sampled ranges.
- Deepened the sampled \(m\)-range to detect larger finite gaps for moderate \(n\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For \(n=20,30,40,50\) and sampled \(m\in[n,n+1000]\), observed max gaps
  \(f(n,m)-f(n,n)=10,13,17,21\), respectively.
- Sampled maxima ratios \(\max_m f(n,m)/n\) are around \(1.9\)-\(1.94\) in these ranges.
- Finite data is consistent with gap growth, matching the now-known qualitative theorem.

## Status
- Partially resolved globally (second statement proved; first remains open).
- Added deeper standalone exact computations over wider \(m\)-windows.

## Added Proof Layer (No New Computation)

### Closed Substatement
The divergence claim
\[
\max_m\bigl(f(n,m)-f(n,n)\bigr)\to\infty
\]
is now proved in the cited 2026 update, so that part is closed.

### Remaining Open Substatement
The near-linear upper-growth target
\[
\max_m f(n,m)\le n^{1+o(1)}
\]
remains open.

### Blocking Lemma
- Need a uniform interval matching theorem controlling the worst-case local
  divisibility assignment overhead in terms of \(n^{o(1)}\), not just existence
  of feasible matchings.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove
\[
\max_m f(n,m)\le n^{1+o(1)}.
\]

### Step A: Hall-Defect Bound at Scale \(L\)
For intervals of length \(L\), bound Hall deficits for the bipartite graph
(positions vs divisibility classes) by \(L^{1-\eta}\) uniformly in \(m\).

### Step B: Defect Repair with Sparse Augmentation
Show every deficit can be repaired by enlarging interval length by only
\(L^{o(1)}\), giving feasible perfect matching for the divisibility assignment.

### Step C: Optimize \(L\sim n\)
Set \(L\) near \(n\) and iterate multi-scale repairs to derive
\[
f(n,m)\le n\cdot n^{o(1)}
\]
uniformly in \(m\).

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a near-optimal Hall-uniformity statement for residue-class availability in
arbitrary shifted intervals:
\[
\forall S\subseteq [n],\quad |N_m(S)|\ge |S|-o(n),
\]
uniformly in \(m\), with an error term summable across scales.

### Method Directions
- Dense matching + additive combinatorics in shifted interval residue systems.
- Tao-style transference from average divisor availability to worst-case subsets.
- Randomized nibble/LLL repair of residual unmatched classes.
