# EP-711

## Problem Statement
Let \(f(n,m)\) be minimal such that interval \((m,m+f(n,m))\) contains distinct integers \(a_1,\dots,a_n\) with \(k\mid a_k\) for all \(1\le k\le n\).
Study:
\[
\max_m f(n,m) \le n^{1+o(1)}
\]
and
\[
\max_m\bigl(f(n,m)-f(n,n)\bigr)\to\infty.
\]

## Literature
- The divergence statement for \(\max_m(f(n,m)-f(n,n))\) is now known affirmative (recent 2026 update).
- The near-linear upper-growth question remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented exact Hopcroft-Karp-based computation of \(f(n,m)\) for sampled ranges.
- Deepened the sampled \(m\)-range to detect larger finite gaps for moderate \(n\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For \(n=20,30,40,50\) and sampled \(m\in[n,n+1000]\), observed max gaps
  \(f(n,m)-f(n,n)=10,13,17,21\), respectively.
- Sampled maxima ratios \(\max_m f(n,m)/n\) are around \(1.9\)-\(1.94\) in these ranges.
- Finite data is consistent with gap growth, matching the now-known qualitative theorem.

## Status
- Partially resolved globally (second statement proved; first remains open).
- Added deeper standalone exact computations over wider \(m\)-windows.
