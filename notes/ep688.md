# EP-688

## Problem Statement
Let \(\epsilon_n\) be maximal such that one can choose residues \(a_p\pmod p\) for all primes \(n^{\epsilon_n}<p\le n\) covering every integer in \([1,n]\).
Estimate \(\epsilon_n\), especially whether \(\epsilon_n=o(1)\).

## Literature
- Known lower bound: \(\epsilon_n\gg \frac{\log\log\log n}{\log\log n}\).
- Whether \(\epsilon_n\to 0\) remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented stochastic covering search over prime windows \((n^\epsilon,n]\).
- For each \(n\), scanned a grid of \(\epsilon\) and tracked best coverage fraction of \([1,n]\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In tested grid (\(\epsilon\le 0.4\)), no full cover found for \(n=200\) to \(3200\).
- Best coverage fractions remain high but below 1 (roughly \(0.96\) to \(0.99\)).
- This finite profile suggests nontrivial difficulty in achieving complete cover with only large primes, consistent with a positive lower threshold in practical regimes.

## Status
- Open.
- Added finite lower-threshold proxy evidence via explicit search.
