## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Establish positive lower density of numbers representable as
\[
n=p+\lfloor C^k\rfloor
\]
for fixed \(C>1\), ideally for all \(C\) outside a thin exceptional set.

### Step A: Exponential-sequence distribution
Control distribution of \(\lfloor C^k\rfloor\) modulo \(q\) and in intervals.

### Step B: Romanoff-type convolution
Combine prime distribution and exponential-sequence distribution via additive
energy/sieve convolution.

### Step C: Density positivity
Derive positive lower density from main term dominance over error terms.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need uniform discrepancy bounds for \(\lfloor C^k\rfloor\) in residue classes
that are strong enough for lower-bound sieve across almost all \(C\).

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition CT1 (Representation count decomposition)
Let
\[
R_C(n)=\#\{(p,k): n=p+\lfloor C^k\rfloor\}.
\]
Then for \(N\),
\[
\sum_{n\le N} R_C(n)
= \sum_{k:\,\lfloor C^k\rfloor\le N}\pi(N-\lfloor C^k\rfloor).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Count representations by fixing \(k\), then summing allowable primes.
\(\square\)

### Proposition CT2 (Density reduction)
If one proves
\[
\sum_{n\le N}\mathbf 1_{R_C(n)\ge1}\ge \delta N
\]
for some \(\delta>0\) and large \(N\), then representable numbers have positive
lower density.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
This is exactly the definition of positive lower density.
\(\square\)
