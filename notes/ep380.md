# EP-380 partial resolution

## Statement
Let `B(x)` count integers up to `x` that lie in at least one "bad" interval,
where the largest prime factor of the interval product appears with multiplicity
at least two. Compare `B(x)` to counts of numbers with `P(n)^2|n`.

## What is resolved
Background provides a proved lower bound:
`B(x) > x^{1-o(1)}`.

It also gives an asymptotic formula for the comparison quantity
`#{n<=x : P(n)^2|n}`.

## What remains open in this note
The proposed asymptotic equivalence between `B(x)` and that comparison count is
not proved in the cited material.

## Status
- nontrivial lower bound and model-count asymptotic: known.
- exact asymptotic matching: unresolved.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Relate the bad-interval counting function \(B(x)\) to
\[
M(x):=\#\{n\le x:\ P(n)^2\mid n\}.
\]
The conjectural form is \(B(x)\sim M(x)\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Show that the bad-interval set decomposes as
\[
\mathcal B=\mathcal M\ \triangle\ \mathcal E
\]
with \(\#(\mathcal E\cap[1,x])=o(M(x))\), where \(\mathcal M:=\{n:\ P(n)^2\mid n\}\).

This directly yields \(B(x)\sim M(x)\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (containment from repeated top prime)
If an interval product condition forces a repeated occurrence of its top prime at \(n\), then necessarily \(P(n)^2\mid n(n+\Delta)\) for some controlled \(\Delta\).

**Proof.**
Repeated top-prime multiplicity means two factors in the interval contribute the same maximal prime \(p\). Hence two terms are divisible by \(p\), giving \(p^2\mid\) the product of two nearby entries; selecting one anchor gives the claimed structured divisibility. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (model dominance)
From Lemma B1 one gets \(B(x)\ll M(Cx)+E_1(x)\) for a fixed \(C\) and explicit structural error \(E_1\).

**Proof.**
Map each bad index to a nearby index where a squared largest-prime event occurs; bounded multiplicity of this map gives the \(M(Cx)\) term, with failures counted by \(E_1\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (reverse dominance with local correction)
There is a converse map implying \(M(x)\ll B(C'x)+E_2(x)\).

**Proof.**
Given \(P(n)^2\mid n\), embed \(n\) into a short interval whose product exhibits repeated top-prime behavior unless a local obstruction holds; obstructions contribute \(E_2(x)\). \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The open core is proving \(E_1(x)+E_2(x)=o(M(x))\): this requires fine control of exceptional local prime-configuration patterns near maximal prime factors.
