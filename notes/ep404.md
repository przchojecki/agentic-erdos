## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Classify boundedness/unboundedness of maximal \(p\)-adic divisibility
\[
f(a,p):=\sup v_p\!\left(a!+\sum_i b_i!\right)
\]
over distinct \(b_i\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a modular-carry criterion in factorial base: unbounded \(f(a,p)\) occurs iff one can build arbitrarily long compatible carry chains in base \(p\) across selected factorial indices.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (valuation equivalence)
\[
p^k\mid \left(a!+\sum_i b_i!\right)\iff v_p\!\left(a!+\sum_i b_i!\right)\ge k.
\]

**Proof.**
Definition of \(p\)-adic valuation. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (factorial valuation baseline)
\[
v_p(m!)=\sum_{j\ge1}\left\lfloor\frac{m}{p^j}\right\rfloor.
\]

**Proof.**
Legendre formula. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies dichotomy)
If A characterizes carry-chain feasibility, then each \((a,p)\) pair is classified as bounded or unbounded for \(f(a,p)\).

**Proof.**
Carry-chain feasibility gives existence/nonexistence of arbitrarily large \(k\) in B1. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The key gap is proving necessity of the carry-chain mechanism (not only sufficiency) for all \((a,p)\).
