# EP-1014

## Problem Statement
For fixed $k\ge 3$, investigate whether
\[
\frac{R(k,\ell+1)}{R(k,\ell)}\to 1\qquad (\ell\to\infty).
\]

## Literature
- Open in the difficult regime $k\ge3$.
- Recursion gives
\[
R(k,\ell+1)\le R(k,\ell)+R(k-1,\ell+1),
\]
so proving $R(k-1,\ell+1)=o(R(k,\ell))$ would imply the target ratio limit.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Kept the recursion-based proof template and reduction.
- Added a deeper standalone constructive proxy for the $k=3$ layer:
  - generate many triangle-free graphs,
  - compute exact independence numbers,
  - derive witness lower bounds for $R(3,t+1)$ from $\alpha(G)\le t$,
  - inspect successive witness-ratio profile as a finite analog of $R(3,\ell+1)/R(3,\ell)$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Constructive witnesses in this run gave bounds up to roughly
\[
R(3,18)\gtrsim 63.
\]
- Successive witness-ratio profile decreased toward $1$ in the scanned range (finite proxy behavior).
- This does not prove the asymptotic limit; it is lower-bound evidence only.
