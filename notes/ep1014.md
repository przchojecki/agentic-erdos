# EP-1014

## Problem Statement
For fixed $k\ge 3$, investigate whether
\[
\frac{R(k,\ell+1)}{R(k,\ell)}\to 1\qquad (\ell\to\infty).
\]

## Literature
- Open in the difficult regime $k\ge3$.
- Recursion gives
\[
R(k,\ell+1)\le R(k,\ell)+R(k-1,\ell+1),
\]
so proving $R(k-1,\ell+1)=o(R(k,\ell))$ would imply the target ratio limit.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Kept the recursion-based proof template and reduction.
- Added a deeper standalone constructive proxy for the $k=3$ layer:
  - generate many triangle-free graphs,
  - compute exact independence numbers,
  - derive witness lower bounds for $R(3,t+1)$ from $\alpha(G)\le t$,
  - inspect successive witness-ratio profile as a finite analog of $R(3,\ell+1)/R(3,\ell)$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Constructive witnesses in this run gave bounds up to roughly
\[
R(3,18)\gtrsim 63.
\]
- Successive witness-ratio profile decreased toward $1$ in the scanned range (finite proxy behavior).
- This does not prove the asymptotic limit; it is lower-bound evidence only.

## Status
- Open.
- Added deeper standalone exact-independence constructive ratio proxy for $k=3$.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
For fixed \(k\ge3\), prove
\[
\frac{R(k,\ell+1)}{R(k,\ell)}\to1\quad(\ell\to\infty).
\]

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Show
\[
R(k-1,\ell+1)=o(R(k,\ell)).
\]
Combined with the standard recursion, this directly forces ratio convergence.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (recursive upper bound)
\[
R(k,\ell+1)\le R(k,\ell)+R(k-1,\ell+1).
\]

**Proof.**
Classical Ramsey recursion from neighborhood partition at one vertex. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (trivial lower monotonicity)
\[
R(k,\ell+1)\ge R(k,\ell).
\]

**Proof.**
Avoiding \(K_k\) and \(\overline K_\ell\) is weaker than avoiding \(K_k\) and \(\overline K_{\ell+1}\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies limit 1)
If A holds, then
\[
1\le \frac{R(k,\ell+1)}{R(k,\ell)}\le 1+\frac{R(k-1,\ell+1)}{R(k,\ell)}=1+o(1).
\]

**Proof.**
Combine B1 and B2 and divide by \(R(k,\ell)\). Squeeze theorem gives limit \(1\). \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is proving sufficiently strong separation between adjacent \(k\)-layers of Ramsey growth.
