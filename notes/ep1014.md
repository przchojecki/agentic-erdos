# EP-1014 deep attempt (open)

## Statement
For fixed k>=3, prove R(k,l+1)/R(k,l)->1 as l->infinity.

## Attempt route
Tried ratio-stability heuristics from known off-diagonal Ramsey growth frameworks.

## Obstacle
Background states this is open even for k=3; current methods do not settle the
ratio-limit claim.

## Status
- no proof or counterexample obtained in this attempt.

## Exact recursion and ratio reformulation
Classical Ramsey recursion gives
\[
R(k,\ell)\le R(k-1,\ell)+R(k,\ell-1).
\]
Setting \(\ell\mapsto \ell+1\),
\[
R(k,\ell+1)\le R(k,\ell)+R(k-1,\ell+1).
\]

Hence
\[
\frac{R(k,\ell+1)}{R(k,\ell)}
\le
1+\frac{R(k-1,\ell+1)}{R(k,\ell)}.
\]
Therefore the target limit
\[
\frac{R(k,\ell+1)}{R(k,\ell)}\to1
\]
would follow from proving
\[
R(k-1,\ell+1)=o(R(k,\ell)).
\]

So EP-1014 can be viewed as a quantitative separation problem between adjacent first-parameter Ramsey layers.

### Trivial lower monotonicity
\[
R(k,\ell+1)\ge R(k,\ell),
\]
so the ratio is always \(\ge1\); the hard part is proving it tends to \(1\).

### Solved edge case \(k=2\)
For \(k=2\), \(R(2,\ell)=\ell\), so
\[
\frac{R(2,\ell+1)}{R(2,\ell)}=\frac{\ell+1}{\ell}\to 1.
\]
Thus the ratio-limit phenomenon is true on the \(k=2\) boundary, and EP-1014 concerns the genuinely harder \(k\ge3\) regime.

### Quantitative implication template
From
\[
1\le \frac{R(k,\ell+1)}{R(k,\ell)}
\le 1+\frac{R(k-1,\ell+1)}{R(k,\ell)},
\]
any upper bound of the form
\[
R(k,\ell)\ge C\,\ell^\gamma,\qquad
R(k-1,\ell+1)\le C' \ell^{\gamma-\delta}\ (\delta>0)
\]
would force
\[
\frac{R(k,\ell+1)}{R(k,\ell)}=1+O(\ell^{-\delta})\to1.
\]
So proving a strict exponent gap between adjacent first-parameter Ramsey layers is a sufficient route to EP-1014.
