# EP-529

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

## Statement
For self-avoiding walk displacement `d_k(n)` in `\mathbb{Z}^k`, determine growth
exponent and constants (diffusive vs super-diffusive behavior by dimension).

## What is known from background
High dimensions are diffusive; low dimensions are subtler/open in sharp forms.

## New Experiments
- 2026-03-12T10:59:45.711Z: standalone deep Monte Carlo run
  (`scripts/ep529.mjs`) using kinetic-growth SAW sampling.
- Large-`n` displacement proxies:
  - `d=2`: `d_2(n)/sqrt(n)` rose from `1.64` (`n=40`) to `1.96` (`n=120`),
  - `d=3`: around `1.22..1.24` for `n=40,80`,
  - `d=4`: around `1.12..1.13` for `n=40,80`.
