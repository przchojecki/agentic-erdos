# EP-866

## Problem Statement
Let $g_k(N)$ be the minimal excess such that every set
$$A\subseteq\{1,\dots,2N\},\quad |A|\ge N+g_k(N),$$
contains distinct $b_1,\dots,b_k$ whose pairwise sums all lie in $A$. Estimate $g_k(N)$.

## Literature
- Significant known results: exact/near-exact behavior for several small $k$ and broad asymptotic bounds for general $k$.
- Full sharp profile across all $k$ remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built finite randomized lower-bound search:
  - seek large pattern-free sets $A$ (no such $k$-tuple),
  - infer finite lower bounds on $g_k(N)$ from best found pattern-free sizes.
- Combined random sampling with parity-seeded greedy augmentation.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Found nontrivial finite lower bounds in tested cases:
  - $(k,N)=(4,40)$: $g_k(N)\gtrsim 4$,
  - $(4,60)$: $\gtrsim 4$,
  - $(5,40)$: $\gtrsim 13$,
  - $(5,60)$: $\gtrsim 13$.
- These are finite computational witnesses only, not asymptotic proofs.
