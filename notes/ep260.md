# EP-260 partial resolution

## Statement
If `a_n` is increasing with `a_n/n -> infinity`, is `sum a_n / 2^{a_n}`
irrational?

## What is resolved
Background gives two stronger hypotheses under which irrationality is proved:
1. `a_{n+1}-a_n -> infinity`.
2. `a_n >> n*sqrt(log n*log log n)`.

## What remains open in this note
The full condition `a_n/n -> infinity` alone is not settled by the cited results.

## Status
- two stronger regimes: resolved.
- full general growth condition: unresolved.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Under only
\[
a_n\uparrow\infty,\qquad \frac{a_n}{n}\to\infty,
\]
prove irrationality of
\[
S=\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{a_n}{2^{a_n}}.
\]

### Step A: Binary-block rigidity reduction
Translate irrationality into non-eventual-periodicity of the binary expansion of
\(S\). Show each summand contributes a localized binary block around bit-scale
\(a_n\), with carry interaction controlled by tails.

### Step B: Sparse-carry separation
Prove infinitely many indices \(n\) where tail mass
\(\sum_{m>n}a_m2^{-a_m}\) is too small to alter a prescribed finite window of bits
created by the \(n\)-th summand.

### Step C: Periodicity contradiction
If expansion were eventually periodic (hence rational), sufficiently far windows
would repeat with fixed period; Step B yields infinitely many non-repeating
witness windows, contradiction.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a uniform "isolated window lemma": existence of infinitely many \(n\) such
that
\[
\sum_{m>n}\frac{a_m}{2^{a_m}}<2^{-a_n-\Delta_n}
\]
with \(\Delta_n\to\infty\), and simultaneously the \(n\)-th summand produces a
nontrivial bit pattern within \([a_n,a_n+\Delta_n]\). This is automatic under
\(a_{n+1}-a_n\to\infty\), but open under only \(a_n/n\to\infty\).

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition DF1 (Rational implies eventual periodicity)
If \(S\in\mathbb Q\), its base-2 expansion is eventually periodic.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Standard characterization of rational numbers in integer bases.
\(\square\)

### Proposition DF2 (Tail localization bound)
For \(S_n=\sum_{m\le n}a_m2^{-a_m}\),
\[
0<S-S_n\le \sum_{m>n}\frac{a_m}{2^{a_m}}.
\]
Hence if RHS \(<2^{-T}\), binary digits of \(S\) and \(S_n\) agree up to position
\(T-1\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Immediate from absolute convergence and binary place-value stability.
\(\square\)

### Proposition DF3 (Reduction to isolated windows)
If infinitely many \(n\) satisfy the blocking lemma above, then \(S\notin\mathbb
Q\).

\[
\textit{Proof sketch.}
\]
Using DF2, each such \(n\) certifies a bit window determined by one summand and
immune to tail carries. Eventual periodicity would force eventual repetition of
all long windows, contradicting infinitely many isolated non-repeating windows.
\(\square\)
