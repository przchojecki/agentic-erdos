# EP-111 partial resolution

## Statement
For `G` with chromatic number `aleph_1`, must `h_G(n)/n -> infinity`, where
`h_G(n)` is the deletion cost to make every `n`-vertex subgraph bipartite?

## What is resolved
Background provides two-sided growth information:
- universal lower bound `h_G(n) >> n` for every such `G`;
- existence of a graph with `h_G(n) << n^{3/2}`.

## What remains open in this note
Whether `h_G(n)/n -> infinity` always holds is not settled by the cited results.

## Status
- baseline lower and upper envelopes (`n` to `n^{3/2}`): known.
- exact asymptotic growth law: unresolved.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
For every graph \(G\) with \(\chi(G)=\aleph_1\), prove
\[
\frac{h_G(n)}{n}\to\infty,
\]
where \(h_G(n)\) is the largest odd-cycle transversal number over \(n\)-vertex
subgraphs.

### Step A: Uniform odd-cycle packing
Show large finite subgraphs of such \(G\) contain many edge-disjoint odd cycles.

### Step B: Transversal lower transfer
Convert odd-cycle packing lower bounds into lower bounds for edge deletions to
bipartite.

### Step C: Growth amplification
Prove packing density grows superlinearly in the right normalized scale, forcing
\(h_G(n)/n\to\infty\).

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a universal packing theorem for uncountably chromatic graphs: finite traces
must contain more than linear odd-cycle edge-disjointness.

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition AE1 (Packing vs transversal)
For any finite graph \(H\), let \(\nu_{\mathrm{odd}}(H)\) be the maximum number
of edge-disjoint odd cycles and \(\tau_{\mathrm{odd}}(H)\) the minimum edges to
delete to make \(H\) bipartite. Then
\[
\tau_{\mathrm{odd}}(H)\ge \nu_{\mathrm{odd}}(H).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Any odd-cycle transversal must meet every odd cycle; in particular it meets each
member of an edge-disjoint family in distinct edges.
\(\square\)

### Proposition AE2 (Reduction for EP-111)
If for every \(G\) with \(\chi(G)=\aleph_1\) and every large \(n\) there exists
an \(n\)-vertex \(H\subseteq G\) with
\[
\nu_{\mathrm{odd}}(H)\ge n\,\omega(n),\qquad \omega(n)\to\infty,
\]
then \(h_G(n)/n\to\infty\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
By AE1, \(h_G(n)\ge \tau_{\mathrm{odd}}(H)\ge \nu_{\mathrm{odd}}(H)\), hence
\(h_G(n)/n\ge \omega(n)\to\infty\).
\(\square\)
