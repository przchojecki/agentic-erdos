# EP-14 partial attempt

## Route
Performed heuristic local search for sets `A subseteq [1,N]` with `|A|=floor(sqrt(N))` minimizing counts of integers not represented in exactly one way as a sum of two elements of `A`.

## Evidence from this batch
- `data/ep14_unique_sum_exception_quick.json` gives best bad-counts `68, 97, 146, 215` for `N=120,180,260,360`.
- These are consistent with a `sqrt(N)`-scale error profile in this range.

## Hard point
Heuristic finite constructions do not settle sharp asymptotic exponents/constants in the infinite problem.

## Status
Open in this batch; finite evidence aligns with known `N^{1/2}`-type scale.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine sharp asymptotics for the minimum number of integers lacking unique
representation as \(a+a'\) with \(a,a'\in A\), \(|A|=\lfloor\sqrt N\rfloor\).

### Step A: Extremal Configuration Structure
Classify near-optimal \(A\subset[1,N]\) with minimal non-unique/absent counts as
perturbations of structured templates (quasi-Sidon-with-density constraints).

### Step B: Lower Bound via Additive Energy
Prove any \(A\) of this size must incur at least
\[
c\sqrt N - o(\sqrt N)
\]
bad integers through energy/representation multiplicity constraints.

### Step C: Matching Upper Construction
Construct families with
\[
c\sqrt N + o(\sqrt N)
\]
bad integers, matching Step B constant.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a mixed Sidon-density inequality linking additive energy of \(A\) to count
of uniquely represented sums in the interval model, with optimal \(\sqrt N\)-scale constants.
