## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Close the borderline growth regime between the negative
\(a_{n+1}/a_n^2\to 0\) zone and the positive superquadratic zone, especially
near \(a_{n+1}\asymp a_n^2\).

### Step A: Quantitative phase diagram
Parameterize
\[
\rho_n:=\log\frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n^2}
\]
and classify outcomes by liminf/limsup behavior of \(\rho_n\).

### Step B: Critical-window analysis
Treat \(\rho_n\to 0\) but oscillatory cases; identify whether sparse positive
spikes in \(\rho_n\) can force irrationality-sequence behavior.

### Step C: Sharp criterion
Formulate necessary and sufficient condition in terms of weighted sums of
\(\rho_n^\pm\) or equivalent product criteria.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a "borderline transfer lemma": if \(\rho_n\) has infinitely many positive
windows of controlled total mass, then the irrationality mechanism from the
superquadratic regime still survives despite neutral stretches.
