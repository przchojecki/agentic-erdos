# EP-761

## Problem Statement
Does large chromatic number force large dichromatic number?  
Does large cochromatic number force existence of a subgraph with large dichromatic number?

## Literature
- Background notes record implication relations between the two questions.
- Both core existence implications remain open in general.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built finite proxy scans on small random graphs:
  - exact chromatic number,
  - exact dichromatic number for random orientations,
  - greedy cochromatic proxy.
- Used this to compare how quickly these parameters grow together in finite samples.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For \(n=7,8,9\), average chromatic values are around 3.1 to 3.5, while average dichromatic values of random orientations stay around 1.6 to 1.9.
- In these samples, dichromatic number \(\ge 3\) did not occur, despite many graphs having chromatic number \(\ge 4\).
- Finite evidence suggests a substantial gap between chromatic and typical oriented dichromatic complexity at small scales.

## Status
- Open.
