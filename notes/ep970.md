# EP-970

## Problem Statement
Let $h(k)$ be Jacobsthal's function in the form: the least $m$ such that for every $n$ with at most $k$ distinct prime factors, every block of $m$ consecutive integers contains an integer coprime to $n$.
Determine the order of magnitude of $h(k)$; in particular, is $h(k)\ll k^2$?

## Literature
- Conjecture: $h(k)\ll k^2$.
- Known upper bound: $h(k)\ll (k\log k)^2$.
- Known lower bounds are superlinear in $k$.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone exact Jacobsthal computations for squarefree moduli:
  - primorial baseline rows,
  - exhaustive scan over all squarefree products of selected small primes with fixed factor count $k$ and modulus cap $n\le 2\times10^6$.
- For each modulus, computed $j(n)$ exactly via periodic blocked-residue runs.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In exhaustive capped scans for $k=2\dots 7$, best observed moduli were primorials:
  - e.g. $k=6$: best $j(n)=22$ at $n=30030$,
  - $k=7$: best $j(n)=26$ at $n=510510$.
- Observed ratio $j(n)/k^2$ for these best examples sits around $0.53$-$0.67$ in tested range.
- Finite profile supports moderate growth but does not resolve $k^2$ versus $(k\log k)^2$ asymptotics.
