# EP-421 partial resolution

## Statement
Does there exist a density-one sequence `d_1<d_2<...` such that all products
`prod_{u<=i<=v} d_i` are distinct?

## What is resolved
Background cites a construction (Selfridge) achieving density greater than
`1/e - epsilon` for any fixed `epsilon>0`.

## What remains open in this note
The full density-one target is not achieved by the cited result.

## Status
- density near `1/e`: known.
- density `1` version: unresolved.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Decide existence of density-one sequence \((d_i)\) such that all contiguous interval products are distinct.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove an additive-log injectivity criterion: if the partial sums of \(\log d_i\) form a Sidon-like set in \(\mathbb R\) up to controlled error, then contiguous interval products are distinct and density can be pushed arbitrarily close to \(1\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (product-sum equivalence)
\[
\prod_{u\le i\le v} d_i = \prod_{u'\le i\le v'} d_i
\iff
\sum_{u\le i\le v}\log d_i = \sum_{u'\le i\le v'}\log d_i.
\]

**Proof.**
Take logarithms of positive products. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (prefix-sum reformulation)
Let \(S_t=\sum_{i\le t}\log d_i\). Then interval-product distinctness is equivalent to all differences \(S_v-S_{u-1}\) being distinct.

**Proof.**
By B1 each interval corresponds to one prefix-sum difference. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies density-one construction route)
If A yields near-Sidon difference uniqueness while keeping deletion density \(o(1)\), then resulting sequence has density \(1\) and required distinctness.

**Proof.**
Delete only sparse exceptional indices; difference uniqueness from A gives product uniqueness via B2. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is balancing near-Sidon uniqueness of prefix differences with high density of retained integers.
