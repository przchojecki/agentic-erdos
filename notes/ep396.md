`\prod_{0\le i\le k}(n-i)\mid\binom{2n}{n}`?

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
For each fixed \(k\), prove existence of \(n\) with
\[
\prod_{i=0}^{k}(n-i)\mid \binom{2n}{n}.
\]

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a simultaneous valuation-surplus lemma: for fixed \(k\), infinitely many \(n\) satisfy
\[
v_p\!\binom{2n}{n}\ge \sum_{i=0}^{k} v_p(n-i)
\]
for every prime \(p\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (target divisibility valuation criterion)
The target divisibility is equivalent to the valuation inequalities in A for all primes \(p\).

**Proof.**
Apply \(v_p\) to both sides; divisibility is equivalent to componentwise valuation dominance. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (Kummer representation for central binomial)
\[
v_p\!\binom{2n}{n}
\]
equals the number of carries when adding \(n+n\) in base \(p\).

**Proof.**
Kummer theorem on \(p\)-adic valuation of binomial coefficients. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies existence for fixed \(k\))
If A holds for infinitely many \(n\), then in particular at least one such \(n\) exists for that \(k\).

**Proof.**
Immediate. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The core obstacle is coordinating carry surpluses across all primes simultaneously while controlling valuations of the descending product \(\prod_{i=0}^k(n-i)\).
