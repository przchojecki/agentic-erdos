## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine asymptotic exponent and leading scale for
\[
\alpha(n)=\max_{|P|=n}\min_{\triangle\subset P}\operatorname{Area}(\triangle).
\]

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a uniform cell-incidence lemma: any \(n\)-point set with all triangle areas \(>\Delta\) induces occupancy constraints in a fine partition that force \(\Delta\le n^{-2+\eta}\) (or stronger), while matching constructions give lower side.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (scaling normalization)
If region area scales by factor \(s^2\), all triangle areas scale by \(s^2\).

**Proof.**
Triangle area is homogeneous of degree two in coordinates. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (minimum-area criterion via triples)
\(\alpha(n)\) is controlled by forbidding too many near-collinear triples at small geometric scales.

**Proof.**
Very small area corresponds to triples close to collinearity; bounding such configurations bounds minimum area from below/above depending on direction. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies exponent bound)
If A yields \(\Delta\le n^{-2+\eta}\) universally and constructions give \(\Delta\ge n^{-2-\eta}\) along sequence, exponent is pinned at \(2\).

**Proof.**
Compare upper/lower exponents and squeeze \(\eta\to0\). \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is converting geometric partition constraints into sharp global counting bounds for all triples, without losing exponent precision.
