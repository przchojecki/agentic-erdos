## New reduction (first question, structured family)
For

`A = {3} U {3p : p in P}`

with distinct odd primes `P`, one can reduce the optimization of covered density
to a partition-type objective.

Fix `a_0 mod 3`; by translation, take `a_0 = 0`.
For each `p in P`, assign the class `a_p mod 3p` to either residue `1 mod 3`
or `2 mod 3` (choosing `0 mod 3` is never beneficial for maximizing coverage,
since it lies inside the already-covered `0 mod 3` class).

If `P = P1 sqcup P2` according to that choice, then

`density = 1 - (1/3) * ( prod_{p in P1}(1-1/p) + prod_{p in P2}(1-1/p) )`.

Hence maximizing density is equivalent to minimizing

`prod_{p in P1}(1-1/p) + prod_{p in P2}(1-1/p)`.

This gives an exact finite optimization for this family and shows a direct
partition-style structure in the first subquestion.

Verification script:
- `scripts/ep278_family_partition_reduction_check.mjs`
- `data/ep278_family_partition_reduction_check.json`

The script checks this formula against full brute-force residue search for
`P={5,7}`, `{5,7,11}`, `{5,7,11,13}` and finds exact agreement.

## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Density of union via one-period counting)
For finitely many congruence classes \(C_i\) modulo \(m_i\), define
\(M=\mathrm{lcm}(m_i)\). Then the asymptotic density of \(\bigcup_i C_i\) equals
\[
\frac{1}{M}\#\Bigl(\bigcup_i C_i \cap [1,M]\Bigr).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Each \(C_i\) is \(M\)-periodic, hence the union is \(M\)-periodic. Density of an
\(M\)-periodic set is exactly period occupancy divided by \(M\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Structured family formula)
For \(A=\{3\}\cup\{3p:p\in P\}\), with \(a_0\equiv0\pmod3\), and partition
\(P=P_1\sqcup P_2\) according to residues \(1\) or \(2\) modulo \(3\), covered
density is
\[
1-\frac13\left(\prod_{p\in P_1}\Bigl(1-\frac1p\Bigr)+
\prod_{p\in P_2}\Bigl(1-\frac1p\Bigr)\right).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Numbers not covered must avoid the class \(0\pmod3\), so lie in residues
\(1\pmod3\) or \(2\pmod3\). In residue \(1\pmod3\), uncovered condition is
simultaneously avoiding one class modulo each \(p\in P_1\), giving factor
\(\prod_{p\in P_1}(1-1/p)\) by CRT independence. Similarly for residue
\(2\pmod3\) with \(P_2\). Average over the two \(1/3\)-density branches.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Optimization reduction)
In the above family, maximizing coverage is equivalent to minimizing
\[
\Phi(P_1,P_2):=
\prod_{p\in P_1}\Bigl(1-\frac1p\Bigr)+
\prod_{p\in P_2}\Bigl(1-\frac1p\Bigr).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Immediate from Lemma 2: covered density is \(1-\frac13\Phi\), affine-decreasing
in \(\Phi\).
\(\square\)
