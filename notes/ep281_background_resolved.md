# EP-281 resolved in background (as written)

## Statement
From the assumption that every full infinite congruence system leaves uncovered density
`0`, ask whether finite truncations can force uncovered density `<epsilon` uniformly.

## Resolution already in background
Background gives an argument via:
- Davenport-Erdos theorem on densities for multiples,
- Rogers' extremal principle that uncovered density is maximized at zero residues.

Together these imply the finite-truncation conclusion.

## Note
This is a background-derived closure, not a new proof contribution here.
