## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Resolve density bounds for rational/integer-distance sets in the plane, and
separate lattice artifacts from genuine geometric constraints.

### Step A: Arithmetic-geometry reduction
Translate distance constraints into rational points on families of algebraic
curves/surfaces.

### Step B: Uniform rational-point control
Apply (conditional or unconditional) bounds to limit combinatorial growth of
distance graphs.

### Step C: Density contradiction
Show any positive-density model would violate Step B bounds for large scales.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a uniform incidence bound for rational-distance graphs that avoids
one-dimensional near-collinear concentration dominating finite samples.

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition CI1 (Graph-density formulation)
Let \(P\subset \mathbb R^2\), and \(G_P\) have edges at allowed distances. If
\(|P\cap B_R|\ge \delta R^2\), then \(e(G_P[B_R])\gg_\delta R^4\) in dense
regimes.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Dense point sets yield quadratically many pairs; edge-density lower bounds follow
from averaging if allowed-distance frequency is bounded below.
\(\square\)

### Proposition CI2 (Reduction)
If one proves \(e(G_P[B_R])\ll R^{4-\eta}\) for all such \(P\), some \(\eta>0\),
then positive planar density is impossible.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Contradicts CI1 for large \(R\).
\(\square\)
