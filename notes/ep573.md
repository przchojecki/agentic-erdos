# EP-573

## Problem Statement
Is
\[
\operatorname{ex}(n;\{C_3,C_4\})\sim (n/2)^{3/2}\ ?
\]

## Literature / Context
- Stronger forbidden-family results are known (e.g., \(C_4\) plus broader odd-cycle exclusions).
- The exact \(\{C_3,C_4\}\)-only asymptotic remains the target.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built deeper randomized-greedy \(C_3,C_4\)-free constructions to track extremal edge growth.
- Finite-\(n\) ratios are measured against \((n/2)^{3/2}\) to test stabilization.
- No new proof yet for the full asymptotic equivalence.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Best-found ratios to \((n/2)^{3/2}\) stay near \(1\) on tested scales, with mild drift at larger \(n\).
- This is consistent with the conjectured leading constant \(1\) in finite windows.

## Next Proof Targets
- Turn finite greedy constructions into explicit pseudorandom algebraic constructions.
- Pair lower-bound constructions with sharper upper-bound stability arguments for near-extremal \(\{C_3,C_4\}\)-free graphs.
