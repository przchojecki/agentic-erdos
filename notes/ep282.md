# EP-282 deeper attempt

## Statement
Classify when restricted-denominator Egyptian-fraction greedy algorithm terminates;
notably odd denominators for rationals with odd denominator.

## Computation in this attempt
I implemented the odd-denominator greedy rule and exhaustively tested reduced
fractions `m/n` with odd `n` in two ranges:

- `n<=199`: all `8150/8150` cases terminated (cap `4000` steps).
  file: `data/ep282_odd_greedy_small_denominators.json`
- `n<=401`: all `32764/32764` cases terminated (cap `6000` steps).
  file: `data/ep282_odd_greedy_small_denominators_extended.json`

Hardest observed case in the extended run took 20 steps.

## Interpretation
Finite-range data supports termination in tested range, but gives no asymptotic proof.

## Background-level progress
Strong representability criteria are known for related restricted denominator classes.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove termination of odd-denominator greedy algorithm for all reduced \(m/n\) with
odd \(n\).

### Step A: Invariant extraction
Identify a strictly decreasing potential under one odd-greedy step (for example a
lexicographic pair involving denominator and adjusted numerator).

### Step B: Descent theorem
Show potential cannot decrease infinitely in \(\mathbb N^2\), forcing
termination.

### Step C: Classification of hard cases
Control near-critical fractions where denominator growth is slowest.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need an odd-step denominator inequality proving each greedy move decreases a
well-founded measure uniformly, without parity failures.

## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Single-step identity)
Given \(0<m<n\) with odd \(n\), choose odd \(q\) by odd-greedy rule and write
\[
\frac{m}{n}=\frac1q+\frac{m'}{n'}.
\]
Then
\[
m' q n = mqn-n = n(mq-1),\qquad n'=nq.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Subtract \(1/q\):
\[
\frac{m}{n}-\frac1q=\frac{mq-n}{nq}.
\]
Set \(m'=mq-n\), \(n'=nq\) and reduce if needed.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Numerator decrease in proper step)
If \(q=\left\lceil n/m\right\rceil_{\text{odd}}\) and \(m<n\), then
\[
0\le m'<m.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
By choice of \(q\), \(mq\ge n\), so \(m'\ge0\). Also \(q< n/m+2\), hence
\[
m'=mq-n < m\left(\frac{n}{m}+2\right)-n =2m.
\]
For proper odd-greedy steps, refinement of ceiling bound gives \(q\le n/m+1\) in
the parity-compatible branch, implying \(m'<m\). (This is the key place where the
full proof needs the missing parity-control lemma below.)
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction to parity-control lemma)
If one proves the parity-control statement guaranteeing the \(q\le n/m+1\) branch
infinitely often cannot fail, termination follows by repeated numerator descent.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Apply Lemma 2 iteratively: numerator is a nonnegative integer strictly
decreasing, so process stops.
\(\square\)
