## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine the asymptotic maximum edge density of \(C_4\)-free subgraphs of the
hypercube \(Q_d\), in particular whether the extremal density tends to \(1/2\).

### Step A: Spectral/isoperimetric upper framework
Translate \(C_4\)-free constraints into quadratic form restrictions on the cube
adjacency operator.

### Step B: Pair-count inequality
Control two-step walks through common neighbors to obtain
\[
e(H)\le \Bigl(\frac12+\varepsilon_d\Bigr)e(Q_d),\qquad \varepsilon_d\to 0.
\]

### Step C: Matching construction side
Produce \(C_4\)-free subgraphs with density \(\ge \frac12-o(1)\), closing the
limit if Step B is sharp.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a cube-specific codegree inequality that beats generic
Kovari-Sos-Turan-type bounds by exploiting Hamming structure.

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition P1 (Codegree counting identity)
For any subgraph \(H\subseteq Q_d\),
\[
\sum_{v\in V(Q_d)} \binom{\deg_H(v)}{2}
=\sum_{\{x,y\}\subseteq V(Q_d)} |N_H(x)\cap N_H(y)|.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Both sides count ordered choices of a middle vertex \(v\) and two incident edges
\((v,x),(v,y)\), grouped by \(v\) on the left and by endpoint pair \(\{x,y\}\)
on the right.
\(\square\)

### Proposition P2 (Reduction to pairwise common-neighbor control)
If one proves
\[
\sum_{\{x,y\}} |N_H(x)\cap N_H(y)|\le \Bigl(\frac14+o(1)\Bigr)\,d\,2^d,
\]
for all \(C_4\)-free \(H\subseteq Q_d\), then the asymptotic upper bound
\(e(H)\le (\frac12+o(1))e(Q_d)\) follows.

\[
\textit{Proof sketch.}
\]
Combine P1 with convexity
\(\sum_v \binom{\deg_H(v)}{2}\ge \frac1{2|V|}(2e(H))^2-O(e(H))\), then solve the
resulting quadratic inequality in \(e(H)\).
\(\square\)
