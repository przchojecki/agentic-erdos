# EP-1084

## Problem Statement
Let $f_d(n)$ be the maximal number of unit-distance pairs possible among $n$ points in $\mathbb{R}^d$ with mutual distances at least $1$.
Estimate $f_d(n)$, especially for $d=3$.

## Literature
- In dimension $2$, exact asymptotics are classical (Harborth’s exact formula).
- For $d=3$, known bounds are of the shape
\[
6n-c_1 n^{2/3} \lesssim f_3(n) \lesssim 6n-c_2 n^{2/3}.
\]
- A modern upper bound of the form $f_3(n)<6n-0.926\,n^{2/3}$ is recorded in the background material.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Replaced the stub with a standalone deep computation (`scripts/ep1084.mjs`).
- Used finite FCC parity-block constructions inside cubes of side $L$ and counted exact unit-contact pairs.
- Measured the deficit profile
\[
6n-E,
\]
normalized by $n^{2/3}$.
- Added repeated exact passes (8 per $L$ in deep mode) to confirm deterministic stability.

## Computation-Guided Observations
Deep run (`DEPTH=4`) on
\[
L\in\{20,30,40,50,60,80,100,120,140,160,180,200,220\}
\]
with 8 exact passes each gave:
- total runtime about $293$ seconds,
- stable counts on all repeated passes,
- for largest tested $L=220$:
  - $n=5{,}324{,}000$,
  - $E=31{,}654{,}260$,
  - $E/n\approx 5.9455785124$,
  - $(6n-E)/n^{2/3}\approx 9.5027599338$.

Across large $L$, the normalized deficit is very stable near $9.5$, matching the expected linear-minus-surface profile in this construction family.
This is construction-side evidence only, not an extremal proof.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
For \(d=3\), determine sharp asymptotics for
\[
f_3(n)=\max\{\#\text{unit pairs}\},
\]
under minimum pairwise distance \(\ge1\), including the \(n^{2/3}\)-order deficit from \(6n\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Show every extremal or near-extremal packing graph satisfies
\[
6n-f_3(n)\ge c\,n^{2/3}
\]
with explicit \(c>0\) from boundary-isoperimetric constraints on contact graphs.

This would lock in the surface-order lower deficit.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (degree bound)
In any 3D unit-distance graph with separation \(\ge1\), each vertex has bounded degree \(\le 12\), hence
\[
f_3(n)\le 6n.
\]

**Proof.**
The kissing-number bound in \(\mathbb R^3\) gives at most 12 unit neighbors around one point. Summing degrees and dividing by 2 yields \(E\le 6n\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (deficit decomposition)
If interior vertices have near-maximal degree while boundary vertices lose a fixed average amount, then
\[
6n-E \asymp |\partial V|.
\]

**Proof.**
Write \(12n-2E\) as total missing degree from 12. Interior contribution is \(o(|\partial V|)\) by hypothesis; boundary contributes a fixed positive fraction per boundary vertex. Divide by 2. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (isoperimetric transfer)
If \(|\partial V|\gg n^{2/3}\) for admissible finite packings, then \(6n-f_3(n)\gg n^{2/3}\).

**Proof.**
Combine Lemma B2 with the boundary lower bound. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The hard step is a uniform isoperimetric/regularity theorem for all near-extremal contact graphs, not just lattice/FCC families.
