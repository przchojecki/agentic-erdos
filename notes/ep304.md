## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Greedy upper bound baseline)
For any reduced \(a/b\in(0,1)\), the standard greedy Egyptian algorithm
terminates in finitely many steps.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
At step \(x/y\), choose \(q=\lceil y/x\rceil\). New numerator is
\(x' = xq-y\), satisfying \(0\le x'<x\). Numerator decreases in
\(\mathbb N\), hence termination.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Length minimization monotonicity)
If \(\mathcal D_1\subseteq \mathcal D_2\) are allowed denominator sets, then
minimum decomposition length satisfies
\[
L_{\mathcal D_2}(a/b)\le L_{\mathcal D_1}(a/b).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Any decomposition using \(\mathcal D_1\) is admissible for \(\mathcal D_2\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction to hardest numerator)
For fixed \(b\), define
\[
N(b):=\max_{1\le a<b,\ \gcd(a,b)=1} L(a/b).
\]
If one proves \(L(a/b)\le F(b)\) uniformly in \(a\), then \(N(b)\le F(b)\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Take maximum over \(a\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 4 (Asymptotic reduction)
If Step B gives a recursion decreasing complexity \(C(b)\) by a fixed factor:
\[
C(b_{j+1})\le C(b_j)^\theta,\quad 0<\theta<1,
\]
with \(O(1)\) term increase each stage, then total length is
\(O(\log\log b)\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Number of stages to reduce \(C(b)\) below constant is \(O(\log\log C(b))\), each
costing \(O(1)\) terms.
\(\square\)
