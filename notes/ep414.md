## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Decide whether all trajectories of \(h(n)=n+\tau(n)\) eventually merge into one trajectory class.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove eventual coalescence criterion: for any two large states \(x<y\), one has a uniform contraction on difference under synchronized iteration
\[
h^{(t)}(y)-h^{(t)}(x)\le (1-\delta)(y-x)
\]
at infinitely many controlled times \(t\), forcing eventual meeting.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (strict monotone growth)
\[
h(n)=n+\tau(n)\ge n+2\quad(n\ge2),
\]
so each orbit is strictly increasing.

**Proof.**
\(\tau(n)\ge2\) for \(n\ge2\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (difference update form)
\[
h(y)-h(x)=(y-x)+(\tau(y)-\tau(x)).
\]

**Proof.**
Subtract definitions of \(h\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies global coalescence)
If A gives repeated net contraction in B2 differences, every pair of orbits meets; hence one component.

**Proof.**
Positive integer difference cannot contract indefinitely without reaching zero; zero difference means collision, after which trajectories coincide. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The core gap is proving robust negative correlation in divisor-count fluctuations \(\tau(y)-\tau(x)\) strong enough to force repeated contractions.
