## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine exact Turán density \(\pi(K_4^3)\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove global stability: if a \(K_4^3\)-free 3-graph has density at least \(5/9-\eta\), then after deleting \(o(n^3)\) edges it is a balanced Turán-type blowup.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (density limit setup)
\[
\pi(K_4^3)=\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{\operatorname{ex}_3(n,K_4^3)}{\binom n3}
\]
exists.

**Proof.**
Standard extremal limit existence for forbidden hypergraphs via averaging/supremum compactness. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (construction lower bound)
Turán-type balanced construction yields
\[
\pi(K_4^3)\ge 5/9.
\]

**Proof.**
Direct edge count and forbidden-subgraph verification. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies exact value)
If A holds, any density \(>5/9\) would force structural contradiction to \(K_4^3\)-freeness, so upper bound matches B2 and \(\pi=5/9\).

**Proof.**
Stability reduces extremal problem to counted construction family. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is proving a full finite-stability theorem in 3-uniform setting with enough quantitative precision to close the current density gap.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine Turán density \(\pi(K_4^3)\) for 3-uniform hypergraphs.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a stability theorem near conjectured extremal density: any \(K_4^3\)-free 3-graph with density \(>5/9-\eta\) is close (in edit distance) to balanced Turán-type construction.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (density definition)
\[
\pi(K_4^3)=\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{\operatorname{ex}_3(n,K_4^3)}{\binom n3}
\]
exists by standard subadditivity/averaging arguments.

**Proof.**
Classical Turán density existence for fixed forbidden hypergraph family. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (lower bound from construction)
Balanced Turán-type constructions yield
\[
\pi(K_4^3)\ge 5/9.
\]

**Proof.**
Compute edge density of construction and verify \(K_4^3\)-freeness. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies exact value)
If A holds and every near-extremal graph is close to construction in B2, then upper bound matches \(5/9\), giving \(\pi(K_4^3)=5/9\).

**Proof.**
Stability converts structural closeness into edge-count upper bound matching lower bound. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is proving global stability in the 3-uniform setting, where local forbidden-pattern constraints admit many nontrivial near-extremal configurations.
