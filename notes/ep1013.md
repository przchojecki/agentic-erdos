# EP-1013

## Problem Statement
Let $h_3(k)$ be the minimum number of vertices in a triangle-free graph of chromatic number $k$. Determine asymptotics and ratio smoothness (e.g. behavior of $h_3(k+1)/h_3(k)$).

## Literature
- Known theory places $h_3(k)$ on roughly $k^2\log k$ scale.
- Mycielski construction gives explicit recursive upper bounds:
\[
h_3(k+1)\le 2h_3(k)+1.
\]

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine asymptotics of \(h_3(k)\), the minimum order of triangle-free \(k\)-chromatic graphs.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a sharp stability lower bound:
\[
h_3(k)\ge c\,k^2\log k + O(k^2)
\]
with matching construction constant \(c\), plus structure of near-extremal witnesses.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (monotonicity)
\[
h_3(k+1)\ge h_3(k)+1.
\]

**Proof.**
A \((k+1)\)-chromatic graph cannot have the same order as a minimal \(k\)-chromatic witness unless that witness were already \((k+1)\)-chromatic; minimality and strict chromatic increase force at least one extra vertex. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (Mycielski upper recursion)
\[
h_3(k+1)\le 2h_3(k)+1.
\]

**Proof.**
Apply Mycielski construction to a minimal triangle-free \(k\)-chromatic graph. It preserves triangle-freeness and increases chromatic number by one with the stated vertex count. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies ratio smoothness)
If \(h_3(k)=c k^2\log k(1+o(1))\), then
\[
\frac{h_3(k+1)}{h_3(k)}\to 1.
\]

**Proof.**
Evaluate quotient of asymptotic expressions at \(k+1\) and \(k\); the relative change of \(k^2\log k\) is \(1+o(1)\). \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The missing ingredient is a full extremal characterization of near-minimal triangle-free \(k\)-chromatic graphs to lock constants and second-order terms.
