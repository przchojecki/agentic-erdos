## Rigorous reformulation and test criterion
For prime \(p\), the multiplicative group \((\mathbb Z/p\mathbb Z)^*\) is cyclic of order \(p-1\).
So \(q<p\) is a primitive root mod \(p\) iff
\[
\operatorname{ord}_p(q)=p-1.
\]

Equivalently, for each prime divisor \(r\mid(p-1)\),
\[
q^{(p-1)/r}\not\equiv 1 \pmod p,
\]
and \(q\not\equiv 0\pmod p\).

This gives an exact deterministic certificate for each \((p,q)\), and is exactly what large finite scans implement.

### Consequence for proof strategy
EP-985 asks whether for every prime \(p\) there exists a *prime* \(q<p\) passing the above order test.
Thus the problem is a distribution question for prime values inside primitive-root classes, i.e. an intersection of:
- primitive-root constraints in \((\mathbb Z/p\mathbb Z)^*\), and
- primality of the representative \(q\).
