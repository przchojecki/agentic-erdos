# EP-883

## Problem Statement
At the threshold
$$|A|>\left\lfloor\frac n2\right\rfloor+\left\lfloor\frac n3\right\rfloor-\left\lfloor\frac n6\right\rfloor,$$
questions ask whether a graph $G(A)$ must contain:
1. all odd cycles up to about $n/3+1$;
2. for fixed $\ell$, a complete tripartite $(1,\ell,\ell)$ subgraph for large $n$.

## Literature
- The second question is known positive in strong form (with growing $\ell$), hence true for each fixed $\ell$ eventually.
- For odd cycles, known results in notes give a weaker linear-range guarantee, not yet the full stated range.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built finite proxy in an inferred graph model:
  - vertices $1..n$,
  - edges by distance set membership $|u-v|\in A$.
- At threshold-size random sets $A$, tested presence of all odd cycle lengths in target range.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In tested instances ($n=60,90,120$), all targeted odd cycle lengths appeared in all trials.
- This supports the statement direction in finite random model, but depends on inferred graph interpretation and is not a proof.

## Status
- First subquestion: open in full stated strength.
- Second subquestion: treated as resolved in literature notes.
- Added standalone finite odd-cycle coverage probe and normalized data integration.
