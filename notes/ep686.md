# EP-686

## Problem Statement
Can every integer \(N\ge 2\) be represented as
\[
N=\frac{\prod_{i=1}^k (m+i)}{\prod_{i=1}^k (n+i)}
\]
for some \(k\ge2\) and \(m\ge n+k\)?

## Literature
- Problem asks universal representability; related interval-product LCM/collision themes appear nearby in the list.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Performed expanded exact finite search:
  - \(2\le k\le 9\),
  - \(n,m\le 260\) with \(m\ge n+k\),
  - tracked representable \(N\le 500\) and witnesses.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Large fraction of \(N\le 500\) are represented in scanned range, with many having multiple witnesses.
- A nontrivial set remains unrepresented within these bounds.
- Finite non-representation in bounded search is not a proof of global impossibility.
