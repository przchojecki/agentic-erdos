# EP-938

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

### Working statement
Let \(n_k\) be powerful numbers in increasing order. Ask whether there are only finitely many 3-term arithmetic progressions among consecutive terms:
\[
n_k,\ n_{k+1},\ n_{k+2}.
\]

### Deep standalone computation (`ep938.mjs`)
- Method: exact scan of powerful numbers up to \(B\), then test
\[
n_k+n_{k+2}=2n_{k+1}
\]
for each consecutive triple.
- Run: \(B=4\cdot 10^7\).
- Counts:
  - powerful numbers \(\le B\): \(13236\)
  - consecutive-index 3-term APs: \(6\)
- Sample APs:
  - \((1728,1764,1800)\)
  - \((6912,7056,7200)\)
  - \((729000,729316,729632)\)

### Implication
The property “finitely many” is not resolved by finite search, but the run gives explicit nontrivial witnesses and suggests structured infinite families may exist.
