# EP-479 partial resolution

## Statement
For fixed `k != 1`, are there infinitely many `n` with
`2^n \equiv k (mod n)`?

## What is resolved
Background reports proofs for `k=2^i` (`i>=1`) and for `k=-1`.

A local scan up to `n<=10^6` found first witnesses for many small `k`
(examples: `k=5` at `n=19147`, `k=11` at `n=262279`), while not finding one for
`k=3` within that bound.

## What remains open in this note
General infinitude for arbitrary `k != 1` is not resolved by the cited material.

## Status
- several infinite families: resolved.
- full `k != 1` statement: unresolved.

## Added Proof Layer (No New Computation)

### Closed Infinite Families
By cited results, infinitude is proved for:
- \(k=2^i\) (\(i\ge1\)),
- \(k=-1\).

### Remaining Open Core
The full statement for arbitrary fixed \(k\neq 1\) remains open.

### Blocking Lemma
- Need a uniform construction (or distribution theorem) producing infinitely many
  \(n\) with
  \[
  2^n\equiv k\pmod n
  \]
  for every fixed \(k\neq1\), beyond currently known structured families.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
For each fixed \(k\neq1\), prove infinitely many \(n\) solve
\[
2^n\equiv k\pmod n.
\]

### Step A: Controlled Composite Moduli Construction
Build families of moduli \(n\) with prescribed CRT factorization where
\(2^n-k\) vanishes modulo each prime-power component.

### Step B: Self-Referential Exponent Compatibility
Ensure exponent \(n\) itself satisfies required congruences in multiplicative
orders across components (the core circularity).

### Step C: Infinite Lift
Iterate construction to infinitely many distinct \(n\).

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a simultaneous order-congruence lifting lemma: given target residues
for \(n\) modulo many \(\lambda(q_i)\), construct \(n\) with
\[
2^n\equiv k \pmod{q_i}
\]
for all \(i\), while retaining \(q_i\mid n\).
