# EP-1035

## Problem Statement
Treat the question as forcing a spanning hypercube $Q_n$ inside very dense graphs on $2^n$ vertices.

## Literature
- Spanning bounded-degree embedding at this density level is substantially harder than non-spanning embedding statements.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added deep standalone finite experiments for spanning $Q_d$ embedding ($d=4,5$):
  - random dense host graphs on $2^d$ vertices,
  - local-search over bijections to minimize missing cube edges,
  - success-rate profile vs edge density.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In this finite proxy, embedding success transitions sharply around density $\sim 0.65$-$0.70$.
- For $d=5$ (32 vertices), success was about $0.23$ at density $0.60$, about $0.88$ at $0.65$, and $1.00$ at $0.70$ and above in this run.
- This is heuristic evidence only: failure of the local search is not a non-embedding proof.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Force a spanning \(Q_n\) inside sufficiently dense graphs on \(2^n\) vertices.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a robust partition-expansion lemma: every host \(G\) with density above threshold admits an equitable partition into \(n\) coordinate-like cuts where every induced bipartite slice has minimum degree high enough for perfect matching constraints of cube edges.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (cube decomposition)
\(Q_n\) is union of \(n\) perfect matchings between opposite \((n-1)\)-dimensional layers.

**Proof.**
Vertices are bitstrings; flipping fixed coordinate gives one perfect matching per coordinate. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (matching-based embedding criterion)
If one can realize all \(n\) coordinate matchings edge-disjointly on the same vertex set under a consistent labeling, then a spanning \(Q_n\) embedding exists.

**Proof.**
Union of coordinate matchings with consistent labels reconstructs all cube edges. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies spanning cube)
Lemma A provides the structural conditions needed for B2, thus yields a spanning \(Q_n\).

**Proof.**
A gives coordinate-like partition and high-degree slice conditions ensuring required perfect matchings exist and can be synchronized. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is synchronizing many matching constraints globally so local density does not fail on one coordinate slice.
