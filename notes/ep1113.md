## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Resolve existence/nonexistence of non-covering Sierpinski-type numbers.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Show that any \(m\) with \(2^k m+1\) composite for all \(k\ge1\) must admit a finite covering system of prime divisors; equivalently rule out genuinely non-covering behavior.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (periodicity of prime divisors in \(k\))
For fixed odd prime \(p\nmid m\), condition \(p\mid 2^k m+1\) is periodic in \(k\) modulo \(\operatorname{ord}_p(2)\) when solvable.

**Proof.**
Equation is \(2^k\equiv -m^{-1}\pmod p\). Solutions form one residue class modulo multiplicative order of \(2\) in \((\mathbb Z/p\mathbb Z)^\times\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (finite covering sufficiency)
If finitely many primes \(p_1,\dots,p_t\) cover all residues \(k\ge1\) through their solution classes from Lemma B1, then all \(2^k m+1\) are composite.

**Proof.**
Each \(k\) hits at least one covering class, yielding a prime divisor \(p_i\) of the term. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies non-covering impossibility)
Lemma A would prove every Sierpinski \(m\) is covering, hence non-covering Sierpinski numbers do not exist.

**Proof.**
Direct implication of statement A. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The difficult step is upgrading finite-window pseudocoverings to all-\(k\) structural necessity of finite coverings.
