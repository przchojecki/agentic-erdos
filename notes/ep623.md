# EP-623

## Problem Statement
At cardinality \(\aleph_\omega\), for set mappings on finite subsets with \(f(A)\notin A\), must there exist an infinite independent \(Y\)?

## Literature / Context
- Below \(\aleph_\omega\), negative constructions are known.
- The \(\aleph_\omega\) threshold is open and may involve independence phenomena.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented finite exact proxy:
  - random set-mappings \(f:\mathcal P([n])\to [n]\) with \(f(A)\notin A\),
  - exact maximum independent subset size by exhaustive subset checking.
- This probes finite behavior only.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For \(n=9,10,11,12\), maximum independent sizes in random instances are typically \(3\)–\(5\).
- The scale is stable and small compared to \(n\), but this does not transfer directly to the infinitary threshold case.

## Next Proof Targets
- Introduce structured finite set-mappings that mimic known lower-cardinal counterexample templates.
- Investigate whether finite extremal patterns suggest canonical obstructions at \(\aleph_\omega\).
