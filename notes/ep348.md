# EP-348 partial resolution

## Statement
For which `0<=m<n` does there exist a complete sequence `A` that remains complete
after deleting any `m` terms but fails completeness after deleting any `n` terms?

## What is resolved
Background gives concrete realizable pairs:
- `(m,n)=(0,1)` via powers of two.
- `(m,n)=(1,2)` via Fibonacci-type example.

Background also records a negative theorem for a stronger completeness notion:
for that stronger notion, no such sequence exists when `2<=m<n`.

## What remains open in this note
Under the intended weaker completeness notion (allowing finitely many exceptions),
cases such as `(m,n)=(2,3)` remain open.

## Status
- some parameter pairs and strong-sense obstruction: known.
- full weak-sense classification: unresolved.

## New Experiments
- 2026-03-05T15:02:09.957Z: enforced deep standalone deletion-robustness surrogate search (randomized search + exhaustive 2/3-deletion checks on finalists, runtime 31.51s).
- Finite signal: sampled search can produce candidates with decent average robustness, but exhaustive checks showed no finite witness of strict (2,3)-type separation under this model (worst-case 2-deletion coverage can collapse while some 3-deletions still cover heavily).
- Proof direction update: robustness is highly nonuniform over deletion choices; any positive construction for weak-sense (2,3) likely needs deliberately engineered redundancy layers, not generic near-geometric random constructions.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Classify all \((m,n)\) for which there exists a sequence complete after any
\(m\)-deletion but not after any \(n\)-deletion.

### Step A: Robustness profile function
Define \(R_t(A)\): worst-case completeness after deleting \(t\) terms, and study
its monotonicity in \(t\).

### Step B: Separation construction/obstruction
Build sequences with \(R_m=1\), \(R_n=0\), or prove impossible for given pair.

### Step C: Full parameter classification
Characterize feasible \((m,n)\) in weak completeness sense.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a compositional robustness lemma: combining blocks with known robustness
profiles yields predictable profile of the union.

## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Monotonicity in deletion count)
If a sequence remains complete after deleting any \(t\) terms, then it remains
complete after deleting any \(s\le t\) terms.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Any \(s\)-deletion is a special case of \(t\)-deletion after adding \(t-s\)
arbitrary deleted indices.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Immediate infeasibility condition)
If a pair \((m,n)\) is feasible, necessarily \(m<n\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
If \(m\ge n\), by Lemma 1 completeness after all \(m\)-deletions implies
completeness after all \(n\)-deletions, contradicting failure requirement.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction to boundary cases)
To classify all feasible \((m,n)\), it suffices to settle adjacent pairs
\((t,t+1)\): closure under Lemma 1 then determines all larger gaps.

\[
\textit{Proof sketch.}
\]
If \((t,t+1)\) realizable with profile control, block/product constructions can
amplify to \((m,n)\) for \(m\le t<n\) once monotonicity constraints are met.
\(\square\)
