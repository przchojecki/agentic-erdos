# EP-992 partial resolution

## Statement
For any integer sequence x_n, ask if for almost all alpha discrepancy satisfies
D(N)<<N^{1/2}(log N)^{o(1)} or stronger loglog-poly factor.

## What is resolved
Background records general almost-sure bounds improved over time:
N^{1/2}(log N)^{5/2+o(1)} to N^{1/2}(log N)^{3/2+o(1)}.
For lacunary sequences, much stronger loglog-scale control is known.

## What remains open in this note
General-sequence bound at the near-square-root log^{o(1)} scale is unresolved.

## Status
- substantial metrical discrepancy bounds known.
- strongest universal logarithmic refinement open.

## Exact discrepancy--exponential-sum reduction
For \(y_n=\{\alpha x_n\}\), Erd\H{o}s--Tur\'an gives
\[
D_N(y_n)\ll \frac1H + \sum_{h=1}^H \frac1h\left|\frac1N\sum_{n=1}^N e(hy_n)\right|.
\]
Because \(e(hy_n)=e(h\alpha x_n)\), this becomes
\[
D_N(y_n)\ll \frac1H + \sum_{h=1}^H \frac1h\left|\frac1N S_h(N,\alpha)\right|,
\quad
S_h(N,\alpha):=\sum_{n\le N} e(h\alpha x_n).
\]

So universal metrical discrepancy bounds are equivalent to obtaining almost-sure control of these exponential sums uniformly in \(h\le H\). Any logarithmic improvement must sharpen this exponential-sum step.
