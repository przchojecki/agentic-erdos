# EP-992

## Problem Statement
Given an integer sequence $(x_n)$, study metric discrepancy of
\[
\{\alpha x_n\}_{n\ge 1}
\]
for almost all $\alpha$, aiming for near-square-root bounds such as
\[
D_N \ll N^{1/2}(\log N)^{o(1)}
\]
(or similarly sharp logarithmic refinements).

## Literature
- General metric discrepancy theory gives substantial almost-everywhere bounds with polylogarithmic losses.
- Stronger log-scale improvements are known for special classes (e.g. lacunary-type structures), while a sharp universal form remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Wrote out the exact reduction from discrepancy to exponential sums via Erd\H{o}s--Tur\'an.
- Added a deeper standalone Monte Carlo metrical profile:
  - sequences: linear, squares, lacunary growth, primes,
  - $N\in\{1000,2000,4000,6000\}$,
  - $120$ sampled irrational-like $\alpha$ values,
  - reporting median, $90\%$ quantile, and max of $D_N/\sqrt N$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Linear sequence shows smallest normalized discrepancy in the sample.
- Prime and square sequences are consistently higher than linear, but remain in a stable finite band.
- The lacunary synthetic profile is much larger in this finite setup, highlighting strong sequence-dependence of finite-$N$ behavior.
- Finite sampling cannot settle the almost-all asymptotic statement.

## Status
- Open in general form.
- Added deeper standalone metrical-discrepancy dataset with larger alpha sample size.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Obtain almost-everywhere discrepancy bounds near \(N^{1/2}\) for \(\{\alpha x_n\}\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove for almost all \(\alpha\):
\[
\sum_{h\le H}\frac1h\left|\sum_{n\le N}e(h\alpha x_n)\right|^2
\ll N(\log N)^{1+o(1)}
\]
uniformly in \(H\le N\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (Erdos-Turan discrepancy bound)
\[
D_N \ll \frac{N}{H}+\sum_{h\le H}\frac1h\left|\sum_{n\le N}e(h\alpha x_n)\right|.
\]

**Proof.**
Classical Erdős-Turán inequality for star discrepancy. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (Cauchy averaging transfer)
Bound in A implies
\[
\sum_{h\le H}\frac1h\left|\sum_{n\le N}e(h\alpha x_n)\right|
\ll \sqrt{\log H}\,\sqrt{N}(\log N)^{1/2+o(1)}.
\]

**Proof.**
Apply Cauchy-Schwarz with harmonic weights \(1/h\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies near-square-root discrepancy)
Choosing \(H\asymp \sqrt N\) in B1 and using B2 gives
\[
D_N\ll \sqrt N(\log N)^{1/2+o(1)}.
\]

**Proof.**
Balance truncation term \(N/H\) with exponential-sum term. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is proving metric second-moment bounds in A for broad sequence classes beyond special lacunary structures.
