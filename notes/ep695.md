# EP-695

## Problem Statement
Let
\[
p_1 < p_2 < \cdots
\]
be primes with
\[
p_{i+1} \equiv 1 \pmod{p_i}.
\]
Is it true that
\[
\lim_{k\to\infty} p_k^{1/k} = \infty?
\]
Does there exist such a chain with
\[
p_k \le \exp\!\bigl(k(\log k)^{1+o(1)}\bigr)?
\]

## Literature
- This is tied to bounds for least primes in arithmetic progressions (Linnik-type phenomena and refinements).
- Current status remains open on both growth-direction questions.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented standalone greedy-chain search for many starting primes.
- For each step, computed the smallest prime of the form \(1 \bmod p_i\), then tracked growth profiles \(p_k^{1/k}\).
- This does not prove asymptotics, but provides finite-scale growth envelopes across many starts.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Deep run parameters: 46 starting primes (all primes \(\le 200\)), chain depth up to \(k=14\), search cap 800000 candidates per edge.
- Largest observed \(p_{14}^{1/14}\) in this run was about \(14.94\) (chain starting at 37).
- Finite behavior supports rapid growth in greedy constructions, but does not separate the two asymptotic questions.
