# EP-272 partial resolution

## Statement
For `N>=1`, let `t(N)` be the maximum size of a family
`A_1,...,A_t \subseteq {1,...,N}` such that for every `i!=j`,
`A_i \cap A_j` is a non-empty arithmetic progression.
Determine `t(N)`.

## What is resolved
Background records Szab\'o (1999):

`t(N) = N^2/2 + O(N^{5/3}(log N)^3)`.

So the asymptotic order and leading constant are known (`t(N)=(1/2+o(1))N^2`).

## Additional resolved subclaims from background
- The earlier "fixed-point arithmetic progression" extremal guess is false.
- The Simonovits--S\'os conjecture `t(N)=C(N,2)+1` is also false; Szab\'o gives larger constructions.

## What remains open in this note
The exact value (or sharp second-order term) is not settled by the cited material.
Background notes a conjectural refinement `t(N)=C(N,2)+O(N)`.

## Status
- Asymptotic leading term: resolved.
- Exact/sharp refinement: unresolved.

t(N)=\frac{N^2}{2}+O\!\left(N^{5/3}(\log N)^3\right).
\]
So the leading asymptotic constant \(1/2\) is established.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Refine
\[
t(N)=\frac{N^2}{2}+O\!\left(N^{5/3}(\log N)^3\right)
\]
to near-linear error, ideally \(\binom N2+O(N)\).

### Step A: Near-Extremal Structure Theorem
Classify families with
\[
t(N)\ge \frac{N^2}{2}-\Delta
\]
as perturbations of a canonical extremal template, with perturbation size
controlled by \(\Delta\).

### Step B: Stability-to-Counting Upgrade
Show each perturbation contributes only \(O(\Delta + N)\) extra/deleted pairs,
sharpening combinatorial slack.

### Step C: Optimize \(\Delta\)
Use Step A+B to force second-order error down from \(N^{5/3}\) scale toward
\(N^{1+\varepsilon}\), then linear.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need an arithmetic-progression intersection stability lemma: if most pairwise
intersections are APs and family size is near \(N^2/2\), then the family must be
edit-distance \(O(\Delta)\) from a rigid model.
