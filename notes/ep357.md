# EP-357 partial attempt

## Route
Backtracking search for maximal `k` at small `n`, enforcing distinctness of all consecutive sums.

## Evidence from this batch
- `data/ep357_consecutive_sums_distinct_search.json` found:
  - `f(20)>=10`, `f(25)>=13`, `f(30)>=15`, `f(35)>=16`, `f(40)>=18`.
- This is strong finite linear-scale behavior (`~0.45n` at `n=40`).

## Hard point
Finite lower bounds do not settle asymptotic growth or the question `f(n)=o(n)`.

## Status
No proof/counterexample for asymptotic claim; finite data suggests substantial growth.

## New Experiments
- 2026-03-06T09:25:22.385Z: enforced deep standalone branch-and-bound search (node-capped exact engine, runtime 26.85s) for n=42,44,46.
- Finite signal: improved lower bounds reached k=18 (n=42) and k=19 (n=44,46) under deep bounded exact search.
- Proof direction update: even with large search trees, near-linear growth candidates persist; proving asymptotic behavior likely needs a structural packing argument on interval-sum conflicts rather than pure exhaustive search.
