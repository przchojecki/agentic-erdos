# EP-510

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

## Statement
For finite sets `A \subset \mathbb{N}` with `|A|=N`, study
\[
\min_{\theta} \sum_{n\in A} \cos(n\theta)
\]
and whether one can force square-root-scale negativity uniformly (versus known
weaker/stronger partial regimes).

## What is known from background
Recent work gives polynomial negative bounds, while the conjectured
`N^{1/2}` scale remains open in full generality.

## New Experiments
- 2026-03-12T10:47:03.812Z: standalone deep grid-minimization run
  (`scripts/ep510.mjs`) with `GRID_M=4096`, `TRIAL_SCALE=2`.
- Random sets:
  - `N=80`: best `-min sum = 34.16` (`3.82 * sqrt(N)`),
  - `N=160`: best `38.55` (`3.05 * sqrt(N)`),
  - `N=320`: best `60.00` (`3.35 * sqrt(N)`).
- Structured Sidon-difference sets (`A=B-B`) were much smaller relative to
  `sqrt(N)` (about `0.97 * sqrt(N)` across tested sizes).
