## Statement
For finite sets `A \subset \mathbb{N}` with `|A|=N`, study
\[
\min_{\theta} \sum_{n\in A} \cos(n\theta)
\]
and whether one can force square-root-scale negativity uniformly (versus known
weaker/stronger partial regimes).

`N^{1/2}` scale remains open in full generality.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove square-root-scale cancellation:
\[
\min_\theta \sum_{n\in A}\cos(n\theta)\le -c\sqrt N
\]
for all \(A\subset\mathbb N\), \(|A|=N\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Establish an \(L^2\)-to-pointwise dip lemma for trigonometric polynomial
\[
S_A(\theta)=\sum_{n\in A}e^{in\theta},
\]
showing variance plus additive-structure control forces \(\Re S_A(\theta)\) to attain \(-c\sqrt N\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (cosine as real part)
\[
\sum_{n\in A}\cos(n\theta)=\Re S_A(\theta).
\]

**Proof.**
\(e^{in\theta}=\cos(n\theta)+i\sin(n\theta)\), sum and take real part. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (mean-zero normalization)
\[
\int_0^{2\pi}\Re S_A(\theta)\,d\theta=0.
\]

**Proof.**
Integral of each \(\cos(n\theta)\) over a full period is zero. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies negative dip)
If A controls \(\|S_A\|_2\) and higher moments to preclude concentration on positive side, then there exists \(\theta\) with \(\Re S_A(\theta)\le -c\sqrt N\).

**Proof.**
Mean-zero plus moment bounds force nontrivial negative tail by Paley–Zygmund/Chebyshev-type arguments on centered variable. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is deriving moment bounds uniform over all sets \(A\), including highly structured additive sets where cancellation is weakest.
