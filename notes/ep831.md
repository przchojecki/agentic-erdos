# EP-831

## Problem Statement
Let $h(n)$ be maximal such that any $n$ points in $\mathbb{R}^2$ (with no three collinear and no four concyclic) determine at least $h(n)$ circles of distinct radii through triples of points. Estimate $h(n)$.

## Literature
- This is a geometric extremal-radius counting problem from the Erdos list.
- The local dataset entry had malformed trailing metadata text; the mathematical statement above is the intended one.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented random general-position sampling for several $n$.
- For each sample, computed exactly the number of distinct circumradii among all triples.
- Used this as a finite proxy profile for possible lower behavior of $h(n)$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For tested $n=10,12,14,16$, the number of distinct radii is extremely close to the total number of triples.
- Minimum observed distinct-ratio stayed above $0.95$ across all tested cases.
- This suggests generic configurations are highly non-degenerate; extremal constructions (if much smaller) must be structured.
