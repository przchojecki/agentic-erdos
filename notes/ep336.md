## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Order implies upper bound transfer)
If a set \(A\) has order \(\le t\) on \([X,2X]\), then every integer in this
interval is representable as sum of at most \(t\) elements of \(A\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
This is the definition of order on the interval.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Exact-order obstruction criterion)
If there exists \(n\) such that every representation of \(n\) using at most \(t\)
summands has multiplicity at least \(2\) (non-unique), then exact order is
\(>t\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Exact order \(t\) requires unique representation at level \(t\) (or the EP-336
exactness condition); failure at one \(n\) rules it out.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction)
To prove stable 2-vs-3 separation it suffices to establish Lemma 1 with \(t=2\)
and Lemma 2 with \(t=2\) on infinitely many scales.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Then order \(\le2\) but exact order \(>2\) on infinitely many scales.
\(\square\)
