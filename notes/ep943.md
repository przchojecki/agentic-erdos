# EP-943

## Problem Statement
Let $A$ be the set of powerful numbers (if $p\mid n$ then $p^2\mid n$).
Is it true that
$$
(1_A*1_A)(n)=n^{o(1)}
$$
for every $n$?

## Literature
- This problem concerns additive representation multiplicities of powerful numbers.
- No complete resolution is recorded in the local background snippets.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone deep finite convolution scan up to $N=50{,}000{,}000$.
- Computed exact powerful-number set up to $N$ and exact additive convolution
$$
r(n)=(1_A*1_A)(n)
$$
for all $n\le N$.
- Tracked growth profile at checkpoints and top representation counts.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Powerful count up to $5\cdot 10^7$: $14{,}819$.
- Maximum observed representation count in tested range: $r(n)=174$ at $n=48{,}024{,}900$.
- Checkpoint profile shows very slow growth relative to $n$ (consistent with subpolynomial-growth heuristics on this range).
