# EP-689

## Problem Statement
For large \(n\), can one choose one residue class modulo each prime \(p\le n\) so that every integer in \([1,n]\) is covered at least twice?

## Literature
- This is the double-cover analogue of Jacobsthal-style single-cover problems and their prime-window variants.
- No general resolution is listed in the provided background.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Ran stochastic optimization maximizing the minimum coverage multiplicity over \([1,n]\), with one class per prime \(p\le n\).
- Tested \(n\) from 120 to 1000.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In all tested runs, best minimum multiplicity remained 0 (i.e., some integers uncovered).
- No instance achieved full single cover, let alone double cover, within these search budgets.
- This is negative finite evidence for naive random/greedy constructions, not a proof of impossibility.
