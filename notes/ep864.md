# EP-864

## Problem Statement
Maximize $|A|\subseteq\{1,\dots,N\}$ under the condition that at most one sum value has more than one representation as $a+b$ with $a\le b$ in $A$. Conjectured sharp scale:
$$|A|\sim \frac{2}{\sqrt3}\,N^{1/2}.$$

## Literature
- Erdos-Freud gave the reflected-Sidon lower construction:
  $$|A|\ge (1+o(1))\frac{2}{\sqrt3}N^{1/2}.$$
- Matching upper constant remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented finite random-greedy search under the one-exception multiplicity rule.
- Measured best and average sizes over many trials for $N=60,100,160,240,320$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Best normalized size $|A|/\sqrt N$ decreases across tested range (about $1.55$ at $N=60$ to about $1.01$ at $N=320$).
- Relative to $(2/\sqrt3)\sqrt N$, best found ratio approaches and then dips below 1 in larger tested $N$ windows.
- Finite greedy results are noisy and do not determine the asymptotic constant, but remain in the expected scale.
