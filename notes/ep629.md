# EP-629

## Problem Statement
Let \(\chi_L(G)\) be the list chromatic number. Determine the minimal number of vertices
\(n(k)\) of a bipartite graph \(G\) such that \(\chi_L(G)>k\).

## Literature
- Erd\H{o}s, Rubin, Taylor showed
  \[
  2^{k-1}< n(k) < k^2 2^{k+2},
  \]
  and the sandwich
  \[
  m(k)\le n(k)\le m(k+1),
  \]
  where \(m(k)\) is the smallest size of a \(k\)-uniform family without Property B.
- Known exact values include \(n(2)=6\), \(n(3)=14\).
- Improved Property-B lower bounds imply stronger lower growth for \(n(k)\).

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented standalone search over \(k\)-uniform families to find small non-Property-B witnesses (via exhaustive 2-coloring checks on a fixed ground set).
- Used local search objective = minimize number of proper 2-colorings.
- Interpreted any discovered non-Property-B family size \(s\) as an induced upper-bound signal through the known \(m(\cdot)\)-to-\(n(\cdot)\) relation.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For \(k=4\), tested family sizes \(s=16\) to \(24\) on universe size \(11\): no non-Property-B family found in tested budget; best proper-coloring count decreased to \(48\) at \(s=24\).
- For \(k=5\), tested \(s=30\) to \(40\) on universe size \(12\): no non-Property-B family found; best proper-coloring count decreased to \(734\) at \(s=40\).
- These are negative search results under finite local-search budgets, not impossibility proofs.

## Status
- Open.
- Exact asymptotics of \(n(k)\) remain tied to deep progress on extremal Property-B thresholds.
