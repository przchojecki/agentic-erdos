# EP-295 deeper attempt

## Statement
`k(N)` is the minimum number of unit fractions with denominators `>=N` summing to `1`.
Question: does `k(N)-(e-1)N -> infinity`?

## Rigorous partial result
If `1 = sum_{i=1}^k 1/n_i` with `N<=n_1<...<n_k`, then
`H_{N+k-1}-H_{N-1} >= 1`.
Hence `k(N) >= k_harm(N)`, where `k_harm(N)` is the minimal `k` with
`H_{N+k-1}-H_{N-1} >= 1`.

I computed this lower-bound curve up to `N=20000`.

Data file:
- `data/ep295_harmonic_lower_bound_scan.json`

Observed range of `k_harm(N) - (e-1)N` on `N<=20000`:
- min `~-0.8591`
- max `~0.1419`

## Interpretation
This gives a clean rigorous lower-bound mechanism, but by itself it does not prove divergence of
`k(N)-(e-1)N`.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Decide whether
\[
k(N)-(e-1)N\to\infty
\]
for minimum-length Egyptian decompositions with all denominators \(\ge N\).

### Step A: Harmonic-envelope lower bound refinement
Strengthen the baseline \(k(N)\ge k_{\mathrm{harm}}(N)\) by incorporating
distinctness and spacing constraints on denominators.

### Step B: Extremal packing upper model
Construct near-optimal decompositions matching lower bounds up to secondary
terms.

### Step C: Secondary-term separation
Show unavoidable positive drift in \(k(N)-(e-1)N\), or produce bounded-drift
countermodel.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a quantitative "distinct reciprocal packing" inequality improving harmonic
lower bound by an additive term \(\omega(1)\).

## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Harmonic majorization)
If \(N\le n_1<\cdots<n_k\), then
\[
\sum_{i=1}^k\frac1{n_i}\le \sum_{j=N}^{N+k-1}\frac1j
=H_{N+k-1}-H_{N-1}.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Among increasing \(k\)-tuples starting at least at \(N\), reciprocal sum is
maximized by the smallest possible tuple \((N,\dots,N+k-1)\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Immediate lower bound)
If \(\sum_{i=1}^k 1/n_i=1\) with \(n_i\ge N\), then
\[
H_{N+k-1}-H_{N-1}\ge 1.
\]
Therefore \(k(N)\ge k_{\mathrm{harm}}(N)\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Apply Lemma 1 to the representing tuple.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Asymptotic harmonic inversion)
Let \(k=k_{\mathrm{harm}}(N)\). Then
\[
k=(e-1)N+O(1)
\]
by asymptotic inversion of harmonic numbers.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Use \(H_x=\log x+\gamma+O(1/x)\). Condition
\[
H_{N+k}-H_N\approx \log\frac{N+k}{N}\approx1
\]
gives \((N+k)/N\approx e\), hence \(k\approx (e-1)N\), with \(O(1)\) from error
terms.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 4 (Reduction to additive improvement)
To prove divergence of \(k(N)-(e-1)N\), it suffices to prove
\[
k(N)\ge k_{\mathrm{harm}}(N)+\omega(1).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Combine Lemma 3 with the displayed inequality.
\(\square\)
