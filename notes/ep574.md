# EP-574

## Problem Statement
For \(k\ge 2\), is it true that
\[
\operatorname{ex}(n;\{C_{2k-1},C_{2k}\})=(1+o(1))(n/2)^{1+1/k}\ ?
\]

## Literature / Context
- Prior notes indicate dedicated treatment for \(k=2\).
- The all-\(k\) asymptotic with the exact leading constant is the main open target.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented standalone randomized-greedy constructions for \(\{C_{2k-1},C_{2k}\}\)-free graphs.
- Measured finite-\(n\) ratios against \((n/2)^{1+1/k}\) for \(k=2,3\).
- This gives quantitative finite evidence but no full asymptotic proof.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Deep run (\(\sim 21.8\)s) yields ratios near:
  - \(k=2\): from \(1.13\) down to \(1.06\) as \(n\) grows.
  - \(k=3\): from \(1.48\) down to \(1.37\) as \(n\) grows.
- The downward trend is consistent with finite-size relaxation toward the conjectural constant.

## Next Proof Targets
- Build explicit algebraic constructions matching the observed scale for \(k=3\) beyond greedy heuristics.
- Isolate a transfer argument from known \(k=2\) machinery to larger odd/even cycle pairs.
