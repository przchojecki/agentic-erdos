# EP-597

## Problem Statement
For \(K_4\)- and \(K_{\aleph_0,\aleph_0}\)-free \(G\) on at most \(\aleph_1\) vertices, is
\[
\omega_1^2 \to (\omega_1\omega,\;G)^2
\]
true?

## Literature / Context
- Prior notes include a known special positive case and a correction showing an older weaker formulation fails without the \(K_{\aleph_0,\aleph_0}\)-free restriction.
- Full general statement remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Used a finite toy partition proxy:
  - red target is a large biclique \(K_{s,t}\),
  - blue target is a fixed small pattern (\(C_5\)).
- This is explicitly non-equivalent to the infinitary statement but gives finite forcing profiles.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Across tested sizes (\(n=18,22,26\)), the event “red \(K_{s,t}\) or blue \(C_5\)” appears with probability \(1\) in sampled random colorings.
- Blue \(C_5\) occurrence is saturated in this window; red biclique hit rates are also high.

## Next Proof Targets
- Replace toy finite proxies with structural reductions that preserve the \(K_4\)- and \(K_{\aleph_0,\aleph_0}\)-free constraints.
- Isolate candidate finite-pattern obstructions that might correspond to the unresolved infinitary case.
