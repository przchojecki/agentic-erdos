# EP-520

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

## Statement
Random multiplicative function partial sums: sharp limsup-scale behavior of
`S_N = \sum_{n\le N} f(n)` under LIL-type normalizations.

## What is known from background
Best known almost-sure upper bounds are near
`N^{1/2}(\log\log N)^{3/4+o(1)}`; sharp normalization constants remain open.

## New Experiments
- 2026-03-12T10:55:12.934Z: standalone deep Monte Carlo run
  (`scripts/ep520.mjs`) with `N=4,000,000` and `96` trials.
- Summary of trial limsup proxies:
  - `S_N / sqrt(N log log N)`: mean `0.977`, median `0.831`, q90 `1.373`.
  - `|S_N| / (sqrt(N)(log log N)^{1/4})`: mean `1.266`, median `1.134`, q90 `1.762`.
