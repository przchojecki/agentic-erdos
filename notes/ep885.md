# EP-885

## Problem Statement
For $D(n)=\{|a-b|: n=ab\}$, is it true that for every $k\ge1$ there exist
$$N_1<\cdots<N_k\quad\text{with}\quad\left|\bigcap_{i=1}^k D(N_i)\right|\ge k?$$

## Literature
- Known solved cases in notes: $k=2,3,4$.
- General $k$ remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Ran deeper finite search up to $N=120000$.
- Built support sets of differences and searched for $k$-tuples of differences with intersection size at least $k$ via bounded-depth intersection DFS.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Found explicit witnesses for $k=2,3$ in the searched range.
- Did not find witnesses for $k=4,5,6$ under this bounded candidate strategy.
- Non-findings are inconclusive and compatible with known positive theorem for $k=4$.

## Status
- Open in full generality.
- Added standalone deeper finite intersection search and normalized data integration.
