# EP-879

## Problem Statement
The problem asks about maximizing total sum for special pairwise-coprime admissible subsets of $\{1,\dots,n\}$ (denoted by a function like $G(n)$) and comparing this with a benchmark quantity $H(n)$, including asymptotic gap behavior and structural questions about prime-factor richness.

## Literature
- Erdos-van Lint established strong bounds of the form
  $$H(n)-n^{3/2-o(1)}<G(n)<H(n),$$
  and divergence of $(H(n)-G(n))/n$.
- Stronger near-linear error terms are known only conditionally in the background notes.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built an exact finite proxy optimization:
  - maximize sum of pairwise-coprime subsets of $[1..n]$ for moderate $n$ exactly,
  - compare against a simple greedy lower construction.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Exact optimizer and greedy lower are often close, but not always equal.
- In tested $n=16,18,20,22,24$, exact maxima are modestly above greedy in several cases.
- This supports that local optimization landscape is nontrivial even at small scales.
