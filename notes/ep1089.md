# EP-1089 partial

## Statement
Estimate `g_d(n)`, the least `m` such that every `m`-point set in `R^d`
determines at least `n` distinct distances.

## Attempt in this batch
Again for `n=3`, I used cross-polytope points (`2d` vertices) as a sharp
two-distance construction and computed distances exactly.

Data file:
- `data/ep1088_ep1089_construction_scan.json`

## Result
Cross-polytope yields exactly 2 distinct distances on `2d` points, hence:
- `g_d(3) >= 2d+1` (explicit lower bound).

## Hard point
The asymptotic question in the problem concerns fixed `n` and `d -> infinity`.
This batch only improves concrete lower-bound evidence in the `n=3` case.
