# EP-893

## Problem Statement
Let
$$
f(n)=\sum_{j=0}^{n-1}\tau(2^j-1),
$$
where $\tau$ is the divisor-counting function. Study the behavior of
$$
\frac{f(2n)}{f(n)}.
$$
In particular, determine whether this ratio has finite limiting behavior.

## Literature
- Recent work (Kovac--Luca, 2025; cited in prior project notes) indicates non-finite limiting behavior, with unbounded limsup in the relevant formulation.
- The problem page is still tracked as open for full sharp asymptotic characterization.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Ran exact finite-prefix computation using explicit factorization-by-trial-division for $\tau(2^k-1)$ up to $k\le 48$.
- Computed exact prefix sums $f(n)$ and ratio profile $f(2n)/f(n)$ for all $2\le n\le 24$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In tested range, ratio spans from $3$ up to about $8.08148$.
- Peak in this run appears at $n=18$.
- The finite data is consistent with highly non-stationary behavior and with an unbounded-limsup heuristic.
