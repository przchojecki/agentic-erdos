# EP-891

## Problem Statement
Let $2=p_1<p_2<\cdots$ be primes and fix $k\ge2$. Must every sufficiently large interval of length
$$p_1p_2\cdots p_k$$
contain an integer with more than $k$ prime factors?

## Literature
- A nearby positive variant is known (with length $p_1\cdots p_{k-1}p_{k+1}$).
- The exact stated length remains open, including classical interest for $k=2$.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Performed finite failure-window scan up to $X=300000$ for $k=2,3$.
- Checked both exact length and nearby variant length.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For $k=2$:
  - exact length 6 has finite failures (at the start),
  - variant length 10 has no failures in scanned range.
- For $k=3$, both tested lengths (30 and 42) show no failures in scanned range.
- Finite behavior aligns with the idea that exact length is tighter/more delicate.
