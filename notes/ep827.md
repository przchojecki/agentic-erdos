# EP-827

## Problem Statement
Let $n_k$ be the minimum integer such that every set of $n_k$ points in $\mathbb{R}^2$ in general position contains a $k$-point subset for which all $\binom{k}{3}$ triples determine circles of distinct radii. Determine $n_k$.

## Literature
- Erdos asked whether such $n_k$ exists.
- A classical simple argument for existence was later found incorrect.
- Martinez-Roldan-Pensado gave a corrected proof with polynomial upper bound
  $$n_k\ll k^9.$$

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented deeper finite random-general-position experiments.
- For each sampled point set, solved exactly (branch-and-bound) for largest subset with all triangle circumradii distinct.
- This is finite exploratory evidence only; no new asymptotic bound is proved.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In tested ranges $n=10,12,13,14$, full-size subsets were found in at least one sample for each $n$.
- Average best subset sizes were close to full size, suggesting strong finite generic behavior.
- These numerics support existence heuristics but do not improve the known polynomial bound.
