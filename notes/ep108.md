# EP-108 partial

## Statement
For `r>=4, k>=2`, does there exist finite `f(k,r)` such that every graph with
chromatic number at least `f(k,r)` contains a subgraph of girth at least `r`
and chromatic number at least `k`?

## Attempt in this batch
I aligned the statement with known solved/open subcases in the background.

## Result
- The `r=4` case is known solved (Rödl, per background).
- The general `r>=5` case remains open in this record.
- The related infinitary version is also recorded open.

## Hard point
Extending from triangle-free high-chromatic subgraphs (`r=4`) to larger girth
requires stronger cycle-control while preserving large chromatic number.
