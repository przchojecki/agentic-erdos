# EP-393 deep attempt with standalone computation

## Statement
Let `f(n)` be the minimal `m>=1` such that
`n!=a_1\cdots a_t` with `a_1<\cdots<a_t=a_1+m`. Determine the behavior of
`f(n)`.

## What is resolved from background
- Known counting bounds show fixed-gap events are sparse.
- Under additional hypotheses (ABC), one gets conditional growth `f(n)\to\infty`.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine asymptotic growth of minimal gap parameter \(f(n)\) in consecutive-product representation of \(n!\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a primitive-prime obstruction for short consecutive products: for any fixed small gap \(m\), all sufficiently large \(n\) have a prime valuation in \(n!\) not reproducible by any product of \(m+1\) consecutive integers.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (consecutive product factorial ratio)
\[
x(x+1)\cdots(x+m)=\frac{(x+m)!}{(x-1)!}.
\]

**Proof.**
Immediate telescoping ratio. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (valuation comparison criterion)
Equality \(n!=\prod_{j=0}^{m}(x+j)\) is equivalent to
\[
v_p(n!)=v_p((x+m)!)-v_p((x-1)!)
\]
for every prime \(p\).

**Proof.**
Take \(v_p\) on both sides using B1. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies \(f(n)\to\infty\))
If A rules out each fixed \(m\) for all large \(n\), then minimal admissible \(m=f(n)\) must diverge.

**Proof.**
For any fixed \(M\), eventually no \(m\le M\) works, so \(f(n)>M\). Since \(M\) is arbitrary, \(f(n)\to\infty\). \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is obtaining uniform valuation-separation primes for every fixed short gap without extra hypotheses (e.g. ABC).
