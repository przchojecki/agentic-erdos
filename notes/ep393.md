# EP-393 deep attempt with standalone computation

## Statement
Let `f(n)` be the minimal `m>=1` such that
`n!=a_1\cdots a_t` with `a_1<\cdots<a_t=a_1+m`. Determine the behavior of
`f(n)`.

## What is resolved from background
- Known counting bounds show fixed-gap events are sparse.
- Under additional hypotheses (ABC), one gets conditional growth `f(n)\to\infty`.

## Attempt route
Used exact finite searches for strongest small-gap proxies:
`n!=x(x+1)`, `n!=x(x+1)(x+2)`, and `n!=x(x+1)(x+2)(x+3)`.

## New Experiments
- 2026-03-12T09:41:33.087Z: standalone exact search (`scripts/ep393.mjs`) with
  ranges `n<=2000` (2-term), `n<=600` (3-term), `n<=260` (4-term).

  - 4 terms: only `n=4,5,7` in scanned range.
- This reinforces the small-gap rarity heuristic, but does not yet yield a

## Status
- strong finite sparsity signal: present.
- definitive asymptotic law for `f(n)`: unresolved.
