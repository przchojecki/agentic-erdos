# EP-455 partial

## Statement
If primes `q_1<q_2<...` satisfy convex gaps
`q_{n+1}-q_n >= q_n-q_{n-1}`, must `q_n/n^2 -> infinity`?

## Attempt in this batch
I searched for long convex-gap prime sequences via greedy extension from many
starting pairs, minimizing the end prime for each target length.

Data file:
- `data/ep455_prime_convex_gap_greedy_scan.json`

## Result
With `Pmax=10^7`, `lenMax=50`, and 6570 starting pairs:
- Found convex-gap sequences up to length `50`.
- Best end prime at length `50` is `q_50=3491`.
- Ratio `q_50/50^2 = 1.3964`.

Finite data stays at constant-scale multiples of `n^2`, consistent with known
positive liminf bounds but not proving divergence to infinity.

## Hard point
This is only a greedy finite search and does not cover all admissible sequences.
Need a universal argument for every convex-gap prime sequence, or an explicit
infinite construction with bounded `q_n/n^2`.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Decide whether convex prime-gap condition implies
\[
\frac{q_n}{n^2}\to\infty.
\]

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a second-difference growth lemma: for any convex-gap prime sequence, cumulative gap growth must eventually exceed every linear profile, i.e.
\[
q_n \ge n^2\,\omega(n)
\]
with \(\omega(n)\to\infty\), or construct explicit counterexample family.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (convex gaps imply quadratic lower bound)
If gaps \(g_n=q_{n+1}-q_n\) are nondecreasing, then
\[
q_n\ge q_1+\sum_{j< n} g_1 = q_1+(n-1)g_1,
\]
and more sharply \(q_n\ge c n^2\) when \(g_j\) grows at least linearly.

**Proof.**
Summation of monotone gap sequence; linear gap growth integrates to quadratic term. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (ratio criterion)
To prove \(q_n/n^2\to\infty\), it suffices to show average gap
\[
\frac1n\sum_{j\le n} g_j \to\infty.
\]

**Proof.**
\(q_n=q_1+\sum_{j<n}g_j\), divide by \(n^2\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies divergence)
If A establishes unbounded average-gap growth, B2 gives target divergence.

**Proof.**
Direct substitution into B2. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is proving mandatory superlinear growth of convex prime gaps for all admissible sequences, not just extremal finite constructions.
