# EP-1057

## Problem Statement
Let $C(x)$ be the number of Carmichael numbers $\le x$. Study growth rate and exponent behavior.

## Literature
- Modern lower-bound exponents improved over time; strongest asymptotic regime remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone Carmichael counting via exact Korselt criterion up to
\[
X=10{,}000{,}000.
\]
- Recorded probe values of $C(x)$, density $C(x)/x$, and empirical exponent proxy $\log C(x)/\log x$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Obtained
\[
C(10^7)=105.
\]
- Probe profile shows increasing $C(x)$ but rapidly decaying density $C(x)/x$ in this range.
- Finite values are compatible with known rarity and do not decide asymptotic exponent claims.

## Status
- Open.
- Added deeper standalone exact count profile to $10^7$.
