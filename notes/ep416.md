## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove precise asymptotics for
\[
V(x)=\#\{n\le x:\exists m,\ \phi(m)=n\},
\]
including whether \(V(2x)/V(x)\to2\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Establish a uniform short-interval totient-value theorem:
\[
V(x+h)-V(x)=\kappa\,\frac{h}{(\log x)^{\alpha}}(1+o(1))
\]
for \(h\in[x^\theta,x]\) with fixed \(\theta<1\), enough to integrate to global ratio.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (ratio from asymptotic)
If \(V(x)\sim F(x)\) with regularly varying \(F\) of index \(1\), then \(V(2x)/V(x)\to2\).

**Proof.**
Regular variation gives \(F(2x)/F(x)\to2\), and asymptotic equivalence transfers ratio to \(V\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (summation from local increments)
Asymptotic for increments in A implies asymptotic for \(V(x)\) by summation/integration over dyadic blocks.

**Proof.**
Sum local increment formula over partition of \([1,x]\); error terms are controlled by uniformity in \(h\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies EP-416 ratio claim)
Combine B2 then B1 to conclude \(V(2x)/V(x)\to2\).

**Proof.**
Direct composition. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The key obstacle is proving sufficiently uniform local distribution of totient values in short intervals.
