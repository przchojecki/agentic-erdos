# EP-1059

## Problem Statement
Find primes $p$ such that for every factorial $k!<p$, the number
\[
p-k!
\]
is composite; study infinitude of such primes.

## Literature
- Classical listed examples include $p=101$ and $p=211$; infinitude remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone deep prime scan up to
\[
p\le 11{,}000{,}000,
\]
using SPF primality checks and all factorial shifts below each prime.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Count of hits up to scan points:
  - $\le 10^6$: $7874$,
  - $\le 2\cdot 10^6$: $15868$,
  - $\le 4\cdot 10^6$: $31793$,
  - $\le 1.1\cdot 10^7$: $78049$.
- Finite data shows many candidates in this range, but does not prove infinitude.

- Open.
