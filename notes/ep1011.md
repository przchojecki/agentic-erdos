# EP-1011

## Problem Statement
Let $f_4(n)$ be the maximum number of edges in an $n$-vertex triangle-free graph with chromatic number at least $4$. Determine $f_4(n)$ asymptotically/explicitly.

## Literature
- Mantel gives the universal cap
\[
f_4(n)\le \left\lfloor\frac{n^2}{4}\right\rfloor.
\]
- Recent work gives much sharper formulas in large ranges; full landscape remains an active extremal topic.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Kept exact finite-certificate framing: every explicit triangle-free $\chi\ge4$ graph gives a rigorous lower bound for $f_4(n)$.
- Added deeper standalone randomized constructive search:
  - $n=11,12,\dots,16,18,20,22,24,26,28$,
  - triangle-free process generation,
  - exact DSATUR chromatic verification,
  - much larger trial counts (up to $9600$ at small $n$).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Feasible $\chi\ge4$ witnesses appear from $n=11$ onward in this run.
- Best edge witnesses found:
  - $f_4(20)\ge 75$,
  - $f_4(24)\ge 107$,
  - $f_4(28)\ge 146$.
- Finite witnesses track below Mantel cap, as expected.

## Status
- Open in full exact/general form.
- Added deeper standalone constructive lower-bound profile with exact chromatic checks.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine asymptotics/exact form of \(f_4(n)\): maximum edges in triangle-free \(n\)-vertex graphs with \(\chi\ge4\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a stability theorem near extremality: if \(e(G)\) is within \(o(n^2)\) of \(f_4(n)\), then \(G\) is close to a structured template family (Andrasfai-like blowups plus controlled perturbations).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (Mantel ceiling)
\[
f_4(n)\le \left\lfloor\frac{n^2}{4}\right\rfloor.
\]

**Proof.**
Every triangle-free graph satisfies Mantel bound. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (certificate lower bounds)
Any explicit triangle-free graph \(G\) on \(n\) vertices with \(\chi(G)\ge4\) yields
\[
f_4(n)\ge e(G).
\]

**Proof.**
\(f_4(n)\) is maximum over that class, so each member gives a lower bound. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies asymptotic determination)
If A classifies all near-extremizers and computes their edge counts up to \(o(n^2)\), then \(f_4(n)\) asymptotics follow.

**Proof.**
Upper bounds come from template optimality; matching lower bounds from explicit template constructions. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The central gap is a complete structural classification of high-edge triangle-free 4-chromatic graphs across all density regimes.
