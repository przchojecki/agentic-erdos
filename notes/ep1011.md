# EP-1011

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

### Working statement
Let \(f_4(n)\) denote the extremal edge count among triangle-free \(n\)-vertex graphs with chromatic number at least \(4\) (equivalently, exactly \(4\) in extremal settings).

### Extremal equivalence
For each \(n,m\),
\[
f_4(n)\ge m
\iff
\exists\ G:\ |V(G)|=n,\ |E(G)|\ge m,\ G\text{ triangle-free},\ \chi(G)\ge4.
\]
So every explicit graph with these properties is a rigorous lower-bound witness for \(f_4(n)\).

### Monotonicity lemma
\[
f_4(n+1)\ge f_4(n).
\]
Proof: take an extremal witness on \(n\) vertices and add one isolated vertex; triangle-freeness, chromatic number, and edge count are preserved.

### Universal upper bound
Every triangle-free graph on \(n\) vertices has at most
\[
\left\lfloor\frac{n^2}{4}\right\rfloor
\]
edges (Mantel).
Therefore
\[
f_4(n)\le \left\lfloor\frac{n^2}{4}\right\rfloor.
\]

So EP-1011 lives strictly below the Turán threshold; the hard part is determining the correct second-order behavior within this cap.

### Computation context
Finite searches in this project found small-\(n\) witnesses and support feasibility of dense triangle-free \(4\)-chromatic graphs, but do not determine asymptotic formulas.
