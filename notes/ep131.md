### Proposition AP1 (Monotonicity and scaling sanity)
The extremal function \(F(N)\) is nondecreasing and subadditive up to constants:
\[
F(N_1+N_2)\le F(N_1)+F(N_2)+O(1).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof sketch.}
\]
Embed extremal constructions on disjoint value ranges and merge with bounded
interaction correction.
\(\square\)

### Proposition AP2 (Exponent squeeze principle)
If
\[
N^{\alpha-o(1)}\le F(N)\le N^{\beta+o(1)},
\]
and one improves either side to \(\alpha' > \alpha\) or \(\beta' < \beta\), the
uncertainty interval \([\alpha,\beta]\) shrinks accordingly; matching
\(\alpha=\beta\) determines the growth exponent.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Immediate from logarithmic normalization:
\(\log F(N)/\log N\in[\alpha-o(1),\beta+o(1)]\).
\(\square\)
