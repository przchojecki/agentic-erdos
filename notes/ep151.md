## Useful reduction
For any graph `G`, taking the complement of a maximum independent set gives
`\tau(G) \le n-\alpha(G)` (this hits every clique of size at least `2`).
So the conjecture would follow from `\alpha(G)\ge H(n)`, which fails in general because `H(n)` is defined via triangle-free extremal behavior.

## Hard point
No mechanism found to transfer triangle-free independence guarantees into clique-transversal control for arbitrary graphs; background already warns the conjecture may be wrongheaded.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine validity of
\[
\tau(G)\le n-H(n)
\]
for all \(n\)-vertex graphs \(G\), or construct a true asymptotic counterexample.

### Step A: Extremal reduction
Reduce to graphs minimizing \(\alpha(G)\) under fixed clique-transversal
requirements.

### Step B: Dual LP / hypergraph-transversal formulation
Model clique hitting as a transversal problem in the clique hypergraph and
analyze fractional-integral gap.

### Step C: Transfer to \(H(n)\)
Either prove the integral gap is bounded tightly enough to force the inequality,
or amplify it into a family violating the bound.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a sharp integrality-gap bound between fractional and integral clique
transversals in dense triangle-rich regimes.

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition BA1 (Basic upper bound)
For any graph \(G\) on \(n\) vertices,
\[
\tau(G)\le n-\alpha(G).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Let \(I\) be a maximum independent set. Then \(V(G)\setminus I\) meets every
clique of size at least \(2\), so it is a clique transversal.
\(\square\)

### Proposition BA2 (Reduction criterion)
If one proves \(\alpha(G)\ge H(n)\) for all \(n\)-vertex graphs in the relevant
extremal class, then \(\tau(G)\le n-H(n)\) follows.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Combine BA1 with the hypothesis.
\(\square\)
