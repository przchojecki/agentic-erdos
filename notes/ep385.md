## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
For
\[
F(n):=\max_{\substack{m<n\\ m\ \mathrm{composite}}}\bigl(m+p(m)\bigr),
\]
prove eventual positivity \(F(n)>n\) and possibly \(F(n)-n\to\infty\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Show that for every large \(n\), there exists a composite \(m<n\) with
\[
p(m)>n-m.
\]
Then \(m+p(m)>n\), proving eventual positivity.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (equivalent reformulation)
\[
F(n)>n
\iff
\exists\, m<n,\ m\ \mathrm{composite},\ p(m)>n-m.
\]

**Proof.**
By definition, \(F(n)\) is the maximum of \(m+p(m)\) over composite \(m<n\). So \(F(n)>n\) exactly when at least one term exceeds \(n\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (short-interval largest-prime-factor bridge)
If there is \(\theta<1\) such that every large interval \((x-x^\theta,x)\) contains a composite \(m\) with \(p(m)>x^\theta\), then \(F(x)>x\) for all large \(x\).

**Proof.**
Take \(n=x\), choose \(m\in(x-x^\theta,x)\), so \(n-m<x^\theta\). If \(p(m)>x^\theta\), then \(p(m)>n-m\), and Lemma B1 gives \(F(n)>n\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (divergence criterion)
If for each \(H\) and all large \(n\) there is composite \(m<n\) with \(p(m)\ge n-m+H\), then \(F(n)-n\to\infty\).

**Proof.**
For such \(m\), \(m+p(m)-n\ge H\). Since \(H\) is arbitrary and holds for all large \(n\), the lower limit of \(F(n)-n\) is unbounded. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is obtaining sharp control on largest-prime-factor spikes inside short intervals among composites; current tools give partial distributional information but not the uniform interval-by-interval strength needed for Lemmas B2/B3.
