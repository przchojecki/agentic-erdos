# EP-857

## Problem Statement
Let $m(n,k)$ be the minimum $m$ such that any family of $m$ subsets of $[n]$ contains a sunflower of size $k$. Estimate $m(n,k)$ (ideally asymptotically).

## Literature
- Weak sunflower problem (Erdos formulation via unions/intersections).
- For $k=3$, closely connected to cap-set-type ideas and known exponential-base bounds.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Ran deeper randomized greedy construction for 3-sunflower-free families.
- For each $n$, searched many random orders and recorded best family size found.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Best lower-bound sizes found for $n=8..12$:
  - 34, 47, 65, 92, 118.
- Ratios to the benchmark base $(3/2^{2/3})^n$ decrease with $n$ in this finite range.
- This gives finite lower-bound evidence but does not narrow asymptotic constants sharply.
