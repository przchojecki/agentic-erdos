# EP-593

## Problem Statement
Characterize finite \(3\)-uniform hypergraphs that must occur in every \(3\)-uniform hypergraph of uncountable chromatic number.

## Literature / Context
- The graph (\(2\)-uniform) analogue is settled in prior notes, but the \(3\)-uniform characterization remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented a finite proxy linking high weak chromatic number in random 3-graphs to forced containment of a concrete pattern (\(K_4^{(3)}\)).
- This is not a proof of the infinitary characterization, but probes whether high finite chromatic pressure co-occurs with small fixed-pattern forcing.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For random 3-graphs at tested \((n,p)\), average weak chromatic estimates rise from about \(3.33\) to \(3.78\).
- Fraction with weak chromatic number at least \(4\) increases strongly with \(n\), while \(K_4^{(3)}\)-containment fraction stays moderate (\(\sim 0.27\)–\(0.35\)).
- Suggests high finite weak chromaticity does not trivially force this single pattern in these sparse windows.

## Next Proof Targets
- Test additional candidate forced families beyond \(K_4^{(3)}\) (e.g., loose cycles / expanded cores).
- Seek structural lemmas connecting high chromaticity to unavoidable subhypergraph templates in the \(3\)-uniform setting.
