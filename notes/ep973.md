# EP-973

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

### Working setup
For \(|z_i|=1\), define
\[
S_k:=\sum_{i=1}^n z_i^k.
\]
The computational objective tracks
\(
\max_{2\le k\le n+1}|S_k|.
\)

### Computation context
Heuristic searches (small \(n\)) did not find exponential decay in this short range of powers.

### Rigorous mean-square lower bound (all powers)
For fixed \(z_1,\dots,z_n\) on the unit circle,
\[
\liminf_{K\to\infty}\frac1K\sum_{k=1}^K |S_k|^2 \ge n.
\]

Proof:
Expand
\[
|S_k|^2=\sum_{i,j=1}^n (z_i\overline{z_j})^k.
\]
Averaging over \(k\le K\):
- diagonal terms \(i=j\) contribute exactly \(n\);
- off-diagonal terms with ratio \(\neq 1\) have Cesaro average tending to \(0\);
- off-diagonal terms with ratio \(=1\) (coincident points) contribute nonnegative amount.
So the asymptotic mean-square is at least \(n\).

### Corollary
\[
\sup_{k\ge 1}|S_k|\ge \sqrt n.
\]
Indeed, otherwise \(|S_k|^2<n\) for all \(k\), contradicting the averaged limit.

### Relevance to EP-973
This unconditional lower bound concerns all powers \(k\), not only the short window \(2\le k\le n+1\). So it does not settle the posed short-range extremal question, but it gives a rigorous obstruction against uniformly tiny power sums across all orders.
