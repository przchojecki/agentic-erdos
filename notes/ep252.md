## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Extend irrationality/transcendence results for
\[
\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{n^k}{2^n}
\]
from known small \(k\) to all \(k\) (or characterize exceptional \(k\), if any).

### Step A: Functional decomposition
Express the series via polylogarithmic/rational combinations and isolate
\(k\)-dependent arithmetic components.

### Step B: Linear-form framework
Construct linear forms in relevant constants with explicit denominator control.

### Step C: Irrationality criterion
Apply lower bounds for linear forms to exclude rationality.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a uniform-in-\(k\) lower bound for linear forms strong enough to beat
dyadic denominator growth of truncations.

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition DA1 (Tail bound)
For
\[
S_k=\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{n^k}{2^n},\quad S_{k,N}=\sum_{n\le N}\frac{n^k}{2^n},
\]
we have
\[
0<S_k-S_{k,N}\ll_k \frac{N^k}{2^N}.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Bound remaining terms by integral/geometric comparison.
\(\square\)

### Proposition DA2 (Rationality reduction)
If one proves that for infinitely many \(N\), no rational with denominator
\(\le Q_N\) lies within \(c_k N^k/2^N\) of \(S_k\), where \(Q_N\) exceeds the
natural denominator scale of \(S_{k,N}\), then \(S_k\) is irrational.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Combine DA1 with contradiction to hypothetical exact rational value.
\(\square\)
