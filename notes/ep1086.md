# EP-1086

## Problem Statement
Let $g(n)$ be the maximum number of equal-area triangles that can be determined by an $n$-point set in $\mathbb{R}^2$.
Estimate $g(n)$.

## Literature
- Classical bounds (Erdős-Purdy and later improvements) place
\[
n^2\log\log n\ll g(n)\ll n^{20/9}
\]
(best listed upper bound due to Raz-Sharir).
- The exact growth exponent and sharp order remain open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Replaced the batch stub with a standalone deep randomized search (`scripts/ep1086.mjs`).
- For each fixed $n$, sampled many distinct integer-grid point sets and computed exact area-multiplicity histograms over all nondegenerate triangles.
- Objective: maximize multiplicity of one area value (i.e., many triangles sharing the same area).

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine growth of \(g(n)\), the maximum multiplicity of one triangle area in an \(n\)-point planar set.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a structural theorem: if one area occurs \(M\) times, then the point set has an incidence-rich bipartite line-point decomposition forcing
\[
M \ll n^{2+\varepsilon}
\]
for all \(\varepsilon>0\) (or stronger).

Combined with known constructions, this would narrow the exponent gap sharply.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (area-line incidence encoding)
Fix ordered pair \((a,b)\). Points \(c\) with \([a,b,c]=A\) lie on at most two lines parallel to \(ab\).

**Proof.**
Area condition \(|[a,b,c]|=A\) is equivalent to fixed perpendicular distance from \(c\) to line \(ab\), giving two parallel offsets (or one if \(A=0\)). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (triple counting reduction)
Total number of triangles of area \(A\) can be bounded by summing point-line incidences over these offset lines generated by ordered pairs.

**Proof.**
For each ordered base \((a,b)\), count admissible \(c\) on the two offset lines from Lemma B1; summing over bases counts all ordered triangles of area \(A\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (incidence bound transfer)
Any incidence estimate \(I(P,\mathcal L)\ll n^\alpha\) for the generated line family implies \(g(n)\ll n^\alpha\) up to constants.

**Proof.**
Apply Lemma B2 and substitute the incidence upper bound. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The key gap is controlling multiplicities in the generated line family (many repeated or highly structured lines), where standard Szemeredi-Trotter bounds lose sharpness.
