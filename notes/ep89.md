## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove the distinct-distance lower bound in the plane at the conjectural scale
\[
D(P)\gg \frac{|P|}{\sqrt{\log |P|}}
\]
for every finite point set \(P\subset\mathbb R^2\), with sharp constants if possible.

### Step A: Energy-incidence decomposition
Control repeated distances via distance energy and incidence bounds with circles
and perpendicular bisectors.

### Step B: Structural dichotomy
Either energy is small (already yielding many distances) or \(P\) has rigid
lattice-like structure.

### Step C: Structured-case optimization
Show the rigid case still satisfies the target lower bound, matching the grid
benchmark up to constants.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a refined structured-case theorem: near-extremizers for distance energy
must be quantitatively close to product/lattice sets.

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition R1 (Energy-to-distinct-distance inequality)
Let \(m_t\) be multiplicities of each realized distance in \(P\), with
\(\sum_t m_t=\binom{n}{2}\), \(n=|P|\). Then
\[
D(P)\ge \frac{\binom{n}{2}^2}{\sum_t m_t^2}.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Cauchy-Schwarz:
\[
\Bigl(\sum_t m_t\Bigr)^2\le D(P)\sum_t m_t^2.
\]
Rearrange.
\(\square\)

### Proposition R2 (Reduction to fourth-moment control)
If one proves
\[
\sum_t m_t^2\ll n^3\sqrt{\log n},
\]
then
\[
D(P)\gg \frac{n}{\sqrt{\log n}}.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Insert into Proposition R1 and use \(\binom{n}{2}\asymp n^2\).
\(\square\)
