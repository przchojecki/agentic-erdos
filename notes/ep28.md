## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Resolve the Erdos-Turan representation-growth implication underlying EP-28:
if additive coverage is near-complete, peak representation multiplicity must be
large at the conjectured scale.

### Step A: Coverage-to-Energy Lower Bound
From near-full sumset coverage on \([1,N]\), derive quantitative additive-energy
lower bound stronger than Cauchy baseline.

### Step B: Energy-to-Peak Conversion
Convert Step A into a lower bound on
\[
\max_n r_{A+A}(n),
\]
with explicit dependence on uncovered fraction.

### Step C: Asymptotic Contradiction/Threshold
Show bounded peak multiplicity is incompatible with persistent near-full
coverage as \(N\to\infty\).

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need an inverse additive-combinatorics theorem at high coverage: any set with
coverage \(1-o(1)\) and low peak multiplicity must lie near a rigid progression
model that fails the same coverage requirement.

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

Let \(A\subset \mathbb Z\) be finite and
\[
r(n):=\#\{(a,a')\in A^2:\ a+a'=n\},\qquad R:=\{n:\ r(n)>0\}.
\]

### Proposition A1 (Coverage => average representation lower bound)
If \(|R|\le L\), then
\[
\max_n r(n)\ge \frac{|A|^2}{L}.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Since \(\sum_n r(n)=|A|^2\), averaging over at most \(L\) nonzero indices gives
\[
\max_n r(n)\ge \frac1{|R|}\sum_n r(n)\ge \frac{|A|^2}{L}.
\]
\(\square\)

### Proposition A2 (Near-full coverage forces large peak under density)
Assume \(A+A\) covers at least \((1-\delta)N\) integers of a target interval of
length \(N\), and all represented sums in that interval lie in a set of size
\(\le N\). Then
\[
\max_n r(n)\ge \frac{|A|^2}{N}.
\]
In particular, if \(|A|\ge c\sqrt N\), then \(\max_n r(n)\ge c^2\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Apply Proposition A1 with \(L\le N\).
\(\square\)

### Proposition A3 (Bounded peak bounds achievable coverage)
If \(r(n)\le M\) for all \(n\), then
\[
|R|\ge \frac{|A|^2}{M}.
\]
Equivalently, for fixed support window length \(N\), bounded \(M\) imposes
\[
|A|^2\le MN.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
From \(|A|^2=\sum_n r(n)\le M|R|\).
\(\square\)

These propositions rigorously justify the final implication in the roadmap:
once Step A/B gives a lower bound on \(|A|\) at scale \(\sqrt N\), bounded peak
multiplicity is incompatible with near-full coverage.
