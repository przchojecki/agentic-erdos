# EP-987 deeper attempt

## Statement
For `A_k = limsup_n |sum_{j<=n} e(k x_j)|`, must `limsup_k A_k = infinity`? Is `A_k=o(k)`
possible?

## Computation in this attempt
Finite-`N` proxies for deterministic sequence models (`N=40000`, `k<=800`).

Data file:
- `data/ep987_exponential_sum_models.json`

Model behavior:
- arithmetic `x_j={j alpha}` with `alpha=sqrt(2)-1`: very small `A_k/k` tail.
- van der Corput base-2: sublinear but larger spikes.
- quadratic `x_j={alpha j^2}`: much larger finite spikes.

## Interpretation
Finite model behavior is diverse and supports that growth strongly depends on structure of the
sequence. This does not settle the asymptotic existence question.

## Rigorous identities and bounds
For fixed \(N\), define
\[
S_N(k):=\sum_{j=1}^N e(kx_j),\qquad e(t):=e^{2\pi i t}.
\]

### Lemma 1 (trivial absolute bound)
\[
|S_N(k)|\le N\quad\text{for all }k.
\]
Proof: triangle inequality and \(|e(kx_j)|=1\).

### Lemma 2 (pair-correlation expansion)
\[
|S_N(k)|^2=\sum_{a,b\le N} e\!\big(k(x_a-x_b)\big).
\]
Hence for \(K\ge1\),
\[
\frac1K\sum_{k=1}^K |S_N(k)|^2

=N+\sum_{a\ne b}\frac1K\sum_{k=1}^K e\!\big(k(x_a-x_b)\big).
\]

This exact identity separates diagonal mass (\(=N\)) from off-diagonal cancellation.

### Consequence for strategy
To force large limsup behavior, one seeks sequence structures where off-diagonal cancellation is weak for infinitely many \(k\); to force small behavior, one needs strong cancellation uniformly in \(k\).
So EP-987 can be viewed as a quantitative cancellation problem in exponential sums indexed by integer frequencies.
