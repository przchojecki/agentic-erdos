# EP-610

## Problem Statement
Estimate clique-transversal bounds \(\tau(G)\) and sharpen known asymptotics beyond the \(n-\sqrt{2n}+O(1)\) barrier.

## Literature / Context
- Baseline general bound: \(\tau(G)\le n-\sqrt{2n}+O(1)\).
- Stronger conjectural forms relate to triangle-free/independence-transfer phenomena.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built exact small-\(n\) computation:
  - enumerate maximal cliques via Bron-Kerbosch,
  - solve minimum clique-transversal size exactly by branch-and-bound hitting set.
- Compared exact \(\tau(G)\) against \(n-\sqrt{2n}\) on random samples.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For tested regimes (\(n=14,16,18\)), sampled exact \(\tau(G)\) averages are around \(0.40n\)–\(0.43n\), well below \(n-\sqrt{2n}\).
- Finite data confirms slack in the classical upper bound at small scales.

## Next Proof Targets
- Use exact small-\(n\) extremizers to identify structural patterns that inflate \(\tau(G)\).
- Build constructions approaching upper-bound scale to test plausible second-order terms.
