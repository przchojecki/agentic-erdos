# EP-424 partial resolution

## Statement
For the Hofstadter sequence generated by `a_i a_j - 1`, is the set of attained
integers of positive density?

## What is resolved
Background clarifies a stronger historical phrasing ("almost all integers") is
false: by a mod-3 invariant, no integer congruent to `1 (mod 3)` appears, so
density is at most `2/3`.

## What remains open in this note
The weaker current question (positive density) is the intended version and is not
resolved by the cited material.

## Status
- stronger "almost all" version: false.
- positive-density version: unresolved.

## Added Proof Layer (No New Computation)

### Closed Part (Historical Strong Form)
The stronger claim "almost all integers are attained" is false by the mod-3
obstruction: values congruent to \(1\pmod 3\) are excluded, so density cannot
exceed \(2/3\).

### Open Part (Current Formulation)
The intended weaker question is whether the attained set has **positive**
density.

### Blocking Lemma
- Need a multiplicative-closure growth theorem giving a nonzero lower-density
  floor for the orbit of the \(a_i a_j-1\) generation rule despite the modular
  obstruction.

Current arguments here only show a residue-class exclusion upper bound, not a
matching positive lower bound.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Show the attained set \(\mathcal A\) has
\[
\underline d(\mathcal A)>0 \quad\text{or at least}\quad \overline d(\mathcal A)>0.
\]

### Step A: Structured Residue Expansion
Prove there exists a modulus family \((q_j)\) such that the attained residues
\(\mathcal A \bmod q_j\) occupy at least \(\delta q_j\) classes for some fixed
\(\delta>0\), uniformly in \(j\).

### Step B: Lift Residue Expansion to Interval Counts
Given Step A, prove a transfer bound of shape
\[
|\mathcal A\cap[1,X]|\ge c(\delta)\,X - E(X),
\]
with \(E(X)=o(X)\) (or a power-saving \(X^{1-\eta}\)).

### Step C: Positive Density Conclusion
From Step B conclude positive upper/lower density.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a bilinear-growth lemma for the operation
\[
(u,v)\mapsto uv-1
\]
on the current attained set: unless the set is trapped in a rigid congruence
subvariety, one generation step must expand residue support by a fixed factor.

### Method Directions
- Sieve/expander style on multiplicative semigroup modulo \(q\).
- Sum-product style anti-concentration in \(\mathbb Z/q\mathbb Z\).
- LLL-style control of residue collisions across many moduli.
