# EP-431 deep attempt with standalone computation

## Statement
Do there exist two infinite sets `A,B` such that `A+B` agrees with the set of
primes up to finitely many exceptions?

## What is resolved from background
- Strong inverse-Goldbach obstructions are known (size constraints for such
  `A,B` if they existed), and full problem is believed negative.

## Attempt route
Computed finite-window sumset prime-coverage using random search plus local
improvement over candidate sets.

## New Experiments
- 2026-03-12T10:15:36.058Z: standalone optimized finite search
  (`scripts/ep431.mjs`) on primes up to `10000`.

## One-by-One Cycle
- Replaced batch-derived placeholder with deeper random+local optimization.
- Best coverage in tested regime reached about `35.3%` of primes (size
  `70x70` sets in a bounded universe), with average random baseline around
  `26.2%`.
- Finite evidence remains far from near-total coverage, supporting the expected
  difficulty of inverse Goldbach.

## Status
- strong finite negative heuristic: present.
- unconditional impossibility theorem: unresolved.
