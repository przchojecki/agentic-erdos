### Proposition BG1 (Ramsey reformulation)
\(R(C_4,K_n)>N\) iff there exists a \(C_4\)-free graph \(G\) on \(N\) vertices
with \(\alpha(G)<n\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Standard two-color edge interpretation: red graph \(G\), blue complement.
\(\square\)

### Proposition BG2 (Exponent-drop reduction)
If every \(C_4\)-free \(N\)-vertex graph satisfies
\[
\alpha(G)\ge N^{\varepsilon}
\]
for some fixed \(\varepsilon>0\) and large \(N\), then
\[
R(C_4,K_n)\ll n^{1/\varepsilon}=n^{2-c}
\]
for \(c=2-1/\varepsilon\) whenever \(1/\varepsilon<2\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
By BG1, if \(R(C_4,K_n)>N\), then \(n>\alpha(G)\ge N^\varepsilon\), so
\(N<n^{1/\varepsilon}\). Rearrangement gives the bound.
\(\square\)
