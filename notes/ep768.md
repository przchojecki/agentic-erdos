# EP-768

## Problem Statement
For a special integer set \(A\) defined by local prime-divisor congruence constraints, determine whether its counting density has the form
\[
\exp\!\bigl(-(c+o(1))\sqrt{\log N}\,\log\log N\bigr).
\]

## Literature
- Reported two-sided bounds currently have different exponent scales:
  - lower model of type \(\exp(-c\sqrt{\log N}\log\log N)\),
  - upper model of type \(\exp(-\sqrt{\log N\log\log N})\).
- Matching asymptotic shape and constants remain open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built standalone quantitative gap profiler for these two model scales across large \(N\).
- Evaluated sensitivity to the unknown constant \(c\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- The upper/lower ratio behavior is highly sensitive to \(c\):
  - for small \(c\) (e.g. 0.5), model lower can exceed the model upper,
  - for larger \(c\), the gap becomes enormous as \(N\) grows.
- This numerically illustrates why current known scales do not yet pin down a unique asymptotic regime.
