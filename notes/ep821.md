# EP-821

## Problem Statement
Let
$$g(n):=\#\{m\in\mathbb N:\varphi(m)=n\}.$$
Is it true that for every $\epsilon>0$ there are infinitely many $n$ such that
$$g(n)>n^{1-\epsilon}?$$

## Literature
- Pillai proved $\limsup g(n)=\infty$.
- Erdos proved that for some constant $c>0$, one has $g(n)>n^c$ for infinitely many $n$.
- The near-linear exponent target $1-\epsilon$ is still open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Ran a deeper exact totient-preimage multiplicity scan up to $N=2\cdot 10^6$.
- Computed all $\varphi(m)$ for $m\le N$, then tabulated frequencies of each value.
- Extracted record progression and top multiplicities.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In this range, the maximum found is
  $$g(483840)=1345,$$
  corresponding to empirical exponent
  $$\frac{\log g(n)}{\log n}\approx 0.5504.$$
- Record exponents in the progression stay around $0.54$-$0.57$, still far from $1$.
