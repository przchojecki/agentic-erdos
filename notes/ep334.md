## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine asymptotic order of minimal smoothness threshold \(f(n)\) in
\(n=a+b\) with both \(a,b\) \(y\)-smooth.

### Step A: Friable counting in additive slices
Estimate number of \(y\)-smooth integers in \([1,n]\) and pair sums hitting \(n\).

### Step B: Exceptional-set control
Bound integers \(n\) with no \(y\)-smooth decomposition by sieve over short
intervals.

### Step C: Optimize \(y=y(n)\)
Choose \(y\) to make exception set sparse enough for all large \(n\).

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a uniform additive friable theorem: for chosen \(y(n)\), every sufficiently
large \(n\) has at least one representation \(n=a+b\) with both \(a,b\in
\Psi(\cdot,y)\).

## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Representation counting identity)
Let \(S_y(n)=\{m\le n:\ P^+(m)\le y\}\). Number of \(y\)-smooth decompositions is
\[
R_y(n)=\#\{a\in S_y(n):\ n-a\in S_y(n)\}.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Each decomposition \(n=a+b\) corresponds to unique \(a\), with \(b=n-a\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Sufficient condition for existence)
If \(R_y(n)\ge1\), then \(f(n)\le y\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Direct from definition of \(f(n)\) as minimal such \(y\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction)
To prove \(f(n)\le Y(n)\) eventually, it suffices to prove \(R_{Y(n)}(n)\ge1\)
for all sufficiently large \(n\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Apply Lemma 2 pointwise for large \(n\).
\(\square\)
