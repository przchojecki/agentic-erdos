# EP-813

## Problem Statement
Let $h(n)$ be the minimum integer such that every graph on $n$ vertices in which every 7-vertex set contains a triangle must contain a clique of size at least $h(n)$. Estimate $h(n)$; in particular, can one show constants $c_1,c_2>0$ with
$$n^{1/3+c_1}\ll h(n)\ll n^{1/2-c_2}?$$

## Literature
- Erdos-Hajnal: classical baseline bounds
  $$n^{1/3}\ll h(n)\ll n^{1/2}.$$
- Bucic-Sudakov (2023): improved lower bound
  $$h(n)\gg n^{5/12-o(1)}.$$

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented deeper one-by-one finite search over random dense graphs.
- For each tested $n$, optimized candidate quality by minimizing sampled violations of the local 7-set triangle condition.
- On best candidates, computed clique number exactly via branch-and-bound over bitmasks.
- No full finite witness satisfying the local condition was found in these runs.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Best sampled candidates at $n=26,30,34,38$ still had nonzero sampled violations.
- Exact clique numbers on best candidates were 7, 7, 7, 8 respectively.
- The finite search is informative about scale but does not yet yield structural proof input.
