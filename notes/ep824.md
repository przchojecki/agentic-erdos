# EP-824

## Problem Statement
Let $h(x)$ count pairs $1\le a<b<x$ such that $(a,b)=1$ and
$$\sigma(a)=\sigma(b),$$
where $\sigma$ is the sum-of-divisors function. Is it true that
$$h(x)>x^{2-o(1)}?$$

## Literature
- Erdos proved $\limsup h(x)/x=\infty$ and indicated stronger growth heuristics.
- Pollack-Pomerance provided a complete proof that $h(x)/x\to\infty$.
- The near-quadratic lower bound target remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented deeper finite counting for $x=10^4,2\cdot10^4,3\cdot10^4,4\cdot10^4,5\cdot10^4$.
- For each $x$, grouped integers by equal $\sigma$ value and counted coprime pairs exactly.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Measured values increase from $h(10^4)=15073$ to $h(5\cdot10^4)=125083$.
- Ratios $h(x)/x$ increase (about $1.51\to 2.50$), matching known superlinear behavior.
- Effective finite log-slope is around $1.08$, still far below the conjectural near-$2$ regime.
