## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Clarify growth of the maxima-count function \(u(r)\), especially the open \(\liminf u(r)=\infty\) direction.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a uniform angular-separation lemma: for every large scale parameter \(T\), there exists an interval of radii \(r\in[T,2T]\) where critical-point arguments of the relevant polynomial family are \(\delta(T)\)-separated with \(\delta(T)\to0\), forcing at least \(h(T)\to\infty\) local maxima.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (critical-point count controls maxima count)
If on an arc/annulus all critical points are simple and alternating with monotonicity changes, then local maxima count is at least half the critical-point count up to boundary terms.

**Proof.**
Between consecutive simple critical points, derivative sign alternates; every second sign change yields a local maximum. Boundary contributes \(O(1)\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (lacunary polynomial proxy transfer)
For lacunary families (e.g. \(z^m+1\)), argument spacing of zeros/critical points gives explicit lower bounds on \(u(r)\) at selected \(r\).

**Proof.**
Roots/critical points lie on near-regular angular grids; counting alternating extrema on circles of fixed radius yields explicit maxima counts. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies \(\liminf\)-growth)
If Lemma A provides \(h(T)\to\infty\) on every dyadic block, then \(\liminf_{r\to\infty}u(r)=\infty\).

**Proof.**
Choose \(r_T\in[T,2T]\) with \(u(r_T)\ge h(T)\); since \(h(T)\to\infty\), minima over tails diverge. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The hard step is obtaining uniform (not subsequential) control of angular critical-point separation in the full non-lacunary setting.
