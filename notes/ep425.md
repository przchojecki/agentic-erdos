# EP-425 partial attempt

Ran repeated greedy constructions for multiplicative Sidon-type sets in `[1,n]` and compared sizes against `pi(n)` and the conjectured `n^(3/4)(log n)^(-3/2)` scale.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine asymptotic size and constant for maximal multiplicative Sidon-type sets \(A\subset[1,n]\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a structural decomposition theorem: every near-extremal \(A\) splits into
1) a prime-dominated part and
2) a sparse composite correction,
with correction size bounded by \(C n^{3/4}(\log n)^{-3/2}(1+o(1))\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (injective product constraint)
Multiplicative Sidon condition is equivalent to injectivity of map \((a,b)\mapsto ab\) on unordered pairs from \(A\).

**Proof.**
By definition no nontrivial equality \(a_1a_2=b_1b_2\) among distinct unordered pairs is allowed. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (energy upper relation)
Injectivity in B1 forces multiplicative energy \(E_\times(A)\) near its diagonal minimum.

**Proof.**
\(E_\times(A)\) counts product collisions; Sidon-type condition suppresses off-diagonal contributions. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies asymptotic constant program)
If A identifies all near-extremal corrections with sharp bound, then maximal \(|A|\) asymptotics and constant follow.

**Proof.**
Combine prime baseline term with bounded correction asymptotic from A. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is proving universal upper structure on composite corrections matching known lower constructions.
