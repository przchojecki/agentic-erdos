## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove finiteness (or infinitude) of interval pairs \((I_1,I_2)\) such that
\[
\sum_{n\in I_1}\frac1n+\sum_{n\in I_2}\frac1n\in\mathbb Z.
\]

### Step A: Denominator growth constraints
Express interval harmonic sums in reduced form and track prime-power valuations of
their denominators.

### Step B: Valuation mismatch forcing
Show for large interval endpoints, at least one prime valuation in denominator
cannot be canceled across two interval sums.

### Step C: Global finiteness deduction
Conclude only bounded endpoint ranges can satisfy integrality.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a sharp \(p\)-adic valuation lemma for interval harmonic sums:
for large intervals, there is a prime \(p\) with a uniquely minimal valuation term
in the block, preventing integer cancellation when adding a second block.

## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Interval harmonic representation)
For interval \(I=[a,b]\),
\[
H(I):=\sum_{n=a}^b \frac1n = \frac{U_{a,b}}{\mathrm{lcm}(a,\dots,b)}
\]
for some integer \(U_{a,b}\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Multiply each \(1/n\) by \(L/\!n\), where \(L=\mathrm{lcm}(a,\dots,b)\). Sum of
integers over common denominator \(L\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Integrality criterion for pair)
For disjoint intervals \(I_1,I_2\),
\[
H(I_1)+H(I_2)\in\mathbb Z
\]
iff numerator in common denominator \(L=\mathrm{lcm}(I_1\cup I_2)\) is divisible
by \(L\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Write
\[
H(I_1)+H(I_2)=\frac{A}{L}
\]
with \(A\in\mathbb Z\). Rational is integer iff \(L\mid A\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction to valuation obstruction)
If one proves that for all sufficiently large interval pairs there exists prime
\(p\) with \(v_p(A)<v_p(L)\) in Lemma 2, then only finitely many integral pairs
exist.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Then \(L\nmid A\), so integrality fails for all large pairs. Hence only finitely
many exceptions remain.
\(\square\)
