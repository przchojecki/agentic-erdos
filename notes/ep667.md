# EP-667

## Problem Statement
Fix \(p,q\). Let \(H(n)=H(n;p,q)\) be the largest \(m\) such that every \(n\)-vertex graph in which each \(p\)-vertex subset spans at least \(q\) edges must contain a clique \(K_m\).
For
\[
c(p,q)=\liminf_{n\to\infty} \frac{\log H(n)}{\log n},
\]
is \(c(p,q)\) strictly increasing in \(q\) for \(1\le q\le \binom{p-1}{2}+1\)?

## Literature
- \(q=1\): Ramsey regime with classical bounds.
- \(q=\binom{p-1}{2}+1\): \(c(p,q)=1\).
- Near-endpoint bound \(c(p,\binom{p-1}{2})\le 1/2\) is known.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Performed sampled graph search for \(p=5\), \(q=1,\dots,7\), small \(n\).
- For each \((n,p,q)\), searched feasible graphs satisfying the local density condition and recorded a proxy minimum clique size.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Feasible sample graphs become much rarer as \(q\) increases.
- The sampled forced-clique proxy tends to increase with \(q\) in tested ranges, matching monotonicity heuristics.
- This is finite stochastic evidence only, not an asymptotic proof for strict monotonicity of \(c(p,q)\).
