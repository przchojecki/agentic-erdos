# EP-694

## Problem Statement
Let
- \(f_{\max}(n)=\max\{m:\varphi(m)=n\}\),
- \(f_{\min}(n)=\min\{m:\varphi(m)=n\}\).
Study
\[
\max_{n\le x}\frac{f_{\max}(n)}{f_{\min}(n)}.
\]

## Literature
- Connected to Carmichael's question on unique preimages of \(\varphi\).
- Erd\H{o}s proved that existence of one unique-preimage \(n\) implies infinitely many.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built full \(\varphi(m)\) table up to \(m\le 300000\).
- For each \(n\), recorded min/max preimages and scanned maxima of the ratio up to various \(x\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- The extremal ratio up to tested \(x\) stabilizes around \(\approx 5.21\) in this range.
- Extremizers come from specific totient values with notably spread preimage sizes.
- This finite profile provides concrete growth calibration but not asymptotic determination.
