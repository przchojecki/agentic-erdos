# EP-825

## Problem Statement
Is there an absolute constant $C>0$ such that every integer $n$ with
$$\sigma(n)>Cn$$
is a distinct sum of proper divisors of $n$?

## Literature
- This is the Benkoski-Erdos problem on bounding abundancy of weird numbers.
- Equivalent viewpoint: is there a universal upper bound on abundancy index among weird numbers?
- Current project notes mark this problem as solved in the affirmative (Larsen update); computations here are consistency checks.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Performed deeper finite scan up to $N=80{,}000$:
  - identify abundant $n$;
  - test semiperfectness via exact subset-sum DP over proper divisors;
  - record weird numbers and maximal abundancy.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Found $19{,}841$ abundant integers and $166$ weird numbers up to $80{,}000$.
- Maximum weird abundancy in-range is about
  $$2.0709492.$$
- On a $0.01$ grid, no counterexample above $C=2.08$ appears in this range.

## Status

- Added standalone deeper scan and normalized data integration.
