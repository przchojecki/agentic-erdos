## Statement
For the greedy Sidon (Mian-Chowla) sequence `A`, determine growth of
`|A cap [1,N]|`; in particular whether it is `>> N^(1/2-eps)` for all eps.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Establish lower bounds for greedy Sidon counting function \(A(N):=|A\cap[1,N]|\),
ideally \(A(N)\gg N^{1/2-\varepsilon}\) for every \(\varepsilon>0\).

### Step A: Increment-control inequality
Bound growth of next term \(a_{m+1}\) in terms of \(m\) by counting forbidden
differences/sums.

### Step B: Differential inequality for inverse counting
Translate \(a_m\) bounds into lower bounds on \(A(N)\) via inversion.

### Step C: Exponent extraction
Optimize Step A constants to approach exponent \(1/2\).

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a sharp forbidden-value count at greedy step \(m\) showing
\(a_{m+1}\le m^{2+o(1)}\).

## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Sidon difference constraint)
For first \(m\) terms \(a_1<\cdots<a_m\), there are \(\binom m2\) distinct positive
differences.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Sidon property implies all pairwise differences are distinct.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Quadratic inversion principle)
If \(a_m\le C m^\alpha\) for all large \(m\), then
\[
A(N)\ge c N^{1/\alpha}
\]
for some \(c>0\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
For given \(N\), let \(m=A(N)\), so \(a_m\le N\). Then
\[
N\ge a_m\ge C^{-1}m^\alpha
\]
up to constants, hence \(m\ll N^{1/\alpha}\) inverted gives lower bound.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction)
To prove \(A(N)\gg N^{1/2-\varepsilon}\), it suffices to prove for every
\(\varepsilon>0\):
\[
a_m\ll m^{2+\varepsilon}.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Apply Lemma 2 with \(\alpha=2+\varepsilon\).
\(\square\)
