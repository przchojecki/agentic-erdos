# EP-1005

## Problem Statement
Let
\[
\frac{a_1}{b_1},\frac{a_2}{b_2},\ldots
\]
be the Farey fractions of order $n\ge 4$. Let $f(n)$ be the largest integer such that whenever
\[
1\le k<\ell\le k+f(n),
\]
the fractions $\frac{a_k}{b_k}$ and $\frac{a_\ell}{b_\ell}$ are similarly ordered, i.e.
\[
(a_k-a_\ell)(b_k-b_\ell)\ge 0.
\]
Estimate $f(n)$; in particular, is there $c>0$ such that
\[
f(n)=(c+o(1))n?
\]

## Literature
- Mayer proved $f(n)\to\infty$.
- Erdős proved $f(n)\gg n$.
- 2025 update (van Doorn):
\[
\left(\frac{1}{12}-o(1)\right)n\le f(n)\le \frac{1}{4}n+O(1),
\]
with conjectured sharp upper-side constant $1/4$.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Replaced batch-derived stub with an exact standalone implementation of the definition (`scripts/ep1005.mjs`).
- Key exact reformulation: if $d_{\min}$ is the smallest index gap producing a discordant pair, then
\[
f(n)=d_{\min}-1.
\]
- Computation scans Farey sequences and finds first discordant witness gap exactly.

## Computation-Guided Observations
Deep exact run for
\[
n\in\{180,240,300,360,480,600,720,840,960,1080,1200\}
\]
gives:
- $f(180)=46$, $f(240)=61$, $f(300)=76$, ..., $f(1200)=301$,
- ratios decrease toward $1/4$:
  - $f(180)/180=0.2555555556$,
  - $f(600)/600=0.2516666667$,
  - $f(1200)/1200=0.2508333333$.
- all first discordant witnesses follow the same central pattern:
\[
\frac{n/2-1}{n} \quad \text{vs.} \quad \frac{n/2}{n-1},
\]
with gap about $n/4+2$, yielding $f(n)=n/4+1$ on this even-$n$ range.

This is finite exact evidence and is consistent with the conjectured $1/4$ constant.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine asymptotic constant in \(f(n)\sim c n\), conjecturally \(c=1/4\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove that every discordant pair within Farey order appears at gap at most
\[
\frac n4+O(1),
\]
and that extremal first discordance is realized by the central pattern
\[
\frac{n/2-1}{n},\ \frac{n/2}{n-1}
\]
up to \(O(1)\)-perturbations.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (first-discordance identity)
If \(d_{\min}\) is smallest index gap with discordant pair, then \(f(n)=d_{\min}-1\).

**Proof.**
By definition, all pairs with gap \(\le f(n)\) are similarly ordered, and some pair with gap \(f(n)+1\) is discordant. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (Farey-neighbor determinant control)
For neighboring Farey fractions \(a/b<c/d\), \(bc-ad=1\). This rigid determinant condition constrains relative numerator/denominator monotonicity in short index windows.

**Proof.**
Standard Farey neighbor property. Determinant identity follows from mediant characterization. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies asymptotic constant)
If A gives matching upper/lower gap location \(n/4+O(1)\), then
\[
f(n)=\frac n4+O(1),
\]
hence \(f(n)=(1/4+o(1))n\).

**Proof.**
Apply B1 and subtract 1; divide by \(n\). \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is proving global optimality of the central discordance pattern over all local Farey configurations.
