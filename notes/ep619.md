# EP-619

## Problem Statement
For connected triangle-free \(G\) on \(n\) vertices, is there \(c>0\) such that
\[
h_4(G)<(1-c)n,
\]
where \(h_4(G)\) is the minimum number of added edges (while preserving triangle-freeness) to achieve diameter \(4\)?

## Literature / Context
- Known neighboring cases in prior notes:
  - \(h_3(G)\le n\),
  - \(h_5(G)\le (n-1)/2\).
- The \(r=4\) threshold remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built sparse triangle-free connected graph generator with typically large starting diameter.
- Ran greedy triangle-preserving augmentation minimizing diameter down to \(4\).
- This is finite heuristic evidence, not a proof.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For tested sizes \(n=24,30,36\), all sampled instances were reduced to diameter \(4\).
- Average added-edge ratio \((\Delta m)/n\) was about:
  - \(0.46\) at \(n=24\),
  - \(0.47\) at \(n=30\),
  - \(0.66\) at \(n=36\),
  with worst observed near \(0.94\) in the hardest sampled case.

## Next Proof Targets
- Replace greedy heuristic by exact/branch-and-bound augmentation on moderate \(n\) instances.
- Construct adversarial sparse triangle-free families to test worst-case linear constants.
