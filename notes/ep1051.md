# EP-1051

## Problem Statement
Investigate irrationality in the weaker-growth regime where
\[
\liminf a_n^{1/2^n}>1
\]
(the note context compares this to known stronger rapid-growth results).

## Literature
- Background records irrationality under stronger growth assumptions.
- The liminf-based weaker condition remains the challenging case in this note.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone finite proxy using model sequences of doubly-exponential scale
\[
a_n\approx c^{2^n},\quad c>1,
\]
and sums of reciprocal terms.
- For each model, computed high-precision numerical sums with tiny tail proxies and searched for close rationals by:
  - continued fractions (large denominator cap),
  - brute nearest-rational scan up to a large denominator threshold.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Across tested growth bases, no small-denominator rational matched at the tail-resolved scale.
- This supports the irrationality heuristic but does not prove the theorem-level statement.

## Status
- Open in the weaker-condition regime described in this note.
- Added standalone deep rational-approximation diagnostics.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove irrationality under the weaker growth hypothesis
\[
\liminf a_n^{1/2^n}>1.
\]

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Show a quantitative rational-approximation barrier: under the liminf condition, truncation tails satisfy
\[
\left|x-\frac{p}{q}\right|>\frac{1}{q^{2+\eta}}
\]
for all large \(q\) and some \(\eta>0\), excluding eventual periodic/too-good rational approximants.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (tail control from growth)
If \(a_n\ge (1+\delta)^{2^n}\) eventually, then reciprocal tails decay doubly exponentially.

**Proof.**
\(1/a_n\le (1+\delta)^{-2^n}\); geometric summation of doubly exponential terms gives tiny tails. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (continued-fraction obstruction)
If a real number is rational, continued fraction expansion terminates, so approximation error by convergents drops to \(0\) exactly at finite stage.

**Proof.**
Standard continued fraction characterization of rationals. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies irrationality)
If A forbids all sufficiently strong rational approximants compatible with eventual termination, then \(x\) cannot be rational.

**Proof.**
Rationality would produce exact finite termination and hence perfect approximation beyond some stage, contradicting A. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is upgrading tail smallness from liminf growth into a uniform Diophantine-approximation exclusion theorem strong enough to force irrationality in full generality.
