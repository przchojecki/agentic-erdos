# EP-609

## Problem Statement
In \(n\)-colorings of \(K_{2^n+1}\), study the minimum monochromatic odd-cycle length and corresponding odd-girth forcing scale.

## Literature / Context
- Recent progress improves upper bounds, while lower bounds remain much smaller.
- The asymptotic gap is still large.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented a deeper local-search optimizer on edge colorings of \(K_{2^n+1}\) for \(n=3,4,5\).
- Objective: maximize the minimum monochromatic odd-cycle length, secondarily maximize number of bipartite color classes.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Best found profiles:
  - \(n=3\): min monochromatic odd cycle length \(=5\),
  - \(n=4\): improved to \(5\) in this run,
  - \(n=5\): currently \(3\) under moderate search budget.
- Finite behavior still indicates strong odd-cycle forcing as color count grows.

## Next Proof Targets
- Push exact/sat-assisted search for \(n=5\) to test whether value \(>3\) is achievable.
- Extract structural templates from best colorings for potential lower-bound constructions.
