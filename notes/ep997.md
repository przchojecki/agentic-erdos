# EP-997

## Problem Statement
Investigate distribution properties of
\[
\{\alpha p_n\}
\]
(where $p_n$ is the $n$-th prime), including well-distribution/equidistribution behavior for irrational $\alpha$.

## Literature
- Weyl criterion reduces the question to exponential sums
\[
\frac1N\sum_{n\le N} e(h\alpha p_n).
\]
- Rational $\alpha$ are immediate obstructions (finite residue support), so irrationality is necessary.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Formalized the discrepancy and exponential-sum equivalence in the note.
- Added a deeper standalone finite proxy computation:
  - first $120{,}000$ primes,
  - window lengths $500,1000,2000,5000,10000$,
  - $30$ sampled $\alpha$ values (named constants + random irrationals),
  - $70$ test intervals,
  - maximal sliding-window relative deviation extraction.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Across tested $\alpha$, max relative deviation decreases as window size grows (roughly from $\sim 0.07$-$0.09$ at $k=500$ down to $\sim 0.011$-$0.015$ at $k=10{,}000$ for named constants).
- Finite data is consistent with gradual averaging, but does not prove asymptotic well-distribution for every irrational $\alpha$.

## Status
- Global theorem remains open.
- Added deeper standalone prime-window discrepancy profile at larger $N$.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Establish equidistribution/well-distribution of \(\{\alpha p_n\}\) for irrational \(\alpha\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a uniform exponential-sum bound for every nonzero integer \(h\):
\[
\sum_{n\le N} e(h\alpha p_n)=o(N)
\]
for irrational \(\alpha\), with quantitative decay enough for discrepancy bounds.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (Weyl criterion)
Equidistribution of \(\{\alpha p_n\}\) is equivalent to B1-type cancellation for all \(h\neq0\).

**Proof.**
Direct Weyl criterion for sequences on \(\mathbb T\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (Vinogradov transfer to primes)
Exponential sums over primes can be controlled by weighted sums over integers via \(\Lambda\) and partial summation.

**Proof.**
Use identity \(\sum_{p\le x} a_p = \sum_{n\le x}\frac{\Lambda(n)}{\log n}a_n + \text{error}\), then Abel summation. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies discrepancy decay)
If A holds with power/log savings, Erdős-Turán gives \(D_N=o(N)\) (or quantitative rates).

**Proof.**
Plug the exponential-sum bound into Erdős-Turán and optimize truncation. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The difficult step is obtaining strong cancellation in \(\sum e(h\alpha p)\) uniformly in \(h\) for general irrational \(\alpha\), beyond existing average-type estimates.
