# EP-413 deep attempt with standalone computation

## Statement
For `\omega(n)` barriers (`m+\omega(m)\le n` for all `m<n`), are there
infinitely many? Also, does some weakened `\epsilon\omega(m)` version have
infinitely many barriers? Related questions for `\Omega` are natural too.

## Attempt route
Computed deep finite barrier-density profiles for both `\omega` and `\Omega`,
including weakened `\epsilon` variants.
