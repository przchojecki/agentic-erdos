## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove infinitude of barrier integers for conditions like
\[
m+\omega(m)\le n\quad\forall m<n
\]
and weakened \(\varepsilon\omega(m)\)-versions.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Show a controlled-extremal lemma: maxima of \(m+\alpha\omega(m)\) on \([1,n)\) are attained on a sparse structured subsequence with explicit growth gaps, enabling construction of infinitely many barrier \(n\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (barrier definition equivalence)
\(n\) is a barrier for coefficient \(\alpha\) iff
\[
\max_{m<n}\bigl(m+\alpha\omega(m)\bigr)\le n.
\]

**Proof.**
Direct rewrite of universal inequality over \(m<n\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (monotone envelope)
Define \(M_\alpha(x):=\max_{m\le x}(m+\alpha\omega(m)-m)=\alpha\max_{m\le x}\omega(m)\). Barrier growth is controlled by increments of this envelope.

**Proof.**
Separate linear \(m\) term and additive fluctuation \(\alpha\omega(m)\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies infinitude)
If A gives infinitely many \(x\) where envelope increment is smaller than next integer step, corresponding \(n\) values are barriers infinitely often.

**Proof.**
At such \(n\), maximal prior value stays below \(n\), satisfying B1. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is obtaining sharp extremal control of \(\omega(m)\) along initial segments with enough uniformity to force infinitely many barriers.
