# EP-566

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

### Source: ep566_partial.md

## Statement
If every `k`-vertex subgraph of `G` has at most `2k-3` edges, is it true that for
all `H` with `m` edges and no isolates,
\[
R(G,H)\ll m?
\]

## Literature Leads
- Erdős-Faudree-Rousseau-Schelp (1993): linear Ramsey-size results in sparser
  classes (notably around `n+1` edges).
- Problem page frames EP-566 as a general Ramsey-size linearity question and
  notes the `2n-2`-edge barrier examples.

## Strategy Notes
- Degeneracy reduction (`2k-3` implies 3-degenerate) is a natural starting point.
- Key missing step: lifting known linear-size classes to full 3-degenerate
  regime for arbitrary no-isolate `H`.
