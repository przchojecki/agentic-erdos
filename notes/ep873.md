# EP-873

## Problem Statement
For an increasing sequence $A=\{a_i\}$ and
$$F(A,X,k):=\#\{i:\operatorname{lcm}(a_i,\dots,a_{i+k-1})<X\},$$
is it true that for every $\epsilon>0$ there exists $k$ with
$$F(A,X,k)<X^\epsilon\ ?$$

## Literature
- For $k=3$, Erdos-Szemeredi established sharp-scale behavior (upper and matching lower-order examples up to logarithmic factors).
- The quantifier-changing statement “for every $\epsilon$, some $k$” remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Computed empirical profiles of $F(A,X,k)$ for multiple model sequences:
  - naturals, odds, primes, powers of 2,
  - $k=3,4,5,6$,
  - $X$ from $10^3$ to $10^6$.
- Recorded endpoint log-log slopes as finite scaling proxies.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For all tested sequences, increasing $k$ lowers the empirical scaling slope.
- Powers-of-2 model gives especially small slopes in this finite range.
- These finite profiles support the direction that larger $k$ suppresses counts, but do not prove the universal quantifier claim.
