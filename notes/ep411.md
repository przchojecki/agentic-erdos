## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Classify all pairs \((n,r)\) such that
\[
g_{k+r}(n)=2g_k(n)\quad\text{for all large }k,\qquad g(m)=m+\phi(m).
\]

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove eventual affine-linearization: along any orbit, \(g_{k+1}(n)=\lambda_k g_k(n)+\eta_k\) with \(\lambda_k\to \lambda\), and exact doubling after shift \(r\) forces \(\lambda^r=2\). Show only \(r=2\) can occur with integer-orbit constraints.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (iterate identity)
\[
g_{k+r}(n)=2g_k(n)\ \forall k\ge K
\]
implies \(g_{k+2r}(n)=4g_k(n)\) for all \(k\ge K\).

**Proof.**
Apply the relation twice: \(g_{k+2r}=2g_{k+r}=4g_k\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (fixed-point-free growth)
For \(m>1\), \(g(m)=m+\phi(m)>m\), so orbits are strictly increasing.

**Proof.**
\(\phi(m)\ge1\) for \(m\ge2\), equality only at \(m=2\), still giving increase. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies \(r=2\)-only classification framework)
If A shows only \(r=2\) survives compatibility, classification reduces to solving finite structural conditions within \(r=2\).

**Proof.**
Exclude \(r\neq2\); remaining case handled separately by explicit restrictions/results. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is turning orbit-growth heuristics into rigorous eventual linearization with enough arithmetic precision to exclude \(r\neq2\).
