# EP-872

## Problem Statement
In the divisibility-avoidance game on $\{2,\dots,n\}$ (no chosen pair with one dividing the other), one player tries to maximize game length and the other to minimize it. How long can the maximizing player guarantee?

## Literature
- Number-theoretic analogue of saturation-game questions.
- Known asymptotic bounds are far apart; linear-scale behavior remains central in the current discussion.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Computed exact minimax values for small $n$ (extended exact range).
- Added heuristic-play profiles for larger $n$ to probe finite scaling.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Exact minimax long-player lengths for $n=10,12,14,16,18,20,22$ are
  $$5,6,7,7,8,9,10,$$
  giving ratios near $0.44$-$0.50$.
- Heuristic larger-$n$ best ratios remain around $0.43$-$0.48$ for tested $n\le 70$.
- Finite data supports persistent linear-length behavior, but does not prove a universal $\varepsilon n$ theorem.
