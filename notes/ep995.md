## Hard point
The exact almost-sure growth scale in full generality remains open.

## Rigorous model theorem (doubling + step function)
In the model
\[
n_k=2^k,\qquad f(x)=\mathbf 1_{[0,1/2)}(x)-\mathbf 1_{[1/2,1)}(x),
\]
and \(\alpha\) uniformly random in \([0,1)\), define
\[
X_k(\alpha):=f(\{2^k\alpha\}).
\]
Then \((X_k)_{k\ge1}\) are i.i.d. Rademacher variables (\(\pm1\) with probability \(1/2\)).

Reason:
\(X_k\) depends only on the \(k\)-th binary digit of \(\alpha\), and binary digits are independent under Lebesgue measure.

Hence
\[
S_N(\alpha):=\sum_{k=1}^N X_k(\alpha)
\]
is a simple random walk.
Therefore, almost surely,
\[
S_N=O\!\left(\sqrt{N\log\log N}\right)
\]
(law of the iterated logarithm scale), and in particular
\[
\frac{S_N}{N\sqrt{\log\log N}}\to 0.
\]

So this benchmark model is provably consistent with the conjectured small growth regime and cannot produce a counterexample.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
For lacunary \(n_k\) and \(f\in L^2\), determine almost-sure growth scale of
\[
S_N(\alpha)=\sum_{k\le N} f(\{\alpha n_k\}).
\]

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Show a martingale-approximation theorem:
\[
S_N = M_N + R_N,\qquad M_N\ \text{martingale with }\langle M\rangle_N\asymp N,\ \ R_N=o(\sqrt{N\log\log N})\ \text{a.s.}
\]

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (model i.i.d. benchmark)
For \(n_k=2^k\) and dyadic step \(f\), \(S_N\) is simple random walk.

**Proof.**
Each term depends on one independent binary digit of \(\alpha\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (LIL consequence)
If \(S_N\) admits decomposition in A with \(R_N\) negligible at LIL scale, then
\[
S_N=O(\sqrt{N\log\log N})\ \text{a.s.}
\]

**Proof.**
Apply LIL to \(M_N\), absorb \(R_N\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies no linear-growth counterexample)
Under A,
\[
\frac{S_N}{N\sqrt{\log\log N}}\to0
\]
along full sequence a.s.

**Proof.**
From B2, numerator is \(O(\sqrt{N\log\log N})\), divide by \(N\sqrt{\log\log N}\). \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The hard part is constructing martingale approximations for general \(L^2\) observables and non-dyadic lacunary sequences with quantitative remainders.
