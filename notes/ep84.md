## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Establish asymptotic lower growth for the number \(f(n)\) of distinct cycle
length sets realizable by \(n\)-vertex graphs, including the stronger
\(f(n)/2^{n/2}\to\infty\) direction.

### Step A: Canonical decomposition
Decompose every graph into a bounded-type core plus ear/cactus attachments that
contribute independently to cycle-length sets.

### Step B: Entropy lower bound
Show the attachment choices generate at least \(\exp(\gamma n)\) distinct cycle
set signatures for some \(\gamma>\tfrac12\log 2\).

### Step C: Collision control
Bound multiplicity of graphs producing the same cycle-length set by
\(\exp(o(n))\), yielding a net super-\(2^{n/2}\) lower bound.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a sharp anti-collision theorem: non-isomorphic attachment profiles should
rarely induce the same cycle-length set, uniformly in \(n\).

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition O1 (Counting with bounded collisions)
Let \(\mathcal F_n\) be a family of \(n\)-vertex graphs and
\(\Phi(G)\subseteq\{3,\dots,n\}\) its cycle-length set. If
\[
|\mathcal F_n|\ge \exp(\alpha n),\qquad
\max_{S} |\{G\in\mathcal F_n:\Phi(G)=S\}|\le \exp(o(n)),
\]
then
\[
f(n)\ge \exp(\alpha n-o(n)).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
By double counting:
\[
|\mathcal F_n|
=\sum_{S} |\{G\in\mathcal F_n:\Phi(G)=S\}|
\le f(n)\exp(o(n)).
\]
Rearrange.
\(\square\)

### Proposition O2 (Reduction of the strong EP-84 lower bound)
If Step B gives \(|\mathcal F_n|\ge \exp(\alpha n)\) with
\(\alpha>\frac12\log 2\), and Step C gives the multiplicity bound in O1, then
\[
\frac{f(n)}{2^{n/2}}\to\infty.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
From O1:
\[
\log f(n)\ge \alpha n-o(n)
\]
and
\[
\log 2^{n/2}=\tfrac12(\log 2)n.
\]
Subtract exponents; positivity of
\(\alpha-\tfrac12\log 2\) gives divergence.
\(\square\)
