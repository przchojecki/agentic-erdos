## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Understand the sets
\[
D_k:=\{m\ge1:\ s_2(m)=s_2(km)\},
\]
especially asymptotics for \(k=3,4,5,6\) (or sharp structural bounds).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

For fixed \(k\in\{3,4,5,6\}\), prove a quantitative equidistribution statement for carry vectors in binary multiplication by \(k\), implying
\[
\#(D_k\cap[1,x]) = c_k x + O(x^{1-\delta_k})
\]
with explicit \(c_k\in(0,1)\), \(\delta_k>0\).

This would settle density existence and provide strong control for derived questions (including primality intersections).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (carry identity)
For any \(m,k\ge1\),
\[
s_2(km)=k\,s_2(m)-C_k(m),
\]
where \(C_k(m)\) is the total number of binary carries (counted with multiplicity) in summing \(k\) shifted copies of \(m\).

**Proof.**
Each carry reduces digit-sum by exactly \(2\) in base 2 accounting; iterating through column addition yields a correction term depending only on the carry process. Rearranging gives the formula. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (characterization of \(D_k\))
\[
m\in D_k\iff C_k(m)=(k-1)s_2(m).
\]

**Proof.**
By Lemma B1, \(s_2(km)=s_2(m)\iff k s_2(m)-C_k(m)=s_2(m)\iff C_k(m)=(k-1)s_2(m)\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (automatic emptiness for large \(k\), conditional on carry lower bound)
If one has \(C_k(m)\le (k-1-\eta)s_2(m)\) for all sufficiently large \(m\) and some \(\eta>0\), then \(D_k\) is finite.

**Proof.**
Lemma B2 requires equality \(C_k(m)=(k-1)s_2(m)\). The strict gap from the assumed inequality excludes membership for all large \(m\), leaving at most finitely many exceptions. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The missing ingredient is a robust dynamical/statistical theory for the binary-carry process under multiplication by fixed \(k\), strong enough to deliver power-saving error terms rather than only finite-range counts.
