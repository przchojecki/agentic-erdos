# EP-633

## Problem Statement
Classify triangles that can only be cut into a square number of congruent triangles.

## Literature
- Soifer showed at least one triangle class with only square-number congruent dissections (e.g. with strong integral-independence conditions).
- Globally, many non-square counts are known to be realizable for some triangles.
- The classification problem remains open and shape-sensitive.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built a deeper arithmetic coverage map from known positive construction families.
- Focused on abundance of non-square realizable counts to separate "global realizability" from "triangle-specific square-only behavior".

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Up to \(X=8000\), known families realize many non-squares (about \(2304\) non-squares among \(1\le n\le 8000\)).
- Represented non-square density remains large in this range (around \(0.29\) at \(X=8000\)).
- This supports the interpretation that square-only behavior is exceptional and depends on strong geometric constraints of specific triangles.

## Status
- Open.
- Current computation maps global arithmetic reachability, but does not classify which specific triangles are square-only.
