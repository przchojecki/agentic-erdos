# EP-10 partial attempt

## Route
Computed finite coverage of integers representable as
`prime + (at most k powers of 2)` for `k=1,2,3,4`.

## Evidence from this batch
- `data/ep10_prime_plus_pow2_scan.json` up to `250000`:
  - `k=2` covers about `97.6%`,
  - `k=3` and `k=4` cover all tested `n>=2` (only `n=1` missing, trivially).

## Hard point
Finite verification cannot prove existence of a universal `k` for all integers.

## Status
Very strong finite evidence for small `k` (not a proof).

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove existence of a universal \(k\) such that every sufficiently large integer
is representable as
\[
n=p+\sum_{i=1}^{k}2^{e_i}
\]
(allowing fewer terms by repetition/zero-count convention as appropriate).

### Step A: Almost-All Representation at Fixed \(k\)
For some fixed \(k\), prove all but \(o(X)\) integers \(n\le X\) are representable.

### Step B: Exceptional Set Compression
Show the exceptional set has strong additive structure enabling one extra power
of 2 shift to absorb all but finitely many remaining exceptions.

### Step C: Finite Tail Elimination
Use explicit congruence-class coverage to remove the remaining tail exceptions.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a transference lemma from density-\(1\) representability to eventual full
representability under bounded extra powers-of-two shifts.
