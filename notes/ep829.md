# EP-829

## Problem Statement
Let $A\subset \mathbb{N}$ be the set of cubes. Is it true that
$$1_A * 1_A(n) \ll (\log n)^{O(1)}?$$

## Literature
- Mordell proved $\limsup_n 1_A*1_A(n)=\infty$.
- Mahler proved infinitely often lower bounds of polylog type.
- Stewart improved the known lower exponent to roughly $(\log n)^{11/13}$ infinitely often.
- The conjectural upper polylog bound remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Ran deeper exact enumeration up to $n\le 10^9$ for sums of two cubes.
- Computed full representation count map $r(n)$ and multiplicity tails.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Maximum multiplicity found in range is $r(n)=3$ (at several values, e.g. $n=119824488$).
- Counts with large multiplicity are very sparse in this range:
  - $\#\{n\le 10^9: r(n)\ge 3\}=8$,
  - no examples with $r(n)\ge 4$ found.
- Finite evidence is compatible with slow growth but does not prove a polylog upper bound.
