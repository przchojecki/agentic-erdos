# EP-584

## Problem Statement
Study short-cycle forcing in dense graphs under edge-density hypotheses, especially the transition from fixed \(\delta\) to sparse \(\delta=n^{-c}\)-type regimes.

## Literature / Context
- Fixed-density settings are substantially better understood than sparse-density analogues.
- The main obstruction is transferring dense short-cycle forcing phenomena to much thinner graphs.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented a deeper random-dense sampling proxy:
  - fraction of random edge pairs lying on cycles of length \(\le 6\) and \(\le 8\) (approximate test),
  - fraction of adjacent edge pairs lying on a \(C_4\).
- This gives quantitative finite evidence for strong short-cycle connectivity in constant-density windows.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- At \(n=88\), \(\delta\in\{0.08,0.12,0.16\}\):
  - sampled pair-on-cycle(\(\le 6\)) fraction is very high (about \(0.97\) up to \(1\)),
  - sampled pair-on-cycle(\(\le 8\)) is essentially \(1\),
  - adjacent-pair-on-\(C_4\) fraction ranges from about \(0.89\) to near \(1\).
- This reinforces that constant-density behavior is very favorable.

## Next Proof Targets
- Turn sampled high-probability signals into deterministic counting inequalities.
- Build a bridge lemma quantifying degradation when density is replaced by \(\delta=n^{-c}\).
