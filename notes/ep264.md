## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Sharpen the obstruction criterion around
\[
\liminf_{n\to\infty} a_n^2\sum_{k>n}\frac1{a_k^2},
\]
and characterize exactly when a sequence can be an irrationality sequence.

### Step A: Tail-energy normalization
Interpret
\[
E_n:=a_n^2\sum_{k>n}a_k^{-2}
\]
as a local "carry-capacity" parameter in binary-tail approximation arguments.

### Step B: Necessity/sufficiency split
Show \(E_n\not\to 0\) blocks irrationality-sequence behavior (known direction);
study converse under mild regularity assumptions.

### Step C: Borderline examples
Construct explicit sequences with \(E_n\to0\) and with \(\liminf E_n>0\) to map
sharpness.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a converse theorem: \(E_n\to0\) plus weak regularity implies existence of
infinitely many protected digit windows sufficient for irrationality-sequence
construction.
