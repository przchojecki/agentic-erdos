# EP-1054

## Problem Statement
For each $n$, consider minimal $m$ such that $n$ is a prefix sum of the sorted divisors of $m$; study growth of this minimal witness function.

## Literature

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine growth of minimal witness \(m(n)\) such that \(n\) is a prefix sum of sorted divisors of \(m\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a constructive covering lemma: every large \(n\) admits \(m\le C n(\log n)^A\) whose sorted-divisor prefix sums hit \(n\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (prefix-sum encoding)
Let divisors of \(m\) be \(d_1<\cdots<d_{\tau(m)}\). Then target condition is
\[
n=\sum_{i=1}^{t} d_i
\]
for some \(t\).

**Proof.**
Direct unpacking of definition. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (monotone witness bound transfer)
If \(m(n)\le g(n)\) for all sufficiently large \(n\), then any asymptotic upper bound for \(g\) is an upper bound for growth of \(m(n)\).

**Proof.**
Immediate by pointwise domination. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies polylog-overlinear growth upper bound)
From A, \(m(n)\ll n(\log n)^A\) eventually.

**Proof.**
A gives explicit witness in that range for each large \(n\). \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The hard step is controlling the additive structure of initial divisor segment sums uniformly across all \(n\), especially near sparse divisor patterns.
