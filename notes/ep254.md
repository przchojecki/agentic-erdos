### Proposition DB1 (Tail completeness criterion)
Let \(R\subset\mathbb N\) be representable integers. If there exists \(X_0\) such
that every interval \([x,x+L(x)]\), \(x\ge X_0\), intersects \(R\), and
\(L(x)=o(x)\) with additive closure property from available summands, then all
sufficiently large integers lie in \(R\).

\[
\textit{Proof sketch.}
\]
Iterate interval-hitting with closure under adding admissible summands to fill
holes beyond a threshold.
\(\square\)
