# EP-576

## Problem Statement
Determine the correct asymptotic order of \(\operatorname{ex}(n;Q_k)\), focusing in particular on cube-type cases such as \(Q_3\).

## Literature / Context
- Existing upper bounds are subquadratic and have improved over older baseline exponents.
- The sharp exponent/constant regime for hypercube-free graphs remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built a standalone random-graph threshold proxy for \(Q_3\) witness emergence.
- This is not a proof of extremal behavior, but a calibration signal for where cube-like subgraphs become typical.
- No theorem-level improvement yet.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Deeper Monte Carlo run over \(6{,}300\) random graphs:
  - heuristic \(p\)-thresholds (50% detection proxy): about \(0.17\) at \(n=32\), \(0.13\) at \(n=44\), \(0.12\) at \(n=56\).
  - corresponding threshold-edge normalization \(e/n^{3/2}\) sits near \(0.42\) to \(0.47\) in this finite window.
- Signal is consistent with sparse-to-intermediate onset of cube witnesses.

## Next Proof Targets
- Replace proxy witness detection by exact \(Q_3\)-subgraph detection for smaller \(n\) with exhaustive search.
- Compare random-threshold behavior against deterministic constructions to isolate plausible extremal scaling.
