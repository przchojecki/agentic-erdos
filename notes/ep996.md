# EP-996 deeper attempt with exact-arithmetic simulation

## Statement
Under Fourier-tail decay assumptions on `f`, does
`(1/N) sum_{k<=N} f({alpha n_k}) -> integral f` a.e. for lacunary `n_k`?

## Experiment in this attempt
Model test with exact arithmetic:
- `n_k=2^k`
- `f(x)=1_{x<1/2}-1_{x>=1/2}` (zero mean)
- `alpha=a/q`, odd prime `q`

Data file:
- `data/ep996_ergodic_average_simulation.json`

Observed decay of sample averages:
- mean `|(1/N)sum_{k<=N} f({alpha n_k})|` drops from about `0.035` (`N=512`)
  to about `0.0028` (`N=65536`).

## Interpretation
Finite experiments are consistent with convergence in this model, but do not prove the
full theorem under the stated general assumptions.

## Rigorous model proof (doubling map)
For the same model
\[
n_k=2^k,\qquad f(x)=\mathbf 1_{[0,1/2)}(x)-\mathbf 1_{[1/2,1)}(x),
\]
let
\[
X_k(\alpha):=f(\{2^k\alpha\}),\qquad A_N(\alpha):=\frac1N\sum_{k=1}^N X_k(\alpha).
\]

As in EP-995, \(X_k\) are i.i.d. mean-zero Rademacher variables under Lebesgue-a.e. \(\alpha\).
By the strong law of large numbers,
\[
A_N(\alpha)\to 0\quad\text{for a.e. }\alpha.
\]

So in this canonical lacunary example, the EP-996 conclusion holds exactly (with explicit probabilistic proof), not just numerically.

This confirms the simulation and isolates the open part as extension from this model case to broad Fourier-tail assumptions and general lacunary sequences.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Under Fourier-tail assumptions on \(f\), prove
\[
\frac1N\sum_{k\le N} f(\{\alpha n_k\})\to \int_0^1 f
\]
for a.e. \(\alpha\) and lacunary \(n_k\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Establish a maximal inequality:
\[
\left\|\sup_{N\ge1}\left|\sum_{k\le N}(f(\alpha n_k)-\hat f(0))\right|\right\|_{L^2_\alpha}
\ll \|f\|_{L^2},
\]
under the stated Fourier-tail+lacunarity hypotheses.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (Fourier expansion reduction)
Write
\[
f(x)-\hat f(0)=\sum_{m\neq0}\hat f(m)e(mx),
\]
then ergodic averages reduce to controlling sums
\[
\frac1N\sum_{k\le N}e(m\alpha n_k).
\]

**Proof.**
Substitute Fourier series termwise; tail assumptions justify \(L^2\) manipulations. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (maximal inequality implies a.e. convergence)
If A holds, then partial sums are \(L^2\)-a.e. Cauchy along \(N\), giving pointwise convergence of averages.

**Proof.**
Doob-type maximal control plus density argument on Fourier truncations implies almost-everywhere convergence. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (limit identification)
The limit equals \(\hat f(0)=\int_0^1 f\).

**Proof.**
Mean-zero part converges to \(0\) by B2; constant Fourier mode remains. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is proving a maximal inequality strong enough for general Fourier-tail classes beyond model dyadic cases.
