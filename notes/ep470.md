## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Resolve odd weird existence and unconditional infinitude of primitive weird numbers.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a parity-structure obstruction: any odd abundant number with deficient proper-divisor subset sums must satisfy a rigid prime-power pattern impossible beyond finite range.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (weird definition split)
\(n\) is weird iff
1) \(n\) is abundant: \(\sigma(n)>2n\),
2) \(n\) is not semiperfect: no subset of proper divisors sums to \(n\).

**Proof.**
Definition of weird numbers. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (primitive weird criterion)
Primitive weird means weird and no proper weird divisor.

**Proof.**
Definition. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies odd-weird finiteness/nonexistence)
If A excludes all sufficiently large odd candidates, only finitely many odd weird numbers can exist; exhaustive check in finite range would decide existence.

**Proof.**
Bounded search space after A. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The key obstacle is non-semiperfectness: subset-sum behavior of proper divisors is hard to control in odd abundant families.
