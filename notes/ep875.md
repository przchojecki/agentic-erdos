# EP-875

## Problem Statement
Let $A=\{a_n\}$ be infinite and define $S_r$ as the set of $r$-term sums from $A$. If the families $S_r$ are pairwise disjoint across different $r$, determine how slowly $A$ can grow (including gap behavior like $a_{n+1}-a_n$).

## Literature
- Deshouillers-Erdos problem (infinite variant of finite partition-sum questions).
- Known constructions are sparse; optimal growth regime remains unclear.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built finite-horizon greedy constructions enforcing disjointness of sum-level sets up to level $R$.
- Tested increasing $R$ cases and tracked achieved prefix length and growth metrics.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Greedy constructions quickly become near-doubling in tested regimes.
- For $R=5,6,7$, built prefixes show average adjacent ratio close to 2.
- Finite-horizon evidence suggests strong sparsity pressure from multi-level disjointness constraints.
