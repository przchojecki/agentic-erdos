# EP-693

## Problem Statement
Fix \(k\ge2\), \(n\) large. Let \(A=\{a_1<a_2<\cdots\}\subset [n,n^k]\) be integers having a divisor in \((n,2n)\).
Estimate
\[
\max_i (a_{i+1}-a_i),
\]
and in particular whether it is polylogarithmic in \(n\).

## Literature
- Background entry contains the statement but no further theorem-level context.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented direct finite scans for \(k=2,3\) across moderate \(n\), explicitly constructing \(A\) and measuring maximum consecutive gap.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For tested ranges, the maximal gaps are modest (small constants / low tens), and normalized by \(\log n\) remain controlled in finite data.
- This is compatible with polylogarithmic-scale optimism on these sizes, but far from asymptotic proof.
