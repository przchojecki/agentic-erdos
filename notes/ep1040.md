# EP-1040 deeper attempt

## Statement
For closed infinite `F subset C`, is `mu(F)` determined by transfinite diameter? In particular,
if diameter is `>=1`, must `mu(F)=0`?

## Computation in this attempt (model case)
I tested `F` on the unit circle via `f_m(z)=z^m-1` (roots in `F`) and estimated area
`|{z:|f_m(z)|<1}|` by Monte Carlo.

Data file:
- `data/ep1040_unitcircle_area_scan.json`

Sample estimates:
- `m=40`: area `~1.324`
- `m=100`: area `~0.818`
- `m=300`: area `~0.344`

## Interpretation
For this canonical `F` (capacity/transfinite diameter `1`), the tested areas decrease strongly
with `m`, consistent with `mu(F)=0` in this special case. This does not settle the general
question for arbitrary closed infinite `F`.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine whether \(\mu(F)\) is governed by transfinite diameter/capacity, especially whether \(\mathrm{diam}(F)\ge1\) implies \(\mu(F)=0\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a capacity-determined upper bound:
\[
\mu(F)\le \Phi(\mathrm{cap}(F)),
\]
with \(\Phi(t)=0\) for \(t\ge1\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (capacity-transfinite diameter identity)
For compact \(F\subset\mathbb C\), transfinite diameter equals logarithmic capacity.

**Proof.**
Classical Fekete-Szegő theorem. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (model case calibration)
For \(F=\partial\mathbb D\), \(\mathrm{cap}(F)=1\), and \(f_m(z)=z^m-1\) gives shrinking sublevel-area trend toward \(0\).

**Proof.**
Direct computation/known asymptotics of \(|z^m-1|<1\) area; numerical profile supports vanishing. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies EP claim)
If A holds with \(\Phi(t)=0\) for \(t\ge1\), then any \(F\) with transfinite diameter \(\ge1\) satisfies \(\mu(F)=0\).

**Proof.**
Apply B1 to replace transfinite diameter by capacity in A. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The hard step is proving a universal extremal-area inequality that depends only on capacity and not finer geometry of \(F\).
