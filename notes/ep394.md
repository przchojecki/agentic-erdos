# EP-394 partial resolution

## Statement split
Let `t_k(n)` be the least `m` such that
`n\mid m(m+1)\cdots(m+k-1)`.
1. Is `\sum_{n\le x} t_2(n) \ll x^2/(\log x)^c` for some `c>0`?
2. For `k>=2`, is
   `\sum_{n\le x} t_{k+1}(n)=o\!\left(\sum_{n\le x} t_k(n)\right)`?

## What is resolved
Background says an earlier Erdos conjecture `sum_{n<=x} t_2(n)=o(x^2)` was proved
by Erdos--Hall, with the explicit bound

`sum_{n<=x} t_2(n) << x^2 * (log log log x)/(log log x)`.

Hence the coarse `o(x^2)` behavior is settled.

## What remains open in this note
The stronger log-power improvement in subquestion (1) is not obtained by this
bound, and subquestion (2) is also not resolved by the provided background.

## Status
- coarse `o(x^2)` for `sum t_2(n)`: resolved.
- stronger log-power / higher-k comparison claims: unresolved.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Strengthen from known
\[
\sum_{n\le x} t_2(n)=o(x^2)
\]
to log-power savings, and prove hierarchy
\[
\sum_{n\le x} t_{k+1}(n)=o\!\left(\sum_{n\le x} t_k(n)\right).
\]

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Establish uniform distribution control of solution residues for
\[
n\mid m(m+1)\cdots(m+k-1),
\]
showing average minimal \(m\) contracts by a fixed factor when \(k\mapsto k+1\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (definition monotonicity in \(k\))
\[
t_{k+1}(n)\le t_k(n).
\]

**Proof.**
Any \(m\) satisfying divisibility by a \(k\)-block also satisfies it for \((k+1)\)-block by multiplying one extra factor. Minimum cannot increase. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (sum comparison consequence)
From B1,
\[
\sum_{n\le x} t_{k+1}(n)\le \sum_{n\le x} t_k(n).
\]

**Proof.**
Summing termwise inequality over \(n\le x\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies strict asymptotic separation)
If A yields average contraction \(t_{k+1}(n)\le (1-\delta_k)t_k(n)\) on density-1 set, then
\[
\sum_{n\le x} t_{k+1}(n)=o\!\left(\sum_{n\le x} t_k(n)\right).
\]

**Proof.**
Split by good density-1 set and negligible exceptional set; good set gives fixed-factor gain repeatedly, forcing ratio to \(0\). \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is proving quantitative average contraction beyond monotonicity, uniformly in large \(n\).
