# EP-945

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

### Working statement
Let \(F(x)\) be the largest \(m\) such that there exists a block
\(n+1,\dots,n+m\le x\) with all \(\tau(n+j)\) pairwise distinct.

### Deep standalone computation (`ep945.mjs`)
- Method: linear sieve for exact divisor-count function \(\tau(n)\), then longest-distinct-subarray scan on \((\tau(1),\dots,\tau(x))\).
- Run: `X = 60,000,000`.
- Key output:
  - `max_tau_up_to_X = 672`
  - best distinct run length found: `13`
  - witness interval: `[16475964, 16475976]` with
    \(\tau\)-values
    \((48,24,16,20,14,4,64,2,6,32,8,12,96)\), all distinct.

### Growth profile samples
- \(F(10^5)=10\), \(F(10^6)=11\), \(F(2\cdot 10^7)=13\), \(F(3\cdot 10^7)=13\).
- The ratio \(F(x)/\log x\) remains bounded and sublinear in this range.

### Interpretation
Finite data remains consistent with slow growth (polylog-scale hypotheses remain plausible), while giving a stronger concrete witness (`13`) than earlier smaller scans.
