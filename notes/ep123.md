### Proposition AK1 (Local-global reduction template)
Suppose for fixed \((a,b,c)\):
\[
\text{(i) all sufficiently large }N\text{ are locally representable mod }M,
\]
\[
\text{(ii) every interval }[N/2,N]\text{ contains a globally representable
integer in each admissible residue class.}
\]
Then the target representation property holds for all large \(N\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Choose admissible class of \(N\) modulo \(M\), apply (ii) in \([N/2,N]\), then
lift by (i) and closure under adding admissible generators.
\(\square\)

### Proposition AK2 (Threshold consequence)
If one proves AK1 with effective constants, then EP-123 is resolved for that
triple family with explicit threshold \(N_0\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Take \(N_0\) as max of constants in (i)-(ii).
\(\square\)
