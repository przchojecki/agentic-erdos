# EP-889

## Problem Statement
For
$$v(n,k)=\#\{p\mid n+k:\ p\nmid n+i\ (0\le i<k)\},$$
equivalently number of prime factors of $n+k$ exceeding $k$, define
$$v_0(n)=\max_{k\ge0} v(n,k).$$
Is $v_0(n)\to\infty$ as $n\to\infty$?

## Literature
- Erdos-Selfridge conjectured stronger variants $v_\ell(n)\to\infty$ for fixed $\ell$.
- Even basic lower bounds are hard in the classical discussion.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Ran deeper bounded-$k$ finite scan with
  $$n\le 5000,\quad 0\le k\le 15000.$$
- Computed exact bounded proxy
  $$v_0^{(K)}(n)=\max_{0\le k\le K} v(n,k).$$

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Maximum observed bounded value in this range is 5.
- Prefix minima remain 1 across all checked prefixes up to 5000.
- Average bounded values increase slowly with prefix size.
- Finite bounded scans do not resolve the unbounded-$k$ asymptotic conjecture.
