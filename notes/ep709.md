# EP-709

## Problem Statement
Define \(f(n)\) as the least constant factor (in the interval-length formulation) forcing, for every \(n\)-set \(A\) of positive integers and every interval of the prescribed length, distinct representatives divisible by each element of \(A\).

## Literature
- Known broad bounds place \(f(n)\) between polylogarithmic and subpolynomial/power-type upper scales (depending on formulation details).
- Exact asymptotics are open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Recast the finite version as bipartite matching:
  - left side: elements of \(A\),
  - right side: numbers in interval,
  - edge if divisibility holds.
- Exhaustively tested all small \(n\)-sets up to max element 22, searching minimal factor in this finite regime.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Deep finite exhaustive scan gave estimated factor 2 for \(n=2,3,4\) in the tested regime.
- In particular, \(A=\{2,3\}\) gives explicit failure of factor 1 on interval \([5,7]\): both divisibility requirements force 6, so distinct choices are impossible.
- This corrects the earlier local claim of exact \(f(2)=1\) in our notes.
