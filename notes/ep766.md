# EP-766

## Problem Statement
For fixed \((k,\ell)\), define
\[
f(n;k,\ell)=\min\{\mathrm{ex}(n;G): |V(G)|=k,\ |E(G)|=\ell\}.
\]
Estimate \(f(n;k,\ell)\) for \(k<\ell\le k^2/4\), and ask strict monotonicity in \(\ell\) (for fixed \(k\), large \(n\)).

## Literature
- Boundary-case estimates are known in background notes (Dirac-Erd\H{o}s type endpoint).
- Full interior behavior and strict monotonicity are open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built standalone sampled finite proxy:
  - sample many \(k\)-vertex, \(\ell\)-edge graphs,
  - approximate \(\mathrm{ex}(n;G)\) by greedy edge insertion avoiding copies of \(G\),
  - take sampled minimum over the sampled \(G\)'s.
- Ran for \(k=6\), \(\ell\in\{7,8,9\}\), \(n\in\{9,10,11\}\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Sampled minima were in a relatively narrow range (about 12 to 19 edges in tested sizes).
- Within this small finite scan, monotonic separation between \(\ell=8\) and \(\ell=9\) was not clear (some rows coincide), suggesting monotonicity is subtle at small scales.
- These are sampled proxies only, not exact values of \(f(n;k,\ell)\).

## Status
- Open.
- Added standalone sampled finite profile for interior \((k,\ell)\) behavior.
