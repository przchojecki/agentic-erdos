# EP-653

## Problem Statement
For \(x_1,\dots,x_n\in\mathbb R^2\), define
\[
R(x_i)=\#\{\lvert x_j-x_i\rvert:j\ne i\},
\]
ordered increasingly, and let \(g(n)\) be the maximum number of distinct values among the \(R(x_i)\).
Is \(g(n)\ge (1-o(1))n\)?

## Literature
- Known bounds: \(g(n)>\tfrac{7}{10}n\) (improving earlier \(\tfrac38 n\)), and \(g(n)<n-cn^{2/3}\) for some \(c>0\).
- So the regime is linear, but near-full \((1-o(1))n\) remains unresolved.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Reworked standalone computation to avoid uninformative random baselines.
- Compared explicit constructions plus targeted 1D local search:
  - arithmetic progression on a line,
  - two parallel arithmetic progressions,
  - 1D coordinate local-search optimization.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Arithmetic progression gives a robust baseline:
  - \(g(n)=n/2\) for \(n=60,80,100,120\).
- Two-line progression is lower in tested runs (about \(0.38n\) to \(0.57n\)).
- Current 1D local search did not beat the arithmetic progression baseline.

## Status
- Open.
- This run contributes concrete construction benchmarks; reaching \(\sim 0.7n\) or above likely needs richer 2D geometric constructions beyond these 1D-based families.
