## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Decide existence of degree-\(\ge2\) polynomial \(f:\mathbb Z\to\mathbb Z\) and \(A\subset\mathbb Z\) giving unique decompositions \(n=a+b\) with \(a\in A,\ b\in f(\mathbb Z)\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a modular uniqueness obstruction: for infinitely many moduli \(m\), image set \(f(\mathbb Z_m)\) has nontrivial Fourier spectrum incompatible with exact tiling by any subset \(A_m\subset\mathbb Z_m\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (mod-\(m\) projection of unique representation)
If unique representation holds in \(\mathbb Z\), then for each modulus \(m\), residues satisfy exact tiling
\[
\mathbb Z_m = A_m \oplus f(\mathbb Z_m).
\]

**Proof.**
Reduce unique integer representation modulo \(m\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (Fourier tiling criterion)
Exact tiling implies
\[
\widehat{1_{A_m}}(\xi)\,\widehat{1_{f(\mathbb Z_m)}}(\xi)=0
\]
for all nonzero frequencies \(\xi\).

**Proof.**
Convolution \(1_{A_m}*1_{f(\mathbb Z_m)}\equiv1\) and discrete Fourier transform. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies global impossibility)
If A gives infinitely many \(m\) where B2 cannot hold for polynomial image set, no global unique-representation pair \((A,f)\) exists.

**Proof.**
Contradiction with B1 for those moduli. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is establishing robust nonvanishing Fourier behavior of polynomial image sets for infinitely many moduli with quantitative strength.
