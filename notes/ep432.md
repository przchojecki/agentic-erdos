## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine maximal possible density of \(A+B\) when elements of \(A+B\) are pairwise coprime.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a prime-support disjointness lemma: pairwise coprimality of all sums forces almost-disjoint prime-support assignment across \(A+B\), implying global density ceiling \(<1\) (and candidate sharp constant).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (pairwise-coprime consequence)
If \(x,y\in A+B\), \(x\neq y\), then \(\gcd(x,y)=1\).

**Proof.**
This is the defining condition. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (prime assignment constraint)
Each prime \(p\) can divide at most one element of \(A+B\).

**Proof.**
If \(p\) divided two distinct sums, those two sums would share gcd \(\ge p>1\), violating B1. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies density upper bound)
If A quantifies how prime assignment limits count in \([1,x]\), then one gets explicit upper bound for \(|(A+B)\cap[1,x]|/x\).

**Proof.**
Count available prime-support resources up to \(x\), map each sum to unique support witness as in A. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is converting local prime-support exclusivity into a sharp asymptotic density constant for realizable sumsets.
