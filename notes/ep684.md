# EP-684

## Problem Statement
For \(\binom nk=uv\), where prime divisors of \(u\) lie in \([2,k]\) and of \(v\) in \((k,n]\), let \(f(n)\) be the smallest \(k\) with \(u>n^2\).
Find bounds for \(f(n)\).

## Literature
- Mahler's theorem implies \(f(n)\to\infty\), but ineffectively.
- Quantitative growth of \(f(n)\) is open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented prime-exponent scan of \(\binom nk\) to compute log small-prime part \(\log u\).
- For each tested \(n\), found first \(k\) with \(\log u > 2\log n\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In tested range up to \(n=2400\), finite \(f(n)\) values are found and generally increase slowly (with fluctuations).
- This supports practical growth of \(f(n)\), consistent with qualitative divergence.
- Finite numerics are far from proving asymptotic bounds.

## Status
- \(f(n)\to\infty\): known.
- Effective quantitative bounds: open.
