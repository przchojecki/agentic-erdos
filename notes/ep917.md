# EP-917

## Problem Statement
Let $k\ge 4$ and let $f_k(n)$ be the maximum number of edges in an $n$-vertex $k$-critical graph.
Main questions include whether $f_k(n)\gg_k n^2$ and, for $k=6$, whether $f_6(n)\sim n^2/4$.

## Literature
- Classical constructions (Dirac and later refinements) give dense $k$-critical lower bounds.
- For $k=6$, the join-of-two-odd-cycles family yields edge counts matching Dirac's formula on infinitely many $n$.
- Full asymptotic characterization remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built standalone exact colorability checker and applied it to Dirac-family graphs
$$
G_t=C_{2t+1}+C_{2t+1}
$$
for $t=2,\dots,12$.
- For each $t$, verified:
  - $G_t$ is not 5-colorable but is 6-colorable,
  - after deleting any single edge, the graph becomes 5-colorable (full edge-by-edge check).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- All tested instances are fully 6-critical in exact checks.
- Edge counts exactly match
$$
|E(G_t)|=4t^2+8t+3,
$$
with density decreasing toward $1/4$ as $t$ grows.
- At $t=12$ ($n=50$), we get $|E|=675$ and edge density $0.27$, consistent with approach to the $n^2/4$ scale from above.
