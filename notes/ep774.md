# EP-774

## Problem Statement
A set \(A\subset\mathbb N\) is dissociated if different finite subcollections have different sums.
Call \(A\) proportionately dissociated if every finite \(B\subset A\) contains a dissociated subset of size \(\gg |B|\).
Must every proportionately dissociated set be a union of finitely many dissociated sets?

## Literature
- The dissociated-partition question remains open in this form.
- Related Sidon-partition negative results do not directly settle this exact formulation.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Performed exact finite scans on random sets:
  - maximal dissociated dimension ratio,
  - minimal number of dissociated classes needed to partition the set.
- Used exact subset-sum uniqueness checks throughout.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For sampled sizes \(m=12,14\), best dissociated-dimension ratios were around 0.64-0.75.
- In these samples, partition into 2 dissociated classes was always feasible.
- Finite results do not determine whether a uniform finite-class bound must follow from proportional dissociation in the infinite setting.
