## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Semiprime denominator valuation bound)
If
\[
\frac ab=\sum_{i=1}^k \frac1{n_i},
\]
with each \(n_i\) semiprime, then for any prime \(p\), denominator valuation
\(v_p(b)\) is bounded by the maximum \(v_p(n_i)\) after reduction.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Clearing denominators by \(L=\mathrm{lcm}(n_i)\) gives
\[
aL/b=\sum_i L/n_i\in\mathbb Z.
\]
Hence \(b\mid aL\). Since \(\gcd(a,b)=1\), \(b\mid L\), so each \(p\)-valuation in
\(b\) is at most \(v_p(L)=\max_i v_p(n_i)\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Monotonicity under denominator-class enlargement)
If semiprimes are replaced by a superset class \(\mathcal C\), representability
can only increase.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Every semiprime decomposition is also a \(\mathcal C\)-decomposition.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction to constructive closure)
To prove broad representability, it suffices to show any admissible rational can
be reduced by finite moves to a base family already known representable, where
moves preserve semiprime class.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Compose inverse moves from base decompositions to recover target rational.
\(\square\)
