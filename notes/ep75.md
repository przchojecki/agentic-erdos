## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Find \(G\) with \(\chi(G)=\aleph_1\) such that for every \(\varepsilon>0\),
every sufficiently large finite \(H\subseteq G\) has
\[
\alpha(H)\ge |V(H)|^{1-\varepsilon}.
\]

### Step A: Sparse uncountably chromatic framework
Construct \(G\) via transfinite forcing/induction so finite neighborhoods remain
highly sparse while global coloring obstruction survives.

### Step B: Quantitative extraction
Prove a hereditary estimate
\[
\alpha(H)\ge c_\varepsilon |V(H)|^{1-\varepsilon}
\]
for all sufficiently large finite \(H\subseteq G\).

### Step C: Upgrade to full statement
Diagonalize in \(\varepsilon=1/m\) to get the asymptotic “for every
\(\varepsilon>0\)” conclusion.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a lemma converting local sparsity/control of short cycles in all finite
traces into near-linear independence uniformly over all induced subgraphs.

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition I1 (Diagonal epsilon-upgrade)
Assume that for each integer \(m\ge1\), there are \(N_m,c_m>0\) such that every
finite \(H\subseteq G\) with \(|V(H)|\ge N_m\) satisfies
\[
\alpha(H)\ge c_m |V(H)|^{1-1/m}.
\]
Then for every \(\varepsilon>0\), all sufficiently large \(H\subseteq G\) obey
\[
\alpha(H)\ge |V(H)|^{1-\varepsilon-o(1)}.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Choose \(m\) with \(1/m<\varepsilon\). For \(|V(H)|\ge N_m\),
\[
\alpha(H)\ge c_m |V(H)|^{1-1/m}
=|V(H)|^{1-\varepsilon}\cdot c_m |V(H)|^{\varepsilon-1/m}.
\]
As \(|V(H)|\to\infty\), the factor \(c_m |V(H)|^{\varepsilon-1/m}\to\infty\),
giving the claimed exponent bound up to negligible loss.
\(\square\)

### Proposition I2 (What remains)
If one constructs \(G\) with \(\chi(G)=\aleph_1\) and verifies the hypothesis of
Proposition I1, EP-75 follows.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Combine Proposition I1 with the chromatic requirement.
\(\square\)
