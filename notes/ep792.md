# EP-792

## Problem Statement
Let \(f(n)\) be maximal such that every \(A\subset\mathbb Z\), \(|A|=n\), contains a sum-free subset \(B\subseteq A\) with \(|B|\ge f(n)\).
Estimate \(f(n)\).

## Literature
- The asymptotic frontier is near \(n/3+o(n)\) in general lower-bound formulations, with ongoing improvements.
- Exact asymptotic behavior remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Exact small-\(n\) search on sampled families (including interval adversaries).
- Heuristic larger-\(n\) extension with greedy sum-free construction.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In tested finite samples, worst-case values stay near \(n/2\):
  - exact rows \(n=16,20,24\): worst is 8,10,12.
  - heuristic rows \(n=28,32,40\): worst is 14,16,20.
- Small/medium finite instances are far from the asymptotic extremal regime driving the \(n/3\)-scale question.
