## Formal Proof Layer

### Claim
Assume every full infinite congruence system has uncovered density \(0\). Then for
every \(\varepsilon>0\) there exists a finite truncation whose uncovered density is
\(<\varepsilon\).

### Lemma 1 (Monotone truncation of uncovered sets)
Let \(U_m\) be uncovered set after first \(m\) congruences, and \(U_\infty\) for
the full system. Then
\[
U_1\supseteq U_2\supseteq \cdots \supseteq U_m\supseteq \cdots \supseteq U_\infty.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Adding congruence classes can only cover additional integers, never uncover new
ones.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Extremal principle at zero residues)
For fixed moduli \(n_1,\dots,n_m\), uncovered density is maximized when all chosen
residues are \(0\) (Rogers extremal principle).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
This is the cited extremal theorem from background.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Davenport-Erdős limit for multiples)
For zero residues, uncovered density of first \(m\) classes tends to uncovered
density of the infinite system as \(m\to\infty\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
This is the cited Davenport-Erdős density theorem for sets avoiding multiples.
\(\square\)

### Proof of Claim
Fix \(\varepsilon>0\). By assumption, full-system uncovered density is \(0\).
By Lemma 3 (applied to the extremal zero-residue configuration), there exists
\(m\) such that zero-residue uncovered density for first \(m\) moduli is
\(<\varepsilon\). By Lemma 2, any residue choice for those \(m\) moduli has
uncovered density at most that extremal value, hence also \(<\varepsilon\). This
is exactly the finite-truncation conclusion.
\(\square\)
