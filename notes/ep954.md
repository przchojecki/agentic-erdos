# EP-954

## Problem Statement
Let $1=a_1<a_2<\cdots$ be defined by $a_1=1$ and $a_{k+1}$ is the smallest integer $n$ such that
$$
\#\{(i,j): i\le j\le k,\ a_i+a_j\le n\}<n-k.
$$
Let
$$
R(x)=\#\{(i,j): i\le j,\ a_i+a_j\le x\}.
$$
Is it true that
$$
R(x)=x+O(x^{1/4+o(1)})?
$$

## Literature
- Rosen introduced this sequence.
- Known from construction: $R(x)\ge x$.
- Even proving $R(x)\le (1+o(1))x$ is already nontrivial/open in the original discussion.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone deep direct generation of the Rosen sequence up to $K=3200$ terms.
- From this initial segment, computed exact $R(x)$ for all $x\le a_K$ using all pair sums from the computed prefix.
- Evaluated error profile $R(x)-x$ on both $x=a_k$ checkpoints and dense $x$-grid.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Generated value: $a_{3200}=4{,}030{,}613$.
- Prefix matches known beginning:
  $$1,3,5,9,13,17,24,31,38,45,\ldots$$
- At $x=a_k$, data shows the exact pattern
$$
R(a_k)-a_k=-k
$$
in tested range, including $R(a_{3200})-a_{3200}=-3200$.
- On dense checkpoints up to $a_{3200}$, max observed absolute error was $3200$.
