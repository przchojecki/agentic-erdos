# EP-954 partial resolution

## Statement
For Rosen's recursively defined sequence, let R(x)=# {(i,j): i<=j, a_i+a_j<=x }.
Ask whether R(x)=x+O(x^{1/4+o(1)}).

## What is resolved
Background gives the built-in lower control R(x)>=x by construction.

## What remains open in this note
Even the upper asymptotic R(x)<=(1+o(1))x was not proved in the cited background,
so the stronger O(x^{1/4+o(1)}) error term is far open.

## Status
- baseline lower bound known.
- target asymptotic upper/error bound unresolved.

## Exact counting structure
Define
\[
R(x)=\#\{(i,j): i\le j,\ a_i+a_j\le x\}.
\]
Then:

1. Monotonicity:
\[
x_1\le x_2 \implies R(x_1)\le R(x_2).
\]

2. If \(M(x):=\#\{i:\ a_i\le x\}\), then
\[
R(x)\le \binom{M(x)+1}{2},
\]
since only indices with \(a_i\le x\) can participate in pairs counted by \(R(x)\).

So any upper bound on the one-dimensional counting function \(M(x)\) immediately transfers to an upper bound on \(R(x)\). The hard part is proving sharp cancellation around the main term \(x\), not just coarse pair-count bounds.

## Consequence of the built-in lower bound \(R(x)\ge x\)
Combining
\[
R(x)\ge x,\qquad
R(x)\le \binom{M(x)+1}{2},
\]
gives
\[
\binom{M(x)+1}{2}\ge x.
\]
Hence
\[
M(x)\ge \frac{\sqrt{8x+1}-1}{2}.
\]
In particular, \(M(x)\gg \sqrt{x}\).

So any proof attempt for the target asymptotic must remain compatible with at least
square-root growth in the count of sequence elements up to \(x\).
