# EP-979

## Problem Statement
For $k\ge2$, let $f_k(n)$ be the number of representations
$$
n=p_1^k+\cdots+p_k^k
$$
with primes $p_i$.
Is it true that
$$
\limsup_{n\to\infty} f_k(n)=\infty?
$$

## Literature
- Recorded as solved for $k=2$ and $k=3$ in classical references/background notes.
- General all-$k$ statement remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone deep finite max-multiplicity scans for:
  - $k=2$ up to $N=2\times 10^7$,
  - $k=3$ up to $N=2\times 10^8$,
  - $k=4$ up to $N=5\times 10^8$.
- Computed exact representation counts on these finite windows and extracted maximum observed multiplicity.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Max observed values in this run:
  - $k=2$: max $f_2(n)=26$,
  - $k=3$: max $f_3(n)=18$,
  - $k=4$: max $f_4(n)=60$.
- Finite maxima are already well above 1 and grow nontrivially within sampled ranges, consistent with large-limsup heuristics.

## Status
- Open in full generality.
- Added deeper standalone finite multiplicity profiles for $k=2,3,4$.
