# EP-979 partial resolution

## Statement
For `k>=2`, let `f_k(n)` count representations

`n = p_1^k + ... + p_k^k`

with primes `p_i`. Is `limsup f_k(n)=infinity`?

## What is resolved
Background states Erdos proved this for `k=2` and also for `k=3`
(the `k=3` proof noted as unpublished in the record).

## What remains open in this note
No general proof for all `k>=2` is provided in the cited background.

## Status
- `k=2,3`: resolved in background record.
- all `k`: unresolved here.

## Exact counting identities
For fixed \(k\ge 2\), let
\[
f_k(n):=\#\{(p_1,\dots,p_k)\in \mathbb P^k:\ n=p_1^k+\cdots+p_k^k\},
\]
with ordered \(k\)-tuples.

Then for every \(N\),
\[
\sum_{n\le N} f_k(n)
=\#\{(p_1,\dots,p_k)\in\mathbb P^k:\ p_1^k+\cdots+p_k^k\le N\}.
\]
This is an exact identity (double counting).

### Immediate consequence
If one proves
\[
\sum_{n\le N} f_k(n)\to\infty
\]
fast enough compared with the number of represented \(n\), then large values of \(f_k(n)\) must occur by pigeonhole principles. This is the standard route from mean-value information to a \(\limsup\) statement.

### Trivial monotonicity
\[
f_k(n)\in\mathbb Z_{\ge0},\qquad
\limsup_{n\to\infty} f_k(n)=\infty
\iff
\forall M\ \exists n:\ f_k(n)\ge M.
\]
So EP-979 is equivalent to existence of arbitrarily high representation multiplicities.
