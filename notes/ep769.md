# EP-769

## Problem Statement
Let \(c(n)\) be the least \(k\) such that the \(n\)-dimensional unit cube can be tiled by \(k\) homothetic \(n\)-cubes. Give good bounds for \(c(n)\), especially whether \(c(n)\gg n^n\).

## Literature
- Known lower and upper bounds are very far apart in absolute scale.
- The growth-rate question relative to \(n^n\) remains central.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Extended the finite numeric landscape of known bound formulas up to \(n=24\).
- Tracked normalized quantities versus \(n^n\) and upper/lower multiplicative gaps.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In the modeled bound set used here, the best available upper proxy is about \(e^2 n^n\), so normalized upper/n^n is near 7.389.
- Lower-hadwiger over \(n^n\) decays very quickly with \(n\), so upper/lower multiplicative gaps explode.
- This confirms large unresolved asymptotic uncertainty even at moderate dimensions.
