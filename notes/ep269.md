## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Rational-tail integrality test)
Let \(S=\sum_{n\ge1} u_n/v_n\) with \(v_n\mid v_{n+1}\), and \(S_N\) the \(N\)-th
partial sum. If \(S=A/B\in\mathbb Q\), then for all \(N\) with \(B\mid v_N\),
\[
v_N(S-S_N)\in \mathbb Z.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Since \(S-S_N=A/B-S_N\), multiplying by \(v_N\) gives
\[
v_N(S-S_N)=\frac{A v_N}{B}-v_N S_N.
\]
Both terms are integers: \(B\mid v_N\) gives \(A v_N/B\in\mathbb Z\), and
\(v_N S_N\in\mathbb Z\) by divisibility chain \(v_k\mid v_N\) for \(k\le N\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Tail-size exclusion)
Assume for infinitely many \(N\),
\[
0< v_N(S-S_N)<1.
\]
Then \(S\notin\mathbb Q\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
If \(S\in\mathbb Q\), choose infinitely many \(N\) with \(B\mid v_N\). By
Lemma 1, \(v_N(S-S_N)\in\mathbb Z\). But an integer strictly between \(0\) and
\(1\) does not exist, contradiction.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction for EP-269 finite \(P\))
If one proves infinitely many \(N\) satisfying Lemma 2's inequality for the
natural denominator chain of EP-269 partial sums, then finite-\(P\) irrationality
follows.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Immediate from Lemma 2.
\(\square\)
