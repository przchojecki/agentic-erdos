## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Strict increase and unboundedness)
Any EP-271 greedy sequence \((a_n)\) is strictly increasing and unbounded.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
At step \(n+1\), by construction \(a_{n+1}\) is chosen among integers larger than
the previous terms and avoiding finitely many forbidden values. Thus
\(a_{n+1}>a_n\). A strictly increasing sequence of integers is unbounded.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Finite-memory admissibility)
For each step \(n\), admissibility of a candidate \(x\) depends only on finitely
many constraints derived from \(\{a_1,\dots,a_n\}\). Hence the forbidden set at
step \(n\) is finite.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Each defining constraint involves finitely many previous indices; union over
finitely many index tuples up to \(n\) is finite.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Existence of next term)
Given Lemma 2, \(a_{n+1}\) exists for every \(n\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Among integers \(>a_n\), only finitely many are forbidden at step \(n\). Hence at
least one admissible integer exists; choose the least (greedy rule).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 4 (Reduction to exponent uniqueness)
If there exist constants \(c_1,c_2,\alpha>1\) such that for all large \(n\),
\[
c_1 n^\alpha\le a_n\le c_2 n^\alpha,
\]
then growth is polynomial of exponent \(\alpha\). Therefore the open part is
uniqueness/identification of \(\alpha\) by seed.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
The inequalities are exactly \(a_n=\Theta(n^\alpha)\).
\(\square\)
