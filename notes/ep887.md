# EP-887

## Problem Statement
Is there an absolute constant $K$ such that for every fixed $C>0$ and all sufficiently large $n$, the interval
$$(n^{1/2},\, n^{1/2}+C n^{1/4})$$
contains at most $K$ divisors of $n$?

## Literature
- Erdos-Rosenfeld established existence of infinitely many $n$ with 4 divisors in the $C=1$ interval, and asked whether 4 is optimal there.
- Global uniform boundedness in $C$ remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Ran a deeper finite scan up to $n\le 3\cdot 10^6$.
- For each $C\in\{1,2,3,4,6\}$, counted divisors in the target interval and tracked maxima.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Max counts found in range:
  - $C=1$: 1,
  - $C=2$: 4,
  - $C=3$: 5,
  - $C=4$: 6,
  - $C=6$: 8.
- Finite evidence is consistent with boundedness for fixed $C$, but no universal constant conclusion follows.
