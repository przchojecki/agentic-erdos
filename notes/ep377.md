## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
For
\[
f(n):=\sum_{\substack{p\le n\\ p\nmid \binom{2n}{n}}}\frac1p,
\]
determine whether \(f(n)\) is uniformly bounded.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a uniform weighted carry bound:
\[
\sum_{p\le n}\frac{\mathbf 1_{p\nmid \binom{2n}{n}}}{p}\le C
\]
with absolute \(C\), by splitting small/medium/large primes and showing enough cancellation/rarity in the no-carry condition for medium and large \(p\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (Kummer reformulation)
\[
p\nmid \binom{2n}{n}\iff \text{no carry in base }p\text{ when adding }n+n.
\]

**Proof.**
Immediate from Kummer: \(v_p\!\binom{2n}{n}\) equals the carry count. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (large-prime sparsity)
If \(p>n/2\), then \(p\mid\binom{2n}{n}\) unless \(n<p\), and thus primes in \((n/2,n]\) contribute at most \(O(1/\log n)\) to \(f(n)\).

**Proof.**
For \(p\in(n/2,n]\), \(v_p((2n)!)=2\) and \(v_p(n!)=1\), so \(v_p\binom{2n}{n}=0\) only in narrow edge configurations; summing \(1/p\) over such primes is bounded by \(\sum_{p>n/2}1/p\ll 1/\log n\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (dyadic reduction)
If for every dyadic block \(P<p\le2P\le n\) one has
\[
\sum_{P<p\le2P}\frac{\mathbf 1_{p\nmid \binom{2n}{n}}}{p}\ll P^{-\delta}
\]
uniformly in \(n\), then \(f(n)\) is bounded.

**Proof.**
Summing the dyadic estimate over \(P=2^j\) gives a convergent geometric series \(\sum_j 2^{-j\delta}\), uniformly in \(n\). \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is obtaining a uniform dyadic saving for the weighted no-carry indicator across all \(n\); existing methods provide average control but not the fully uniform bound needed for global boundedness.
