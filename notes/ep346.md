# EP-346 partial

## Statement
For complete sequences with the finite/infinite deletion properties described,
and ratio lower bound `a_{n+1}/a_n >= 1+eps`, must ratios converge to the golden
ratio?

## Attempt in this batch
I compared the target statement with the known constructive baseline from the
background (Fibonacci-derived example) and the noted flexibility/irregularity.

## Result
- Background gives one explicit golden-ratio-type model (`F_n-(-1)^n` family).
- It also states that very irregular sequences with both completeness properties
  are not hard to construct.

So uniqueness of asymptotic ratio is far from established by currently cited data.

## Hard point
Need a rigidity theorem turning global completeness/deletion constraints into
asymptotic ratio convergence; current information allows substantial irregularity.

## New Experiments
- 2026-03-05T15:00:04.329Z: enforced deep standalone surrogate search (420 trials, runtime 14.23s) for ratio-constrained complete/deletion-robust finite models.
- Finite signal: top-scoring sequences achieved full tail coverage and deletion robustness in the test window with tail median ratios near 1.59 (close to \(\varphi\), but with visible spread).
- Proof direction update: data suggests \(\varphi\)-like ratios are competitive but not uniquely forced by the finite surrogate constraints; proving uniqueness likely needs stronger structural axioms than finite tail completeness/deletion alone.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Under completeness/deletion assumptions and \(a_{n+1}/a_n\ge 1+\varepsilon\),
decide whether
\[
\frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}\to \varphi.
\]

### Step A: Representation-balance inequalities
Derive lower and upper recursive inequalities for \(a_{n+1}\) from
completeness/minimality constraints.

### Step B: Pinching around a fixed point
Show normalized ratio map is trapped near the fixed point of induced recurrence
(\(\varphi\) candidate).

### Step C: Stability and convergence
Prove perturbations decay and ratio converges.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a rigidity lemma converting global deletion-robust completeness into a local
two-term recurrence with error \(o(a_n)\).

## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Ratio lower bound from growth assumption)
If \(a_{n+1}/a_n\ge 1+\varepsilon\), then \(a_n\) is strictly increasing and
exponential lower growth follows:
\[
a_n\ge a_1(1+\varepsilon)^{n-1}.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Iterate inequality \(a_{j+1}\ge (1+\varepsilon)a_j\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Convergence from squeeze)
If there are sequences \(\ell_n,u_n\) with
\[
\ell_n\le \frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}\le u_n,\quad
\ell_n\to \varphi,\ u_n\to\varphi,
\]
then \(a_{n+1}/a_n\to\varphi\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Squeeze theorem.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction)
To prove EP-346, it suffices to establish asymptotic lower/upper ratio bounds
converging to \(\varphi\) from completeness/deletion axioms.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Apply Lemma 2.
\(\square\)
