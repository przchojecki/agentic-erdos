# EP-635

## Problem Statement
Let \(t\ge 1\) and \(A\subseteq\{1,\dots,N\}\) such that whenever \(a,b\in A\) with
\(b-a\ge t\), one has \((b-a)\nmid b\).
How large can \(|A|\) be? Is
\[
|A|\le \left(\tfrac12+o_t(1)\right)N
\]
true?

## Literature
- Erd\H{o}s noted exact optimum for \(t=1\): \(|A|=\lfloor (N+1)/2\rfloor\) (odd numbers witness).
- For \(t=2\), Erd\H{o}s gave constructions with \(|A|\ge N/2+c\log N\).
- The asymptotic upper bound near \(N/2\) for fixed \(t\) remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built exact branch-and-bound solver for finite \((N,t)\).
- Computed exact maxima for:
  - \(t=1,2\) up to \(N=34\),
  - \(t=3,4\) up to \(N=30\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- \(t=1\): ratio at \(N=34\) is exactly \(0.5\), matching known theorem.
- \(t=2\): at \(N=34\), exact optimum is \(20\) (ratio \(0.5882\)), consistent with super-half behavior.
- \(t=3,4\): finite maxima ratios at \(N=30\) are \(0.6\) and \(0.6333\), suggesting larger allowed density as \(t\) increases.
- These are finite-\(N\) values and do not settle fixed-\(t\) asymptotics.

## Status
- Open.
- Finite exact tables now available for several \(t\) and moderate \(N\).
