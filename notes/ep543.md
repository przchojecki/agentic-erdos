# EP-543 deeper attempt with group experiments

## Statement
`f(N)`: least random subset size in an abelian group of order `N` so subset sums cover
all of `G` with probability at least `1/2`.

## Experiment in this attempt
I estimated the 50% threshold (`k50`) in several group families of size `N=2^d`.

Data file:
- `data/ep543_group_subsetsum_thresholds.json`

Signal:
- In `F_2^d`, `k50 - log_2 N` was about `1`.
- In cyclic / near-cyclic models, `k50 - log_2 N` was about `2` to `3`.

This supports a very small additive overhead at tested scales (consistent with
`log_2 N + O(1)` behavior in finite ranges), but is not a proof of the conjectured
`o(log log N)` refinement.
