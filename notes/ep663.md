# EP-663

## Problem Statement
For fixed \(k\ge 2\), let \(q(n,k)\) be the least prime that does not divide
\(\prod_{i=1}^k (n+i)\).
Is it true that for fixed \(k\) and large \(n\),
\[
q(n,k)<(1+o(1))\log n?
\]

## Literature
- Easy bound: \(q(n,k)<(1+o(1))k\log n\).
- The sharp fixed-\(k\) form with factor 1 is open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented sampled prime scans for multiple fixed \(k\) and \(n\)-scales.
- Measured average and max observed \(q(n,k)\), normalized by \(\log n\) and \(k\log n\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Empirical \(q(n,k)/(k\log n)\) is far below 1 in tested ranges.
- \(q(n,k)/\log n\) remains around a modest constant depending on \(k\), consistent with \(\log n\)-scale behavior.
- Finite numerics support plausibility of improved constants but do not settle asymptotic \((1+o(1))\log n\).
