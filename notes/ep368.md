## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Let
\[
\mathcal E(x):=\#\{n\le x:\ P(n(n+1))\le (\log n)^2\}.
\]
The natural strong form is to prove \(\mathcal E(x)=o(x)\), and ideally a power-saving upper bound.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

There exist \(c,\delta>0\) such that for all large \(x\),
\[
\#\{n\le x:\ P(n)\le (\log x)^2,\ P(n+1)\le (\log x)^2\}\le x\exp\!\bigl(-c(\log x)^\delta\bigr).
\]

If this is proved, then the EP-368 event has zero density and in fact a strong rarity bound.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (event factorization)
For \(n\ge 3\),
\[
P(n(n+1))=\max(P(n),P(n+1)).
\]
Hence
\[
P(n(n+1))\le y\iff P(n)\le y\ \text{and}\ P(n+1)\le y.
\]

**Proof.**
Because \(\gcd(n,n+1)=1\), prime factors of \(n(n+1)\) are exactly the union of prime factors of \(n\) and of \(n+1\); therefore the largest prime factor is the maximum of the two largest prime factors. The equivalence follows immediately. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (log-threshold normalization)
For \(n\le x\), \(P(n(n+1))\le (\log n)^2\) implies
\[
P(n)\le (\log x)^2,\qquad P(n+1)\le (\log x)^2.
\]
Therefore
\[
\mathcal E(x)\le \#\{n\le x:\ P(n)\le (\log x)^2,\ P(n+1)\le (\log x)^2\}.
\]

**Proof.**
If \(n\le x\), then \((\log n)^2\le (\log x)^2\). Apply Lemma B1 with \(y=(\log x)^2\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies density-zero statement)
If Lemma A holds, then \(\mathcal E(x)=o(x)\).

**Proof.**
Lemma B2 bounds \(\mathcal E(x)\) by the left side in Lemma A; the latter is \(x\exp(-c(\log x)^\delta)=o(x)\). \(\square\)

### C. What remains genuinely open

The bottleneck is a **correlation bound for adjacent friable events** at the tiny friability scale \(y=(\log x)^2\). Existing one-variable smooth-number asymptotics are not enough; the missing input is a sharp two-point estimate for
\[
\mathbf 1_{P(n)\le y}\mathbf 1_{P(n+1)\le y}
\]
in this extreme regime.
