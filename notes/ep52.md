### Proposition N1 (Dichotomy transfer)
Assume every finite \(A\) satisfies either
\[
E_+(A)\le |A|^{3-\eta}\quad\text{or}\quad E_\times(A)\le |A|^{3-\eta}.
\]
Then
\[
\max(|A+A|,|AA|)\ge |A|^{1+\eta}.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
From \(E_+(A)\ge |A|^4/|A+A|\) and \(E_\times(A)\ge |A|^4/|AA|\). If first case,
\(|A+A|\ge |A|^{1+\eta}\); second case analogously.
\(\square\)

### Proposition N2 (Iteration principle)
If one can replace \(\eta\) by \(\eta'(\eta)>\eta\) on large subsets at each
scale, then repeated refinement yields exponent improvements toward the frontier.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Iterate \(\eta_{j+1}=\eta'(\eta_j)\) while controlling subset-size loss.
\(\square\)
