# EP-704

## Problem Statement
Let \(G_n\) be the unit-distance graph in \(\mathbb R^n\): vertices are points of \(\mathbb R^n\), edges connect pairs at Euclidean distance 1.
Estimate \(\chi(G_n)\). Does it grow exponentially in \(n\)? Does
\[
\lim_{n\to\infty}\chi(G_n)^{1/n}
\]
exist?

## Literature
- Classical bounds show exponential lower and upper behavior with a substantial gap between bases.
- Existence/value of an asymptotic base remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built a finite random geometric proxy model for each dimension:\
  sample points on the unit sphere, connect pairs with distance in a narrow unit window \(|\|x-y\|-1|\le\varepsilon\).
- Computed for each finite sample graph:
  - clique-based lower bound,
  - greedy coloring upper bound.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Deep run used 260 sampled points, \(\varepsilon=0.02\), dimensions 2 through 9.
- Observed finite-slice bounds were typically in a narrow range (clique lower 2-4, greedy upper 3-6).
- These are heuristic finite-model indicators only, not rigorous bounds for the full infinite graph \(G_n\).
