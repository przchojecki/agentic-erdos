# EP-677

## Problem Statement
Let
\[
M(n,k)=\operatorname{lcm}(n+1,\dots,n+k).
\]
Is it true that for all \(m\ge n+k\),
\[
M(n,k)\ne M(m,k)?
\]

## Literature
- Thue--Siegel implies only finitely many collisions for fixed \(k\).
- Erd\H{o}s expected very few solutions in general, and essentially none in stronger regimes.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Ran deeper finite collision scans:
  - same-\(k\) collisions up to \(n\le 1600\), \(k\le 10\),
  - cross-\(k\) collisions up to \(n\le 600\), \(k\le 8\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- No same-\(k\) collisions found in scanned range with the required separation \(m\ge n+k\).
- Cross-\(k\) scan reproduces known small examples and a tiny number of additional finite coincidences.
- This supports practical rarity for fixed \(k\), but cannot prove finiteness.

## Status
- Open.
- Added larger finite evidence in favor of rarity of collisions.
