# EP-714

## Problem Statement
For fixed \(r\), is it true that
\[
\mathrm{ex}(n;K_{r,r}) \gg n^{2-1/r}?
\]

## Literature
- K\H{o}v\'ari-S\'os-Tur\'an gives the matching upper-scale exponent \(2-1/r\).
- Lower bounds at this scale are known in special cases (notably \(r=2,3\)); the general fixed-\(r\) picture remains difficult.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built standalone random-greedy constructions of bipartite \(K_{r,r}\)-free graphs for \(r=2,3\).
- Measured best edge counts versus the scale \(n^{2-1/r}\) across several \(n\)-values.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Deep run (
\(r=2,3\), \(n=20,30,40,50,60\), 40 trials each) produced edge counts with
\[
\frac{e}{n^{2-1/r}}
\]
roughly in the range 0.31-0.39.
- Ratios are stable and positive across the tested sizes, consistent with the target exponent scale in finite experiments.

## Status
- Open in full generality.
- Added deeper standalone finite lower-bound construction scan for \(r=2,3\).
