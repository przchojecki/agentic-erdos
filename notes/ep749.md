# EP-749

## Problem Statement
For each \(\varepsilon>0\), is there \(A\subset\mathbb N\) such that lower density of \(A+A\) is at least \(1-\varepsilon\), while representation counts
\[
r_{A+A}(n)=\#\{(a,b)\in A^2:a+b=n\}
\]
remain uniformly bounded?

## Literature
- This asks for simultaneous high additive coverage and global bounded representation.
- Standard sparse bounded-representation constructions and dense-sumset constructions pull in opposite directions.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built standalone local-search optimizer on finite windows \([0,N]\):
  - enforce cap \(r_{A+A}(s)\le R\) for all \(s\le 2N\),
  - maximize sumset coverage density over \([0,2N]\).
- Scanned multiple \((N,R)\) pairs with long search budgets.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- With strict caps \(R=3,4\), best finite densities are moderate (roughly 0.4-0.7 in tested windows).
- Relaxing cap to \(R=5,6\) improves finite densities (occasionally above 0.8), but still far from uniform near-1 behavior.
- Finite data reflects the core tension: stronger representation bounds noticeably suppress achievable coverage.
