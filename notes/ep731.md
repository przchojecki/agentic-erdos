# EP-731

## Problem Statement
For almost all \(n\), estimate the least \(m\) such that
\[
m\nmid \binom{2n}{n}.
\]

## Literature
- Reported scale is of stretched-exponential type in \(\log n\), often phrased near
\(\exp((\log n)^{1/2+o(1)})\) for the least non-divisor parameter.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built standalone incremental valuation engine for \(\binom{2n}{n}\):
  - updated prime exponents from \(n\) to \(n+1\) via
  \(\binom{2n}{n}=\binom{2n-2}{n-1}\frac{(2n-1)(2n)}{n^2}\),
  - scanned least \(m\le M_{\max}\) not dividing \(\binom{2n}{n}\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Deep run to \(n\le 15000\), with \(M_{\max}=4000\), had zero unresolved cases.
- Sampled statistic \(\log m / \sqrt{\log n}\) stays in a moderate band, median about 0.933 over sampled checkpoints.
- Finite behavior is compatible with the known qualitative scale, without proving the almost-all law.

## Status
- Open (asymptotic almost-all behavior).
- Added deeper standalone scan with incremental valuation updates and expanded range.
