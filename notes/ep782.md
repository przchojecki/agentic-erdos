# EP-782

## Problem Statement
Do squares contain arbitrarily long quasi-progressions?  
That is: does there exist a constant \(C>0\) such that for every \(k\), there are squares
\(x_1,\dots,x_k\) and a step \(d\) with
\[
x_i+d \le x_{i+1} \le x_i+d+C \quad (1\le i<k)?
\]
Related question: do squares contain high-dimensional additive cubes.

## Literature
- The full arbitrary-length quasi-progression/cube questions remain open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built deeper standalone search in squares up to \(3\cdot 10^6\):
  - exact DP for best quasi-progression length for fixed \(C\),
  - additive-cube probes (2D exhaustive-style finite scan, 3D random search).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Best quasi-progression lengths found rise with tolerance:
  - \(C=0,1\): 3,
  - \(C=2,4\): 4,
  - \(C=8\): 7,
  - \(C=12\): 9,
  - \(C=16\): 12.
- Many 2D additive-cube configurations were found in the tested range; no 3D hit appeared in 450000 random probes.
