# EP-627

## Problem Statement
Let
\[
f(n):=\max_{|V(G)|=n}\frac{\chi(G)}{\omega(G)}.
\]
Does
\[
\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{f(n)}{n/(\log n)^2}
\]
exist?

## Literature
- Erd\H{o}s proved \(f(n)\asymp n/(\log n)^2\).
- If the above limit exists, known arguments constrain it to a fixed nontrivial interval.
- Existence of the limit itself remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added an exact-construction track using Mycielski iterates of \(C_5\), giving explicit triangle-free graphs with known exact \((\chi,\omega)=(3+t,2)\).
- Added deep random-graph proxy track with high-restart greedy \(\chi\)-upper and clique-lower profiling.
- The Mycielski track gives rigorous finite-\(n\) lower-bound examples for \(f(n)\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Mycielski family up to \(n=191\) shows normalized values \((\chi/\omega)/(n/\log^2 n)\) around \(0.58\) to \(1.07\), with decreasing trend along this sparse explicit sequence.
- Random dense-graph proxy runs for \(n=120,180,240\) give normalized proxy constants near \(0.55\) to \(0.57\).
- Both tracks are consistent with \(n/(\log n)^2\)-scale behavior, but finite-\(n\) constants remain noisy and model-dependent.

## Status
- Open.
- No convergence proof for the normalized ratio from current methods in this note.
