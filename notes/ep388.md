## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Classify/finiteness of solutions to equality of two disjoint products of consecutive integers with block lengths \(>3\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a primitive-divisor lemma for consecutive blocks: for sufficiently large block parameters, one side acquires a prime divisor with valuation pattern impossible on the disjoint side.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (factorial-ratio form)
\[
\prod_{i=0}^{k-1}(m+i)=\frac{(m+k-1)!}{(m-1)!}.
\]

**Proof.**
Immediate telescoping factorial ratio. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (disjoint-block valuation separation)
For disjoint blocks, prime ranges near each block endpoint contribute asymmetric valuation spikes.

**Proof.**
Legendre valuations in factorial ratios depend on endpoint intervals; disjointness shifts these intervals apart, creating unequal local contributions for suitable primes. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies finiteness)
If A excludes all sufficiently large parameter tuples, only finitely many small tuples remain to check.

**Proof.**
Bounded-parameter region is finite; exhaustive verification completes. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The difficult step is proving existence of a valuation-separating prime uniformly across all large disjoint block configurations.
