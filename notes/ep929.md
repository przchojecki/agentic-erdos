# EP-929

## Problem Statement
Let $k\ge 2$ and let $S(k)$ be the minimal $x$ such that there is a positive-density set of $n$ for which
$$
n+1,n+2,\dots,n+k
$$
are all divisible only by primes $\le x$.
Estimate $S(k)$, in particular whether $S(k)\ge k^{1-o(1)}$.

## Literature
- Classical sieve lower bounds and modern prime-gap arguments give nontrivial lower/upper constraints.
- A substantial asymptotic gap remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone deep finite-density proxy using sliding-window maxima of largest prime factors.
- Parameters in this run:
  - $N=20{,}000{,}000$ start points,
  - $k\in\{6,8,10,12,16,20,24,28,32,40,48,56,64\}$,
  - threshold scan up to $x=120$,
  - multiple density thresholds $\varepsilon\in\{10^{-5},5\cdot 10^{-5},10^{-4},2\cdot10^{-4}\}$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Finite minimal $x$ with any occurrence increases roughly linearly with $k$ in tested range:
  - e.g. $x_{\mathrm{any}}(16)=17$, $x_{\mathrm{any}}(32)=31$, $x_{\mathrm{any}}(64)=61$.
- In this range and at this $N$, no tested pair reached density thresholds $10^{-4}$ or $2\cdot10^{-4}$.
- Signal is consistent with nontrivial growth of effective smoothness threshold as block length grows.
