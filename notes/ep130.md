## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Decide whether the chromatic number of the integer-distance graph under the
general-position constraints can be infinite.

### Step A: Finite Radius Coloring Bound
For each radius/diameter scale \(R\), prove a uniform bound \(\chi_R\le C\) for
the induced finite graph class.

### Step B: Scale-Compatibility (Compactness)
Show the colorings from Step A can be made consistent across growing scales
(inverse-limit / compactness argument).

### Step C: Infinite Coloring
Deduce a global finite coloring of the full infinite graph.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a robust decomposition lemma: every such finite integer-distance graph in
general position admits bounded-degeneracy (or bounded arboricity) after removing
a controlled sparse exceptional set.
