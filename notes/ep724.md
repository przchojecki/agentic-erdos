# EP-724

## Problem Statement
If \(f(n)\) is the maximum number of mutually orthogonal Latin squares of order \(n\), is it true that
\[
f(n) \gg n^{1/2}?
\]

## Literature
- Known unconditional lower bounds improve progressively in the literature (e.g. exponents around \(1/14.8\)), still far from \(1/2\).

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Computed explicit guaranteed lower bounds via a MacNeish-type product bound:
\[
f(n) \ge \min_{p^a\parallel n}(p^a-1).
\]
- Generated full profile for all \(2\le n\le 500\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Dense scan confirms strong variability by arithmetic structure:
  - prime-power \(n=q\): bound is \(q-1\), much larger than \(\sqrt n\),
  - highly composite \(n\) with a small prime-power factor can force much smaller guaranteed bounds.
- So current guaranteed behavior is highly non-uniform; the universal \(n^{1/2}\) lower target remains far beyond this baseline method.
