# EP-675

## Problem Statement
A set \(A\subset\mathbb N\) has the translation property if for every \(n\), there exists \(t_n\ge 1\) such that for all \(1\le a\le n\),
\[
a\in A \iff a+t_n\in A.
\]
Open instances include sums of two squares and related sieve-defined sets; also growth of minimal \(t_n\) in specific examples.

## Literature
- Squarefree numbers are known to have the translation property (elementary sieve).
- More generally, Brun-sieve criteria give translation property under pairwise-coprime exclusion sets with a harmonic-sum sparsity condition.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented direct search for minimal shifts \(t_n\) for squarefree-indicator agreement on prefixes \([1,n]\).
- Computed minimal found \(t_n\) up to search cap \(T_{\max}=120000\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Minimal shifts grow rapidly in tested range (e.g. very large \(t_n\) already by \(n\approx 80\) to \(100\)).
- For \(n=108,120\), no shift found within cap, suggesting continued growth beyond this bound.
- Finite numerics support fast-growth behavior for squarefree \(t_n\), but do not establish a sharp asymptotic law.

## Status
- Translation property itself: known for squarefree set.
- Quantitative growth of minimal shifts: open.
