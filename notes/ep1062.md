# EP-1062

## Problem Statement
Let $f(n)$ be the largest size of a subset $A\subset\{1,\dots,n\}$ such that no element of $A$ divides two other elements of $A$.
Study asymptotic behavior of $f(n)$.

## Literature
- Classical bounds place $f(n)$ in a narrow linear window around a constant multiple of $n$.
- Precise limiting behavior remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Replaced batch fragment with standalone exact branch-and-bound solver.
- Ran a deep bounded-time profile for
\[
n=30,32,\dots,54
\]
with per-$n$ search budget and node-count instrumentation.
- For smaller $n$ in this range, values are exact; for larger $n$ that hit budget, recorded best certified lower bounds.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Exact values obtained through $n=40$ in this run.
- For $n\ge 42$, the run hit the time budget and still produced strong lower bounds with ratios clustered near
\[
\frac{2}{3}.
\]
- The deep run traversed very large search trees (up to about $1.2\times10^9$ nodes on budget-hit instances), confirming this is now a genuinely heavy one-by-one pass.
