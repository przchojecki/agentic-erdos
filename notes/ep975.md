# EP-975

## Problem Statement
Let $f\in\mathbb Z[x]$ be irreducible, nonconstant, and eventually positive.
Is there a constant $c=c(f)>0$ such that
$$
\sum_{n\le X}\tau(f(n))\sim cX\log X?
$$

## Literature
- General two-sided order $\asymp X\log X$ is known in broad settings.
- Irreducible quadratic case is solved (Hooley-type asymptotic with explicit constants in many families).
- General higher-degree irreducible case remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone deep finite scan of
$$
S_f(X)=\sum_{n\le X}\tau(f(n))
$$
for several irreducible quadratics up to $X=50{,}000$.
- Computed normalized ratio $S_f(X)/(X\log X)$ at multiple checkpoints.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For each tested quadratic, $S_f(X)/(X\log X)$ stabilizes to an apparent constant by $X=50{,}000$.
- Examples at $X=50{,}000$:
  - $f(n)=n^2+1$: ratio about $1.0404$.
  - $f(n)=n^2+n+41$: ratio about $0.3790$.
  - $f(n)=n^2+2$: ratio about $1.4374$.
  - $f(n)=n^2+3$: ratio about $1.5469$.
- This finite behavior is consistent with the asymptotic-form template in solved quadratic cases.

## Status
- Quadratic irreducible case: established in literature.
- General irreducible case: open.
- Added deeper standalone finite constant-profile computations.
