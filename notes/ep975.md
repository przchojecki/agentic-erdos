# EP-975 partial resolution

## Statement
For irreducible nonconstant `f in Z[x]` (eventually positive), ask whether

`sum_{n<=X} tau(f(n)) ~ c(f) X log X`.

## What is resolved
Background provides:
- general two-sided order of magnitude:
  `sum_{n<=X} tau(f(n))` is between constants times `X log X`;
- full asymptotic is known when `f` is irreducible quadratic (Hooley);
- explicit constants are worked out in several quadratic families.

So the quadratic irreducible case is solved.

## What remains open in this note
The general irreducible case (higher degree in particular) is not resolved by the
provided background.

## Status
- Irreducible quadratic case: resolved.
- General irreducible case: unresolved here.

## Proof framework: exact decomposition
For
\[
S_f(X):=\sum_{n\le X}\tau(f(n)),
\]
use \(\tau(m)=\sum_{d\mid m}1\) to get
\[
S_f(X)=\sum_{d\ge 1} A_f(X;d),\qquad
A_f(X;d):=\#\{n\le X:\ f(n)\equiv 0\pmod d\}.
\]
Since \(f(n)\ll_f X^{\deg f}\), only \(d\ll_f X^{\deg f}\) contribute.

This identity is exact and is the starting point of all asymptotic analyses.

### Local-root model
Let
\[
\rho_f(d):=\#\{a \bmod d:\ f(a)\equiv 0\pmod d\}.
\]
On interval scales, one expects
\[
A_f(X;d)\approx \frac{\rho_f(d)}{d}X.
\]
Formally inserting this into the exact decomposition gives
\[
S_f(X)\approx X\sum_{d\le X^{\deg f}}\frac{\rho_f(d)}{d},
\]
and logarithmic divergence of this weighted root-sum yields the \(X\log X\) main scale.

So the whole problem is reduced to controlling averaged root counts \(\rho_f(d)\) and the error in replacing \(A_f(X;d)\) by \(\rho_f(d)X/d\).
