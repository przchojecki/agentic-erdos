# EP-940

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

### Working formulation
A natural finite proxy is: for each \(r\ge 3\), how densely can integers be represented as sums of at most \(r\) many \(r\)-powerful numbers?

### Deep standalone computation (`ep940.mjs`)
- Method: exact SPF classification of \(r\)-powerful numbers up to `maxN`, then dynamic programming for representability by exactly/at-most \(t\le r\) summands.
- Run: `maxN = 5,000,000`, \(r\in\{3,4,5\}\).
- Counts of available \(r\)-powerful summands:
  - \(r=3\): 553
  - \(r=4\): 191
  - \(r=5\): 98
- Coverage by at most \(r\) summands (up to `5e6`):
  - \(r=3\): `4,371,713` values (`0.8743426` density)
  - \(r=4\): `4,618,651` values (`0.9237302` density)
  - \(r=5\): `4,683,905` values (`0.936781` density)

### Interpretation
Finite coverage is very high but not complete at this scale. This supports the additive-basis direction as plausible while leaving global exact statements open.
