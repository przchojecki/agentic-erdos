# EP-676 partial

## Statement
Is every sufficiently large integer representable as
`n = a p^2 + b` with prime `p`, `a>=1`, `0<=b<p`?

## Attempt in this batch
I scanned representability via the equivalent modular condition
`n mod p^2 < p` for some prime `p<=sqrt(n)`.
I also scanned the variant without primality (`m` instead of prime `p`) on a
smaller range.

Data file:
- `data/ep676_representation_scan.json`

## Result
- With prime condition, up to `1e6` there are `83811` exceptions.
- Without prime condition, up to `2e5` there are `1684` exceptions.
- Exception density decreases but remains clearly nonzero in these finite ranges.

## Hard point
Finite exception counts do not decide whether all sufficiently large integers are
eventually representable (or whether infinitely many exceptions persist).
