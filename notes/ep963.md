# EP-963

## Problem Statement
Let $f(n)$ be the largest $k$ such that every $n$-element set $A\subset\mathbb R$ contains a dissociated subset $B\subseteq A$ with $|B|\ge k$.
In particular, is
$$
f(n)\ge \lfloor \log_2 n\rfloor?
$$

## Literature
- Classical greedy argument gives
$$
f(n)\ge \lfloor \log_3 n\rfloor.
$$
- Closing the base-3 to base-2 gap remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone deep finite adversarial search over integer sets:
  - sample many sets $A\subset [1,U]$ with $|A|=n$,
  - compute exact maximum dissociated-subset size in each sampled set by branch-and-bound,
  - track the minimum observed value (worst sampled instance).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Tested sizes $n=8,10,12,14,16,18,20$ with hundreds of random adversarial trials each.
- Minimum observed max-dissociated sizes were:
  - $3$ for $n\in\{8,10,12\}$,
  - $4$ for $n\in\{14,16,18,20\}$.
- In this finite proxy, the observed minima track near $\lfloor\log_2 n\rfloor$ at moderate $n$, and remain above the known $\lfloor\log_3 n\rfloor$ baseline.
