# EP-603

## Problem Statement
For countably infinite sets \((A_i)\) with \(|A_i\cap A_j|\neq 2\), determine the minimum number of colors required to avoid monochromatic \(A_i\).

## Literature / Context
- The analogous \(|A_i\cap A_j|\neq 1\) variant has known countable-color sufficiency (as noted in prior background).
- The exact minimum for the \(\neq 2\) condition remains unresolved in local notes.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built a finite minimum-color proxy:
  - generate finite families with pairwise intersection never equal to \(2\),
  - compute minimum vertex-color count (up to \(k=5\)) via exact backtracking.
- This is finite evidence only.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In all built instances across tested windows, minimum colors found was \(2\):
  - no sampled instance required \(3+\) colors.
- Histogram is fully concentrated at \(2\) in these regimes.

## Next Proof Targets
- Construct adversarial families (if they exist) that force more than 2 colors under \(\neq 2\) intersections.
- Develop structural criteria distinguishing the \(\neq 1\) and \(\neq 2\) regimes at infinite scale.
