# EP-912

## Problem Statement
If
$$
n!=\prod_i p_i^{k_i}
$$
is the prime factorization of $n!$, let $h(n)$ be the number of distinct exponent values among the $k_i$.
Is there a constant $c>0$ such that
$$
h(n)\sim c\left(\frac{n}{\log n}\right)^{1/2}?
$$

## Literature
- Erdos--Selfridge proved the order of growth
$$
h(n)\asymp \left(\frac{n}{\log n}\right)^{1/2}.
$$
- The exact leading constant $c$ remains unresolved in standard references/notes, with heuristic predictions discussed in background commentary.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented exact incremental computation of $h(n)$ from $n=2$ up to $N=5\cdot 10^7$.
- Maintained exact prime-exponent profile of $n!$ and tracked the number of distinct exponent values at sampled checkpoints.
- Computed normalized ratio
$$
\frac{h(n)}{\sqrt{n/\log n}}
$$
to estimate finite-range constant behavior.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Final sample at $n=50{,}000{,}000$ gives
$$
\frac{h(n)}{\sqrt{n/\log n}}\approx 2.6485.
$$
- Over tail samples in this run, the ratio stays in a narrow band about $2.639$ to $2.666$, with mean about $2.6519$.
- Finite evidence strongly supports the known $\sqrt{n/\log n}$ scale and suggests stable constant-like behavior in this range.

## Status
- Order of growth: established in literature.
- Exact asymptotic constant: still open.
- Added deep standalone exact computation up to $N=5\times10^7$.
