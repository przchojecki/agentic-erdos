## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Mutual coprimality)
\(\gcd(n,n+1)=1\) for all integers \(n\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Any common divisor divides their difference \(1\), hence is \(1\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Powerful pair factorization consequence)
If both \(n\) and \(n+1\) are powerful, then each prime dividing \(n(n+1)\)
appears with exponent at least \(2\) in one of the two coprime factors.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
By definition of powerful and Lemma 1, prime factors split between \(n\) and
\(n+1\) with no overlap.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction to family counting)
If an infinite parameterized family yields distinct consecutive powerful pairs and
all such pairs arise from finitely many families up to bounded multiplicity, then
pair-count asymptotics reduce to parameter-count asymptotics.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Upper/lower counts differ only by bounded multiplicative factors from overlap
control.
\(\square\)
