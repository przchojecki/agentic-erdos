# EP-1068

## Problem Statement
EP-1068 asks for an infinite/ countable-subgraph connectivity-chromatic tension statement (infinite setting), with finite high-chromatic constructions as proxy intuition.

## Literature
- Problem remains open in its infinite form.
- Mycielski-type finite constructions are natural stress tests for high chromatic number under structural constraints.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Replaced batch stub with a standalone deep finite proxy experiment:
  - built the family $C_5, M(C_5), M^2(C_5), M^3(C_5)$,
  - exact DSATUR chromatic computation on family members,
  - k-core and random-cut connectivity-lower probes,
  - heavy induced-subgraph campaign on $M^3(C_5)$ with exact bounded DSATUR runs.
- Deep run scale:
  - $2800$ induced samples for each of five sizes (total $14{,}000$ exact coloring runs),
  - full-family exact checks up to $n=47$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Family chromatic numbers increase as expected: $3,4,5,6$.
- On $M^3(C_5)$, induced-sample chromatic proxies tighten with size; by $n=46$, sampled subgraphs were consistently near maximal chromatic load in this model.
- These finite signals reflect strong high-chromatic/connected tension, but do not imply the infinite theorem.

## Status
- Open (infinite statement).
