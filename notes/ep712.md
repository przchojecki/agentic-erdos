# EP-712

## Problem Statement
Determine asymptotics of
\[
\frac{\mathrm{ex}_r(n,K_k^r)}{\binom{n}{r}}
\]
for fixed \(k>r>2\).

## Literature
- The graph case \(r=2\) is classical (Tur\'an theorem).
- The hypergraph regime \(r>2\) is much harder and remains broadly open in this generality.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built standalone finite \(K_k^r\)-free hypergraph experiments for \((r,k)=(3,4)\):
  - random-greedy lower-bound constructions,
  - comparison with balanced \((k-1)\)-partite baseline density.
- These are finite lower-bound experiments only.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For \(n=10,12,14,16,18,20\), best random-greedy densities decrease from about 0.58 to 0.47.
- Over this range, random-greedy densities are substantially above the simple balanced 3-partite baseline (about 0.30 down to 0.26).
- This highlights that naive partite constructions are far from extremal in small finite \(3\)-uniform \(K_4^3\)-free experiments.

## Status
- Open.
