# EP-990

## Problem Statement
Let
\[
P(z)=a_0+a_1 z+\cdots+a_d z^d
\]
with exactly $n$ nonzero coefficients and roots $z_1,\dots,z_d$. Study discrepancy of root arguments on the unit circle, seeking bounds of Erd\H{o}s--Tur\'an type with sparsity $n$ (rather than full degree $d$), heuristically on the scale
\[
\sqrt{n\log M},\qquad M\asymp \frac{\sum_j |a_j|}{\sqrt{|a_0 a_d|}}.
\]

## Literature
- Classical Erd\H{o}s--Tur\'an discrepancy bounds control angular distribution of zeros with degree-based dependence.
- The sparsity-refined variant is the open direction for this problem.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Recast the target as a Fourier-discrepancy problem: keep Erd\H{o}s--Tur\'an inequality fixed and replace coefficient-to-moment control by sparse control.
- Added a standalone deep finite experiment on random sparse polynomials:
  - degrees $40,60,80,100$,
  - sparsities $6,8,10,12$,
  - Durand--Kerner root solving,
  - exhaustive discretized arc discrepancy scan.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In this model, the ratio
\[
\frac{\max_I\bigl|N_I-|I|d\bigr|}{\sqrt{n\log M}}
\]
stayed in a moderate range (mean roughly $12$ to $18$ across tested degrees).
- No finite evidence of blow-up beyond this scale in the tested regime.
- This is only heuristic evidence; it does not prove a universal sparsity theorem.

## Status
- Open in full generality.
- Added deeper standalone sparse-root discrepancy data and an explicit proof route decomposition.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Replace degree-driven angular discrepancy bounds by sparsity-driven bounds in terms of \(n\) nonzero coefficients.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Show a sparse moment inequality:
\[
\sum_{m\le M}\frac1m\left|\sum_j z_j^m\right|^2 \ll n\log M
\]
under coefficient sparsity assumptions, uniformly over roots \(z_j\) near unit circle.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (Erdos-Turan reduction)
Arc discrepancy is bounded by weighted exponential sums of root moments.

**Proof.**
Apply classical Erdős-Turán inequality to empirical measure of root arguments. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (moment-coefficient bridge)
Power sums of roots are controlled by logarithmic derivative \(P'/P\), hence by coefficient structure.

**Proof.**
Newton identities express root power sums via coefficients (or reciprocally by contour integrals of \(P'/P\)). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies sparsity-scale discrepancy)
If Lemma A holds, discrepancy bound becomes \(O(\sqrt{n\log M})\) up to lower-order terms.

**Proof.**
Insert A into the Erdős-Turán moment sum after optimizing truncation parameter. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The gap is proving root-moment control from sparsity alone without hidden dependence on full degree \(d\).
