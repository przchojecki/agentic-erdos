### Proposition CM1 (Congruence exclusion principle)
If \(n\equiv a \pmod q\) where every residue class in an interval block forces an
odd exponent of some \(p\equiv3\pmod4\), then no number in that block is a sum of
two squares.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
By Fermat's criterion: sums of two squares have even exponents at all
\(p\equiv3\pmod4\).
\(\square\)

### Proposition CM2 (Order reduction)
If one proves
\[
c_1 \frac{\log x}{\log\log x}\le G_{2\square}(x)\le
c_2 \frac{\log x}{\log\log x},
\]
then maximal gap \(G_{2\square}(x)\) has logarithmic-over-loglog order.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Direct two-sided asymptotic comparison.
\(\square\)
