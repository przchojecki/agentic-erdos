# EP-173 partial resolution

## Statement
In any 2-coloring of `R^2`, for all but at most one triangle `T`, must there be
a monochromatic congruent copy of `T`?

## What is resolved
Background reports Shader proved the statement for a single right triangle.

## What remains open in this note
The full "all but at most one triangle" claim is not established by the cited
result.

## Status
- right-triangle case: resolved.
- full near-universal triangle claim: unresolved.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove (or refute) that in every 2-coloring of \(\mathbb R^2\), all but at most
one triangle type have a monochromatic congruent copy.

### Step A: Triangle-type parameter reduction
Parametrize congruence classes by side-length triples and isolate exceptional
parameter sets.

### Step B: Ergodic/coloring regularity input
Apply measurable/combinatorial regularity to force monochromatic copies for a
full-measure family of parameters.

### Step C: Exceptional-set collapse
Show exceptional family has size at most \(1\).

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a transference theorem from positive-density color classes to monochromatic
realization of a continuum of triangle types with quantitative exceptional control.

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition BO1 (Parameter-space reformulation)
Let \(\mathcal T\) be the space of triangle congruence types. If for a coloring
every type in \(\mathcal T\setminus E\) is monochromatically realizable, then the
EP claim is equivalent to \(|E|\le 1\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Direct translation of the statement into exception-set language.
\(\square\)

### Proposition BO2 (Reduction)
If one proves \(E\) is finite and also proves no two distinct types can both be
exceptional, then \(|E|\le 1\) and EP-173 follows.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Finite set plus pairwise exclusion implies cardinality at most one.
\(\square\)
