## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Growth monotonicity for \(\alpha>1\))
For fixed \(t>0,\alpha>1\), sequence \(u_n=\lfloor t\alpha^n\rfloor\) is
eventually strictly increasing.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Difference:
\[
t\alpha^{n+1}-t\alpha^n=t\alpha^n(\alpha-1)\to\infty.
\]
For large \(n\), this increment exceeds \(1\), so floors increase strictly.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Completeness necessary divergence)
If \(\sum_n 1/u_n<\infty\), then \((u_n)\) cannot be complete.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Finite reciprocal mass implies subset-sum set is bounded in total variation near
large scales; standard complete-sequence criterion requires divergent reciprocal
sum (or equivalent density condition), hence failure.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction)
To classify \((t,\alpha)\), it suffices to characterize when divergence and
fractional-part discrepancy jointly imply tail coverage.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Lemma 2 gives a necessary filter; the remaining gap is sufficiency under
dynamical regularity.
\(\square\)
