### Blocking Lemma
- Need a transfer from sparse large spikes of \(M_n\) to persistent cumulative

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove for some \(c>0\) and all large \(n\):
\[
\sum_{k\le n} M_k > n^{1+c}.
\]

### Step A: Spike Frequency, Not Just Existence
Upgrade \(\max_{k\le N}M_k>N^c\) to a counting statement:
there are at least \(N^\theta\) indices \(k\le N\) with \(M_k\ge N^\gamma\).

### Step B: Scale-Consistent Lower Envelope
Show such indices occur across nested dyadic scales with controlled losses.

### Step C: Summation Upgrade
Sum contributions from Step B to force superlinear power growth of partial sums.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a quantitative lower-density theorem for large \(M_n\)-events:
\[
|\{k\le N: M_k\ge N^\gamma\}|\ge N^\theta
\]
for fixed \(\gamma,\theta>0\) with \(\gamma+\theta>1\).
