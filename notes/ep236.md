\(f(n)=\#\{(p,k): n=p+2^k\}\), including whether \(f(n)=o(\log n)\).

### Step A: Mean-value baseline
Estimate average and second moment of \(f(n)\) over \(n\le X\).

### Step B: Large-values control
Use sieve/exponential-sum methods to bound frequency of \(n\) with large \(f(n)\).

### Step C: Maximal-order inference
Convert tail bounds into upper bounds for \(\max_{n\le X} f(n)\).

powers-of-two offsets.

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition CP1 (Second-moment implication)
If
\[
\sum_{n\le X} f(n)^2 \le A(X),
\]
then
\[
\max_{n\le X} f(n)\le \sqrt{A(X)}.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Maximum squared is bounded by sum of squares.
\(\square\)

### Proposition CP2 (Reduction)
If one proves \(A(X)=o(X\log^2 X)\) together with sharp first-moment asymptotics,
then one gets nontrivial improvements toward \(f(n)=o(\log n)\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Apply CP1 plus averaging normalization.
\(\square\)
