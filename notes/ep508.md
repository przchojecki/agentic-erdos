## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine exact value of \(\chi(\mathbb R^2)\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a finite-forcing compactness lemma at threshold \(k\): if every sufficiently large bounded region contains a finite unit-distance subgraph with chromatic number \(>k\), then \(\chi(\mathbb R^2)>k\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (finite-subgraph lower bound)
If a finite unit-distance graph \(G\) embeds in \(\mathbb R^2\), then \(\chi(\mathbb R^2)\ge \chi(G)\).

**Proof.**
Any coloring of plane restricts to a coloring of \(G\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (upper-bound coloring transfer)
A measurable/proper coloring of the plane with \(k\) colors yields \(\chi(\mathbb R^2)\le k\).

**Proof.**
Definition of chromatic number for unit-distance graph on \(\mathbb R^2\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A bridges finite to global)
If A supplies finite witnesses above \(k\) in growing regions, compactness (or direct embedding) yields global lower bound \(>k\).

**Proof.**
Apply B1 to each witness; one witness suffices for strict lower bound. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is constructing/controlling finite unit-distance witnesses strong enough to force \(\chi\ge6\), or a matching 5-coloring proof.
