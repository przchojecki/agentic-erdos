# EP-420 partial attempt

## Route
Computed finite profiles of
`F(f,n)=tau((n+floor(f(n)))!)/tau(n!)`
for `f=(log n)^C` using logarithmic divisor-count ratios.

## Evidence from this batch
- `data/ep420_factorial_tau_ratio_scan.json`:
  - for sampled `n`, `log F((log n)^1,n)` fluctuates near small positive values;
  - higher exponents (`C=2,3`) give very large growth in the sampled range.

## What is resolved from background
- `liminf F(c log n,n)=1` for any fixed `c>0`.
- `F(n^{4/9},n)->infinity` is known.

## Hard point
The open regime is exactly around logarithmic scales where `F` can be very close to `1` infinitely often, making monotone-growth proofs delicate.

## Status
Partially resolved with strong known results; logarithmic-scale dense-value questions remain open.
