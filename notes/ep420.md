# EP-420 partial attempt

## Route
Computed finite profiles of
`F(f,n)=tau((n+floor(f(n)))!)/tau(n!)`
for `f=(log n)^C` using logarithmic divisor-count ratios.

## Evidence from this batch
- `data/ep420_factorial_tau_ratio_scan.json`:
  - for sampled `n`, `log F((log n)^1,n)` fluctuates near small positive values;
  - higher exponents (`C=2,3`) give very large growth in the sampled range.

## What is resolved from background
- `liminf F(c log n,n)=1` for any fixed `c>0`.
- `F(n^{4/9},n)->infinity` is known.

## Hard point
The open regime is exactly around logarithmic scales where `F` can be very close to `1` infinitely often, making monotone-growth proofs delicate.

## Status
Partially resolved with strong known results; logarithmic-scale dense-value questions remain open.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Resolve logarithmic-scale behavior for
\[
F(f,n)=\frac{\tau((n+\lfloor f(n)\rfloor)!)}{\tau(n!)}
\]
when \(f(n)\) is near \((\log n)^C\), including dense-value / liminf questions.

### Step A: Prime-Sum Expansion at Log Scale
Derive precise asymptotic expansion for \(\log F(f,n)\) in terms of weighted
prime counts over \((n,n+f(n)]\), with error uniform for \(f(n)\le (\log n)^C\).

### Step B: Oscillation Control
Show expansion has both positive and near-zero phases infinitely often (or
exclude one of them), via distribution of prime powers around \(n\).

### Step C: Dense-Value/Liminf Resolution
Translate Step B to final statements on whether \(F(f,n)\) is frequently near 1
or forced away from 1 for given \(C\).

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a uniform short-window divisor-valuation theorem controlling
\[
\sum_{p^\nu}\left(v_p((n+\lfloor f(n)\rfloor)!)-v_p(n!)\right)\log\!\left(1+\frac1{\nu}\right)
\]
at logarithmic \(f(n)\)-scales with power-saving error.
