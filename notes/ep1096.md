# EP-1096 partial attempt

## Route
Computed finite subset-sum spectra
`{sum_{i=0}^M c_i q^i : c_i in {0,1}}`
for several `q in (1,2)` and measured tail gap statistics.

## Evidence from this batch
- `data/ep1096_q_subset_gap_scan.json` (`M=18`) shows very small average tail gaps in the last quartile of sorted values, though occasional large (`~1`) gaps persist in finite truncations.

## What is resolved from background
- Pisot numbers are excluded from the target vanishing-gap property.
- Multiple equivalent/related gap criteria are known for Pisot vs non-Pisot behavior.

## Hard point
Finite truncations do not decide asymptotic behavior of the full infinite ordered set.

## Status
Heuristic finite evidence only; threshold behavior remains open.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
For \(q\in(1,2)\), analyze gap behavior in ordered sums
\[
\Sigma_q:=\left\{\sum_{i\ge0} c_i q^i:\ c_i\in\{0,1\},\ \text{finite support}\right\},
\]
especially vanishing-gap versus persistent-gap regimes.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Establish a dichotomy by algebraic type of \(q\): non-Pisot \(q\) implies arbitrarily small normalized tail gaps in \(\Sigma_q\), while Pisot \(q\) implies a uniform positive gap floor.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (finite-level approximation)
Let \(\Sigma_q^{(M)}\) be sums up to \(q^M\). Then \(\Sigma_q^{(M)}\subset\Sigma_q\) and sorted-gap statistics on \(\Sigma_q^{(M)}\) approximate lower-tail behavior of \(\Sigma_q\) as \(M\to\infty\).

**Proof.**
Nestedness is immediate. Every infinite set element is limit of truncations; thus lower-tail gap properties are probed by large \(M\) levels. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (Pisot rigidity direction)
If \(q\) is Pisot, then fractional parts of \(q^m\) tend to 0 exponentially, inducing discrete separation patterns in \(\Sigma_q\).

**Proof.**
Pisot property gives exponential decay of conjugate contributions, yielding near-integer behavior and hence rigid spacing constraints for digit sums. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (non-Pisot density transfer)
If additive semigroup images modulo 1 are dense with quantitative discrepancy decay, then sorted gaps in \(\Sigma_q\) must have liminf \(0\).

**Proof.**
Quantitative density modulo 1 forces arbitrarily close projected sums; lifting back to ordered real sums gives arbitrarily small gaps. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The hardest ingredient is a uniform quantitative non-Pisot equidistribution theorem strong enough to turn qualitative density into explicit gap decay rates.
