# EP-817

## Problem Statement
For $k\ge 3$, let $g_k(n)$ be the smallest $N$ such that there exists $A\subseteq [1,N]$ with $|A|=n$ and the subset-sum set
$$\Sigma(A):=\left\{\sum_{a\in S} a: S\subseteq A\right\}$$
contains no nontrivial $k$-term arithmetic progression. In particular, for $k=3$, is it true that
$$g_3(n)\gg 3^n?$$

## Literature
- Erdos-Sarkozy established a near-target lower bound of the shape
  $$g_3(n)\gg \frac{3^n}{n^{O(1)}}.$$
- The remaining gap is removal of the polynomial loss in $n$.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented a deeper randomized-greedy finite search:
  - pick $A\subseteq [1,N]$;
  - compute all subset sums exactly;
  - reject if a nontrivial 3-term AP appears in $\Sigma(A)$.
- This yields finite constructive lower bounds for achievable $|A|$ at selected $N$.
- No asymptotic proof was obtained.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Best found sizes were $|A|=5$ for $N=80,120,160$ and $|A|=6$ for $N=220,300$.
- The finite search confirms feasibility of sparse AP-free subset-sum behavior, but at scales far below asymptotic regime.
