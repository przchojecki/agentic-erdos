# EP-301 partial resolution

## Statement
Let `f(N)` be the maximum size of `A subseteq {1,...,N}` with no distinct
`a,b1,...,bk in A` satisfying

`1/a = 1/b1 + ... + 1/bk`.

In particular, is `f(N)=(1/2+o(1))N`?

## What is resolved
Background records

`f(N) >= (1/2+o(1))N`

from the interval example, and

`f(N) <= (25/28+o(1))N`

from an elementary disjoint-configuration argument.

## What remains open in this note
The exact asymptotic constant is not determined by these bounds.

## Attempt in this batch
Rechecked whether the known `25/28` upper-bound mechanism can be tightened by
local forbidden-configuration amplification; no improvement was obtained.

## Status
- two-sided density bounds: known.
- exact asymptotic constant: unresolved.

## New deep computation (2026-03-10)

Script: `scripts/ep301.mjs`  
Method: exact branch-and-bound on a hypergraph of forbidden configurations, for
fixed-\(k\) relaxations.

### Finite exact relaxation for fixed \(k\)
The original EP-301 condition forbids
\[
\frac1a=\frac1{b_1}+\cdots+\frac1{b_k}
\]
for some (unspecified) \(k\ge 2\). As a relaxation, we computed exact maxima when
only one fixed equation-size is forbidden:

1. \(k=2\) only (\(1/a=1/b+1/c\), distinct):
exact values for \(N=20,\dots,50\) gave densities
\[
f_{2}(N)/N \in [0.86,0.90].
\]

2. \(k=3\) only (\(1/a=1/b+1/c+1/d\), distinct):
exact values for \(N=16,\dots,36\) gave densities
\[
f_{3}(N)/N \in [0.8333,0.875].
\]

These are rigorous exact finite-\(N\) optima for the fixed-\(k\) models.

## Interpretation
The fixed-\(k\) relaxations are much less restrictive than full EP-301: very
dense avoiding sets exist in tested ranges. So the nontrivial pressure toward the
known \(1/2\)-type regime must come from simultaneously forbidding all lengths
\(k\), not from any single small \(k\) alone.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine the exact asymptotic constant in
\[
f(N)=cN+o(N),
\]
in particular whether \(c=1/2\).

### Step A: Multi-\(k\) forbidden-configuration coupling
Quantify how simultaneously forbidding all reciprocal-sum identities forces
global sparsity beyond any fixed-\(k\) restriction.

### Step B: Density-increment contradiction
Assume \(A\subset[1,N]\) has density \(>1/2+\delta\) and derive a reciprocal
identity by combinatorial/energy amplification.

### Step C: Matching lower constructions
Show interval-type constructions at density \(1/2\) are asymptotically optimal.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a uniform decomposition theorem: dense \(A\) must contain a structured
subset generating one of the forbidden reciprocal identities with unrestricted
length \(k\).

## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Monotonicity of \(f\))
\[
f(N+1)\ge f(N).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Any forbidden-identity-free subset of \([1,N]\) is also such a subset of
\([1,N+1]\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Fixed-\(k\) relaxation dominance)
Let \(f_k(N)\) be maximum size avoiding only identities of length exactly \(k\).
Then
\[
f(N)\le f_k(N)\quad\text{for every }k\ge2.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Avoiding all lengths implies avoiding each fixed length.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Density upper-bound reduction)
If for some \(\delta>0\), every \(A\subset[1,N]\) with \(|A|>(1/2+\delta)N\)
contains at least one forbidden identity (some \(k\)), then
\[
f(N)\le \left(\frac12+\delta\right)N.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Contrapositive: if \(|A|>(1/2+\delta)N\) and \(A\) were valid, it would violate
the hypothesis.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 4 (Constant extraction)
If Lemma 3 holds for every fixed \(\delta>0\) and lower bound
\[
f(N)\ge \left(\frac12-o(1)\right)N
\]
is known, then \(f(N)=(1/2+o(1))N\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Upper bound from Lemma 3 gives \(f(N)\le (1/2+\delta)N\) for all \(\delta>0\),
hence \(f(N)\le (1/2+o(1))N\). Combine with lower bound.
\(\square\)
