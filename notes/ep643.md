# EP-643

## Problem Statement
For \(t\)-uniform hypergraphs on \(n\) vertices, let \(f(n;t)\) be the threshold forcing four edges \(A,B,C,D\) with
\[
A\cup B=C\cup D,\qquad A\cap B=C\cap D=\emptyset.
\]
Estimate \(f(n;t)\); in particular, is
\[
f(n;t)=(1+o(1))\binom{n}{t-1}
\]
true for \(t\ge 3\)?

## Literature
- For \(t=2\), this is the \(C_4\)-free graph threshold scale \(\asymp n^{3/2}\).
- For \(t=3\), known upper bounds are constant-factor multiples of \(\binom n2\).
- For general \(t\ge 3\), only broad constant-factor windows are known.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented deeper greedy construction search for \(t=3\), explicitly avoiding disjoint-pair union collisions.
- Extended finite probes up to \(n=24\) with stronger restart counts.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Best normalized lower-bound ratios \(\text{best}/\binom n2\):
  - \(n=12\): \(0.636\),
  - \(15\): \(0.610\),
  - \(18\): \(0.608\),
  - \(21\): \(0.610\),
  - \(24\): \(0.587\).
- Finite behavior sits well below current \(t=3\) upper constants, leaving a significant constant-factor gap.
