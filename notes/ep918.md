# EP-918

## Problem Statement
Is there a graph with $\aleph_2$ vertices and chromatic number $\aleph_2$ such that every subgraph on $\aleph_1$ vertices has chromatic number $\leq\aleph_0$?
Is there a graph with $\aleph_{\omega+1}$ vertices and chromatic number $\aleph_1$ such that every subgraph on $\aleph_\omega$ vertices has chromatic number $\leq\aleph_0$?

## Literature
- Erdos--Hajnal proved strong precursor results: for each finite $k$, there is a graph of chromatic number $\aleph_1$ whose subgraphs on fewer than $\aleph_k$ vertices are countably colorable.
- The stronger cardinal-jump formulations above remain open in standard references used in this repo.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Used finite proxy searches for global/local chromatic separation:
  - look for finite graphs that are globally not $k$-colorable,
  - while all medium-size induced subgraphs are $\ell$-colorable.
- Added one exhaustive regime (`n=18`, `m=9`) checking all $\binom{18}{9}$ subgraphs per trial, and one larger random-sampling regime (`n=28`, `m=16`).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- No finite proxy witnesses were found in these deep runs.
- In particular, the exhaustive proxy on `n=18,m=9` found essentially no graphs forcing global chromatic jump beyond 4 under the tested densities, and none satisfying the full separation condition.
- This negative finite signal aligns with the intuition that strong global/local chromatic separation is delicate and structurally constrained.
