# EP-851

## Problem Statement
Given $\epsilon>0$, does there exist $r=r(\epsilon)$ such that integers of the form
$$2^k+n\quad\text{with }\Omega(n)\le r$$
have lower density at least $1-\epsilon$?

## Literature
- Romanoff proved positive lower density for the prime case ($r=1$), i.e. numbers of the form $2^k+p$.
- The near-full-density bounded-$r$ strengthening is open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Computed finite coverage profile up to $N=10^6$.
- For each $r\in\{1,2,3,4,5\}$, measured fraction of $m\le N$ representable as $m=2^k+n$ with $\Omega(n)\le r$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Empirical coverage densities:
  - $r=1$: $0.53897$
  - $r=2$: $0.887019$
  - $r=3$: $0.990158$
  - $r=4$: $0.999831$
  - $r=5$: $0.999998$
- Finite data strongly suggests rapid approach toward density 1 as $r$ increases.
