# EP-1110

## Problem Statement
Fix coprime integers $p>q\ge 2$.
A number $n$ is representable if it can be written as a sum of terms $p^a q^b$ such that no chosen term divides another.
For $\{p,q\}\ne\{2,3\}$, study density of non-representable numbers and existence of infinitely many coprime non-representables.

## Literature
- It is known that finite non-representable set occurs iff $\{p,q\}=\{2,3\}$.
- Modern partial results establish broad regimes with density-zero or infinite coprime non-representables.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Replaced stub with standalone antichain-sum enumeration (`scripts/ep1110.mjs`).
- Terms are represented by exponent pairs $(a,b)$; non-divisibility is antichain condition in product order.
- Enumerated representable sums up to cutoff $N$ exactly per tested pair (all runs here finished, no timeouts).

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
For coprime \(p>q\ge2\), classify density and coprime-subset behavior of non-representables under antichain sums of \(p^a q^b\), separating exceptional \(\{2,3\}\) from generic pairs.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove an antichain-sum sparsity theorem: for \(\{p,q\}\neq\{2,3\}\), representable numbers up to \(N\) are at most \(N^{1-\delta_{p,q}}\) (or explicit zero-density upper bound with power saving).

This would immediately imply density 1 for non-representables and sharpen quantitative claims.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (poset reformulation)
Allowed summand sets correspond to antichains in \(\mathbb N^2\) under coordinatewise order via \((a,b)\mapsto p^a q^b\).

**Proof.**
\(p^{a_1}q^{b_1}\mid p^{a_2}q^{b_2}\) iff \(a_1\le a_2\) and \(b_1\le b_2\). Non-divisibility among chosen terms is exactly antichain condition. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (counting via rank layers)
Number of candidate antichain terms with value \(\le N\) is \(O((\log N)^2)\), and any antichain has size \(O(\log N)\).

**Proof.**
Bounds on \(a,b\) are \(a\le \log_p N\), \(b\le \log_q N\). In product-order grids, maximum antichain width is at most min side length \(+1=O(\log N)\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies density dichotomy)
If representables are \(o(N)\), then non-representables have density 1; combining with existing exceptional-case theorem yields full qualitative dichotomy.

**Proof.**
Non-representable count is \(N-\#\text{repr}(N)\). If \(\#\text{repr}(N)=o(N)\), quotient tends to 1. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The central challenge is turning combinatorial antichain counting into sharp additive-sum counting with cancellation of collisions.
