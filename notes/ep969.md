# EP-969

## Problem Statement
Let $Q(x)$ be the number of squarefree integers in $[1,x]$.
Write
$$
Q(x)=\frac{6}{\pi^2}x+E(x).
$$
Determine the true order of magnitude of $E(x)$.

## Literature
- Classical bounds and refinements place $E(x)$ well below $x^{1/2}$, but the expected true order is much smaller and unresolved.
- Even under RH, the exact order remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone deep scan up to $X=30{,}000{,}000$ using a linear Mobius sieve.
- Computed exact $Q(x)$ and tracked $E(x)$, running maxima of $|E(x)|$, and scale-normalized diagnostics at large checkpoints.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Maximum observed $|E(x)|$ up to $3\times 10^7$ was about $55.24$ (at $x=17{,}199{,}380$).
- Checkpoint exponent proxy
$$
\frac{\log(\max_{t\le x}|E(t)|)}{\log x}
$$
stayed around $0.22$-$0.24$.
- Finite data remains consistent with very slow growth relative to $x^{1/4}$ on this range, though this does not imply asymptotic order.
