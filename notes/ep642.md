# EP-642

## Problem Statement
Let \(f(n)\) be the maximal number of edges in an \(n\)-vertex graph such that every cycle has more vertices than diagonals (chords).
Is \(f(n)\ll n\)?

## Literature
- Chen, Erd\H{o}s, Staton proved \(f(n)\ll n^{3/2}\).
- Dragani\'c, Methuku, Munh\'a Correia, Sudakov (2024) improved this to
  \[
  f(n)\ll n(\log n)^8.
  \]
- Linear growth remains the core open direction.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented deeper standalone greedy extremal search with exact cycle-violation checking at each edge insertion.
- Increased search range from tiny batch probes to \(n=8\) through \(12\) with larger restart budgets.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Best edges found: \(15,17,20,21,23\) for \(n=8,9,10,11,12\).
- Ratio \(e/n\) stays near \(1.9\) to \(2.0\) in this range.
- Finite data is strongly consistent with near-linear scaling, but too small to distinguish \(n\), \(n\log n\), or other slowly varying factors.

## Status
- Open.
- Current evidence is lower-bound/profiling only; no asymptotic proof.
