# EP-1029

## Problem Statement
Study whether
\[
\frac{R(k,k)}{k2^{k/2}}\to\infty
\]
as $k\to\infty$.

## Literature
- Classical probabilistic lower bounds already give
\[
R(k,k)\gg k2^{k/2}
\]
up to absolute constants.
- The open point is superconstant gain over this normalization.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Kept the first-moment baseline derivation in the note.
- Added a deeper standalone constructive search:
  - random 2-colorings,
  - exact monochromatic clique detection in both colors,
  - finite lower bounds for $R(k,k)$ at $k=4,5,6$ with large trial budgets.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- This run found constructive bounds
\[
R(4,4)\ge 12,\quad R(5,5)\ge 20,\quad R(6,6)\ge 31
\]
(from explicit colorings).
- Corresponding normalized finite ratios stayed bounded (about $0.65$-$0.75$ here), consistent with the fact that finite experiments alone cannot see asymptotic divergence.

## Status
- Open.
- Added deep standalone exact-clique constructive lower-bound scan (runtime-heavy pass).

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove
\[
\frac{R(k,k)}{k2^{k/2}}\to\infty.
\]

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Establish a superconstant multiplicative improvement to probabilistic lower bounds:
\[
R(k,k)\ge k2^{k/2}\,\omega(k),\qquad \omega(k)\to\infty.
\]

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (random-coloring baseline)
First-moment method yields \(R(k,k)\gg k2^{k/2}\).

**Proof.**
Compute expected monochromatic \(K_k\) count in random 2-coloring; choose \(n\) where expectation \(<1\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (normalization monotonicity)
If \(R(k,k)\ge c_k k2^{k/2}\), then divergence of \(c_k\) is equivalent to EP-1029 statement.

**Proof.**
Divide both sides by \(k2^{k/2}\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies target)
Lemma A gives \(c_k=\omega(k)\to\infty\), hence normalized ratio diverges.

**Proof.**
Immediate from B2. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The missing ingredient is a mechanism beyond first moment (e.g. refined nibble/dependency control) that accumulates superconstant gain in the diagonal Ramsey lower bound.
