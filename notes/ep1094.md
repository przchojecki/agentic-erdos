# EP-1094

## Problem Statement
Conjecture: for all $n\ge 2k$, the least prime factor of
\[
\binom{n}{k}
\]
is at most
\[
\max\left(\frac{n}{k},k\right),
\]
with only finitely many exceptions.

## Literature
- Erdős-Lacampagne-Selfridge list 14 specific exceptions and suggest stronger variants.
- The finite-exception formulation remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Replaced batch stub with standalone exhaustive scan (`scripts/ep1094.mjs`).
- For each pair $(n,k)$ with $4\le n\le N_{\max}$ and $2\le k\le n/2$, computed exact least prime factor of $\binom{n}{k}$ via p-adic valuations.
- Compared all detected exceptions against the known 14-term exception set.

## Computation-Guided Observations
Deep run (`DEPTH=4`) with
\[
N_{\max}=2600
\]
checked all eligible pairs and found:
- exception count = 14,
- no missing known exceptions in range,
- no new exceptions beyond the known 14 in range,
- probe counts remain at 14 all the way to $n=2600$.

This is strong finite evidence for the finite-exception conjecture, but not a proof beyond scanned range.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove that
\[
\operatorname{lpf}\!\binom{n}{k}\le \max\!\left(\frac nk,k\right)
\]
for all \(n\ge 2k\), with only finitely many exceptions.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Show that any exception must satisfy a simultaneous digital-carry obstruction in all primes \(p\le \max(n/k,k)\), forcing \((n,k)\) into finitely many explicit parametric families.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (least-prime-factor criterion)
\(\operatorname{lpf}\binom{n}{k}>B\) iff \(p\nmid\binom nk\) for every prime \(p\le B\).

**Proof.**
By definition, least prime factor exceeds \(B\) exactly when no prime \(\le B\) divides the number. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (Lucas/Kummer divisibility test)
For each prime \(p\), divisibility \(p\mid\binom nk\) is decidable by base-\(p\) digits (Lucas), equivalently by positive carry count (Kummer).

**Proof.**
Lucas gives \(\binom nk\not\equiv0\pmod p\) iff each base-\(p\) digit of \(k\) is \(\le\) corresponding digit of \(n\). Kummer gives the carry formulation. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies finite-exception theorem)
If Lemma A reduces exceptions to finitely many families and each family is finite effectively, then only finitely many exceptions exist.

**Proof.**
Finite union of finite sets is finite. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The key challenge is proving that simultaneous nondivisibility by all small primes cannot persist outside boundedly many structured carry configurations.
