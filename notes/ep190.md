## Evidence from this batch

- script: `scripts/ep190_k3_coloring_exact.mjs`
- data: `data/ep190_k3_coloring_exact.json`

Here one seeks colorings of `[1..N]` where every 3-term AP is neither
monochromatic nor rainbow (equivalently every 3-AP uses exactly 2 colors).

## Result
Exact search gives:
- avoider exists up to `N=8`,
- no avoider at `N=9`.

So for `k=3`, this certifies `H(3)=9`.

## Limit
This is a small-`k` base case only; the main question about growth of `H(k)` as
`k->infinity` remains open.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine growth of \(H(k)\), the least \(N\) such that every coloring of
\([1,N]\) has a \(k\)-term AP that is monochromatic or rainbow.

### Step A: Avoider construction bounds
Construct long colorings where every \(k\)-AP uses exactly two colors.

### Step B: Forcing threshold bounds
Prove upper bounds on maximal avoider length via AP hypergraph methods.

### Step C: Asymptotic matching
Pin down exponent/base of \(H(k)\).

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a sharp container theorem for colorings avoiding both monochromatic and
rainbow \(k\)-APs simultaneously.

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition BY1 (Monotonicity)
For fixed \(k\), if no avoider exists on \([1,N]\), then no avoider exists on
\([1,N']\) for \(N'\ge N\). Hence \(H(k)\) is well-defined.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Any avoider on \([1,N']\) would restrict to one on \([1,N]\).
\(\square\)

### Proposition BY2 (Growth reduction)
If one proves lower constructions of length \(L_k\) and forcing at length \(U_k\)
with \(L_k\le H(k)\le U_k\), then asymptotic growth of \(H(k)\) reduces to
matching \(L_k,U_k\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Direct sandwich argument.
\(\square\)
