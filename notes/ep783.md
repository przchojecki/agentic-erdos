# EP-783

## Problem Statement
Fix \(C>0\), large \(N\), and pairwise-coprime \(A\subseteq\{2,\dots,N\}\) with
\[
\mu(A):=\sum_{a\in A}\frac1a\le C.
\]
Minimize the density of integers \(m\le N\) not divisible by any \(a\in A\). Erd\H{o}s suggested the extremizer is a prime tail.

## Literature
- Weak asymptotic form is known (Tao 2026):
  \(\Sigma(N,C)\ge \rho(e^C)+o(1)\).
- Follow-up work gives asymptotic rigidity toward prime-tail structure.
- Exact finite-\(N\) extremal theorem remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Ran standalone finite optimization under pairwise-coprime + harmonic-budget constraints:
  - baseline: prime-tail construction,
  - competitor: local-search over prime-only pairwise-coprime families (multi-start),
  - objective: exact uncovered count on \([1,N]\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Tested \(N\in\{5000,10000,20000\}\), \(C\in\{0.6,1.0,1.4\}\).
- In most tested pairs, prime-tail had lower uncovered count than the local-search competitor; only small improvements over prime-tail appeared in 2 low-\(C\) cases.
- This supports prime-tail competitiveness in finite windows, while not proving optimality.

## Status
