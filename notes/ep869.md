# EP-869

## Problem Statement
If $A_1,A_2$ are disjoint additive bases of order $2$, must $A=A_1\cup A_2$ contain a minimal additive basis of order $2$?

## Literature
- Erdos-Nathanson problem.
- General additive bases without minimal subbases are known, but this disjoint-order-2 setup is more structured.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built finite-interval analog experiments:
  - sampled disjoint sets $A_1,A_2\subseteq[1..N]$,
  - required each to be a 2-basis on a target interval $[L,U]$,
  - checked whether union admits a finite minimal core on $[L,U]$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In tested cases, full coverage by each part and union occurred repeatedly.
- Finite minimal cores for the union were found in multiple trials (e.g. 6 and 10 instances in two tested regimes).
- This finite evidence is compatible with positive behavior, but does not settle the infinite question.
