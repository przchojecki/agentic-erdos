# EP-317 partial resolution

## Statement split
1. Existence of very small nonzero signed harmonic sums with magnitude
   `O(2^{-n})`.
2. Strict lower-bound version of the lcm barrier for nonzero sums.

## What is resolved

`2^{-n * (logloglog n)^{1+o(1)} / log n}`.

For (2), the non-strict lcm lower bound is immediate, and strict inequality is
known to fail for small `n`.

## What remains open in this note
Sharp order for (1) and eventual strict inequality behavior for (2) remain open
in the record.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine sharp asymptotics of the minimum nonzero signed harmonic sum magnitude,
and eventual strictness versus \(1/[1..n]\).

### Step A: Exact integer-lattice reduction
Scale by \(L_n=[1,\dots,n]\) to an integer subset-sum minimum problem.

### Step B: Lower-bound amplification
Prove anti-concentration for signed sums near \(0\) in that lattice.

### Step C: Strictness and asymptotic law
Show equality with \(1/L_n\) is finite-exceptional, and derive decay order.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need an arithmetic anti-concentration theorem for coefficients \(L_n/k\) near
zero that beats trivial gcd-based bounds.

## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Scaled minimum identity)
Let
\[
\mu_n:=\min\Bigl\{\Bigl|\sum_{k=1}^n \varepsilon_k\frac1k\Bigr|:\varepsilon_k\in\{-1,0,1\},\ \text{not all }0\Bigr\}.
\]
Then
\[
\mu_n=\frac{1}{L_n}\min\Bigl\{\Bigl|\sum_{k=1}^n \varepsilon_k\frac{L_n}{k}\Bigr|:\varepsilon_k\in\{-1,0,1\},\ \text{not all }0\Bigr\}.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Multiply each candidate sum by \(L_n\), an exact bijective scaling of values.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Baseline lower bound)
\[
\mu_n\ge \frac1{L_n}.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
By Lemma 1 the scaled minimum is a nonzero integer in absolute value, hence at
least \(1\), then divide by \(L_n\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Strictness criterion)
Equality \(\mu_n=1/L_n\) holds iff the scaled signed-sum set contains \(\pm1\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
From Lemma 1 and Lemma 2, minimum equals \(1/L_n\) exactly when integer minimum is
\(1\).
\(\square\)
