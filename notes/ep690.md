# EP-690

## Problem Statement
For fixed \(k\ge 1\), let \(d_k(p)\) be the density of integers whose \(k\)-th smallest prime factor equals \(p\).
Is \(d_k(p)\) unimodal in \(p\)?

## Literature
- Known results now separate regimes: unimodality for small \(k\), non-unimodality for many larger \(k\).
- The background cites concrete progress up to explicit ranges of \(k\).

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Ran exact prime-divisibility density recursion for many \(k\) values and first 300 primes.
- Counted strict local maxima in sampled \(p\)-profiles and recorded argmax locations.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Clear non-unimodality appears in moderate \(k\) ranges in sampled profiles.
- At larger \(k\), profiles become very flat/noisy at tiny scales; local-peak count can drop in finite sampling.
- Finite recursion profiles align with the modern negative trend for general \(k\), while preserving small-\(k\) unimodal behavior.

## Status
- Significant progress known; broad non-unimodality now established for many \(k\).
- Full all-\(k\) classification remains outside this computation.
