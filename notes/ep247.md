### Proposition CU1 (Periodic-tail criterion)
If a binary expansion is eventually periodic, the represented real number is
rational.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Eventually periodic base-\(2\) expansions correspond to rational geometric tail
sums.
\(\square\)

### Proposition CU2 (Reduction to approximation bounds)
If one proves that for infinitely many \(m\), every rational \(p/q\) with
\(q\le Q_m\) has
\[
\left|x-\frac pq\right|\ge Q_m^{-2-\eta},
\]
and this contradicts any algebraic-degree-\(D\) approximation bound pattern, then
transcendence follows.

\[
\textit{Proof sketch.}
\]
Combine lower approximation barrier with Roth/Subspace-type upper restrictions for
algebraic numbers.
\(\square\)
