# EP-460 partial attempt

## Route
Converted the greedy condition into a prime-support exclusion process on `b=n-a`, validated against naive construction for small `n`, and scanned up to `n=12000`.

## Evidence from this batch
- `data/ep460_greedy_sequence_scan.json`: observed reciprocal sums `sum_{0<a_i<n} 1/a_i` in roughly `2.2-3.2` range over scanned `n`, with maximum about `3.159` at `n=10343`.

## Hard point

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine asymptotic behavior of reciprocal sums from the greedy exclusion process in EP-460.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a prime-support sieve lemma: excluded elements \(b=n-a\) with \(P^-(b)\le a\) contribute a controlled reciprocal mass, while complement contributes dominant term with explicit asymptotic.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (change of variables)
Greedy condition in \(a\) is equivalent to exclusion condition on \(b=n-a\), turning reciprocal sum over \(a\) into weighted reciprocal sum over \(b\).

**Proof.**
Substitute \(a=n-b\) bijectively on relevant range. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (least-prime-factor split)
Partition by \(P^-(b)\le a\) vs \(P^-(b)>a\) gives disjoint decomposition of reciprocal mass.

**Proof.**
Every \(b\ge2\) has well-defined least prime factor; dichotomy is exhaustive and exclusive. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies asymptotic law)
If A gives asymptotics for both parts with error control, combine to obtain asymptotic for total reciprocal sum.

**Proof.**
Add decomposed contributions from B2 and propagate errors. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is precise control of reciprocal contribution from borderline \(b\) where least-prime-factor threshold is close to \(a\).
