# EP-860

## Problem Statement
Let $h(n)$ be such that for every $m\ge 1$, the interval $(m,m+h(n))$ contains distinct integers $a_i$ ($1\le i\le \pi(n)$) with
$$p_i\mid a_i,$$
where $p_i$ is the $i$-th prime. Estimate $h(n)$.

## Literature
- Erdos-Pomerance proved upper bounds of order roughly $n^{3/2}/\sqrt{\log n}$.
- Erdos-Selfridge gave linear lower bounds $(3-o(1))n$.
- Ruzsa proved $h(n)/n\to\infty$.
- The true growth remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented a deeper finite Hall-matching proxy:
  - for fixed $n$ and $m$, binary-searched the smallest interval length $h$ admitting a matching from primes $\le n$ to distinct multiples in $(m,m+h]$;
  - took worst case over $1\le m\le m_{\max}$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For tested finite ranges $(m\le 3000)$:
  - $n=30$: worst $h=46$,
  - $n=50$: worst $h=86$,
  - $n=80$: worst $h=94$,
  - $n=100$: worst $h=97$.
- Ratios $h/n$ are near-constant scale in these finite windows, but this does not determine asymptotic behavior.
