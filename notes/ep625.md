# EP-625

## Problem Statement
Let \(\zeta(G)\) be the cochromatic number of a graph \(G\), and \(\chi(G)\) its chromatic number.
For \(G\sim G(n,1/2)\), is it true that almost surely
\[
\chi(G)-\zeta(G)\to\infty\quad (n\to\infty)?
\]

## Literature
- Problem of Erd\H{o}s and Gimbel.
- Classical bounds give
  \[
  \frac{n}{2\log_2 n}\le \zeta(G)\le \chi(G)\le (1+o(1))\frac{n}{2\log_2 n}
  \]
  a.s.
- Recent results (Heckel, Steiner, 2024) show \(\chi(G)-\zeta(G)\) is not bounded with high probability and force polynomial-size growth along infinite subsequences.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Ran deeper standalone random-graph experiments with significantly larger sizes and restart budgets than earlier quick scans.
- Used independent greedy upper-bound heuristics for \(\chi\) and \(\zeta\) to profile finite-\(n\) gap behavior.
- This is numerical evidence only; it does not prove asymptotic divergence.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For \(n=120,180,240,320\), average observed heuristic gaps were about \(0.75\) to \(1.00\).
- Positive gaps appeared frequently (roughly \(60\%\) to \(86\%\) of trials).
- At \(n=180\), a rare negative heuristic gap occurred (\(-1\)), indicating finite-size/heuristic noise.
- Overall finite evidence is compatible with growth but not yet diagnostic of the true asymptotic scale.

## Next Proof Targets
- Replace heuristic \(\chi,\zeta\) surrogates by tighter algorithmic bounds to reduce false gap sign flips.
- Quantify concentration of \(\chi-\zeta\) in random graphs and compare to candidate growth laws.
