# EP-706

## Problem Statement
Let \(L(r)\) be the maximum chromatic number of a planar graph realizable by points in \(\mathbb R^2\) with edges at one of \(r\) allowed distances. Estimate \(L(r)\), in particular whether \(L(r)\le r^{O(1)}\).

## Literature
- Even the one-distance case \(r=1\) (Hadwiger-Nelson) is only bracketed between finite constants.
- General growth in \(r\) remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built standalone finite proxy experiments:
  - sample planar point sets,
  - choose \(r\) target distances,
  - connect near those distances,
  - compute greedy coloring upper bound and clique-based lower bound.
- This is heuristic finite evidence, not a theorem for \(L(r)\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Deep run: \(r=1\) to \(6\), 40 trials each, 160 points per trial.
- Mean greedy upper bounds increased with \(r\) (about 4.4, 5.7, 7.5, 9.0, 10.7, 12.2).
- Max observed upper bounds reached 15 at \(r=6\), suggesting clear growth in these finite models.
