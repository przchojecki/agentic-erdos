# EP-451 partial

## Statement
`n_k` is the smallest integer `>2k` such that
`prod_{1<=i<=k}(n_k-i)` has no prime factor in `(k,2k)`.
Estimate `n_k`.

## Attempt in this batch
I performed direct modular scans for `k<=80`, searching `n` up to `capFactor*k`
with `capFactor=1000`.

Data file:
- `data/ep451_nk_scan.json`

## Result
- Exact values found for many small/moderate `k`, often very large relative to `k`
  (e.g. `n_25=7653`, `n_32=30783`).
- In this finite cap, 49 values up to `k=80` remained unresolved (only lower bounds).

## Hard point
The search cap limits exact determination for larger `k`; asymptotic behavior of
`n_k` remains wide open.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Estimate growth of
\[
n_k:=\min\left\{n>2k:\ P\!\left(\prod_{i=1}^{k}(n-i)\right)\notin(k,2k)\right\}.
\]

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a prime-avoidance interval lemma: for each \(k\), among \(k\)-blocks \((n-k,\dots,n-1)\), the first block avoiding primes in \((k,2k)\) appears at \(n_k\) with asymptotic scale governed by a Poisson model of forbidden prime divisibility.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (forbidden-prime criterion)
Condition on \(n_k\) is equivalent to
\[
v_p\!\left(\prod_{i=1}^{k}(n-i)\right)=0\quad\forall p\in(k,2k).
\]

**Proof.**
No prime factor in interval \((k,2k)\) means each such prime has valuation zero. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (block divisibility events)
For fixed \(p\in(k,2k)\), event \(p\mid \prod_{i=1}^k(n-i)\) is equivalent to \(n\equiv r\pmod p\) for one of \(k\) residues.

**Proof.**
Product includes \(k\) consecutive residues; divisibility occurs when one term is \(0\mod p\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies \(n_k\) asymptotic)
If A gives accurate hazard rate for simultaneous avoidance of events in B2, expected first-hit location yields asymptotic for \(n_k\).

**Proof.**
First-hit in Bernoulli/Poisson process has inverse hazard-scale; apply with modeled avoidance probability. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The key obstacle is controlling dependence between many prime-divisibility events across overlapping residue conditions.
