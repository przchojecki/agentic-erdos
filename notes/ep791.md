# EP-791

## Problem Statement
Let \(g(n)\) be the minimum size of a set \(A\subseteq\{0,\dots,n\}\) such that
\[
\{0,\dots,n\}\subseteq A+A.
\]
Estimate \(g(n)\). In particular, is \(g(n)\sim 2\sqrt n\)?

## Literature
- The conjectural constant \(2\) is known to be false; best constants remain separated.
- Asymptotic order is open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Exact computation for small \(n\) via branch-and-bound feasibility by basis size.
- Greedy constructive upper bounds for larger \(n\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Exact rows \(n=10,15,20,24,28\): \(g(n)^2/n\) is roughly 2.4 to 2.89.
- Greedy rows \(n=40\) to 220 produce larger constants around 3.6 to 4.37.
- Finite profile matches the known gap between lower and constructive upper constants.
