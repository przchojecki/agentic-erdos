# EP-1017

## Problem Statement
For $K_4$-free graphs with
\[
e(G)\ge \left\lfloor\frac{n^2}{4}\right\rfloor+q,
\]
study guaranteed edge-disjoint triangle packing, i.e. lower bounds for
\[
\nu_3(G).
\]

## Literature
- In the $K_4$-free regime, strong positive results are known for the excess-over-Tur\'an formulation.
- The broad sharpened landscape beyond specific structural regimes remains active.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Kept explicit deterministic construction:
  - start from balanced $K_{a,b}$,
  - add $q$ matching edges inside one side,
  - build $q$ pairwise edge-disjoint triangles explicitly.
- Added standalone deterministic verification script over a large finite grid:
  - even $n\in[20,200]$,
  - many $q$ values per $n$,
  - exact structural checks recorded for each instance.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- All tested constructive instances satisfy the certified target
\[
\nu_3(G)\ge q.
\]
- The construction simultaneously keeps $K_4$-freeness and exact edge excess $q$ over $\lfloor n^2/4\rfloor$ by design.

## Status
- Strong constructive family validated at scale.
- General-form refinements beyond this family remain open.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Quantify optimal lower bounds for edge-disjoint triangle packing \(\nu_3(G)\) in \(K_4\)-free graphs with edge excess \(q\) above \(\lfloor n^2/4\rfloor\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a stability theorem: every near-extremal \(K_4\)-free graph with excess \(q\) is close to bipartite plus sparse internal edges, and each internal edge can be assigned to an edge-disjoint triangle except for \(o(q)\) losses.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (constructive lower family)
In the balanced bipartite-plus-matching construction, one gets \(\nu_3(G)\ge q\).

**Proof.**
Each added internal matching edge \(uu'\) forms a triangle with a distinct vertex on opposite side; matching ensures disjointness across triangles. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (edge-excess accounting)
\[
e(G)=\left\lfloor\frac{n^2}{4}\right\rfloor+q
\]
means exactly \(q\) edges beyond Turán bipartite baseline.

**Proof.**
Immediate by definition of excess parameter \(q\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies asymptotic sharpness)
If A yields \(\nu_3(G)\ge q-o(q)\) universally and construction gives \(\nu_3(G)\le q\) examples up to lower-order terms, then optimal guarantee is \(q(1-o(1))\).

**Proof.**
Combine universal lower with matching family upper behavior. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is handling adversarial internal-edge geometry where triangles compete for shared cross edges, beyond clean matching constructions.
