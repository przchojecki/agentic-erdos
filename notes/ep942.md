# EP-942

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

### Working statement
Let
\[
h(n)=\#\{m\ \text{powerful}:\ n^2\le m < (n+1)^2\}.
\]
Study growth and distribution of \(h(n)\).

### Deep standalone computation (`ep942.mjs`)
- Method: exact generation of powerful numbers via \(m=a^2b^3\) with squarefree \(b\), then two-pointer counting on intervals \([n^2,(n+1)^2)\).
- Run: \(N=50,000,000\) (thus \(m<(N+1)^2\)); runtime \(\approx 17.64\)s.
- Key output:
  - total powerful numbers below \((N+1)^2\): `108,460,774`
  - \(\max_{n\le N} h(n)=10\), first attained at \(n=524827\)
  - top frequencies:
    - \(h=2\): density `0.3959558`
    - \(h=1\): density `0.2771419`
    - \(h=3\): density `0.2306791`

### Interpretation
Compared with earlier smaller scans, the maximal observed value increased (from 8 to 10), giving stronger finite evidence for eventual growth in \(h(n)\), though still far from an asymptotic proof.
