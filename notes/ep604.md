# EP-604

## Problem Statement
Pinned-distance problem in the plane: determine the best lower bounds for the maximum number of distinct distances from a single point in an \(n\)-point set.

## Literature / Context
- The extremal obstruction scale is classically benchmarked by grid-like sets, near \(n/\sqrt{\log n}\).
- Random-like sets exhibit much larger pinned-distance counts, highlighting the structured-vs-random gap.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built a standalone deeper pinned-distance profile on:
  - square grids \(m\times m\),
  - random integer point sets of matching size.
- Measured both max pinned count and average pinned count per point.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Grid families remain around \(0.45n\) to \(0.49n\) for max pinned count in tested range.
- Random sets are near \(n\) (about \(0.99n\)).
- Normalized by \(n/\sqrt{\log n}\), grid values stay near \(\sim 1.09\), consistent with known obstruction scale.

## Next Proof Targets
- Convert grid-benchmark behavior into sharper universal lower-bound barriers.
- Investigate pseudogrid perturbations to test stability of the obstruction constant.
