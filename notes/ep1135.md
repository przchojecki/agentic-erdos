## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove global convergence for the accelerated Collatz map (EP-1135 formulation).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Show there is no divergent orbit and no nontrivial cycle by proving a uniform descent-on-average inequality on every sufficiently long block of iterates.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (minimal counterexample reduction)
If a nontrivial cycle or divergent orbit exists, there exists one with minimal odd part (or minimal height in an appropriate ordering).

**Proof.**
Take infimum in well-ordered positive integers among odd representatives encountered in the bad set; choose witness attaining minimal value. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (parity-vector encoding)
Any cycle of length \(m\) determines a parity vector and exact rational identity
\[
n = \frac{A}{2^m-3^s}
\]
with integers \(A,s\) derived from the parity pattern.

**Proof.**
Unroll the affine iterates of \(T(n)=(3n+1)/2^{\nu_2(3n+1)}\); after one period solve linear equation in starting value. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A rules out bad objects)
If Lemma A gives strict average descent over every long block, then cycles with average multiplier \(\ge1\) and divergent trajectories are impossible.

**Proof.**
Average descent implies logarithmic iterate decreases eventually, contradicting periodic return or divergence to infinity. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is proving a deterministic, all-orbit average-descent estimate strong enough to dominate exceptional parity patterns.
