# EP-1103

## Problem Statement
Find growth constraints for an infinite integer sequence $A$ such that every element of
\[
A+A
\]
is squarefree.

## Literature
- Known lower bounds (including modern improvements) force superlinear growth in index form.
- Known constructions are subexponential in index; polynomial growth is believed unlikely.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Replaced batch stub with standalone deep construction script (`scripts/ep1103.mjs`).
- Precomputed a non-squarefree bitset and ran greedy constructions at very large cutoff.
- Added multiple seeded greedy variants (not just one canonical run) to test construction sensitivity.

## Computation-Guided Observations
Deep run (`DEPTH=4`) at
\[
\text{MAX\_A}=20{,}000{,}000
\]
used 12 seeded variants plus canonical unseeded greedy run.

Compute scale:
- total compatibility checks across runs:
\[
509{,}796{,}375.
\]

Canonical unseeded greedy:
- terms up to cutoff: $609$,
- at $x=20{,}000{,}000$:
  \[
  \frac{|A\cap[1,x]|}{\log x}\approx 36.2258.
  \]

Seeded variants:
- found noticeable spread in term counts by seed,
- best among shown variants reached $634$ terms (seed $13$), exceeding canonical $609$.

This indicates finite construction quality is sensitive to initialization, and non-canonical seeded greedy can give denser examples in this range.
