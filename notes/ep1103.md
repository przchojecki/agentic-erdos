# EP-1103

## Problem Statement
Find growth constraints for an infinite integer sequence $A$ such that every element of
\[
A+A
\]
is squarefree.

## Literature
- Known lower bounds (including modern improvements) force superlinear growth in index form.
- Known constructions are subexponential in index; polynomial growth is believed unlikely.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Quantify minimal growth of infinite \(A\subset\mathbb N\) such that every element of \(A+A\) is squarefree.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a uniform local obstruction lemma: for every large modulus \(M\), the residue set \(A\bmod M\) must avoid a positive fraction of classes forced by square divisors, yielding
\[
|A\cap[1,x]| \le x^{1-\delta}
\]
for some \(\delta>0\), or matching near-optimal upper profile.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (square-divisor congruence obstruction)
If \(p^2\mid (a+a')\), then \((a,a')\) lies in a union of residue hyperplanes modulo \(p^2\).

**Proof.**
Condition \(a+a'\equiv0\pmod{p^2}\) is linear in residues, giving one hyperplane (and unions across primes). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (pair-avoidance to additive energy)
Avoiding all such hyperplanes for all pairs in \(A\) imposes strong constraints on additive energy \(E_+(A)\).

**Proof.**
Each forbidden congruence excludes many additive coincidences modulo \(p^2\); aggregating over primes suppresses structured high-energy configurations. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (energy bound to counting bound)
If \(E_+(A)\) satisfies a power-saving upper bound in intervals, then \(|A\cap[1,x]|\) obeys the corresponding growth restriction.

**Proof.**
Apply standard additive-combinatorial inequalities linking energy and set size in intervals (Balog-Szemeredi/Gowers-type transfer). \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is combining infinitely many square-modulus constraints into a single sharp global density/growth theorem.
