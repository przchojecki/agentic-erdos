# EP-652

## Problem Statement
For \(x_1,\dots,x_n\in\mathbb R^2\), let
\[
R(x_i)=\#\{\lvert x_j-x_i\rvert:j\ne i\},
\]
and order so that \(R(x_1)\le\cdots\le R(x_n)\).
Let \(\alpha_k\) be minimal such that for all large \(n\) there exists an \(n\)-point set with
\(R(x_k)<\alpha_k n^{1/2}\).
Is \(\alpha_k\to\infty\) as \(k\to\infty\)?

## Literature
- Classical facts: \(R(x_1)=1\) is possible, and \(R(x_2)\ll n^{1/2}\) is possible; also \(R(x_1)R(x_2)\gg n\).
- Elekes showed for fixed \(k\), one can keep \(R(x_k)\ll_k n^{1/2}\).
- Mathialagan gives bipartite distinct-distance lower bounds implying \(R(x_k)\gg (kn)^{1/2}\) for \(2\le k\le n^{1/3}\).

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Ran deeper ordered pinned-distance profiles on large grids (up to \(30\times 30\), \(n=900\)).
- Added random-point baselines (best-of-trials) for comparison.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Grid families keep small-order statistics near \(\sqrt n\)-scale; e.g. for \(n=900\):
  - \(R(x_2)/\sqrt n\approx 3.97\),
  - \(R(x_{64})/\sqrt n\approx 5.47\).
- Random sets have much larger values (near \(n\), so normalized by \(\sqrt n\) they are very large).
- Structured sets continue to be the relevant low-\(R(x_k)\) candidates.
