# EP-770

## Problem Statement
Define \(h(n)\) as the least \(H\ge 3\) such that
\[
2^n-1,\ 3^n-1,\dots,\ H^n-1
\]
are pairwise coprime. Study distribution questions for \(h(n)\), including densities of \(h(n)=p\), behavior of \(\liminf h(n)\), and related prime-divisor criteria.

## Literature
- Known from background: \(h(n)=n+1\) iff \(n+1\) is prime, and \(h(n)\) is unbounded on odd \(n\).
- Main distribution/asymptotic questions remain open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented direct BigInt pairwise-gcd scan for \(n\le 160\), searching \(H\le 220\).
- Corrected scan to exclude vacuous \(H=2\) case.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Within this finite window, many \(n\) already have no witness \(H\le 220\) (87 unresolved), consistent with potentially large \(h(n)\).
- Among resolved sampled rows, \(h(n)=3\) appears frequently.
- Finite evidence supports strong variability and possible large growth, but scan bounds are still restrictive.
