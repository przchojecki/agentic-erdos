# EP-326 partial resolution

## Statement
If `A subset N` is an additive basis of order 2, must there be a subbasis
`B subseteq A` such that `lim b_k/k^2` does not exist?

## What is resolved
Background notes the stronger original form with `A=B` was disproved by Cassels.

## What remains open in this note
The weakened subbasis version in the current statement is not resolved by the
provided background.

## Status
- stronger `A=B` version: false.
- current subbasis version: unresolved.

## New Experiments
- 2026-03-05T14:35:43.865Z: enforced deep standalone subbasis-pruning search (RUNS=70, STEPS=120000, runtime 48.33s) on explicit order-2 bases up to N=180000.
- Finite signal: exact-coverage pruning found very rigid bases (almost no removable elements) but consistently large tail oscillation proxies for b_k/k^2 (amplitude about 0.245 to 0.248).
- Proof direction update: rigidity under exact 2-basis constraints suggests looking for a constructive perturbation family (not random deletions) that preserves coverage while forcing alternating growth blocks in b_k/k^2.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
For every additive basis \(A\) of order \(2\), find a subbasis \(B\subseteq A\)
with nonconvergent ratio \(b_k/k^2\).

### Step A: Controlled thinning with coverage preservation
Construct sparse deletions of \(A\) on long blocks while maintaining order-2 basis
property.

### Step B: Alternating-density blocks
Choose deletions so counting function of \(B\) alternates between two quadratic
regimes along subsequences.

### Step C: Ratio nonconvergence
Translate alternating counting regimes into failure of convergence of
\(b_k/k^2\).

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a block-replacement lemma: one can modify \(A\) on disjoint far-apart
intervals to prescribe local density behavior without breaking global 2-basis
coverage.

## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Subsequence criterion for nonconvergence)
If there exist subsequences \(k_j,\ell_j\to\infty\) such that
\[
\limsup_{j\to\infty}\frac{b_{k_j}}{k_j^2}>\liminf_{j\to\infty}\frac{b_{\ell_j}}{\ell_j^2},
\]
then \(\frac{b_k}{k^2}\) does not converge.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Convergent sequences have equal limsup and liminf.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Counting-function inversion)
Let \(B(x)=|B\cap[1,x]|\). If for two subsequences \(x_j,y_j\to\infty\),
\[
B(x_j)\sim \alpha\sqrt{x_j},\qquad B(y_j)\sim \beta\sqrt{y_j},\ \alpha\ne\beta,
\]
then \(b_k/k^2\) has distinct subsequential limits.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Inverse relation \(k=B(b_k)\) implies
\[
b_k\sim (k/\alpha)^2
\]
along the \(x_j\)-scale and similarly with \(\beta\). Hence distinct quadratic
constants.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction)
To prove EP-326, it suffices to construct a subbasis \(B\) whose counting function
has two different \(\sqrt{x}\)-asymptotic constants along subsequences.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Apply Lemma 2, then Lemma 1.
\(\square\)
