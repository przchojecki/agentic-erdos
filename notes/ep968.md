# EP-968 deeper attempt

## Statement
For `u_n=p_n/n`, is `{n : u_n<u_{n+1}}` positive density?

## Computation in this attempt
I computed sign frequencies of `u_{n+1}-u_n` for primes up to index about `10^6`
(using a sieve; stored file below).

Data file:
- `data/ep968_u_ratio_direction_counts.json`

Observed (prefix data):
- At `n=500000`: increase density `~0.402652`, decrease density `~0.597348`.
- Overall in this run (`n<=999999`): increase `~0.406140`, decrease `~0.593860`.

## Interpretation
Empirically both directions occur frequently in tested range, but this does not prove
positive density asymptotically for increases.

## Known theorem-level background
- `sum |u_{n+1}-u_n|` has order `(log x)^2`.
- Decrease set has positive density.

## Exact reformulation and proof lemmas

Let \(p_n\) be the \(n\)-th prime, \(g_n:=p_{n+1}-p_n\), and \(u_n:=p_n/n\).

### Lemma 1 (exact increment identity)
\[
u_{n+1}-u_n=\frac{n\,g_n-p_n}{n(n+1)}.
\]

Proof:
\[
\frac{p_{n+1}}{n+1}-\frac{p_n}{n}
=\frac{n(p_n+g_n)-(n+1)p_n}{n(n+1)}
=\frac{n g_n-p_n}{n(n+1)}.
\]

### Corollary 1 (sign criterion)
\[
u_{n+1}>u_n \iff g_n>\frac{p_n}{n}=u_n.
\]
Similarly,
\[
u_{n+1}<u_n \iff g_n<u_n.
\]

So the EP-968 set
\[
\{n:\,u_n<u_{n+1}\}
\]
is exactly
\[
\{n:\,g_n>u_n\}.
\]

### Heuristic bridge
Since \(u_n\sim \log n+\log\log n\) and typical prime gaps are also of logarithmic scale,
the sign is governed by the fluctuation of \(g_n\) around the local mean scale.
This explains why both signs appear with substantial frequency in computations,
while density questions remain subtle.

### Arithmetic progression obstruction (rational \(\alpha\))
If \(\alpha=a/b\in\mathbb Q\), then \(\{\alpha p_n\}\) lies in the finite set
\[
\left\{0,\frac1b,\dots,\frac{b-1}{b}\right\},
\]
so it cannot be equidistributed in \([0,1)\).

Thus irrationality assumptions in equidistribution variants are logically necessary; rational parameters are rigidly periodic modulo 1.
