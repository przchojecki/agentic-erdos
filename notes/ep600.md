# EP-600

## Problem Statement
Let \(e(n,r)\) be minimal such that every \(n\)-vertex graph with at least \(e(n,r)\) edges, each edge contained in a triangle, has an edge contained in at least \(r\) triangles. Ask whether
\[
e(n,r+1)-e(n,r)\to\infty,\qquad \frac{e(n,r+1)}{e(n,r)}\to 1.
\]

## Literature / Context
- Prior background includes \(e(n,r)=o(n^2)\) for fixed \(r\) (Ruzsa-Szemeredi framework).
- Sharp separation behavior between consecutive \(r\)-levels remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Replaced placeholder script with standalone deeper search using triangle systems:
  - enforce per-edge triangle multiplicity cap \(t_{\max}\),
  - every present edge belongs to at least one triangle by construction,
  - infer \(e(n,r)\ge m+1\) for \(r=t_{\max}+1\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For \(t_{\max}=1\) (\(r=2\)), lower bounds rise to:
  - \(e(18,2)\ge 139\),
  - \(e(22,2)\ge 208\),
  - \(e(26,2)\ge 292\).
- For \(t_{\max}=2\) (\(r=3\)), lower bounds rise to:
  - \(e(18,3)\ge 153\),
  - \(e(22,3)\ge 232\),
  - \(e(26,3)\ge 321\),
  - \(e(30,3)\ge 429\).

## Next Proof Targets

- Compare finite-gap growth \(e(n,3)-e(n,2)\) against explicit asymptotic lower-envelope candidates.
