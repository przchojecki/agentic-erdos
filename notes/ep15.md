## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Unconditionally prove convergence of
\[
\sum_{n\ge1}(-1)^n\frac{n}{p_n}.
\]

### Step A: Precise Expansion for \(n/p_n\)
Use PNT with explicit remainder to write
\[
\frac{n}{p_n}=\frac{1}{\log n}\Big(1+\frac{\log\log n-1}{\log n}+O(\rho(n))\Big),
\]
with \(\rho(n)\) summably small after alternation.

### Step B: Alternating Summation Transfer
Apply Abel/Dirichlet summation to convert Step A into convergence of the
alternating series by controlling first differences of \(n/p_n\).

### Step C: Error Closure
Show remaining oscillatory error terms are absolutely summable after one
summation-by-parts layer.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a sharp monotonicity/variation bound:
\[
\sum_{n\ge N}\left|\Delta\!\left(\frac{n}{p_n}\right)\right|<\infty
\]
with explicit asymptotic control compatible with unconditional prime-error bounds.
