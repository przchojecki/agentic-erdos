# EP-696

## Problem Statement
For \(n\), define \(h(n)\) as the maximum length of a prime chain
\[
p_1,\dots,p_t\mid n,\qquad p_{i+1}\equiv 1\pmod{p_i},
\]
and \(H(n)\) analogously allowing integer divisors instead of only prime divisors.
Study normal-order growth of \(h(n),H(n)\), and whether
\[
\frac{H(n)}{h(n)}\to\infty
\]
for almost all \(n\).

## Literature
- Background results establish \(h(n)\to\infty\) for almost all \(n\).
- Sharp asymptotics and the ratio-divergence question remain unresolved.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Computed exact \(h(n)\) and \(H(n)\) for all \(2\le n\le N\) by explicit DAG longest-path calculations:
  - prime-divisor DAG for \(h(n)\),
  - full-divisor DAG for \(H(n)\).
- Used this to measure empirical mean growth and frequency of strict separation \(H(n)>h(n)\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Deep run to \(N=120000\).
- At \(n\le 120000\):
  - mean \(h\approx 1.8174\), mean \(H\approx 2.2183\),
  - fraction with \(H(n)>h(n)\) about \(0.2699\).
- Best observed gap in this range: \(n=27720\) with \((h,H)=(3,12)\), ratio \(H/h=4\).
