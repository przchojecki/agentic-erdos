# EP-478 deep attempt with standalone computation

## Statement
For prime `p`, with `A_p={k! \bmod p:1\le k<p}`, is
`|A_p| \sim (1-1/e)p`?

## What is resolved from background
- Lower bounds are far below linear scale.
- `|A_p|=p-2` appears extremely rare in data and is open in general.

## Attempt route
Extended direct computation of `|A_p|` on larger samples and exhaustive small
prime scans.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove asymptotic
\[
|A_p|\sim (1-1/e)p,\qquad A_p=\{k!\bmod p:1\le k<p\}.
\]

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Show factorial residues behave like near-random occupancy among \(p\) classes after appropriate mixing window, with collision count matching Poisson occupancy and yielding \(1-1/e\) uncovered proportion.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (incremental walk form)
Sequence \(x_k=k!\bmod p\) satisfies
\[
x_{k+1}\equiv (k+1)x_k\pmod p.
\]

**Proof.**
Immediate from factorial recurrence. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (occupancy identity)
\[
|A_p|=p-\#\{\text{residue classes not hit by }x_k\}.
\]

**Proof.**
Definition of image set size as covered classes. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies \(1-1/e\) constant)
If A gives Poisson-like occupancy with mean 1 per class, uncovered fraction tends to \(e^{-1}\), hence \(|A_p|/p\to1-1/e\).

**Proof.**
Standard occupancy limit: \(\mathbb P(\text{unhit})\to e^{-1}\). \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The key obstacle is proving sufficient pseudorandomness/mixing for multiplicative factorial walk modulo primes.
