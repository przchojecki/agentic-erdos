# EP-478 deep attempt with standalone computation

## Statement
For prime `p`, with `A_p={k! \bmod p:1\le k<p}`, is
`|A_p| \sim (1-1/e)p`?

## What is resolved from background
- Lower bounds are far below linear scale.
- `|A_p|=p-2` appears extremely rare in data and is open in general.

## Attempt route
Extended direct computation of `|A_p|` on larger samples and exhaustive small
prime scans.

## New Experiments
- 2026-03-12T10:28:19.256Z: standalone deeper profile (`scripts/ep478.mjs`)
  including sample primes up to `1,000,003` and exhaustive scan up to `50000`.

## One-by-One Cycle
- Replaced batch-derived placeholder with standalone enlarged runs.
- Sampled large primes remain tightly near `(1-1/e)p`.
- In scan up to `50000`, exactly one prime had `|A_p|=p-2`, reinforcing rarity
  of maximal-size behavior.

## Status
- strong finite support for `(1-1/e)` scale and maximal-size rarity: present.
- asymptotic proof remains open.
