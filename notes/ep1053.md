# EP-1053

## Problem Statement
Study possible integer multipliers $k$ in
\[
\sigma(n)=k n
\]
and asymptotic behavior of large multipliers.

## Literature
- Large-multiplier asymptotics remain subtle; finite scans mainly provide rarity profiles.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone divisor-sum sieve profile up to
\[
N=10{,}000{,}000.
\]
- Tracked running maximum multiplier, counts of $k\ge3$, and sample values by multiplier class.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In this range, largest observed multiplier remains $k=4$ (first attained at $n=30240$).
- Only a small number of $k\ge3$ cases appear up to $10^7$.
- Finite data supports rarity of large multipliers in this range.

## Status
- Asymptotic claims remain open.
- Added deeper standalone multiplier profile with larger exact scan range.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Understand possible multipliers \(k\) in
\[
\sigma(n)=kn
\]
and asymptotic behavior of large \(k\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a density-decay theorem for abundancy values:
\[
\#\{n\le x:\sigma(n)/n\ge K\}\le x\,\Psi_K(x),
\]
with \(\Psi_K(x)\to0\) rapidly as \(K\to\infty\) uniformly in \(x\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (abundancy factorization)
\[
\frac{\sigma(n)}{n}=\prod_{p^a\parallel n}\frac{p^{a+1}-1}{p^a(p-1)}
 =\prod_{p^a\parallel n}\left(1+\frac1p+\cdots+\frac1{p^a}\right).
\]

**Proof.**
Use multiplicativity and prime-power divisor-sum formula. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (multiplier integrality filter)
If \(\sigma(n)=kn\), then \(\sigma(n)/n\in\mathbb Z\); hence large \(k\) requires highly structured cancellation among local factors in B1.

**Proof.**
Integrality is immediate; local factors are rational with varying denominators, so integer product is restrictive. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies rarity of large multipliers)
If A holds, counts with \(k\ge K\) are sparse for large \(K\), yielding asymptotic rarity of large multipliers.

**Proof.**
\(\{n:\sigma(n)=kn,\ k\ge K\}\subseteq\{n:\sigma(n)/n\ge K\}\), apply A. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is combining multiplicative probabilistic behavior of \(\sigma(n)/n\) with exact integrality constraints defining integer multipliers.
