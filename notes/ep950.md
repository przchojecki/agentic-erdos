# EP-950 partial resolution

## Statement
For f(n)=sum_{p<n}1/(n-p), ask about liminf, limsup, and growth bounds.

## What is resolved
Background provides substantial information:
- average and second moment: sum_{n<x} f(n) ~ x and sum_{n<x} f(n)^2 ~ x;
- certain short-interval prime lower bounds imply liminf f(n)>0;
- additional strong prime-distribution hypotheses would imply f(n)<<log log log n.

## What remains open in this note
Conjectured liminf=1, limsup=infinity, and universal f(n)=o(log log n) are not proved.

## Status
- robust moment-level partial results known.
- pointwise liminf/limsup and sharp growth questions unresolved.

## Rigorous consequences of the moment asymptotics
Assume the background asymptotics
\[
\sum_{n\le x} f(n)\sim x,\qquad
\sum_{n\le x} f(n)^2\sim x.
\]
Then:

1. Mean level:
\[
\frac1x\sum_{n\le x} f(n)\to 1.
\]

2. Mean-square level:
\[
\frac1x\sum_{n\le x} (f(n)-1)^2
=
\frac1x\sum f(n)^2 -2\frac1x\sum f(n)+1
\to 0.
\]
So \(f(n)\to 1\) in Cesàro \(L^2\)-sense.

3. Density concentration (Chebyshev):
for every \(\varepsilon>0\),
\[
\frac1x\#\{n\le x:\ |f(n)-1|\ge\varepsilon\}
\le
\frac{1}{\varepsilon^2}\cdot
\frac1x\sum_{n\le x}(f(n)-1)^2
\to 0.
\]

Hence almost all \(n\) (in natural-density sense) satisfy \(f(n)\in(1-\varepsilon,1+\varepsilon)\).
This is a strong averaged form of the conjectural pointwise behavior, while still leaving limsup/liminf extremal questions open.

## Immediate extremal corollaries (unconditional from the first moment)
From
\[
\frac1x\sum_{n\le x} f(n)\to 1,\qquad f(n)\ge 0,
\]
one gets:

1. \(\liminf_{n\to\infty} f(n)\le 1\).
Proof: if \(\liminf f(n)\ge 1+\delta\), then eventually all \(f(n)\ge 1+\delta\),
forcing the Cesaro mean to be at least \(1+\delta\), contradiction.

2. \(\limsup_{n\to\infty} f(n)\ge 1\).
Proof: if \(\limsup f(n)\le 1-\delta\), then eventually all \(f(n)\le 1-\delta\),
contradicting mean \(\to1\).

So the conjectured pair
\[
\liminf f(n)=1,\qquad \limsup f(n)=\infty
\]
is currently narrowed to at least
\[
\liminf f(n)\le 1\le \limsup f(n),
\]
with density-one concentration around 1 from the second-moment input.
