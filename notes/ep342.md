### Lemma 1 (Unique representation criterion)
At each step, \(u_{n+1}\) is the least integer \(m>u_n\) such that
\[
\#\{(i,j):1\le i<j\le n,\ u_i+u_j=m\}=1.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
This is the defining rule of Ulam sequences.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Monotonicity and unboundedness)
\((u_n)\) is strictly increasing and unbounded.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
By definition \(u_{n+1}>u_n\). Strictly increasing integer sequence is unbounded.
\(\square\)
