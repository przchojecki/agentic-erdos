# EP-1049

## Problem Statement
For rational $t>1$, study irrationality of
\[
\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{1}{t^n-1}=\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{\tau(n)}{t^n}.
\]

## Literature
- Integer case $t\in\mathbb Z_{\ge2}$ is known (Erd\H{o}s result in background).
- Non-integer rational $t>1$ remains the unresolved direction in this note.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone deep numerical diagnostics for several integer and non-integer rational $t$:
  - long partial sums,
  - explicit tail bounds,
  - continued-fraction rational approximants,
  - brute nearest-rational scan up to large denominator caps.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For tested non-integer examples ($3/2,5/3,7/5,9/7$), no very-small-denominator rational matched within the rigorous tail budget.
- This is consistent with irrationality but remains purely computational evidence.

## Status
- Integer case resolved.
- General rational non-integer case remains open.
- Added standalone small-denominator exclusion evidence with explicit tail-gap accounting.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
For rational non-integer \(t>1\), prove irrationality of
\[
S(t)=\sum_{n\ge1}\frac1{t^n-1}.
\]

### Step A: Linear Form Construction
Construct integer-linear forms
\[
L_M = A_M S(t)-B_M
\]
with explicit denominator control from partial-fraction / q-series structure.

### Step B: Upper and Lower Bounds
Show \(0<|L_M|<\exp(-\alpha M)\) while proving arithmetic lower bound
\[
|L_M|\ge \exp(-\beta M)
\]
for rational \(S(t)\), with \(\alpha>\beta\) contradiction.

### Step C: Irrationality Conclusion
Contradiction from Step B yields irrationality.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a denominator-growth lemma for rational \(t=a/b\notin\mathbb Z\): after
clearing powers of \(a,b\), remaining common-factor cancellations in
\(\prod_{n\le M}(t^n-1)\) are too small to destroy exponential separation in Step B.
