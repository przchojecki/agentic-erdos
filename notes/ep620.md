# EP-620

## Problem Statement
If \(G\) is \(K_4\)-free on \(n\) vertices, how large a triangle-free induced subgraph must \(G\) contain?

## Literature / Context
- Known asymptotic window is near \(n^{1/2+o(1)}\), with current best bounds differing by logarithmic factors.
- Sharp behavior is still open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented deeper random maximal \(K_4\)-free graph probing.
- Extracted large triangle-free induced subsets via repeated triangle-deletion heuristic.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Best found sizes:
  - \(n=60\): \(25\),
  - \(n=80\): \(32\),
  - \(n=100\): \(36\).
- Ratios are around \(3.2\) to \(3.6\) times \(\sqrt n\), consistent with \(n^{1/2}\)-type scale in finite tests.

## Next Proof Targets
- Replace heuristic extraction by provable algorithmic lower bounds on induced triangle-free sets in \(K_4\)-free graphs.
- Test adversarial \(K_4\)-free constructions rather than random maximal ones.
