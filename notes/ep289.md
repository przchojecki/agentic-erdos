# EP-289 partial

## Statement
For all sufficiently large `k`, are there distinct non-overlapping non-adjacent
intervals `I_1,...,I_k`, each of length at least 2, such that
`sum_i sum_{n in I_i} 1/n = 1`?

## Attempt in this batch
I ran exact randomized searches with integerized harmonic sums (common denominator
`lcm(1..Nmax)`) for decompositions of 1 into interval blocks.

Data file:
- `data/ep289_interval_egyptian_search.json`

## Result
No exact decomposition was found in these searches for
- `Nmax=90`, `k<=10`
- `Nmax=140`, `k<=8`
within the tested attempt budgets.

## Hard point
This is negative finite evidence only; non-findings under randomized search do
not imply nonexistence for larger ranges or different constructions.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine whether for all sufficiently large \(k\) one can represent \(1\) as a
sum of reciprocal interval-block sums with \(k\) disjoint non-adjacent intervals
of length at least \(2\).

### Step A: Interval block algebra
Parameterize interval sums as
\[
H[a,b]=\sum_{n=a}^b\frac1n=\frac{U_{a,b}}{L_{a,b}}
\]
and track denominators under disjoint unions.

### Step B: Sparse gluing mechanism
Develop a controlled concatenation that inserts new intervals far away while
preserving exact equality to \(1\) via compensating local modifications.

### Step C: Arbitrary-\(k\) inflation
Iterate Step B to increase number of intervals without breaking non-overlap and
non-adjacency.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a "gap-preserving refinement lemma": from one exact representation with
\(k\) intervals, produce one with \(k+1\) intervals by splitting a block into two
non-adjacent interval blocks plus a finite correction identity.

## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Common-denominator representation)
For finite interval family \(\mathcal I=\{[a_i,b_i]\}\),
\[
\sum_{I\in\mathcal I} H(I)=\frac{A}{L},
\]
where \(L=\mathrm{lcm}\{a_i,\dots,b_i\ :\ I\in\mathcal I\}\) and \(A\in\mathbb Z\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Each \(H(I)\) is a rational with denominator dividing \(L\); summing yields
integer numerator over \(L\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Disjointness stability under dilation/translation)
If intervals \(I_i\) are pairwise disjoint and non-adjacent, then for any
integer \(M\ge 2\), intervals \(MI_i+t\) remain pairwise disjoint and non-adjacent.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Scaling by \(M\ge2\) multiplies endpoint gaps by at least \(2\); adding a common
translation preserves differences.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction to one-step inflation)
If every valid \(k\)-interval representation can be transformed into a valid
\(k+1\)-interval representation, then existence for one large \(k_0\) implies
existence for all \(k\ge k_0\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Iterate the one-step transformation \(k-k_0\) times.
\(\square\)
