## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Decide whether \(\lim_{k\to\infty} R(3;k)^{1/k}\) exists and is finite.

### Step A: Multiplicative sub/super-structure
Establish near-submultiplicative inequalities for \(R(3;k)\) up to
subexponential factors.

### Step B: Lower/upper root stabilization
Prove matching exponential bases via explicit constructions and recursive upper
bounds.

### Step C: Limit extraction
Apply Fekete-type argument (with error control) to show convergence of roots.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a quasi-submultiplicativity theorem:
\[
R(3;k+\ell)\le R(3;k)\,R(3;\ell)\,e^{o(k+\ell)}.
\]

\[
a_{k+\ell}\le a_k a_\ell e^{o(k+\ell)}

\(\lim a_k^{1/k}\) exists if a matching quasi-supermultiplicative lower bound
holds.
