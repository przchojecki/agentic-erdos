## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Complement criterion)
If \(A\subseteq[1,M]\) avoids subset sum \(n\), then any \(B\subseteq A\) also
avoids subset sum \(n\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Any subset of \(B\) is a subset of \(A\); if one summed to \(n\), \(A\) would
contain such subset.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Large-element pruning bound)
Elements \(>n\) are irrelevant for forming sum \(n\). Thus maximal avoider size
decomposes as
\[
F(M,n)= (M-n)_+ + F(n,n).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
No subset containing element \(>n\) can sum to \(n\) (all terms positive), so all
elements \(n+1,\dots,M\) may be included freely. Remaining optimization is inside
\([1,n]\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction)
Asymptotics in \(M=\lfloor cn\rfloor\) reduce to core case \(F(n,n)\) via Lemma 2.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Direct substitution:
\[
F(\lfloor cn\rfloor,n)=(\lfloor cn\rfloor-n)_+ + F(n,n).
\]
\(\square\)
