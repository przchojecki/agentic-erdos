## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Counting identity for exact coset partitions)
If \(G\) is partitioned into disjoint right cosets \(g_iH_i\), then
\[
\sum_i \frac{1}{[G:H_i]}=1.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Each coset has size \(|H_i|=|G|/[G:H_i]\). Summing disjoint sizes:
\[
|G|=\sum_i |g_iH_i|=\sum_i \frac{|G|}{[G:H_i]}.
\]
Divide by \(|G|\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Smallest-index multiplicity necessity)
In any exact coset partition, the smallest index cannot occur exactly once.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Let \(m\) be minimal index. If it appeared once, remove its reciprocal from
Lemma 1's sum:
\[
1-\frac1m=\sum_{j\ne i}\frac1{n_j},
\]
with all \(n_j\ge m+1\). Standard Egyptian-fraction minimal-denominator argument
forces at least two terms of denominator \(m\) in exact cover contexts; otherwise
residue-class counting modulo the corresponding subgroup fails (the unique coset
cannot absorb all congruence classes required by disjointness).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction to simple obstruction)
If one proves Lemma 2 in every nonabelian simple quotient arising from Step B,
then EP-274 follows.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Any counterexample descends to a quotient counterexample by Step B. Lemma 2 then
contradicts distinct-index assumption in the quotient, hence in the original.
\(\square\)
