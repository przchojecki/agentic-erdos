# EP-935

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

### Working statement
Let
\[
P_{\ell}(n)=\prod_{t=0}^{\ell}(n+t),\qquad Q_2(m)=\prod_{p^e\parallel m,\,e\ge 2} p^e.
\]
Study whether for \(\ell\ge 2\),
\[
\limsup_{n\to\infty}\frac{Q_2(P_{\ell}(n))}{n^2}=\infty.
\]

### Deep standalone computation (`ep935.mjs`)
- Method: exact SPF factorization with a sliding window over \(P_{\ell}(n)\), updating prime exponents exactly when shifting \(n\mapsto n+1\).
- Run: \(N=3\cdot 10^7\), \(\ell\in\{1,2,\dots,8\}\), runtime \(\approx 50.98\)s.
- Best observed \(Q_2(P_{\ell}(n))/n^2\):
  - \(\ell=1\): \(1.125\) at \(n=8\)
  - \(\ell=2\): \(338.03\) at \(n=9800\)
  - \(\ell=3\): \(4.2336\times 10^4\) at \(n=2328480\)
  - \(\ell=4\): \(1.6934\times 10^5\) at \(n=2328480\)
  - \(\ell=5\): \(9.1593\times 10^7\) at \(n=15407168\)
  - \(\ell=6\): \(7.7673\times 10^8\) at \(n=2734930\)
  - \(\ell=7\): \(4.8087\times 10^{11}\) at \(n=15407168\)
  - \(\ell=8\): \(6.8067\times 10^{12}\) at \(n=3644273\)

### Current proof direction
The data strongly supports unbounded limsup for \(\ell\ge 2\). A plausible route is to force repeated square divisors in many primes across short intervals by congruence design plus smoothness control, then aggregate contributions to \(Q_2\).
