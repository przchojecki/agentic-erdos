# EP-462 deep attempt with standalone computation

## Statement
Let `p(n)` be the least prime factor of `n`. Given
`\sum_{n<x,\ n\text{ composite}} p(n)/n \sim c x^{1/2}/(\log x)^2`,
ask whether there is `C>0` such that
`\sum_{x\le n\le x+C x^{1/2}(\log x)^2} p(n)/n \gg 1` for all large `x`.

## Attempt route
Computed finite short-window masses of `p(n)/n` over windows of the target
length scale `C\sqrt{x}(\log x)^2`.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove existence of \(C>0\) such that for all large \(x\),
\[
\sum_{x\le n\le x+C\sqrt{x}(\log x)^2}\frac{p(n)}{n}\gg1.
\]

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Show uniform short-interval lower bound for least-prime-factor mass:
\[
\sum_{x<n\le x+H}\frac{p(n)}{n}
\ge c_0
\]
for \(H=C\sqrt{x}(\log x)^2\), using distribution of \(p(n)\) classes.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (SPF decomposition)
\[
\sum_{n\le y}\frac{p(n)}{n}
=
\sum_{p\le y} p\!\!\sum_{\substack{n\le y\\ p(n)=p}}\frac1n.
\]

**Proof.**
Partition integers by least prime factor. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (interval difference form)
Target window mass equals difference of prefix sums:
\[
S(x+H)-S(x),\quad S(y):=\sum_{n\le y}\frac{p(n)}{n}.
\]

**Proof.**
Telescoping definition of partial sums. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies EP-462 claim)
If A gives \(S(x+H)-S(x)\ge c_0\) uniformly for large \(x\), the conjectured \(\gg1\) statement follows.

**Proof.**
\(\gg1\) means bounded below by positive constant. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is obtaining uniform (all-\(x\)) lower bounds in windows at critical \(\sqrt{x}(\log x)^2\) length, where fluctuation could in principle be large.
