# EP-776

## Problem Statement
For an antichain family on \([n]\), if each realized set size occurs with multiplicity at least \(r\), how large must \(n\) be (in terms of \(r\)) to realize \(n-3\) distinct sizes?

## Literature
- Known landscape in background:
  - for \(r=1\), max number of realized sizes is \(n-2\) (for \(n>3\));
  - for \(r>1\), \(n-2\) is impossible, while \(n-3\) is eventually achievable for large enough \(n\).
- Quantitative threshold \(n(r)\) is open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built finite local-search optimizer over antichains for small \(n\):
  maximize number of realized sizes with multiplicity at least \(r\).
- Reported best found size-spectrum counts for \(r=2,3\), \(n=8\) to 12.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In this small range, best found realized-size counts remain well below \(n-3\) targets.
- Gaps are larger for \(r=3\), consistent with stronger multiplicity constraints.
- This supports that the eventual threshold phenomenon likely begins beyond small \(n\).

## Status
- Open (quantitative threshold unknown).
- Added standalone finite optimization data for the \(n-3\) target gap under multiplicity constraints.
