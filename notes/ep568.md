# EP-568

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

### Source: ep568_partial.md

## Statement
If `R(G,T_n)\ll n` for all trees and `R(G,K_n)\ll n^2`, must one have
\[
R(G,H)\ll m
\]
for every `H` with `m` edges and no isolates?

## Literature Leads
- Problem page labels this as Ramsey-size linearity (`#568: [EFRS93]`) and keeps
  it open (updated January 2026).
- EP-566/567 serve as concrete special-case tracks feeding into this question.

## Strategy Notes
- Candidate approach: combine tree-control (sparse expansion) and clique-control
  (dense obstruction control) to force linear-size embedding of general `H`.
- Finite testbed: representative `G` families with known/expected good behavior
  against `mK_2` targets.

## New Experiments
