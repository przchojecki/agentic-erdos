# EP-888

## Problem Statement
Maximize $|A|\subseteq\{1,\dots,n\}$ subject to:
for $a\le b\le c\le d$ in $A$, if $abcd$ is a square then necessarily $ad=bc$.

## Literature
- Known progress in notes:
  - upper bound $|A|=o(n)$,
  - lower bounds from primes and semiprimes, including
    $$|A|\gg \frac{n\log\log n}{\log n}.$$
- Sharp order of growth remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Computed exact optima for small $n$ by branch-and-bound search.
- Computed greedy finite profiles for larger $n$ across multiple trials.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Exact small-$n$ best sizes for $n=22,24,26,28$ are $14,15,16,16$.
- Greedy best sizes for $n\in\{80,100,120,150,180\}$ remain a substantial fraction of $n$ in this finite range.
- Finite data shows dense feasible families exist locally, but asymptotic behavior is still unresolved.
