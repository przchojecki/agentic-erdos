# EP-892

## Problem Statement
Characterize growth sequences $b_n$ for which there exists a primitive sequence $a_n$ with
$$a_n \ll b_n.$$

## Literature
- Classical necessary conditions are known (from Erdos and Erdos-Sarkozy-Szemeredi work), but a full characterization remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built finite greedy feasibility probe:
  - for model growth laws $b_n$,
  - test whether one can greedily construct a primitive sequence with $a_n\le C b_n$ for all $n\le M$.
- Used multiple $C$ values and models (`linear`, `nlogn`, `n_log2`, `quadratic`, `powers2`).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Linear-scale $b_n$ needs larger constant before long finite success appears.
- Faster growth models (`n log n`, `n log^2 n`, quadratic) support full finite prefix construction even at small constants in this probe.
- Very sparse exponential-step model (`powers2`) fails early for moderate constants.
- These are heuristic finite diagnostics, not a proof of necessary/sufficient criteria.
