# EP-450 deep attempt with standalone computation

## Statement
How large must `y=y(\epsilon,n)` be so that integers in `(x,x+y)` having a
divisor in `(n,2n)` are at most `\epsilon y`? (Quantifier on `x` is subtle.)

## Attempt route
Computed deeper `x`-window minimum/maximum/mean densities for multiple `n` and
multiple `y` scales.

## New Experiments
- 2026-03-12T10:18:56.088Z: standalone deep density profile
  (`scripts/ep450.mjs`) over `x<=300000`, with `n in {120,300,700,1200}`.

## One-by-One Cycle
- Replaced batch-derived placeholder with larger-range direct divisor-window
  counting.
- For all tested parameter sets, minimum density over `x` stayed bounded well
  away from zero (typically `0.24` to `0.38`), while means were around
  `0.32`-`0.41`.
- Finite data supports the view that uniform-in-`x` very small densities are
  hard/impossible in broad regimes.
