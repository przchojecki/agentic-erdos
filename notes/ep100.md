## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Improve lower bounds on minimal possible diameter of \(1\)-separated planar
\(n\)-point sets with all pairwise distances distinct.

### Step A: Distinct-distance forcing
Use additive-combinatorial incidence bounds to force many distances in bounded
radius regions.

### Step B: Packing contradiction
Show too-small diameter cannot host enough distinct distances under
\(1\)-separation.

### Step C: Asymptotic optimization
Balance parameters to push diameter lower bound from \(n/\log n\)-type toward
linear.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a radius-sensitive distinct-distance lower bound for separated sets in disks.

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition Y1 (Area-packing baseline)
If \(P\subset \mathbb R^2\) is \(1\)-separated and contained in a disk of radius
\(R\), then
\[
n\ll R^2.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Disjoint disks of radius \(1/2\) around points of \(P\) fit in radius \(R+1/2\);
compare areas.
\(\square\)

### Proposition Y2 (Reduction to distinct-distance count)
If every such \(P\) in radius \(R\) satisfies
\[
D(P)\le F(R,n),
\]
and \(D(P)=\binom n2\) (all distances distinct), then
\[
\binom n2\le F(R,n),
\]
giving a lower bound on \(R\) by inversion of \(F\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Direct substitution.
\(\square\)
