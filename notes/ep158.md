## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Decide whether
\[
\liminf_{N\to\infty}\frac{F_2^{(2)}(N)}{\sqrt N}>0
\]
for \(B_2[2]\)-type sets (unordered representation count at most \(2\)).

### Step A: Additive-energy lower barrier
Bound representation multiplicities and derive necessary sparsity/energy profile.

### Step B: Probabilistic construction with correction
Construct dense random candidate sets and prune local representation violations.

### Step C: Uniform density certificate
Show corrected construction keeps size \(\gg \sqrt N\) with explicit constant.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a concentration inequality for the number of offending sums after dependent
pruning, strong enough to keep positive \(\sqrt N\)-density.

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition BF1 (Counting reduction)
If \(A\subset[1,N]\) satisfies \(r_{A+A}(n)\le 2\) for all \(n\), then
\[
|A|^2=\sum_n r_{A+A}(n)\le 2|A+A|.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Sum representation counts over all sums.
\(\square\)

### Proposition BF2 (Density criterion)
If one constructs infinitely many \(N\) with \(B_2[2]\)-sets \(A_N\subset[1,N]\)
and \(|A_N|\ge c\sqrt N\), \(c>0\), then
\[
\liminf_{N\to\infty}\frac{F_2^{(2)}(N)}{\sqrt N}\ge c.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
By definition \(F_2^{(2)}(N)\ge |A_N|\) on those \(N\); take liminf.
\(\square\)
