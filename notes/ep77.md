## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition J1 (Subexponential error does not change base)
Let \(a_k,b_k>0\). If
\[
a_k\le R(k,k)\le b_k,\qquad
\log b_k-\log a_k=o(k),
\]
then
\[
\limsup_{k\to\infty} R(k,k)^{1/k}
=\liminf_{k\to\infty} R(k,k)^{1/k}
=\lim_{k\to\infty} a_k^{1/k}
\]
whenever the last limit exists.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Divide by \(k\):
\[
\frac1k\log a_k\le \frac1k\log R(k,k)\le \frac1k\log b_k
=\frac1k\log a_k+o(1).
\]
Exponentiating gives equality of upper/lower limits.
\(\square\)

### Proposition J2 (Reduction of EP-77 to matched asymptotics)
If Step A yields an upper model \(b_k\), Step B yields a lower model \(a_k\),
and \(\log b_k-\log a_k=o(k)\), then EP-77 reduces to evaluating one model base.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Apply Proposition J1.
\(\square\)
