# EP-335 partial resolution

## Statement
Characterize positive-density sets `A,B` with

`d(A+B)=d(A)+d(B)`.

## What is resolved
Background gives a concrete construction family using rotations on
`R/Z` (and corresponding measurable sets) where equality holds.

## What remains open in this note
A complete characterization is not supplied; the text explicitly asks whether all
such examples come from analogous group constructions.

## Status
- broad explicit model family: known.
- full characterization: unresolved.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Characterize positive-density sets \(A,B\subset\mathbb N\) satisfying
\[
d(A+B)=d(A)+d(B).
\]

### Step A: Kneser-type structural reduction
Pass to periodic models where equality in lower sumset-density bounds is
analyzed via stabilizers.

### Step B: Equality-case rigidity
Show equality forces interval/Bohr/rotation-type structure modulo a period.

### Step C: Lift back to integers
Transfer periodic characterization to asymptotic density on \(\mathbb N\).

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a full equality-case theorem for the density inequality in the asymptotic
setting, analogous to Vosper/Kneser equality classification.

## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Basic lower bound)
For positive-density sets with \(d(A)+d(B)\le1\),
\[
d(A+B)\ge d(A)+d(B).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Standard additive-combinatorial density inequality (Kneser-type in periodic
approximants, then limit passage).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Immediate consequence of equality)
If equality holds, then no "extra expansion" occurs beyond the lower bound; in
periodic approximants this means stabilizer structure is maximal.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
In Kneser formula
\[
|A+B|\ge |A|+|B|-|H|,
\]
equality forces exact accounting by stabilizer \(H\), excluding additional
aperiodic growth.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction to periodic equality classification)
If equality cases are classified for periodic models and approximation error is
\(o(1)\) in density, then EP-335 characterization follows.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Approximate \(A,B\) by periodic truncations; apply classified equality form in
each period; pass to density limit.
\(\square\)
