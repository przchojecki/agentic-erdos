# EP-863

## Problem Statement
Let $c_r$ be the optimal constant in the sum-representation $B_2[r]$ setting and $c'_r$ the analogous constant for differences. For $r\ge 2$, compare $c_r$ and $c'_r$ (in particular, whether $c'_r<c_r$).

## Literature
- Baseline $r=1$ is classical Sidon: $c_1=c'_1=1$.
- For $r\ge2$, sharp constant comparison remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built finite random-greedy proxy searches on $[1..N]$ for both constraints:
  - sum constraint: each sum has at most $r$ representations,
  - difference proxy: each positive difference has at most $r$ representations.
- Compared best and average sizes across repeated runs.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In tested ranges ($N=80$ to $240$, $r=2,3$), sum-constrained best sizes are consistently larger than difference-constrained best sizes.
- Example at $N=240$:
  - $r=2$: best sum size 26 vs best diff size 22,
  - $r=3$: best sum size 33 vs best diff size 27.
- This finite evidence points in the conjectural direction $c'_r<c_r$, but is not asymptotic proof.
