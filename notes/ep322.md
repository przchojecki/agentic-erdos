## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine growth of maximal representation multiplicity
\[
r_{k,3}(n):=\#\{(a,b,c)\in\mathbb Z_{\ge0}^3:\ a^k+b^k+c^k=n\}
\]
along infinitely many \(n\), especially polynomial lower growth in \(n\).

### Step A: Parametric-family production
Construct algebraic families of equal-sum \(k\)-th-power triples generating many
collisions at the same \(n\).

### Step B: Height counting on parameter space
Count distinct parameter values up to size \(T\) and transfer to \(n\)-scale.

### Step C: Multiplicity lower bound
Infer \(r_{k,3}(n)\ge n^\alpha\) infinitely often (for some \(\alpha>0\)).

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a nondegeneracy lemma: parameterized collision family produces
pairwise-distinct triples after removing a thin exceptional set.

## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Collision-to-multiplicity transfer)
If there are \(M\) distinct triples \((a_i,b_i,c_i)\) with the same value
\(a_i^k+b_i^k+c_i^k=n\), then \(r_{k,3}(n)\ge M\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Definition of \(r_{k,3}(n)\) is exactly the number of such triples.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Infinite-often principle)
Suppose for infinitely many parameters \(T\) one can produce \(M(T)\) distinct
triples with common sum \(n(T)\), and \(M(T)\ge n(T)^\alpha\). Then
\[
r_{k,3}(n)\ge n^\alpha
\]
for infinitely many integers \(n\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Apply Lemma 1 at \(n=n(T)\) for infinitely many \(T\).
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction)
To prove a polynomial lower bound infinitely often, it suffices to build one
parameterized family satisfying Lemma 2 after deduplication.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Immediate from Lemma 2.
\(\square\)
