### Proposition G1 (Collision bound from Sidon property)
If \(A\subset[1,N]\) is Sidon, then
\[
|A|(|A|-1)\le 2N-2.
\]
Hence \(|A|\le \sqrt{2N}+1\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Distinct unordered pairs \(\{a,b\}\) with \(a\neq b\) yield distinct sums
\(a+b\in[2,2N]\), giving at most \(2N-1\) possibilities. So
\[
\binom{|A|}{2}\le 2N-1.
\]
Rearrange for \(|A|\).
\(\square\)

### Proposition G2 (Implication-chain endpoint)

\(\theta<1/2\), then after finitely many iterations one reaches any
\(1/2-\varepsilon\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Iterate \(\theta_j=\theta_0+j\eta\). Choose \(j\) minimal with
\(\theta_j\ge 1/2-\varepsilon\).
\(\square\)
