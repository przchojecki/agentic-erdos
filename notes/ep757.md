# EP-757

## Problem Statement
Let \(A\subset \mathbb{R}\), \(|A|=n\), and assume every 4-subset \(B\subseteq A\) satisfies \(|B-B|\ge 11\).
Find the best constant \(c>0\) such that \(A\) must contain a Sidon subset of size at least \(cn\).

## Literature
- Current known global bounds place the optimal \(c\) strictly between \(1/2\) and \(3/5\) (from background notes).
- Exact optimal constant remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built finite sampling + exact search framework:
  - sample \(n\)-sets in a wide interval,
  - keep only sets satisfying the local 4-point difference condition,
  - compute exact maximum Sidon subset size by branch-and-bound.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- With ambient range width 3000, accepted samples for \(n=10,12,14\) had very high Sidon ratios (average about 0.99; worst around 0.86-0.92).
- This indicates that in very spread-out finite models, the local condition strongly biases toward near-Sidon behavior.
- These finite results do not identify the asymptotic extremal constant; they likely overestimate typical ratios for adversarial constructions.

## Status
- Open (optimal constant unknown).
- Added standalone exact small-\(n\) extraction experiments under the local \(|B-B|\ge 11\) constraint.
