## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

Let \(Q(N)\) denote the extremal quantity in EP-30.

### Proposition B1 (Asymptotic conclusion from matched bounds)
Suppose there exist functions \(L(N),U(N)\) with
\[
L(N)\le Q(N)\le U(N),
\]
and
\[
L(N)=\sqrt N+\ell(N),\qquad U(N)=\sqrt N+u(N),
\]
where \(\ell(N),u(N)=o(\sqrt N)\). Then
\[
Q(N)=\sqrt N+o(\sqrt N).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Subtract \(\sqrt N\):
\[
\ell(N)\le Q(N)-\sqrt N\le u(N).
\]
Since both endpoints are \(o(\sqrt N)\), squeeze gives
\[
Q(N)-\sqrt N=o(\sqrt N).
\]
\(\square\)

### Proposition B2 (Constant-error regime implies sharp first-order asymptotic)
If one improves to
\[
\sqrt N - C_1 \le Q(N)\le \sqrt N + C_2
\]
for absolute constants \(C_1,C_2\), then
\[
Q(N)=\sqrt N+O(1).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Immediate from two-sided inequality.
\(\square\)

These are rigorous implication steps; the genuinely open part is proving the
needed upper/lower bounds themselves at the conjectured error scale.
