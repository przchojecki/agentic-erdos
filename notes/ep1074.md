# EP-1074

## Problem Statement
Define:
- $S$ as the set of integers $m$ for which there exists a prime $p$ with
\[
m!\equiv -1\pmod p
\]
and $p\not\equiv 1\pmod m$;
- $P$ as the set of primes $p$ that occur in such a witness pair.

Study infinitude and density behavior of $S$ and $P$.

## Literature
- Existing notes in this repository record infinitude of both sets as known, while fine density limits remain open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Replaced stub with a standalone exact finite scan (`scripts/ep1074.mjs`).
- For each prime $p\le \text{LIMIT}$, iterated exact residues of $m!\bmod p$ and detected the first witness with $m!\equiv -1\pmod p$ and $p\not\equiv 1\pmod m$.
- Built empirical profiles for:
  - $|S\cap [1,x]|/x$,
  - $|P\cap \mathbb P_{\le x}|/\pi(x)$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
Deep run at
\[
\text{LIMIT}=180{,}000
\]
reported:
- $|S\cap [1,180000]|=7892$, density $0.0438444444$,
- $|P\cap \mathbb P_{\le 180000}|=8576$ out of $\pi(180000)=16342$, proportion $0.5247827683$,
- total workload: about $9.26\times 10^8$ modular factorial multiplications.

Finite trends suggest:
- prime-side proportion in $P$ is fairly stable near $0.525$ across larger probes,
- raw $S$-density decreases strongly with larger $x$ in this finite window.

These remain finite computations, not asymptotic proofs.
