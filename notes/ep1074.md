# EP-1074

## Problem Statement
Define:
- $S$ as the set of integers $m$ for which there exists a prime $p$ with
\[
m!\equiv -1\pmod p
\]
and $p\not\equiv 1\pmod m$;
- $P$ as the set of primes $p$ that occur in such a witness pair.

Study infinitude and density behavior of $S$ and $P$.

## Literature
- Existing notes in this repository record infinitude of both sets as known, while fine density limits remain open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Replaced stub with a standalone exact finite scan (`scripts/ep1074.mjs`).
- For each prime $p\le \text{LIMIT}$, iterated exact residues of $m!\bmod p$ and detected the first witness with $m!\equiv -1\pmod p$ and $p\not\equiv 1\pmod m$.
- Built empirical profiles for:
  - $|S\cap [1,x]|/x$,
  - $|P\cap \mathbb P_{\le x}|/\pi(x)$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
Deep run at
\[
\text{LIMIT}=180{,}000
\]
reported:
- $|S\cap [1,180000]|=7892$, density $0.0438444444$,
- $|P\cap \mathbb P_{\le 180000}|=8576$ out of $\pi(180000)=16342$, proportion $0.5247827683$,
- total workload: about $9.26\times 10^8$ modular factorial multiplications.

Finite trends suggest:
- prime-side proportion in $P$ is fairly stable near $0.525$ across larger probes,
- raw $S$-density decreases strongly with larger $x$ in this finite window.

These remain finite computations, not asymptotic proofs.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
For sets \(S,P\) defined by witness pairs \((m,p)\) with
\[
m!\equiv -1\pmod p,\qquad p\not\equiv 1\pmod m,
\]
prove refined density/asymptotic statements beyond infinitude.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Establish a quantitative equidistribution theorem for prime witnesses \(p\) across congruence classes modulo \(m\), strong enough to show
\[
\#(P\cap[1,x]) = \delta\,\pi(x)+o(\pi(x))
\]
for some explicit \(\delta\in(0,1)\), and deduce corresponding growth for \(S\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (membership implication)
If \(p\in P\), then there exists \(m<p\) with \(m!\equiv -1\pmod p\) and \(m\nmid (p-1)\).

**Proof.**
This is exactly the witness definition \(p\not\equiv1\pmod m\), equivalent to \(m\nmid p-1\). Also \(m<p\) is necessary for nonzero factorial residue mod \(p\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (Wilson endpoint separation)
For every prime \(p\), \(m=p-1\) always satisfies \(m!\equiv -1\pmod p\), but never contributes to \(S\) since \(p\equiv1\pmod{p-1}\).

**Proof.**
Wilson gives \((p-1)!\equiv -1\pmod p\). Also \(p=1\cdot (p-1)+1\), so \(p\equiv1\pmod{p-1}\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (prime-side density transfer)
If \(P\) has natural density \(\delta\) inside primes and the witness map \(p\mapsto m\) has bounded average multiplicity on \(m\le x\), then \(S\) has positive lower density up to \(x\) (or explicit growth lower bound).

**Proof.**
Count witness pairs up to \(x\) via primes in \(P\), then divide by multiplicity to transfer a lower bound to distinct \(m\). \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The critical gap is a rigorous distribution theory for non-endpoint factorial-congruence witnesses \(m\) modulo varying primes \(p\), with power-saving error terms.
