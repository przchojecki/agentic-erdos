# EP-408 deep attempt with standalone computation

## Statement
With `f(n)=\min\{k:\phi_k(n)=1\}`, study whether `f(n)/\log n` has a
distribution / normal order, and how large the largest prime factor of
`\phi_k(n)` is for `k\approx\log\log n`.

## What is resolved from background
- Conditional results (Elliott--Halberstam type assumptions) indicate positive
  answers for distribution/normal-order questions.

## Attempt route
Computed large finite profiles for both depth ratio `f(n)/\log n` and largest
prime factor compression under `k=\lfloor\log\log n\rfloor` iterates.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Establish unconditional distribution/normal-order behavior for \(f(n)\) (totient depth) and largest-prime-factor decay after \(k\approx\log\log n\) iterations.

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a uniform contraction lemma for typical integers:
\[
P(\phi(m))\le m^{1-\delta}
\]
on a density-1 subset with effective \(\delta>0\), iterably enough to control depth statistics.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (depth recursion)
\[
f(n)=1+f(\phi(n))\quad(n>1),\qquad f(1)=0.
\]

**Proof.**
By definition \(f\) counts number of totient iterations to reach \(1\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (iteration-count from size contraction)
If \(\phi(m)\le m^\theta\) typically with \(\theta<1\), then typical depth is \(O(\log\log n)\) and normalized \(f(n)/\log n\) concentrates.

**Proof.**
Repeated exponentiation contraction reduces \(\log m\) geometrically; solve iteration count to reach \(O(1)\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies prime-factor compression statement)
Iterating A for \(k\sim \log\log n\) forces largest prime factors of \(\phi_k(n)\) below \(n^\eta\) for small \(\eta\).

**Proof.**
Each step shrinks prime-factor scale by fixed exponent drop; after \(O(\log\log n)\) steps exponent becomes arbitrarily small. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The key obstruction is obtaining strong unconditional contraction for \(P(\phi(m))\) on density-1 sets with enough uniformity across iterations.
