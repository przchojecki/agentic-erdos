## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Under the hypothesis of \(c n^2\) lines containing \(>3\) points, prove the
maximum collinearity parameter \(h_c(n)\to\infty\) (even slowly).

### Step A: Rich-line multiplicity profile
Quantify distribution of line occupancies and show low \(h_c(n)\) forces very
high overlap between rich lines.

### Step B: Incidence rigidity
Prove such overlap implies near-grid/projective-product structure.

### Step C: Obstruction analysis
Either derive contradiction with \(c n^2\) rich-line count, or classify true
obstructions and bound their achievable \(c\).

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a sharp inverse theorem for rich-line incidence matrices in the regime where
many lines have occupancy \(4\) but maximal occupancy is bounded.

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition AA1 (Pair counting through rich lines)
If \(L_t\) is number of lines containing exactly \(t\) points of \(P\), then
\[
\sum_{t\ge 4} \binom{t}{2} L_t
\le \binom{n}{2}.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Each unordered point pair determines at most one line.
\(\square\)

### Proposition AA2 (Immediate occupancy consequence)
If \(h_c(n)\le H\), then
\[
\sum_{t=4}^{H} L_t \le \frac{\binom{n}{2}}{\binom{4}{2}}= \frac{n(n-1)}{12}.
\]
So achieving \(c n^2\) rich lines requires \(c\le 1/12+o(1)\), and any
improvement beyond this threshold must exploit higher occupancies or structure.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
From AA1 and \(\binom{t}{2}\ge 6\) for \(t\ge 4\):
\[
6\sum_{t=4}^{H}L_t\le \binom n2.
\]
\(\square\)
