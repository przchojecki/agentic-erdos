# EP-955

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

### Working statement
For \(s(n)=\sigma(n)-n\), and sparse sets \(A\subset\mathbb{N}\) with density zero, study whether
\[
\#\{n\le x: s(n)\in A\}=o(x).
\]

### Deep standalone computation (`ep955.mjs`)
- Method: exact divisor-sum table up to `N`, then density tracking for target sets
  - primes,
  - powers of 2,
  - squares,
  - sums of two squares.
- Deep run: `N=10,000,000`.

### Results
Observed preimage densities at `x=10^7`:
- primes: `0.0653069`
- powers of 2: `0.0664611`
- squares: `0.0674801`
- sums of two squares: `0.2682036`

All four monitored densities decrease across the sampled scales from `10^5` to `10^7`.

### Interpretation
Finite evidence supports downward trend for several sparse targets, but remains far from asymptotic zero-density proofs.

## Exact preimage-count formulation
For any target set \(A\subset\mathbb N\), define
\[
N_A(x):=\#\{n\le x:\ s(n)\in A\}.
\]
Then EP-955 asks whether \(N_A(x)=o(x)\) for every density-zero \(A\).

### Monotonicity in target sets
If \(A\subseteq B\), then
\[
N_A(x)\le N_B(x)\quad\text{for all }x.
\]
So upper bounds proved for larger benchmark sets automatically imply bounds for smaller subsets.

### Union bound
For any \(A,B\subset\mathbb N\),
\[
N_{A\cup B}(x)\le N_A(x)+N_B(x).
\]
Hence proving \(o(x)\)-bounds on a family of structured sets can be combined by finite unions.

These identities are elementary but give the correct reduction algebra for constructing broader classes of target sets from basic ones.
