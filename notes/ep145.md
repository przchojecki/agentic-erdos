### Proposition AW1 (Moment convergence criterion)
If \(M_\alpha(X)\) satisfies
\[
M_\alpha(X)=C_\alpha+E_\alpha(X),\qquad E_\alpha(X)\to 0,
\]
then \(\lim_{X\to\infty}M_\alpha(X)=C_\alpha\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Immediate from the definition of limit.
\(\square\)

### Proposition AW2 (Interpolation reduction)
Suppose convergence is proved for integer \(\alpha\) in an interval and
\(\alpha\mapsto M_\alpha(X)\) is uniformly Hölder/Lipschitz with modulus
independent of \(X\). Then convergence extends to all real \(\alpha\) in that
interval.

\[
\textit{Proof sketch.}
\]
Approximate real \(\alpha\) by nearby proved points and pass \(X\to\infty\) using
uniform continuity.
\(\square\)
