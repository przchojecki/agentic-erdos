# EP-569

## Problem Statement
For fixed \(k \ge 1\), determine the best constant \(c_k\) such that
\[
R(C_{2k+1},H)\le c_k m
\]
for every graph \(H\) with \(m\) edges and no isolated vertices.

## Literature / Context
- This is a linear-Ramsey-growth question for odd cycles versus sparse targets.
- The sharp dependence in \(m\) is the key object; finite-case constants are not decisive.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Reduction idea: certify forced blue matchings when red odd-cycle avoidance is imposed.
- Finite proxy used: random 2-colorings of \(K_N\), checking red \(C_{2k+1}\)-avoidance and blue matching number.
- No theorem-level proof for the optimal \(c_k\) yet.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In tested windows for \(k=2,3\) and \(m=2,3,4\), sampled avoiders were not found once \(N\) reached small linear thresholds (\(N\approx 3m\) in this proxy).
- This supports (heuristically) linear behavior in \(m\), but does not identify the sharp constant.

## Next Proof Targets
- Derive a deterministic implication of the form
  \[
  \delta_{\mathrm{red}}(K_N\setminus C_{2k+1}) \Longrightarrow \nu(\mathrm{blue})\ge m
  \]
  with explicit constants.
- Replace random-coloring evidence by a counting argument based on odd-cycle-free structural decompositions.
