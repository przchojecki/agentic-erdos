# EP-778

## Problem Statement
Alice and Bob alternately color edges of \(K_n\) (Alice red first, Bob blue). Alice wins if her largest red clique exceeds every blue clique. Does Bob have a winning strategy for all \(n\ge 3\)?

## Literature
- Problem remains open; recent progress gives large-density sets of \(n\) where Bob wins.
- Universal winning-strategy classification is unresolved.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Ran deeper simulation suite with multiple policies (`random`, `greedy_triangle`, `greedy_degree`) on both sides.
- Computed outcome rates and average clique-size gap across \(n=10,12,14,16\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Strong strategy sensitivity persists:
  - symmetric strategy play is often near-balanced (with ties),
  - asymmetric play strongly favors the stronger heuristic side.
- First-player advantage appears in several symmetric settings, but not uniformly across all heuristic choices.
- Simulations remain non-conclusive for universal Bob-winning strategy.
