# EP-740

## Problem Statement
For infinite cardinal \(m\), if \(\chi(G)=m\), must \(G\) contain a subgraph of chromatic number \(m\) with no odd cycle of length \(\le r\)?

## Literature
- Specific cases are known (e.g. \((m,r)=(\aleph_0,3)\) in the historical notes), but the full general statement is open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built a finite analogue test on random graphs:
  - compute exact chromatic number,
  - search induced subgraphs preserving chromatic number,
  - require odd girth \(>r\).
- This is only a finite proxy for intuition.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For sampled graphs on \(n=10,11,12\):
  - success rates for preserving \(\chi\) with odd girth \(>3\) are around 0.15-0.30,
  - for odd girth \(>5\) they drop to about 0.04-0.06.
- Finite proxy suggests the constraint becomes quickly harder as odd-cycle threshold increases.

## Status
- Open (general infinite-cardinal statement).
- Added standalone finite analogue computations quantifying feasibility rates by odd-girth threshold.
