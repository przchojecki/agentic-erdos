# EP-430 partial resolution

## Statement
For the descending sequence `a_i` defined by prime-factor constraints in `[1,n)`,
must some term be composite for all sufficiently large `n`?

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove eventual existence of a composite term in the EP-430 descending prime-factor sequence (equivalent to EP-385 first part).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Show short-interval large-prime-factor prevalence: for every large \(n\), some composite \(m<n\) satisfies
\[
P(m)>n-m.
\]

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (equivalence to witness inequality)
Existence of such \(m\) is equivalent to eventual composite-term appearance in the sequence formulation.

**Proof.**
This is the recorded equivalence with EP-385/coverage model: large smallest distance to \(n\) compensated by largest prime factor condition. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (coverage monotonicity)
If \(n\) is covered by some witness \(m\), then nearby \(n'\) with \(n'-n\) below a stability margin remain covered by same \(m\).

**Proof.**
Inequality \(P(m)>n-m\) persists under small perturbations of right-hand side. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies eventual theorem)
If A gives a witness for every large \(n\), B1 yields eventual composite-term result.

**Proof.**
Direct implication through equivalence. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is proving universal short-interval existence of composites with sufficiently large largest prime factor.
