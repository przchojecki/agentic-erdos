# EP-750

## Problem Statement
Let \(f(m)\to\infty\). Does there exist a graph \(G\) of infinite chromatic number such that every \(m\)-vertex subgraph has an independent set of size at least
\[
\frac m2 - f(m)?
\]

## Literature
- The linear-defect regime (fixed \(\varepsilon m\)) is known in background references.
- The fully general sublinear unbounded-defect regime remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built finite proxy tests on a high-chromatic triangle-free family (Mycielski level \(n=23\)).
- Sampled induced \(m\)-vertex subgraphs and measured deficit
\[
\Delta(m)=\frac m2-\alpha(H).
\]

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For sampled \(m=8,10,12,14,16\), worst observed deficits were small (0,1,1,1,2).
- Normalized deficits stayed modest (e.g. worst \(\Delta/\sqrt m\le 0.5\) in this range).
- This finite proxy is compatible with slowly growing defect, but does not address infinite-chromatic existence directly.

## Status
- Open (general \(f(m)\to\infty\) formulation).
- Added standalone finite induced-subgraph deficit scan in a canonical high-chromatic family.
