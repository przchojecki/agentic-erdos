## Statement

for |A|=n. Ask whether f(n)/log n->infinity.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Show
\[
\frac{f(n)}{\log n}\to\infty,
\]
where \(f(n)\) is the guaranteed number of distinct prime divisors of
\(\prod_{a\ne b\in A}(a+b)\), \(|A|=n\).

### Step A: Prime-support expansion
Prove many distinct residue classes mod \(p\) must appear among pair sums as
\(A\) varies.

### Step B: Multiplicative large-sieve transfer
Convert residue-spread into lower bounds for number of contributing primes.

### Step C: Uniform minimax extraction
Upgrade average prime-divisor growth to worst-case guaranteed growth over all
\(A\) of size \(n\).

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a minimax large-sieve inequality tailored to the pair-sum multiset
\(\{a+b:a\ne b\in A\}\).

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition AN1 (Prime divisor counting identity)
Let
\[
P_A=\prod_{a\ne b\in A}(a+b),\qquad \omega(P_A)=\#\{p:\ p\mid P_A\}.
\]
Then
\[
\omega(P_A)=\sum_{p} \mathbf 1_{\exists a\ne b,\ p\mid (a+b)}.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
By definition, \(p\mid P_A\) iff \(p\) divides at least one factor \(a+b\).
\(\square\)

### Proposition AN2 (Reduction to prime-hit lower bounds)
If one proves that for every \(|A|=n\),
\[
\#\{p\le X:\exists a\ne b,\ p\mid(a+b)\}\ge \log n\cdot \omega_1(n),
\quad \omega_1(n)\to\infty,
\]
for some \(X=X(n)\), then \(f(n)/\log n\to\infty\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
By AN1, \(f(n)\ge \omega(P_A)\) for worst-case \(A\), and the displayed bound
forces super-logarithmic growth.
\(\square\)
