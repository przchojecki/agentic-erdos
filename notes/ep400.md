# EP-400 partial resolution

## Statement
For `g_k(n)` defined via factorial-divisibility constraints, ask whether
`sum_{n<=x} g_k(n) ~ c_k x log x` and whether typical values satisfy
`g_k(n)=c_k log x + o(log x)` for almost all `n<x`.

## What is resolved
Background records the uniform upper bound `g_k(n) <<_k log n`.

## What remains open in this note
Best constants and distributional asymptotics are not settled by the cited
material.

## Status
- logarithmic-order upper bound: known.
- sharp constants and almost-all asymptotics: unresolved.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove asymptotics
\[
\sum_{n\le x} g_k(n)\sim c_k x\log x
\]
and almost-all law \(g_k(n)\sim c_k\log n\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Show a local limit theorem for valuation-feasibility vectors of factorial factors, yielding concentration of \(g_k(n)\) around \(c_k\log n\) with integrable error.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (known logarithmic upper scale)
\[
g_k(n)\ll_k \log n.
\]

**Proof.**
This is the cited known bound from background. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (summatory-from-pointwise transfer)
If \(g_k(n)=c_k\log n+o(\log n)\) for density-1 \(n\), then
\[
\sum_{n\le x} g_k(n)=c_k x\log x+o(x\log x).
\]

**Proof.**
Average the pointwise relation; exceptional set contributes lower-order term by density-1 assumption and B1 bound. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies both targets)
If A yields almost-all concentration with explicit \(c_k\), then B2 gives summatory asymptotic, and the almost-all statement is immediate.

**Proof.**
Direct application of B2 and definition of concentration. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is deriving a precise probabilistic model for interacting prime valuations in factorial-divisibility constraints with enough uniformity to identify constants \(c_k\).
