# EP-920

## Problem Statement
Let $f_k(n)$ be the maximum possible chromatic number of a $K_k$-free graph on $n$ vertices.
For $k\ge 4$, is it true that
$$
f_k(n) \gg \frac{n^{1-1/(k-1)}}{(\log n)^{c_k}}
$$
for some constant $c_k>0$?

## Literature
- Classical upper bounds (Graver--Yackel type) and modern Ramsey improvements give nontrivial bounds.
- For $k=4$, known results imply lower bounds of type
$$
f_4(n) \gg \frac{n^{2/3}}{(\log n)^{4/3}}
$$
from current $R(4,t)$ estimates, but the target form remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone deep finite study centered on $k=4$:
  - deeper Mycielski-chain verification (K4-free checks and chromatic profile),
  - randomized search over maximal $K_4$-free graphs with exact DSATUR chromatic computation.
- Ran a 10-seed ensemble; each seed performs exact-coloring search for $n\in\{20,24,28,32,36\}$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Total multi-seed compute time in this run: about $13.7$ seconds.
- Ensemble best values found:
  - $n=20$: $\chi=5$,
  - $n=24$: $\chi=6$,
  - $n=28,32,36$: best remained $\chi=6$.
- The normalized quantity $\chi/n^{2/3}$ decreases across tested $n$, consistent with finite constructions still far from proving sharp asymptotic exponents.
- Mycielski rows remain robust finite lower-bound witnesses and stay $K_4$-free in verified range.
