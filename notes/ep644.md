# EP-644

## Problem Statement
Let \(f(k,r)\) be minimal such that if \(A_1,A_2,\dots\) is a family of \(k\)-sets with the property:
for every \(r\)-tuple of sets there exists a pair \(\{x,y\}\) intersecting all of them,
then there is a global transversal of size \(f(k,r)\).
Is \(f(k,7)=(1+o(1))\tfrac34 k\)? More generally, does
\(f(k,r)=(1+o(1))c_rk\) hold for each fixed \(r\ge 3\)?

## Literature
- Known exact small-\(r\) values: \(f(k,3)=2k\), \(f(k,4)=\lfloor3k/2\rfloor\), \(f(k,5)=\lfloor5k/4\rfloor\), \(f(k,6)=k\).
- The \(r\ge 7\) asymptotic constant regime remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built local-search generator of finite \(k\)-uniform families satisfying the \(r\)-tuple pair-hitting condition.
- For each generated family, computed exact transversal number and optimized for large transversal size.
- Targeted \(r=7\) finite probes.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Best finite ratios found:
  - \((v,k,r,m)=(10,4,7,9)\): \(\tau=3\), so \(\tau/k=0.75\).
  - \((11,4,7,10)\): \(\tau=3\), \(\tau/k=0.75\).
  - \((12,5,7,10)\): \(\tau=3\), \(\tau/k=0.6\).
- These are small finite witnesses and do not determine asymptotic \(c_7\), but they are compatible with sublinear-in-\(k\) constants below 1.
