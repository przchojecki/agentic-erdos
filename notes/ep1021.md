# EP-1021

## Problem Statement
For fixed $k$, ask whether every $n$-vertex graph with no subdivision of $K_k$ has at most
\[
O\!\left(n^{1+c_k}\right)
\]
edges for some constant $c_k>0$.

## Literature
- This is resolved affirmatively in the cited background literature.
- Conlon--Lee established the existential bound form.
- Janzer gave explicit improved exponent
\[
c_k=\frac{1}{4k-6},
\]
which directly implies the original existential statement.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Kept the theorem-level reduction (resolved as written).
- Added deeper finite consistency computation in the $k=3$ proxy case (subdivision relation with $C_6$-free behavior):
  - greedy $C_6$-free construction,
  - $n=16,20,\dots,56$,
  - $160$ trials per $n$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Best-edge ratios against $n^{7/6}$ stay bounded and trend downward over tested range (roughly $1.42$ at $n=16$ to $1.12$ at $n=56$ in this run).
- Finite profile is consistent with sub-$n^{3/2}$ growth expectations in the proxy model.

## Status
- Resolved in literature (problem statement true).
- Added deeper standalone finite consistency profile for the concrete $k=3$ proxy.
