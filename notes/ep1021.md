# EP-1021

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

### Working statement
For each fixed \(k\), determine whether every \(n\)-vertex graph with no subdivision of \(K_k\) has at most
\[
O\bigl(n^{1+c_k}\bigr)
\]
edges for some constant \(c_k>0\).

### Resolution status
This is resolved affirmatively in the literature cited in background:
- Conlon--Lee established the existential bound form for each fixed \(k\).
- Janzer improved the exponent to an explicit value
\[
c_k=\frac{1}{4k-6}.
\]

So the EP-1021 statement (existence of some positive \(c_k\) for each fixed \(k\)) is true.

### Formal reduction
The problem asks existence of *some* \(c_k>0\) such that
\(e(G)=O(n^{1+c_k})\) under the subdivision exclusion.
A theorem giving an explicit admissible \(c_k\) immediately implies the existential claim.
Therefore Janzer's explicit exponent implies the original EP statement as a corollary.

Finite computations in this repository are only consistency checks and are not needed for the proof-level conclusion.
