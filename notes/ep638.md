# EP-638

## Problem Statement
Let \(S\) be a family of finite graphs such that for every finite \(n\), some \(G_n\in S\)
forces a monochromatic triangle under every \(n\)-edge-coloring.
Must it follow that for every infinite cardinal \(\aleph\), there is a graph \(G\)
(all finite subgraphs in \(S\)) such that every \(\aleph\)-edge-coloring of \(G\)
contains a monochromatic triangle?

## Literature
- Erd\H{o}s posed this as a compactness-style Ramsey lifting question and noted broad consequences if true.
- No definitive positive/negative resolution is recorded in the local dataset note.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built finite-color Ramsey forcing profile experiments as a proxy diagnostic.
- For random \(q\)-colorings of \(K_n\), estimated frequency of monochromatic triangles across several \((q,n)\) grids.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For sampled ranges (including \(q=2,3,4,5\) at moderate/large \(n\)), monochromatic triangles appear with very high frequency, often essentially 1.
- This supports the robustness of finite forcing in dense complete-graph models, but does not address the core infinitary transfer mechanism.

## Status
- Open.
- Current script provides finite empirical profile only; no proof/counterexample for the infinite-cardinal statement.
