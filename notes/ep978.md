# EP-978

## Problem Statement
For irreducible $f\in\mathbb Z[x]$ of degree $k>2$ (with $k\neq 2^\ell$), one asks about $(k-1)$-power-free and $(k-2)$-power-free values.
Highlighted case: are there infinitely many $n$ such that
$$
n^4+2
$$
is squarefree?

## Literature
- Strong general results exist for large-degree regimes.
- The specific quartic squarefree infinitude question remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone deep two-layer computation for $n^4+2$:
  1. Exact squarefreeness by 64-bit Pollard-Rho factorization for $n\le 7000$.
  2. Large-$N$ proxy up to $n\le 10^6$ by filtering divisibility by $p^2$ for all primes $p\le 2000$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Exact layer (up to $7000$): squarefree density stays near $0.756$-$0.759$.
- Proxy layer (up to $10^6$): survivor density is extremely stable near $0.75672$.
- Finite data strongly supports a positive-density heuristic, but is not a proof of infinitude.
