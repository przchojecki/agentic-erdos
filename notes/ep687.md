# EP-687

## Problem Statement
Let \(Y(x)\) be the largest \(y\) such that one can choose residues \(a_p \pmod p\) for all primes \(p\le x\) covering every integer in \([1,y]\).
Estimate \(Y(x)\). In particular, can one prove \(Y(x)=o(x^2)\) or even \(Y(x)\ll x^{1+o(1)}\)?

## Literature
- Best known upper bound remains \(Y(x)\ll x^2\) (Iwaniec).
- Best known lower bound is superlinear by logarithmic factors (Ford--Green--Konyagin--Maynard--Tao).
- Conjectural growth is much closer to near-linear times polylog factors.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Ran deeper stochastic hillclimb over prime residue choices, with larger search caps and budgets than prior quick scans.
- Measured best covered prefix for \(x\) from 40 up to 200.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Best found \(Y(x)\) is still very small relative to \(x^2\) in tested range.
- Ratios \(Y(x)/x\) decrease with larger \(x\) in this finite search, indicating no visible drift toward quadratic scale.
- This finite behavior is compatible with subquadratic conjectural growth, but is not asymptotic evidence by itself.
