# EP-1039

## Problem Statement
Study the largest inscribed-disc radius $\rho$ in polynomial lemniscates
\[
\{z:\ |P(z)|<1\}
\]
for degree-$n$ polynomials with roots in the unit disk, and compare with the $1/n$-scale conjectural behavior.

## Literature
- Current bounds still fall short of a full uniform $1/n$ theorem in the strongest formulation.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Replaced batch stub with a standalone deep numerical estimator:
  - complex-grid sublevel sampling,
  - directional boundary binary search for local inscribed radius,
  - circle-root and random-root ensembles,
  - degrees $n=8,12,16,20$ with many random trials.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Circle-root runs give finite $n\rho$ values in a low constant range in this estimator.
- Random-root ensembles produce much larger $\rho$ in this search, showing strong configuration dependence.
- Numerical caveat: coarse gridding can bias absolute constants, so trends should be treated as heuristic.

## Status
- Open.
- Added deeper standalone lemniscate-radius computation with higher workload and explicit caveats.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Prove sharp \(1/n\)-scale bounds for maximal inscribed-disc radius \(\rho\) in \(\{|P|<1\}\) for degree-\(n\) polynomials with roots in \(\overline{\mathbb D}\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Show a universal distortion estimate: for any such \(P\), each connected component of \(\{|P|<1\}\) has inradius at least \(c/n\) (or identify the extremal component controlling the maximum \(\rho\)).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (log-subharmonic framework)
\(\log|P(z)|\) is subharmonic, and \(\{|P|<1\}\) is an open sublevel set.

**Proof.**
\(|P|\) is modulus of holomorphic function; \(\log|P|\) is subharmonic off zeros and extends as subharmonic globally. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (component conformal mapping)
On a simply connected component \(U\subset\{|P|<1\}\), conformal radius at a point controls Euclidean inscribed-disc radius up to universal constants (Koebe-type).

**Proof.**
Apply Koebe quarter theorem to Riemann map from disk to \(U\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies \(1/n\) bound)
If A gives conformal-radius lower bound \( \gtrsim 1/n\), then Euclidean inradius \(\rho\gtrsim 1/n\) follows by B2.

**Proof.**
Translate conformal to Euclidean scale using B2 constants. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is obtaining uniform conformal-radius control across all root configurations, especially highly clustered roots.
