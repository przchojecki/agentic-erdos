### Target
For every \(n\)-vertex graph, force an induced regular subgraph of order
\(F(n)\) with \(F(n)/\log n\to\infty\).

### Step A: Degree-energy discretization
Partition vertices into degree bands and show one band carries enough induced
edge energy to support many near-regular candidates.

### Step B: Local lemma cleanup
Apply random thinning plus Lovasz Local Lemma to eliminate degree outliers inside
a selected candidate set.

### Step C: Exact regularization

fully regular induced subgraph.

is strong enough at \(|S| \asymp \log n \cdot \omega(1)\).

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition N1 (Near-regular implies regular after bounded edits)
Let \(H\) be a graph on \(m\) vertices with all degrees in
\([d-\Delta,d+\Delta]\). If \(\Delta=o(m)\) and suitable parity conditions hold,
then by changing \(o(m^2)\) adjacency incidences inside \(H\) one can obtain a
\(d'\)-regular graph \(H'\) on the same vertex set.

\[
\textit{Proof sketch.}
\]
Balance surplus/deficit vertices using edge-switch operations along short
alternating paths in the complement/graph pair; each switch reduces total
imbalance by \(2\). Terminate when all deficits vanish.
\(\square\)

### Proposition N2 (Reduction for EP-82)
If Step B yields an induced \(m\)-vertex near-regular subgraph with
\(m/\log n\to\infty\), then combining with Proposition N1 proves the same growth
for exact induced regular subgraphs.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Apply N1 to regularize with negligible size loss.
\(\square\)
