# EP-1038

## Problem Statement
For monic real polynomials with all roots in $[-1,1]$, study extremal values of
\[
\left|\{x:\ |f(x)|<1\}\right|.
\]

## Literature
- Reported status: supremum understood, infimum still not pinned exactly.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added deep standalone numerical scan:
  - degree range $n=6,8,10,12,14$,
  - structured roots (Chebyshev/equally spaced),
  - random root search (1250 trials per $n$),
  - grid-based length estimation of $\{|f|<1\}$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In this finite search, random-root minima were around $2.01$-$2.10$ over tested $n$.
- Structured configurations were consistently larger (about $2.41$-$2.50$ in this setup).
- Results are numerical and depend on search space + discretization; they do not settle the exact infimum.

## Status
- Exact infimum remains open.
- Added deeper standalone extremal-search numerics in normalized per-problem form.
