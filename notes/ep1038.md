# EP-1038

## Problem Statement
For monic real polynomials with all roots in $[-1,1]$, study extremal values of
\[
\left|\{x:\ |f(x)|<1\}\right|.
\]

## Literature
- Reported status: supremum understood, infimum still not pinned exactly.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added deep standalone numerical scan:
  - degree range $n=6,8,10,12,14$,
  - structured roots (Chebyshev/equally spaced),
  - random root search (1250 trials per $n$),
  - grid-based length estimation of $\{|f|<1\}$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In this finite search, random-root minima were around $2.01$-$2.10$ over tested $n$.
- Structured configurations were consistently larger (about $2.41$-$2.50$ in this setup).
- Results are numerical and depend on search space + discretization; they do not settle the exact infimum.

## Status
- Exact infimum remains open.
- Added deeper standalone extremal-search numerics in normalized per-problem form.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine the exact infimum of
\[
|\{x\in[-1,1]: |f(x)|<1\}|
\]
over monic real degree-\(n\) polynomials with roots in \([-1,1]\), and asymptotics as \(n\to\infty\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a potential-theoretic barrier: for any admissible \(f\), the sublevel set \(\{|f|<1\}\) must contain a union of intervals with total length at least \(2+\varepsilon_n\), with explicit \(\varepsilon_n\to0\) (or matching constant if different).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (root-factor form)
\[
f(x)=\prod_{j=1}^n (x-r_j),\qquad r_j\in[-1,1].
\]

**Proof.**
Monic polynomial with all roots in \([-1,1]\) factors over \(\mathbb R\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (log-potential representation)
\[
\log|f(x)|=\sum_{j=1}^n \log|x-r_j|,
\]
so \(|f(x)|<1\) is equivalent to negativity of this potential sum.

**Proof.**
Take logarithm of absolute value of product in B1. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies extremal lower bound)
If A gives universal length lower bound \(L_n\), then infimum is at least \(L_n\); matching constructions give equality/asymptotics.

**Proof.**
Lower bound applies to every admissible \(f\); infimum over \(f\) inherits it. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The key gap is turning potential-theoretic average control into sharp measure lower bounds for one-dimensional real sublevel sets.
