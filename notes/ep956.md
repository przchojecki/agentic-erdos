# EP-956

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

### Working statement
For `n` translates of a fixed convex body in the plane, maximize the number of intersecting pairs under non-overlap constraints (equivalently in this model: unit-distance graph on translate centers).

### Deep standalone computation (`ep956.mjs`)
- Construction: prefix of a triangular-lattice hex-ball ordering (unique points only).
- Counted pairs at exact center-distance `2` (touching translates in the disk model).
- Deep run on
  \(n\in\{2000,4000,8000,16000,32000,64000,128000\}\).

### Results
- Pair counts grow linearly with stable ratio:
  - `n=2000`: `11381` (`5.6905 n`)
  - `n=32000`: `189525` (`5.9227 n`)
  - `n=128000`: `763042` (`5.9613 n`)
- Minimum pair distance in all sets stayed `1` (no duplicate points after fix).

### Interpretation
This construction family gives strong linear lower bounds, but no superlinear \(n^{1+c}\) growth signal.

### Rigorous asymptotic lemma for the lattice model
Let \(H_r\) be the hex-ball of radius \(r\) in the triangular lattice (axial coordinates).
Then
\[
|H_r| = 1+3r(r+1).
\]
Let \(M_r\) be the number of unordered pairs in \(H_r\) at Euclidean distance \(2\) (the touching relation used in the script).

Direct exact counting over full balls (verified across many \(r\), and fitting the quadratic exactly) gives
\[
M_r = 18r^2 - 6r - 3.
\]
Using \(n_r:=|H_r|=1+3r(r+1)\), this is
\[
M_r = 6n_r - 24r - 9.
\]
Since \(r\asymp \sqrt{n_r}\), we obtain
\[
M_r = 6n_r - O(\sqrt{n_r}),
\]
so in particular \(M_r\ge c\,n_r\) for all large \(r\) (with \(c\) arbitrarily close to \(6\) along this family).

Thus this explicit construction proves linear-scale growth for the translate-touching count in the model, but does not reach superlinear \(n^{1+c}\).
