## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
For every \(k\)-chromatic graph \(G\), prove a uniform lower comparison
\[
R(G)\ge c\,R(k)
\]
or the stronger \((1-\varepsilon)^k R(k)\)-type inequality.

### Step A: Core extraction
From \(G\), extract a \(k\)-critical core \(G^\star\) with controlled size-growth
relative to \(k\).

### Step B: Embedding-resistant coloring model
Construct 2-colorings on \(N\) vertices that simultaneously avoid monochromatic
copies of all graphs in a complexity-bounded family containing \(G^\star\).

### Step C: Transfer to \(R(k)\)-scale
Show admissible \(N\) is a fixed proportion of the diagonal Ramsey threshold
scale for \(K_k\), yielding the desired comparison.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a universal container/entropy lemma for monochromatic embeddings of
\(k\)-critical graphs that is sharp enough to compare to \(K_k\) thresholds.

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition Q1 (Critical-core reduction)
Let \(G\) be \(k\)-chromatic and \(G^\star\subseteq G\) a \(k\)-critical
subgraph. Then
\[
R(G)\ge R(G^\star).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Any monochromatic copy of \(G\) contains a monochromatic copy of \(G^\star\).
Hence avoiding monochromatic \(G^\star\) implies avoiding monochromatic \(G\),
so the Ramsey threshold for \(G\) is at least that for \(G^\star\).
\(\square\)

### Proposition Q2 (Scale-transfer criterion)
Assume there exists \(c>0\) such that for every \(k\)-critical \(H\), one has
\[
R(H)\ge c\,R(k).
\]
Then for every \(k\)-chromatic \(G\),
\[
R(G)\ge c\,R(k).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Apply Proposition Q1 to \(G^\star\subseteq G\), then use the hypothesis on
\(G^\star\).
\(\square\)
