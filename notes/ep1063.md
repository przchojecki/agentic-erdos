# EP-1063

## Problem Statement
For each $k$, let $n_k$ be the least $n$ such that among
\[
(n-i)\mid \binom{n}{k},\quad i=0,1,\dots,k-1,
\]
exactly one divisibility relation fails. Study growth and structure of $n_k$.

## Literature
- Problem remains open; known values show irregular behavior and broad upper bounds.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Replaced batch snippet with standalone exact prime-valuation engine:
  - checks divisibility via $p$-adic valuations of binomial coefficients,
  - scans full range rather than stopping at first hit,
  - records first several solutions per $k$.
- Deep run parameters:
  - $k\le 32$,
  - $n\le 1{,}280{,}000$ for each $k$,
  - full-range scan completed (no timeout rows).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Recovered known small values ($n_2=4$, $n_3=6$, $n_4=9$, $n_5=12$).
- Confirmed strong non-monotonicity for larger $k$.
- In scanned range, some $k$ (e.g. $k=30,32$) had no found solution up to $1.28\times10^6$, while nearby $k$ did, highlighting irregular local structure.
