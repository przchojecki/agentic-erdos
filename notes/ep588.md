# EP-588

## Problem Statement
Given \(n\) points in the plane with no \(k+1\) collinear points, bound the maximum possible number of lines containing at least \(k\) points, and determine whether this count is \(o(n^2)\) for fixed \(k\ge 4\).

## Literature / Context
- Known constructions keep lower bounds comparatively high for fixed \(k\), so proving strong subquadratic decay is delicate.
- The asymptotic status remains open in the regime targeted here.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built a deeper local-search optimizer over point sets with a hard no-\((k+1)\)-collinear constraint.
- Objective: maximize number of \(k\)-rich (or richer) lines for \(k=4,5\) at growing \(n\).
- No theorem-level bound is proved yet.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Deep run (\(\sim 14.4\)s):
  - \(k=4\): best \(\#\{\ge k\text{-rich lines}\}/n^2\) drops from \(\approx 0.0189\) at \(n=30\) to \(\approx 0.0140\) at \(n=50\).
  - \(k=5\): values around \(0.0068\) to \(0.0072\) in tested window.
- Finite behavior remains non-negligible and does not yet show a sharp collapse.

## Next Proof Targets
- Relate local-search extremizers to structured constructions (grids/projective-type patterns).
- Prove a rigidity lemma forcing reduced \(k\)-rich-line density under no-\((k+1)\)-collinear constraints.
