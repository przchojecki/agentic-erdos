## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Eventual positivity criterion)
If there exists \(N_0\) such that \(f(n)\ge2\) for all \(n\ge N_0\), then
\(\liminf_{n\to\infty} f(n)\ge2\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
By definition of liminf and tail lower bound.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Counterexample criterion)
If infinitely many \(n\) satisfy \(f(n)=0\), then eventual \(f(n)\ge2\) fails.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Any eventual lower bound is contradicted by infinitely many zeros.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction)
To prove eventual \(f(n)\ge2\), it suffices to show only finitely many \(n\) have
\(f(n)\le1\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Then beyond largest such \(n\), all values satisfy \(f(n)\ge2\).
\(\square\)
