## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition I1 (Liminf from scale-wise lower bounds)
If there exist \(c>0\), \(N_0\) such that for all \(N\ge N_0\),
\[
F(N)\ge c\,\Phi(N),
\]
then
\[
\liminf_{N\to\infty}\frac{F(N)}{\Phi(N)}\ge c.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Immediate from definition of liminf.
\(\square\)

### Proposition I2 (Counterexample criterion)
If there exists \(N_j\to\infty\) with
\[
\frac{F(N_j)}{\Phi(N_j)}\to 0,
\]
then any conjectured positive liminf lower bound fails.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Directly, liminf over all \(N\) is at most limit along subsequence \(N_j\), hence
equals \(0\) or less than any claimed positive constant.
\(\square\)

So the open mathematics is establishing uniform lower bounds of I1 type (or a
true I2-type construction), not the implication logic.
