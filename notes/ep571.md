# EP-571

## Problem Statement
Determine whether every rational \(\alpha \in [1,2)\) can occur as a Turan exponent of a single bipartite graph.

## Literature / Context
- Known constructions realize many rational exponents through several parameterized families.
- The frontier is full coverage of all rationals in \([1,2)\), not just dense subfamilies.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built a denominator-bounded coverage map from currently implemented exponent families.
- This gives an explicit finite testbed for where known constructions already hit and where gaps remain.
- No proof of complete coverage (or obstruction) yet.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Coverage remains partial across all tested denominator bounds.
- Uncovered rationals persist in low denominators, indicating the known families in the script are still insufficient for full closure.

## Next Proof Targets
- Add additional exponent families from recent constructions and retest finite coverage.
- Group persistent misses by arithmetic pattern (e.g., residue/continued-fraction features) to guide new construction attempts.
