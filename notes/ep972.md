# EP-972

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

### Working statement
For irrational \(\alpha>1\), are there infinitely many primes \(p\) such that \(\lfloor \alpha p\rfloor\) is also prime?

### Exact reformulations
Define
\[
N_\alpha(x):=\#\{p\le x:\ p\text{ prime},\ \lfloor \alpha p\rfloor\text{ prime}\}.
\]
Then EP-972 is equivalent to
\[
N_\alpha(x)\to\infty.
\]

For a prime \(q\),
\[
\lfloor \alpha p\rfloor=q
\iff
\frac{q}{\alpha}\le p < \frac{q+1}{\alpha}.
\]
Hence
\[
N_\alpha(x)=\#\left\{q\le \alpha x\text{ prime}:\left[\frac q\alpha,\frac{q+1}{\alpha}\right)\cap\mathbb P\neq\varnothing\right\}.
\]

### Monotonicity/injectivity lemma
For \(\alpha>1\), the map
\(p\mapsto \lfloor \alpha p\rfloor\) on primes is strictly increasing.

Proof:
If \(p_2>p_1\), then
\(\alpha p_2-\alpha p_1\ge \alpha>1\), so
\(\lfloor \alpha p_2\rfloor>\lfloor \alpha p_1\rfloor\).

So each target prime \(q\) can come from at most one source prime \(p\).

### Interpretation
These exact rewrites turn the problem into a prime-occurrence question in short Beatty-type intervals. They clarify structure but do not yet prove infinitude.
