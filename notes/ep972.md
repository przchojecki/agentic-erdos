# EP-972

## Problem Statement
Let $\alpha>1$ be irrational. Are there infinitely many primes $p$ such that
$$
\lfloor \alpha p\rfloor
$$
is also prime?

## Literature
- Vinogradov's distribution results imply many Beatty-type prime occurrences, but this specific prime-to-prime floor map remains open in general.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone deep multi-$\alpha$ finite scan up to $X=60{,}000{,}000$.
- For each selected irrational $\alpha$, computed
$$
N_\alpha(x)=\#\{p\le x:\ p,\lfloor\alpha p\rfloor\ \text{both prime}\}
$$
at large checkpoints.
- Tested 12 irrational choices (including $\sqrt2,\sqrt3,\pi,e,\varphi,\sqrt5$ and others).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Counts grow substantially for all tested $\alpha$ up to $x=6\times 10^7$.
- Ratios $N_\alpha(x)/\pi(x)$ in this range are stable near about $0.056$-$0.060$ across tested $\alpha$.
- Heuristic normalization against $x/\log^2 x$ is around constant scale in this finite regime, consistent with expected sparse prime-pair behavior.
