# EP-445 partial attempt

## Route
Ran finite prime scans for interval length `p^c` using sampled shift classes `n mod p`, checking existence of `a,b` with `ab=1 mod p`.

## Evidence from this batch
- `data/ep445_inverse_interval_scan.json` (`p<=20000`):
  - sampled success ratio near `0` for `c=0.55`,
  - about `0.84` for `c=0.65`,
  - about `0.994` for `c=0.70`,
  - full sampled success for `c>=0.75`.

## What is resolved from background
- Rigorous theorem already known for all `c>3/4`.

## Hard point
Bridging from finite/sampled near-threshold behavior to a proof for every `c>1/2` remains open.

## Status
Partial theorem known (`c>3/4`), lower-threshold extension open.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Lower the known exponent threshold from \(c>3/4\) toward conjectural \(c>1/2\) for guaranteed inverse pairs in intervals of length \(p^c\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a bilinear equidistribution estimate:
\[
\#\{a\in I,\ b\in J:ab\equiv1\!\!\!\pmod p\}
=\frac{|I||J|}{p}+O(p^{-\eta}|I||J|)
\]
uniformly for \(|I|,|J|\ge p^{1/2+\delta}\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (inverse-graph counting)
Condition \(ab\equiv1\pmod p\) identifies points of graph \(b=a^{-1}\) over \(\mathbb F_p^\times\) inside interval boxes.

**Proof.**
Invertibility gives unique inverse map; counting solutions is incidence with inverse graph. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (main-term positivity criterion)
If \(|I||J|/p\) dominates error term, at least one solution exists.

**Proof.**
Count equals main term plus error; positivity gives existence. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies threshold improvement)
Applying B2 with \(|I|=|J|=p^c\) and A yields existence for \(c>1/2+\delta\), improving the guaranteed exponent.

**Proof.**
Main term is \(p^{2c-1}\); with power-saving relative error from A, positivity holds above threshold. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The key obstacle is obtaining uniform bilinear cancellation near square-root interval lengths.
