## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Settle finiteness of \(\Lambda(k,3)\) for odd \(k\ge5\) (with \(\Lambda(k,2)\) already solved).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a character-sum rigidity lemma: for fixed odd \(k\ge5\), long runs of consecutive \(k\)-th power residues modulo \(p\) force an exceptional multiplicative-subgroup alignment that can occur only for finitely many \(p\).

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (residue-run interpretation)
\(\Lambda(k,3)\) asks for maximal length of consecutive integers all in \(k\)-th-power residue set (with 0-convention fixed).

**Proof.**
Direct translation of definition. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (character encoding)
Membership in \(k\)-th powers mod \(p\) is detected by multiplicative characters of order dividing \((p-1)/\gcd(k,p-1)\).

**Proof.**
Indicator of subgroup can be expressed as average of characters over its dual. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies finiteness)
If A bounds admissible \(p\) for run length \(>L(k)\), then \(\Lambda(k,3)\) is finite.

**Proof.**
Take maximum over finite exceptional prime set and bounded lengths elsewhere. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is obtaining a uniform anti-concentration estimate for short linear patterns of subgroup indicators strong enough to forbid arbitrarily long runs.
