# EP-436 partial attempt

## Route
Separated the solved and unsolved parts from background, then ran a finite-prime scan for `m=3` and odd `k` under the convention that `0` counts as a `k`th-power residue.

## Evidence from this batch
- `data/ep436_lambda_scan.json` up to primes `p <= 20000`:
  - `k=3`: max observed `r=281`
  - `k=5`: max observed `r=3329`
  - `k=7`: max observed `r=6832`
  - `k=9`: max observed `r=17189`

## What is resolved
The first question (`\Lambda(k,2)` finite for all `k`) is already solved in background (Hildebrand, 1991).

## Hard point
For odd `k >= 5`, finiteness of `\Lambda(k,3)` is still open; finite scans show growth but do not determine boundedness.

## Status
Partially resolved historically; remaining `m=3` odd-`k` part still open.
