# EP-332 partial

## Statement
For `A subseteq N`, let `D(A)` be differences occurring infinitely often as
`a1-a2` with `a1,a2 in A`. Find sufficient conditions ensuring bounded gaps in
`D(A)`.

## Attempt in this batch
I ran finite proxies on several families (`squares`, `primes`, `powers of 2`,
random sparse/dense sets), tracking difference-frequency gap behavior.

Data file:
- `data/ep332_difference_set_gap_scan.json`

## Result
Finite scans show dense/random sets quickly produce very small difference gaps,
while sparse structured sets (e.g. powers of 2) keep large gaps under stricter
frequency thresholds.

This is consistent with the background point that positive density is a strong
sufficient condition.

## Hard point
Finite frequency proxies are not equivalent to the infinite-occurrence definition
of `D(A)`, so this does not produce new sharp sufficient criteria.
