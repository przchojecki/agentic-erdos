# EP-911

## Problem Statement
Let $\hat{R}(G)$ denote the size Ramsey number of $G$, i.e. the minimum number of edges
$m$ such that there exists a graph $H$ with $m$ edges and
$H \to (G,G)$ (every red/blue edge-coloring of $H$ has a monochromatic copy of $G$).

Is there a function $f$ with
\[
\frac{f(x)}{x}\to\infty \quad (x\to\infty)
\]
such that for all sufficiently large $C$, whenever $G$ has $n$ vertices and
$e(G)\ge Cn$, one has
\[
\hat{R}(G) > f(C)\,e(G)?
\]

## Literature
- The local dataset entry for EP-911 is malformed, but the intended statement above is recoverable from the readable prefix.
- For cliques, the size-Ramsey identity
\[
\hat{R}(K_t)=\binom{R(t,t)}{2}
\]
is classical (often attributed to Chvátal).
- Classical Ramsey bounds used computationally here:
\[
R(t,t) > 2^{t/2}
\]
(Erdős probabilistic lower bound), and
\[
R(t,t)\le \binom{2t-2}{t-1}
\]
(Erdős-Szekeres upper bound).

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Added standalone family computation (`scripts/ep911.mjs`) on dense graphs $G=K_t$:
  - computes $C=(t-1)/2$ and $e=\binom t2$;
  - converts Ramsey bounds into explicit lower/upper bounds on
    $\hat{R}(K_t)/e$ via $\hat{R}(K_t)=\binom{R(t,t)}2$;
  - tracks growth of $\bigl(\hat{R}(K_t)/e\bigr)/C$.

## Computation-Guided Observations
Deep run (`DEPTH=5`, `TMAX=80`) finds rapid growth in the clique family:
- already for moderate $t$, lower bounds force $\hat{R}(K_t)/e$ to dominate $C$ strongly;
- at $t=80$ ($C=39.5$), the lower-bound ratio satisfies
\[
\frac{\hat{R}(K_t)}{e} \gtrsim 1.9\times 10^{20},
\qquad
\frac{\hat{R}(K_t)/e}{C}\gtrsim 4.8\times10^{18}.
\]

This supports a strong positive trend on one dense family but does not resolve the
full EP-911 quantifier over all dense graphs.
