# EP-661

## Problem Statement
For large \(n\), do there exist \(x_1,\dots,x_n,y_1,\dots,y_n\in\mathbb R^2\) such that
\[
\#\{d(x_i,y_j):1\le i,j\le n\}=o\!\left(\frac{n}{\sqrt{\log n}}\right)?
\]

## Literature
- In \(\mathbb R^4\), Lenz-type constructions give constant cross-distance count (all \(d(x_i,y_j)=1\)).
- The planar version remains open in the listed background.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented explicit planar bipartite-construction scan:
  - two parallel lines,
  - line vs parabola,
  - two circles,
  - grid split.
- Measured distinct cross-distances \(D\) and normalized quantity \(D\sqrt{\log n}/n\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- `line_vs_parabola` has very large \(D\) (far from target).
- `two_circles` gives \(D\approx n\) (still much larger than \(n/\sqrt{\log n}\)-scale target).
- `grid_split` is best among tested explicit families (e.g. \(D/n\approx 0.456\) at \(n=360\)), but still not in little-o\((n/\sqrt{\log n})\) regime in tested sizes.

## Status
- Open.
- No tested planar explicit family here reaches the target asymptotic scale.
