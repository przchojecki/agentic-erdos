# EP-626

## Problem Statement
Let \(k\ge 4\), and \(g_k(n)\) be the largest \(m\) for which there exists an \(n\)-vertex graph with
\(\chi(G)=k\) and girth \(>m\). Does
\[
\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{g_k(n)}{\log n}
\]
exist?

Conversely, if \(h^{(m)}(n)\) is the maximal chromatic number of an \(n\)-vertex graph with girth \(>m\), does
\[
\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{\log h^{(m)}(n)}{\log n}
\]
exist, and what is its value?

## Literature
- Known two-sided bounds for \(g_k(n)\) are logarithmic in \(n\) (Kostochka lower bound, Erd\H{o}s upper bound).
- For \(h^{(m)}(n)\), Erd\H{o}s proved exponent-range bounds and conjectured sharpness in odd \(m\).
- Exact limits remain unresolved.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built deeper standalone random maximal short-cycle-free graph generator (forbidden cycle thresholds \(m=4,5,6\)).
- Combined DSATUR upper bounds with bounded exact colorability checks for small \(c\) to get partial certified chromatic windows.
- Produced sample-based lower-bound proxies for \(g_4(n)\) at tested \(n\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For threshold \(m=4\) at \(n=42,54\), many instances were certified with exact \(\chi=4\), and girth \(=5\).
- For thresholds \(m=5,6\) in tested ranges, samples were consistently 3-colorable by the implemented checks.
- Sample-derived proxy rows yield \(g_4(n)\ge 4\) at \(n=42,54\) (from found \(\chi=4\), girth \(5\) examples).

## Status
- Open.
- Current work gives finite certified witnesses and heuristic scaling signals, not asymptotic limit existence.
