# EP-685

## Problem Statement
For \(n^\varepsilon<k\le n^{1-\varepsilon}\), estimate the number of distinct prime divisors of \(\binom nk\).
Is it
\[
(1+o(1))\,k\sum_{k<p<n}\frac1p
\]
in this mid-range?

## Literature
- Baseline: number of prime divisors \(> \log\binom nk / \log n\).
- Near-top \(k\)-range has asymptotic sharpness for this baseline.
- Main mid-range formula remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Computed \(\omega\!\left(\binom nk\right)\) by prime-exponent tests across a grid of \((n,k)\).
- Compared against heuristic \(k\sum_{k<p<n}1/p\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In tested ranges, \(\omega\!\left(\binom nk\right)\) is consistently larger than the simple heuristic by a moderate factor.
- Ratios vary with \((n,k)\), suggesting significant secondary-term structure in finite regimes.
- This does not decide the asymptotic formula but helps calibrate finite-scale deviations.

## Status
- Open for the proposed broad mid-range asymptotic.
