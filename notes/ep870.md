# EP-870

## Problem Statement
For additive bases of order $k\ge 3$, does a logarithmic lower bound on representation counts force existence of a minimal basis of order $k$?

## Literature
- Erdos-Nathanson proved a sharp-type positive theorem for $k=2$ under explicit logarithmic representation threshold.
- The $k\ge3$ extension remains open.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built a finite-horizon $k=3$ proxy:
  - sampled random subsets $A\subseteq[1..N]$,
  - measured triple-sum representation counts on interval $[L,U]$,
  - checked existence of finite minimal 3-basis cores for that interval.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- In both tested regimes, full interval coverage was frequently achieved.
- High minimum finite representation counts were observed (best min-rep around 69 in these windows).
- Finite minimal cores were found in nearly all/full successful trials.
- These are finite analog signals only, not an infinite $k\ge3$ theorem.

## Status
- Open for $k\ge3$.
- Added standalone finite threshold-vs-minimal-core probe and normalized data integration.
