## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
With
\[
A(x):=\#\{n\le x:\ P(n)<P(n+1)\},
\]
prove
\[
\frac{A(x)}{x}\to \frac12.
\]

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Show a quantitative decorrelation estimate
\[
\sum_{n\le x}\Big(\mathbf 1_{P(n)<P(n+1)}-\tfrac12\Big)=o(x),
\]
ideally with power saving \(O(x^{1-\delta})\).

This single statement implies the desired asymptotic density.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (tie decomposition)
Define
\[
T(x):=\#\{n\le x:\ P(n)=P(n+1)\}.
\]
Then
\[
A(x)+A'(x)+T(x)=x+O(1),
\]
where \(A'(x):=\#\{n\le x:\ P(n)>P(n+1)\}\).

**Proof.**
For each \(n\), exactly one of \(<,=,>\) holds between \(P(n)\) and \(P(n+1)\). Summing indicator functions over \(n\le x\) gives the identity. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (symmetry reduction via small tie set)
If \(T(x)=o(x)\) and
\[
A(x)-A'(x)=o(x),
\]
then \(A(x)=\frac{x}{2}+o(x)\).

**Proof.**
From Lemma B1, \(A+A'=x-T+O(1)=x+o(x)\). Solving the linear system with \(A-A'=o(x)\) yields \(A=x/2+o(x)\). \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies B2 immediately)
The blocking lemma in A implies \(A(x)=x/2+o(x)\).

**Proof.**
The sum in A is exactly \(A(x)-x/2+O(1)\), so it being \(o(x)\) gives the conclusion. \(\square\)

### C. What still blocks a proof

The core missing input is a distribution theorem for the largest-prime-factor comparison process \(P(n)\) vs. \(P(n+1)\) with enough cancellation in signed sums. Current methods give strong averaged or logarithmic-density evidence, but not the full quantitative decorrelation needed for natural density \(1/2\).
