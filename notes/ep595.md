# EP-595 partial

## Statement
Is there an infinite `K_4`-free graph that is not the union of countably many
triangle-free graphs?

## Attempt in this batch
I reformulated the covering condition as an edge-coloring viewpoint:
"union of countably many triangle-free graphs" is equivalent to
countable edge-coloring with no monochromatic triangle.
I then tried to lift known finite lower bounds to an infinite witness.

Background already gives strong finite information:
for every finite `n`, there exists a finite `K_4`-free graph that is not a union
of `n` triangle-free graphs.

## Result
Finite color demand is unbounded in the known finite family, but that alone
does not force existence of a graph requiring uncountably many layers.

## Hard point
Standard compactness does not directly transfer "not a union of countably many"
because countability is not a finite first-order constraint.
The gap is from arbitrarily large finite requirements to failure of all
countable decompositions in a single infinite graph.
