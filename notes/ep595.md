# EP-595

## Problem Statement
Is there an infinite \(K_4\)-free graph that is not the union of countably many triangle-free graphs?

## Literature / Context
- Finite analogues show arbitrarily large finite triangle-free-layer demand in \(K_4\)-free graphs.
- The infinitary jump to failure of all countable decompositions is the unresolved core.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Used the edge-coloring reformulation: decomposition into triangle-free layers equals edge-coloring with no monochromatic triangle.
- Implemented exact backtracking to find minimal triangle-free layer count on dense finite \(K_4\)-free samples.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For dense \(K_4\)-free samples at \(n=10,11,12\), the exact minimum layers found is \(2\).
- This confirms nontrivial layering pressure in finite windows, but does not approach the countable-vs-uncountable barrier.

## Next Proof Targets
- Build explicit finite families with provably growing minimal layer count (not just sampled instances).
- Investigate non-compactness mechanisms that can convert unbounded finite requirements into a true countable obstruction.
