## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine whether the longest prime arithmetic progression length \(L(N)\) up to
\(N\) satisfies \(L(N)=o(\log N)\), or identify the correct order.

### Step A: Upper-bound sieve framework
Bound counts of long prime APs by majorant/sieve weights with uniform control in
length \(k\).

### Step B: Lower construction/heuristic calibration
Build admissible-pattern lower bounds that certify occurrence of \(k\)-term APs at
chosen scales.

### Step C: Threshold inversion
Translate AP-count bounds into asymptotics for maximal \(k=L(N)\).

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a uniform upper bound for \(k\)-term prime AP counts with explicit dependence
on \(k\) strong enough to compete with combinatorial \(N^2\)-progression growth.

## LaTeX Proofs for A => B => C Skeleton

### Proposition CD1 (Count-to-maximum reduction)
Let \(A_k(N)\) be number of \(k\)-term prime APs with all terms \(\le N\). Then
\[
L(N)<k \iff A_k(N)=0.
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
By definition, \(L(N)\) is maximum \(k\) with at least one such progression.
\(\square\)

### Proposition CD2 (Asymptotic reduction)
If one proves \(A_k(N)=0\) whenever \(k\ge \phi(N)\), then \(L(N)<\phi(N)\). If
one also proves \(A_k(N)>0\) for \(k\le \psi(N)\), then
\[
\psi(N)\le L(N)<\phi(N).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Apply CD1 to upper and lower sides.
\(\square\)
