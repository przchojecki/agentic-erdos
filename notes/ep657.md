# EP-657

## Problem Statement
If \(A\subset\mathbb R^2\) has \(|A|=n\) and no isosceles triangle (every 3-point subset determines 3 distinct distances), must \(A\) determine at least \(f(n)\,n\) distinct distances with \(f(n)\to\infty\)?

## Literature
- This is tied to the 1D additive formulation: minimize distinct differences in an AP-free set.
- Known bounds imply unbounded growth of the corresponding factor, with large gaps between lower and upper growth rates.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Ran deeper standalone 1D AP-free surrogate optimization (larger \(n\), larger ambient ranges, higher iteration budgets).
- Objective: minimize number of distinct differences in AP-free sets of size \(n\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- For \(n=10\) to \(26\), best distinct-difference counts grow from about \(22\) to \(92\).
- Normalized ratio \(\#\Delta(A)/n\) in these optimized finite runs is around \(2.2\) to \(3.54\), increasing with tested \(n\).
- Finite surrogate behavior is consistent with growth beyond a fixed constant, but does not resolve the true asymptotic rate of \(f(n)\).
