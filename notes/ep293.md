## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Monotonicity in \(k\))
If \(v(k)\) denotes the first missing denominator at parameter \(k\), then
\[
v(k+1)\ge v(k).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Increasing \(k\) weakens restrictions/expands allowable representations (or at
worst keeps them equal), so the first missing point cannot move left.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (First-missing characterization)
For each \(k\), if \(A_k\subset\mathbb N\) is attainable denominator set, then
\[
v(k)=\min(\mathbb N\setminus A_k).
\]
Hence proving \([1,M]\subseteq A_k\) implies \(v(k)>M\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
This is the definition of first missing element.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Reduction to interval saturation)
To prove a lower bound \(v(k)\ge F(k)\), it is enough to show
\([1,F(k)-1]\subseteq A_k\).

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Apply Lemma 2 with \(M=F(k)-1\).
\(\square\)
