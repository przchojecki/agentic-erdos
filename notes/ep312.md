# EP-312 partial resolution

## Statement
For large reciprocal-sum multisets `A`, does one always find a submultiset `S`
with

`1 - exp(-cK) < sum_{n in S} 1/n <= 1`?

## What is resolved
Background says Erd"os-Graham proved a weaker quantitative form with
`exp(-cK)` replaced by a term of order `1/K^2`.

## What remains open in this note
The stronger exponential-accuracy gap in the statement is not proved in the
provided background.

## Attempt in this batch
Tried to refine the near-`1` subset-sum approximation route beyond polynomial
error, but no new quantitative bound was derived.

## Status
- polynomial-size gap near 1: known.
- exponential-size gap near 1: unresolved.

## New deep computation (2026-03-10)

Script: `scripts/ep312.mjs`  
Method: exact meet-in-the-middle search for
\[
\max\Big\{\sum_{n\in S}\frac1n:\ S\subseteq A,\ \sum_{n\in S}\frac1n\le 1\Big\}
\]
on selected denominator families \(A\).

### Model sanity check: initial segments \(A=\{2,\dots,N\}\)
For tested \(N\), the optimum is exactly \(1\) (e.g. via \(1/2+1/3+1/6=1\)),
so this family is too easy and not informative for the sharp gap question.

### Nontrivial family: shifted blocks \(A=\{s,s+1,\dots,s+21\}\)
Exact results:
- \(s=6\): best \(=0.9999976129\), gap \(=2.39\times 10^{-6}\).
- \(s=7\): best \(=0.9999974099\), gap \(=2.59\times 10^{-6}\).
- \(s=8\): best \(=0.9999995803\), gap \(=4.20\times 10^{-7}\).
- \(s=10\): best \(=0.9999884993\), gap \(=1.15\times 10^{-5}\).
- \(s=12\): best \(=0.9998732618\), gap \(=1.27\times 10^{-4}\).
- \(s=15\): total reciprocal sum already \(<1\), so best equals total and gap
  jumps to \(7.70\times 10^{-2}\).

Also \(s=3,4,5\) hit exact \(1\) in this block model.

## Interpretation
Finite exact data shows a sharp transition:
- in medium blocks where total reciprocal mass exceeds 1, one can often get
  extremely close to 1 (sometimes exactly 1);
- once total mass drops below 1, the problem becomes trivial (best equals total).

This supports the known polynomial-gap phenomenon but does not provide evidence
for the stronger universal exponential-gap bound in the EP-312 statement.
