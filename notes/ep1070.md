# EP-1070

## Problem Statement
Study extremal independence behavior for high-chromatic unit-distance graph settings (EP-1070 formulation), with known construction-side upper barriers and open lower-target regime.

## Literature
- Open problem; modern bounds include upper-side constructions around $(1/4+o(1))n$ scale and classical spindle-based templates.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Replaced stub with a standalone deep randomized construction search based on spindle-gluing proxy families.
- For each size, ran many trials and computed MIS by bounded exact branch-and-bound.
- Deep run scale:
  - sizes: $n\in\{49,61,73,85\}$,
  - $96$ trials per size,
  - bounded exact MIS kernel per trial,
  - total runtime about $107$ seconds.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Best-found ratios in this proxy family stayed near
\[
\alpha/n\approx 0.306\text{ to }0.315,
\]
close to the classical spindle barrier regime.
- Mean ratios clustered near $0.325$ across tested sizes.
- Many large-size trials hit MIS budget caps; reported values there are lower-bound style from bounded exact search rather than full exact global optima.
