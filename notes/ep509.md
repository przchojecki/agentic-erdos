# EP-509

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

### Source: ep509_partial.md

## Statement
Given monic non-constant `f \in \mathbb{C}[z]`, can
\[
\{z\in\mathbb{C}: |f(z)|\le 1\}
\]
always be covered by circles with total radius at most `2`?

## What is resolved from background
Recorded partial progress:
- Cartan: total radius bound `2e`;
- Pommerenke: improved bound `2.59`;
- Pommerenke (connected case): target constant `2` holds when the lemniscate is
  connected.

## What remains open in this note
General (not-necessarily-connected) sharp bound `2` remains unresolved here.

## New Experiments
- 2026-03-12T10:45:42.341Z: standalone random-polynomial lemniscate cover
  heuristic (`scripts/ep509.mjs`).
- Method: sample `|f(z)|<=1` on a fine grid, split into components, cover each
  component by one enclosing circle (Ritter heuristic), then sum radii.
- Over tested random monic families (`deg=3,5,8,12`), all sampled instances had
  heuristic total radius sum `<=2` (means around `1.05..1.31`).
