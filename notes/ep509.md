## Statement
Given monic non-constant `f \in \mathbb{C}[z]`, can
\[
\{z\in\mathbb{C}: |f(z)|\le 1\}
\]
always be covered by circles with total radius at most `2`?

- Pommerenke (connected case): target constant `2` holds when the lemniscate is
  connected.

## Added Proof Layer (No New Computation)

### Closed Subcase
If the lemniscate
\[
\{z:|f(z)|\le 1\}
\]
is connected, the sharp total-radius constant \(2\) is known (Pommerenke, as
recorded in background). So the connected case is theorem-level solved.

### Remaining Open Core
The unresolved part is the fully general (possibly disconnected) case with the
same sharp constant \(2\).

### Blocking Lemma
- Need a component-interaction inequality that sums local coverings without
  losing mass across many disconnected components, matching the connected-case
  constant exactly.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
For every monic nonconstant \(f\),
\[
\{z:|f(z)|\le1\}
\]
admits a circle cover with total radius \(\le2\).

### Step A: Component Decomposition with Sharp Budget
Write the lemniscate as disjoint components \(K_1,\dots,K_s\) and assign each a
local budget \(R_i\) with \(\sum R_i\le2\) candidate target.

### Step B: Capacity/Area-to-Radius Transfer
Prove each \(K_i\) can be covered by circles of total radius \(\le R_i\), where
\(R_i\) is controlled by a potential-theoretic quantity additive (or
subadditive with negligible defect) across components.

### Step C: Global Assembly
Sum Step B bounds to get total radius \(\le2\).

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need an additive inequality for disconnected lemniscate components at the sharp
constant:
\[
\sum_i \Phi(K_i)\le 2,
\]
where \(\Phi\) majorizes minimal circle-cover radius and equals \(2\) in the
connected extremal regime.

### Method Directions
- Potential theory / logarithmic capacity decomposition.
- Polynomial critical-value geometry controlling component interaction.
- Red-blue decomposition of components by level-set ancestry to bound overlap
  losses in cover assembly.
