## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

## Statement
Let `f(d)` be the largest size of a set in `R^d` such that every triangle formed
by three points is isosceles. Determine growth/precise values of `f(d)`.

## What is known from background
Known explicit lower constructions achieve roughly quadratic size
(`\binom{d+1}{2}` and variants), while best general upper bounds are also
quadratic scale with a nontrivial gap in constants/precise values.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine exact asymptotic constant in quadratic growth of \(f(d)\).

### A. Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)

Prove a distance-multiplicity rigidity lemma: in an all-triples-isosceles set \(S\subset\mathbb R^d\), the number of distinct distance classes from any point is uniformly bounded, forcing strong global structure close to known constructions.

### B. Reduction Lemmas (with proofs)

#### Lemma B1 (triple condition reformulation)
“Every triangle is isosceles” is equivalent to: for any distinct \(x,y,z\in S\), at least one of \(|x-y|=|x-z|\), \(|x-y|=|y-z|\), \(|x-z|=|y-z|\) holds.

**Proof.**
Definition of isosceles triangle in metric terms. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B2 (star-graph constraint)
Fix \(x\in S\). Partition \(S\setminus\{x\}\) by distances to \(x\). Any large spread of classes induces a non-isosceles triple with \(x\), unless inter-class pair distances satisfy rigid equalities.

**Proof.**
If \(y,z\) are in distinct classes from \(x\), then to keep triangle \(xyz\) isosceles one must force \(|y-z|\) to equal one of the two radii; repeating across many classes imposes strong compatibility constraints. \(\square\)

#### Lemma B3 (A implies quadratic extremal law)
If A forces near-classification into known structured families, then \(f(d)\) matches their quadratic size up to lower-order terms.

**Proof.**
Upper bound from structural rigidity and counting; lower bound from explicit construction. \(\square\)

### C. Main unresolved point

The bottleneck is proving full rigidity of inter-class distances in high dimension without assuming additional symmetry.
