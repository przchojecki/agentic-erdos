# EP-669

## Problem Statement
For \(n\) planar points, let \(F_k(n)\) be the maximum number of distinct lines containing at least \(k\) points, and \(f_k(n)\) the analogue for exactly \(k\) points.
Estimate these quantities; in particular study limits of \(F_k(n)/n^2\) and \(f_k(n)/n^2\).

## Literature
- \(k=3\) (Orchard-type) is asymptotically settled: both \(f_3(n)\) and \(F_3(n)\) are \(n^2/6 - O(n)\).
- For general \(k\), only broad bounds are known.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Built explicit-family rich-line counter with exact normalized line representation.
- Computed \(F_k\)- and \(f_k\)-proxies for:
  - square grids,
  - perturbed-circle families,
  with \(k=3,4,5\).

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Square grids produce nontrivial \(k\)-rich line counts; normalized values are small but structured.
- Perturbed-circle family contributes almost no \(k\ge 4\)-rich lines in tested ranges.
- Finite family behavior shows strong construction dependence, reinforcing that \(k=3\) asymptotics do not directly transfer to larger \(k\).

## Status
- \(k=3\): asymptotically resolved.
- General \(k\): open.

## Added Proof Layer (No New Computation)

### Closed Subcase
For \(k=3\), Orchard-type asymptotics are known:
\[
f_3(n),F_3(n)=\frac{n^2}{6}-O(n).
\]
So the \(k=3\) limit behavior is theorem-level settled.

### Remaining Open Core
For fixed \(k\ge4\), sharp asymptotics and limits of \(F_k(n)/n^2\),
\(f_k(n)/n^2\) remain unresolved in this note.

### Blocking Lemma
- Need a higher-\(k\) incidence structure theorem matching the sharpness of the
  \(k=3\) case, with controlled error terms at quadratic scale.

## Deeper Proof Program (A => B => C)

### Target
Determine existence/value of limits
\[
\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{F_k(n)}{n^2},\qquad
\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{f_k(n)}{n^2}
\]
for fixed \(k\ge4\), with sharp constants.

### Step A: Extremal Template Identification
Identify candidate near-extremal geometric templates for \(k\)-rich lines and
prove they attain lower bounds at quadratic scale.

### Step B: Upper-Bound Stability
Prove any near-extremal configuration must be close to one of those templates
(incidence stability).

### Step C: Constant Extraction
Compute template constants and pass to limits via Step B.

### Concrete Blocking Lemma (Most Critical)
Need a quantitative incidence-stability theorem for \(k\)-rich lines:
if \(F_k(n)\ge c n^2-o(n^2)\), then the point set is \(o(n)\)-edit-close to a
structured family with explicitly computable \(k\)-rich-line density.
