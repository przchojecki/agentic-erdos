# EP-836

## Problem Statement
For an $r$-uniform intersecting hypergraph $G$ with chromatic number $\chi(G)=3$:
1. must $|V(G)|=O(r^2)$?
2. must there exist two edges with intersection size $\gg r$?

## Literature
- Erdos-Shelah problem.
- Alon provided a construction disproving the first claim (super-polynomial in $r$ size).
- Erdos-Lovasz proved that some pair of edges has intersection at least $\gg r/\log r$.

## Our Approaches / What Is Proven
- Implemented finite construction checks for Alon-style family at $r=3,4,5,6$.
- Verified pairwise-intersection property directly.
- For small instances ($r=3,4$), brute-force coloring search confirms not 2-colorable and 3-colorable.

## Computation-Guided Observations
- Construction sizes grow quickly: e.g. $(|V|,|E|)=(16,35)$ at $r=4$, $(43,126)$ at $r=5$.
- Finite checks match the role of this family as a counterexample to the $O(r^2)$ vertex bound.
- The second question (forcing $\gg r$ intersections) remains unresolved by these computations.

## Status
- First subquestion: false.
- Second subquestion: open.
- Added standalone construction-verification computation and normalized data integration.
