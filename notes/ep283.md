\[
m=\sum_i p(n_i)
\]

coverage/noncoverage laws by polynomial class.

### Step A: Decomposition algebra
Translate split operations on unit fractions into additive moves on \(m\)-values.

### Step B: Semigroup generation analysis

## Lemma Chain (with Proofs)

### Lemma 1 (Split identity)
For \(a,b>0\),
\[
\frac1a=\frac1{a+b}+\frac1{a(a+b)/b}
\]
whenever \(b\mid a(a+b)\). This creates a legal refinement of an Egyptian
decomposition.

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Compute RHS:
\[
\frac{1}{a+b}+\frac{b}{a(a+b)}=\frac{a+b}{a(a+b)}=\frac1a.
\]
\(\square\)

### Lemma 2 (Additive update on polynomial weight)
If a decomposition step replaces denominator \(a\) by \(u,v\), then weighted sum
updates by
\[
m\mapsto m-p(a)+p(u)+p(v).
\]

\[
\textit{Proof.}
\]
Definition of \(m=\sum p(n_i)\) is additive over terms.
\(\square\)

### Lemma 3 (Semigroup reduction)
If the set of possible increments
\[
I:=\{-p(a)+p(u)+p(v)\}
\]
from admissible splits is finite modulo gcd \(g\), then reachable \(m\)-values are
eventually periodic modulo \(g\) and form finite-union translates of a numerical
semigroup.

\[
\textit{Proof sketch.}
\]
Repeated application of Lemma 2 yields walk on \(\mathbb Z\) by increments in
\(I\). Standard numerical-semigroup/automaton arguments give eventual periodicity
modulo \(g=\gcd(I)\).
\(\square\)
