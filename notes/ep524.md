# EP-524

## Proof Attempts and Literature Notes

### Source: ep524_partial.md

## Statement
For random-sign binary-digit polynomials, study
\[
M_n(t)=\max_{x\in[-1,1]}\left|\sum_{k\le n}(-1)^{\epsilon_k(t)}x^k\right|
\]
and determine typical almost-sure order.

## What is resolved from background
Known partial bounds show both small fluctuations along infinitely many `n` and
large limsup growth above `n^{1/2-\varepsilon}`.

## New Experiments
- 2026-03-12T10:57:09.282Z: standalone deep Monte Carlo sup-norm run
  (`scripts/ep524.mjs`) over `n=200..1200`.
- Empirical averages satisfy:
  - `E[M_n]/sqrt(n) \approx 1.08..1.14`,
  - `E[M_n] / sqrt(n/loglog n) \approx 1.45..1.53` on tested range.
